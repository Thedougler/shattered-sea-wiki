Right then. Roll the recorder, breathe from the belly, and read this like you mean it — every silly syllable. The machine behind the curtain is listening to the *shape* of your voice, not the sense of the words, so give it range: low growls, high squeaks, a whisper, a bellow.

Brave Benjamin the brittle brigand baked a batch of barnacle biscuits below the bilge. Quick! Quiet! Quibble! Quack! The quivering quartermaster quietly questioned the quality of the quince.

Now soft, like you're confessing a secret to a seagull: "I have never, not once, trusted a man who keeps his teeth that clean." Hold it. Let it sit. Then snap into a shout — "ALL HANDS, SHE'S COMING ABOUT!" — loud enough to wake the drowned.

She sells sea-shells, but the shells she sells are surely stolen, so the sheriff seized seventeen sacks of suspicious shellfish by the shimmering shore. Say it faster. Faster. Now slow it down until each word drips like tar off a railing.

Zebras and xylophones and a jittery jackdaw juggling jagged jade. The phlegmatic philosopher photographed a phosphorescent pheasant. Th-th-this, that, these, those — feel your tongue trip and let it.

Do a voice now. Any voice. The grizzled captain. The nervous cabin boy. The smug merchant who has never once paid full price for anything. Then drop it and just be you again, narrating the weather: "Grey. Wet. The kind of cold that gets into the rope and never leaves."

Vivid, vexing, voluminous vowels: aaah, eeee, iiii, ooooh, you. Roll an R until it runs out of road — rrrrrrum, rrrrrudder, rrrrrogue. Hum. Sigh. Laugh once, for real, because this is a ridiculous thing to be doing and that's exactly why it works.

One last line, full chest, no holding back, and then you can stop: "I am the salt in the wound and the wind in the sail, and I have a great deal more to say — but the recording, mercifully, ends here."
