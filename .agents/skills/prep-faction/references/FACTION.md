# Wiki DnD — Faction Prep

> **Prerequisite:** `../PREP.md` loaded (philosophy, universal rules, PC gravity, naming conventions).

---

## Interview

If the user message doesn't already answer these, ask all at once:

- Setting/tone
- Faction concept (what holds them together)
- Which PC is entangled, and how

---

## Toy Fields (6)

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| `primary_goal` | What they always want |
| `consistent_method` | How they pursue it |
| `active_problem` | What's going wrong |
| `off_screen_action` | What they do when the party isn't watching — the sandbox engine |
| `performance_hooks` | How members are recognized — visual identity, vibe |
| `link_of_relevance` | Which PC is entangled |

`off_screen_action` is the most important field. It makes the world move between sessions. It must describe something observable — evidence the party can find, not internal faction state.
- ✓ "Seizes the Harbormaster's ledgers and installs their own inspector."
- ✗ "Continues to grow in power and influence."

Update this field after every session.

---

## Key Rules

- No org charts. One crisp table for Structure if needed — key nodes only, not political hierarchy.
- No council/political procedural content unless the GM explicitly asks.
- Factions pursue goals on timelines. If there's no ticking clock, it's a backdrop, not a faction.

---

## Output Sections

1. `## Lore` — 2–5 sentences. What holds them together, what they hide.
2. `## Structure` — optional table; key nodes only
3. `## Connections` — wikilinks to related entities
