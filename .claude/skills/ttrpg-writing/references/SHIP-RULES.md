> Summary: Ship mechanics for the Shattered Sea campaign. The ship as vehicle, home base, and mobile bastion. Uses 2024 D&D bastion rules as the core framework with adaptations for a moving vessel and a shared party bastion.

# Ship Mechanics — The Shattered Sea

# mechanics #ships #reference

---

## Overview

The party's ship functions as vehicle, home base, and mobile bastion. This document uses the **2024 DMG Bastion system** as its foundation, adapted for a ship and a **shared party bastion** — one structure owned and directed collectively by all PCs. Bastion facilities are available as soon as the party has the resources and access to install them — there is no level gate. Where 2024 rules are used directly, that is noted. Where something is adapted or homebrewed, that is also noted.

Ship combat and chases are not covered here. Use the vehicle rules in the 2024 DMG for those when needed.

---

## Ship Tiers

Three tiers. Tier determines capability, crew requirements, upkeep cost, and how many bastion facilities can fit aboard.

| Tier | Vessel Examples | Cargo | Guns | Cost (used) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Sloop, cutter, lugger | 20 tons | 2–6 | 800–2,000gp |
| **2** | Brigantine, schooner | 60 tons | 8–16 | 4,000–10,000gp |
| **3** | Frigate, galleon | 150 tons | 20–40 | 15,000–35,000gp |
| **4** | Ship of the Line, Man-of-War | 300 tons | 60–100 | 60,000–120,000gp |

Used/prize vessels cost 40–60% of new. A Tier 1 ship is attainable in early play. Tier 2 requires a significant job, prize, or faction backing. Tier 3 is late campaign. Tier 4 is end-game — fleet-command territory requiring serious wealth, faction patronage, or a major prize.

### Tier Progression Principle

Each higher tier is superior in almost all respects. A Tier 2 vessel is faster, tougher, better armed, and carries more cargo than a Tier 1. A Tier 3 surpasses Tier 2 in the same ways. A Tier 4 capital ship surpasses Tier 3 in hull strength, armament, cargo, and facilities — but not speed.

**The speed exception:** Large vessels sacrifice speed for mass. Tier 3 is slower than Tier 2; Tier 4 is slower than Tier 3. This is intentional design, not an error. A speed-optimized Tier 1 sloop may outrun a sluggish Tier 2 schooner, and a speed-built Tier 2 clipper may match a lumbering Tier 3 galleon — but these are exceptional configurations. The speed table represents a typical vessel of that tier, not a ceiling.

**The tier floor holds for all variants.** Within each tier, ships can be specialized toward speed, arms, defense, or cargo. That specialization does not push a vessel across tier boundaries. A Tier 2 cargo ship carries more than a Tier 1 armed ship. A Tier 3 speed-built frigate outpaces a Tier 2 speed-built clipper under equivalent conditions. A ship's tier determines its floor; customization determines which quality it maximizes.

- **Condition**: Pristine / Worn / Damaged / Wrecked — tracked narratively, with mechanical effects applied when relevant.

## Crew Roles

Every ship has a set of roles that must be filled for normal operation. Each role can be taken by a **PC** or a **hireling**.

- **PC in a role:** The PC adds their relevant ability modifier to checks made for that role's function.
- **Hireling in a role:** The role is performed competently at baseline. No modifier beyond the hireling's own stat block if a check is required.
- **Role unfilled:** That function either cannot be performed or is attempted at disadvantage.

### Role List

| Role | Function | Key Ability | Hireling Weekly Wage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Captain** | Command decisions, crew direction, morale. Required. | Charisma | — (PC or major NPC) |
| **Navigator** | Plotting course, reef avoidance, Maw crossings. Required for open-water voyages. | Intelligence (Navigator's Tools) | 10gp |
| **Bosun** | Rigging, sails, on-deck crew management. Required. | Strength or Dexterity | 8gp |
| **Gunner** | Weapon maintenance and direction. Required if guns are used. | Dexterity | 8gp |
| **Carpenter** | Hull repair, below-decks damage control. Required for repairs at sea. | Intelligence (Carpenter's Tools) | 6gp |
| **Cook** | Provisions management and crew sustenance. Required for voyages over 3 days. | Wisdom | 4gp |
| **Surgeon** | Treating wounds, managing casualties. Not required for operation, required for the Hospital facility. | Wisdom (Medicine) | 8gp |
| **Ordinary Sailor** | All other duties. Required in sufficient number to crew the ship. | — | 2gp |

### Minimum Crew by Tier

The minimum crew fills all required roles and provides enough ordinary sailors to operate the ship. Below minimum, all ship-related checks are made with disadvantage and speed is reduced by 20%.

| Tier | Minimum Crew | Full Crew |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 2–4 | 8–12 |
| 2 | 8–10 | 20–30 |
| 3 | 20–25 | 55–80 |
| 4 | 60–80 | 150–250 |

Minimum crew means the required roles are filled and the ship can operate. Full crew means all gun mounts can be manned and the ship performs at its full capability.

## Upkeep

Weekly cost regardless of whether the ship is sailing.

| Cost Component | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 | Tier 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Crew wages | 8–20gp | 40–80gp | 150–250gp | 400–700gp |
| Provisions | 5–10gp | 20–40gp | 70–120gp | 200–350gp |
| Maintenance | 5gp | 15gp | 40gp | 100gp |
| Berthing fees | 2–5gp | 5–10gp | 10–20gp | 30–60gp |
| **Total (approx)** | **20–40gp/wk** | **80–145gp/wk** | **270–430gp/wk** | **730–1,210gp/wk** |

### Crew Wages and Facility Hirelings

**[Homebrew clarification for shared bastion.]** The 2024 bastion rules list separate costs for facility hirelings. On a ship, crew members frequently fill both a crew role and a bastion facility role (e.g., the Surgeon crewing the Surgeon's Berth). When a hireling fills both a crew role and a facility role, they are paid at the **higher of the two rates**, not both. The crew wage totals above assume this single-rate rule. Facility hirelings who have no corresponding crew role are an additional cost on top of upkeep.

Missing upkeep is handled narratively. Crew will not work indefinitely without pay. Officers have options elsewhere. The DM determines when patience runs out.

## Travel and Navigation

### Speed

| Tier | Good Wind | Poor Wind | Calm (oars if fitted) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 70 miles/day | 35 miles/day | 15 miles/day |
| 2 | 90 miles/day | 45 miles/day | — |
| 3 | 75 miles/day | 35 miles/day | — |
| 4 | 60 miles/day | 25 miles/day | — |

**Note on Tier 3–4 speed:** Large vessels sacrifice speed for firepower, cargo, and survivability. Tier 3 is slower than Tier 2; Tier 4 is slower than Tier 3. This is by design. Speed-built variants of Tier 3 and 4 vessels (+15–20 mi/day) can recover much of this gap but cannot exceed the tier above at baseline.

**Speed-built variants:** Apply the Speed-Built profile (see Ship Variants) to represent racing hulls, courier configurations, or ships stripped of excess weight. These vessels are faster than the typical ship of their tier but retain all other tier advantages over the tier below.

Transit times from the geography document apply under standard conditions.

### Navigation Checks

When conditions make navigation uncertain — Midchain reef channels, Maw approaches, storms, unfamiliar waters — the Navigator makes an Intelligence (Navigator's Tools) check. Success: the route proceeds normally. Failure: add time and apply a complication.

| DC | Failure Consequence |
|---|---|
| 10 | Off course. +1 day. |
| 14 | Significantly off course. +2 days, minor encounter likely. |
| 18 | Lost. +1d4 days, serious complication. |

Tidewardens' charts grant advantage in charted waters. Contradictory Midchain charts do not.

**The Maw:** Compasses are unreliable within 5 miles of the Maw's edge. Magical navigation instruments give false readings. Star navigation is unaffected. A Compass Rose (see Magic Items) solves the compass problem directly. Crisdalynn's cartography background reduces Maw approach DCs by 2.

### Downtime at Sea

On voyages of 3+ days, characters can perform **downtime activities** per the 2024 rules. Activities requiring a specific location or instructor cannot be performed at sea. Bastion facility benefits still apply if the facility is aboard the ship.

Long rests: standard 2024 rules. A ship with a functioning Cook role allows characters to recover an additional 1d6 HP on a long rest. A storm during the rest requires a DC 12 Constitution saving throw or the rest grants no benefits.

## The Ship as a Bastion

The party's ship functions as a **mobile shared bastion** using the 2024 DMG Bastion system. All PCs are co-owners of a single bastion. Bastion facilities can be installed as soon as the party has the resources and access — either through a shipyard (for structural modifications) or through a skilled craftsperson (for instruments and magic installations).

### When Bastion Mechanics Activate

**Immediately**, once the first facility is installed. There is no level requirement to begin building. The Bastion Turn (once per in-game week) applies from the moment the first assignable facility is operational.

### Ship Tier and Facility Space

The 2024 bastion uses a space unit system. Adapt it to ship tier:

| Ship Tier | Available Space Units | Special Facility Slots |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 4 | 0 |
| 2 | 8 | 1 |
| 3 | 16 | 2 |
| 4 | 24 | 3 |

Upgrading to a larger ship expands the bastion. Facilities from a smaller ship can be transferred to a larger one during a refit: same installation cost, half the time, requires a port with a shipyard.

**Special Facility Slots:** Special facilities (the advanced 2024 bastion options normally unlocked at character levels 5+) are instead unlocked by ship tier. A Tier 2 ship can install one special facility; a Tier 3 ship can install two. Special facilities requiring a fixed location (Teleportation Circle, Greenhouse, Stable) remain excluded.

### Facility List

The 2024 DMG facility list maps to shipboard equivalents below. Use the 2024 rules for each facility's benefits, costs, hireling slots, and bastion turn actions. Rename for tone.

| 2024 Facility | Ship Equivalent | Space |
|---|---|---|
| Arcane Study | Navigator's Chart Room | 1 |
| Armory | Weapons Locker | 1 |
| Barracks | Crew Berths | 1 |
| Gaming Den | Officer's Mess | 1 |
| Garden | Provisions Store | 2 |
| Guild Hall | Trading Post | 2 |
| Hospital | Surgeon's Berth | 1 |
| Laboratory | Tinker's Workshop | 2 |
| Library | Chart Archive | 2 |
| Pub | Ship's Galley | 1 |
| Reliquary | Trophy Room | 1 |
| Sanctuary | Waveservant Shrine | 1 |
| Smithy | Carpenter's Shop | 1 |
| Storehouse | Expanded Cargo Hold | 2 |
| Trophy Room | Captain's Cabin | 1 |
| War Room | Helm and Command | 1 |
| Workshop | Rigger's Workshop | 1 |

2024 facilities with no nautical equivalent (Demiplane, Greenhouse, Stable, Teleportation Circle, Theater, Sacristy) are excluded. Don't force them.

**Note on the Sanctuary (Waveservant Shrine):** Installing this facility represents a permanent propitiation arrangement with Umberlee's clergy. The passive benefit is that the ship is treated as having made the standard departure offering before every voyage. This does not guarantee safety — it means Umberlee has been paid. See Umberlee Lore.

### The Bastion Turn (Moving Vessel Adaptation)

**[Homebrew adaptation of 2024 DMG Bastion Turn rules]**

The 2024 Bastion Turn assumes a stationary structure. The following adaptations apply to a mobile bastion:

**Bastion Turn frequency:** Once per in-game week, same as 2024 RAW. The turn resolves at the end of each week regardless of whether the ship is in port or underway. Port weeks use the standard 2024 Bastion Event table as written.

**Facility function while underway:** All facilities function normally at sea provided the ship has its minimum crew filled. A facility whose hireling is also crewing the ship (e.g. the Surgeon who is also filling the Surgeon's Berth) counts for both purposes.

### Shared Bastion Direction

**[Homebrew — shared party bastion rule.]** In 2024 RAW, each character directs their own separate bastion. This ship is one shared bastion. The following rules govern how five players direct a single structure.

**The Captain is the Bastion Manager.** The Captain has final authority over the Bastion Turn — they assign hirelings to facility actions and resolve any disputes. If the Captain role is held by a PC, that player makes these calls. If the Captain is an NPC, the party decides collectively and the NPC executes.

**Each PC may personally direct one facility.** On the Bastion Turn, any PC may claim one facility and direct its action for the week. They receive any personal benefits that facility grants to its director (crafting results, research outcomes, downtime bonuses). The Captain cannot override a PC's personal facility direction unless that facility is currently unassigned.

**Passive benefits go to everyone.** All PCs aboard the ship benefit from passive facility effects: the Cook's HP recovery on long rests, the Hospital's healing options, the Chart Archive's navigation references. A PC does not need to have directed a facility to receive its passive benefit.

**Active directed benefits go to the director only.** If the Navigator's Chart Room grants a downtime bonus, the PC who directed it that week receives it. If no PC directs a facility, the Captain assigns a hireling and the benefit is held in reserve or lost, per the 2024 facility rules.

### Bastion Events (Moving Vessel)

The 2024 Bastion Event table assumes a fixed location. Replace the location-based events (local threats, neighbors, weather damage) with the following when rolling on the event table at sea:

| 2024 Event Type | Shipboard Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Local threat / attack | A hostile vessel has been tracking the ship. The DM determines the nature and timing. |
| Beneficial contact | A passing ship offers useful information, trade, or an unexpected opportunity. |
| Structural damage | A storm or incident has damaged a facility. Repair cost as per the 2024 rules. |
| Windfall | A current or wind has dramatically shortened the current voyage. Arrive 1d4 days early. |
| Hireling issue | One hireling has a problem the party needs to resolve. Use the 2024 hireling event rules. |

If the ship is in port during the bastion turn, use the 2024 Bastion Event table as written.

### Bastion Defense (Moving Vessel Adaptation)

**[Homebrew adaptation of 2024 DMG Bastion Defense rules]**

The 2024 defense rules assume the party is absent from a stationary bastion. Adapt as follows:

**While the party is aboard:** No defense roll needed. Threats are handled through play.

**While the party is absent and the ship is in port:** Use the 2024 Bastion Defense rules as written. The ship is effectively stationary and the same rules apply.

**While the party is absent and the ship is underway** (crewed by hirelings): The ship is treated as a stationary bastion for defense purposes, with the following modification: instead of a fixed location's defense rating, use the ship's **crew quality** as the defense modifier. Full crew with experienced hirelings: +3. Skeleton crew or poor hirelings: +0. Below minimum crew: −2.

The nature of "attacks" on an underway crewless ship are limited to piracy, patrol interception, or weather events — all of which are narratively resolved based on the defense outcome.

## Acquiring a Ship

### Buy

Available at any port with a shipyard. Tidefall has a broker on the Dockfront. Asking price runs 10–15% high. A successful Charisma (Persuasion) check (DC 12) with a reasonable counter-offer closes the gap.

Second-hand ships may have undisclosed problems. The DM rolls secretly: on a 1 (d6), there is a meaningful defect the seller was aware of.

### Steal

Resolve through the fiction. The party needs a plan: who owns the ship, where she is moored, what the watch schedule is, how to get her out of harbour. Tidefall's main harbour is hard to steal from — the garrison watches the piers and logs everything. South Harbour on Calder's Tooth's eastern side is loosely administered. Ships in the outer bay waiting for a berth are accessible by rowboat and can simply sail once crewed.

### Mutiny

Built on crew relationships. Not every sailor flips because the party asks. Approach potential allies privately before the move. The outcome reflects the trust built beforehand.

### Prize

Taking a ship in combat. A sinking vessel can be saved if the winning crew boards and controls the flooding fast enough. The prize then needs crew to sail her to port. Stripping a ship for parts instead of taking her whole yields 2,000–4,000gp in parts (Tier 2) at the right buyer.

### Build

Requires a shipyard. Same cost as buying new, but custom specifications are possible. Build times: Tier 1 — 2–4 months. Tier 2 — 6–12 months. Tier 3 — 18–36 months. Tier 4 — 36–60 months. Catarina Da'Virelli can advise on unusual specifications and install modifications during construction at standard rates.

## Ship Variants and Customization

Ships within the same tier can be specialized at the time of construction or through later refit. A variant profile shifts which qualities the ship optimizes for. The tier floor holds regardless of variant: even a heavily cargo-optimized Tier 2 ship carries more than any Tier 1 vessel. Customization moves stats within the tier; it does not compress the gap between tiers.

Refit costs apply when converting an existing ship to a variant: roughly 10–20% of the ship's base cost at a shipyard, with 2–8 weeks of work depending on the scope of changes.

### Speed-Built

*Racing sloop, courier brigantine, pursuit frigate*

Lighter hull, extra canvas, stripped weight. The trade is speed against capacity and firepower.

- Speed +15–20 mi/day in good wind, +5–10 mi/day in poor wind
- Cargo reduced by roughly one-third
- Gun mounts reduced by roughly one-quarter
- Cost: +10–15% above tier baseline (custom rigging, light framing)

A speed-built Tier 2 clipper may match a standard Tier 3 galleon under ideal conditions. This is the intended exception — not a reason to treat tiers as equivalent.

### Armed

*Privateer, ship-of-war, heavily armed brigantine*

Maximum gun ports for the hull, reinforced gun decks, reduced carrying space.

- Gun mounts +25–30% above tier baseline
- Speed −10 mi/day in good wind
- Cargo reduced by roughly one-fifth
- Cost: +10% (gun port work, structural reinforcement)

### Defended

*Armored merchantman, tough coastal trader*

Heavier planking, copper sheathing, reinforced gunwales. Harder to sink; harder to sail fast.

- Hull Points +20%, AC +1
- Speed −5 mi/day in good wind
- Cargo reduced by roughly one-tenth
- Cost: +15% (materials and labor)

### Cargo

*Fluyt, bulk carrier, transport*

Deep hold, wide beam, minimal armament. Built to carry, not to fight.

- Cargo capacity +40–50% above tier baseline
- Gun mounts reduced by half (minimum 2 guns for self-defense at any tier)
- Speed −10 mi/day in good wind
- Cost: same or slightly below (simpler construction, less specialized materials)

### Crew-Optimized

*Indiaman, well-found merchant, company ship*

Better quarters, larger galley, dedicated officer spaces. Crew morale and retention are meaningfully better.

- +1 bastion facility space unit (better-organized interior)
- Hireling loyalty checks made with advantage
- No change to cargo, guns, or speed
- Cost: +5–10% (outfitting and interior work)

## Magical Enhancements

These are purchaseable items and installations. Not wonders — professional tools. Catarina is the primary access point for unusual items. Items marked **[RAW]** use a 2024 item directly or as a reskin. Items marked **[HB]** are homebrew.

### Common (Available in Tidefall)

**Weatherglass** [HB] — 200gp. Accurate 24-hour weather forecast within 100 miles. Does not function within 5 miles of the Maw edge.

**Signal Lantern (paired)** [HB] — 300gp per pair. Sends a message up to 25 words to the partner lantern at up to 30 miles.

**Mending Resin** [HB] — 150gp per application. Repairs 2d8 Hull HP over 8 hours, no check required. One application per hull section per 24 hours. Does not work on structural damage.

**Compass Rose** [HB] — 400gp. Immune to compass drift including the Maw. Gives true north reliably.

**Gale Lantern** [RAW — Lantern of Revealing reskin] — 200gp. Prow-mounted. Illuminates 120ft through rain and light fog.

### Uncommon (Faction contacts or specialist traders)

**Wind Caller's Boom** [HB] — 1,200gp. Fitted to the mainmast. Once per day: favourable wind for 4 hours, +30 miles/day regardless of conditions. Does not function in a storm or the Doldrums.

**Ghost Keel Coating** [HB] — 900gp, requires shipyard application. Advantage on Stealth checks at sea. Reapplication every 6 months.

**Fog Cannon** [HB — based on Fog Cloud] — 1,500gp. Deck-mounted. 60ft radius fog bank for 1 hour. Recharges after 24 hours.

**Depth Eye** [HB] — 800gp. Hull-mounted lens. See through water to 60ft (30ft murky) from below-decks.

**Sending Stone Anchor** [RAW — Sending Stones installed in helm] — 1,000gp. Sending spell once per day from the ship's helm.

**Tide Reader** [HB] — 600gp. Mounted instrument. Current speed and direction to 20ft depth; tidal predictions for known ports within 200 miles. Advantage on navigation checks in reef-heavy waters.

### Rare (Cat Da'Virelli, senior faction reward, or significant expenditure)

**Cartographer's Table** [HB] — 8,000gp. Fixed chart table. Automatically maps the ship's route in real time. Charts produced are saleable (50–200gp each to Tidewardens or merchants). Does not function over the Maw below 200ft depth.

**Wardstone Figurehead** [HB] — 12,000gp. Ship has advantage on saves against magical effects. Hostile divination targeting the ship requires a DC 17 spell save or fails.

**Spirit Crew** [HB — Waveservant ritual required] — 4,500gp. 1d4+2 spectral sailors. Count as ordinary sailors: no wages, no provisions. They have their own temperament and will not perform actions that violate what binds them. The Waveservants ask one question about how they died before performing the ritual.

**Arcane Artillery** [HB — Cat Da'Virelli design] — 3,000gp per gun mount. Force-bolt cannon, no ammunition. 6 charges per short rest. Unaffected by wet or fire. Cannot use special ammunition.

### Legendary (Late campaign only)

**The Drowned Keel** [HB — Antheri salvage] — 25,000gp. The ship can submerge to 60ft and operate for up to 2 hours per day. Crew do not need air during submersion. The hull becomes semi-translucent at depth.

## The Ship as Living Space

### Quarters by Tier

**Tier 1:** Captain's bunk in the stern, hammocks forward. Party and crew share close quarters. Bastion facility space is tight — 4 units total.

**Tier 2:** Captain's cabin (private, lockable). Two shared officer berths. Forward crew hammocks. Galley. Cargo hold. 8 units of facility space.

**Tier 3:** Officer cabins, wardroom, captain's suite. Full privacy for the party. 16 units of facility space.

**Tier 4:** Flag officer suites, captain's great cabin, full wardroom and gunroom, midshipmen's berths, dedicated surgeon's and carpenter's quarters. The party can maintain entirely private accommodations with personal stewards. 24 units of facility space.

### Captain's Locker

Standard on Tier 2 and above. Lockable iron box bolted to the captain's cabin floor. DC 18 to break or pick.

### Ship Reputation

**[Homebrew — simple tracking.]** The ship's name and flag are known. Track reputation with each major faction and in key ports. Reputation affects berth access, hiring quality, and NPC first impressions.

| Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| Respected | Reduced berth fees. Willing hires. Faction contacts available. |
| Known | Treated on actual deeds. Neutral default. |
| Wanted | Port access restricted or denied. Bounties posted. |
| Notorious | Capable crew seek them out. Some targets surrender rather than fight. |

Flying no flag is suspicious. Flying a false flag is common and carries legal risk if caught by the Dravosi.

## Starting Situation

Level 1. No ship. Getting one is the first practical problem.

Immediate options in Tidefall:

- **Hire on as crew** to earn money and position.
- **The unflagged ship** in the outer bay has been there eleven days, has not communicated with the harbormaster, and has not tried to leave.
- **Two decommissioned hulls at South Harbour** — one is impounded by the Council on a debt that has been sitting unresolved for three years.
- **Dav Morrow** at the Saltgrave Market has a salvage contact with a problem.
- **Take a job that pays in ship access** — the Tessarine need discrete couriers; merchants need one-run crews with finder's fees.

Bastion facilities are available the moment the party can afford and install them. A Tier 1 sloop with 4 space units and one facility is still a bastion.
