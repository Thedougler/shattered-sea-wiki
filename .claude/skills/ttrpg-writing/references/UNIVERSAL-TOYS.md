# Universal Toys — Campaign Setup, Clocks, Session Zero, Theatrical Mechanics

Load this file when the request involves: campaign setup, Toy Chest, Session Zero, clock design, theatrical mechanics, or stakes-first narration.
Do NOT load for single NPC/encounter/location/faction/monster requests — those route to sub-skills.

---

## Clocks

Every toy has a timeline that advances regardless of player action.

```
Day 1: [early pressure — visible but ignorable]
Day 2: [escalation — someone is affected]
Day 3: [faction involvement — harder to ignore]
Day 5: [point of no return — world changed]
```

Players do nothing → things change anyway. Urgency, consequence, living world.

---

## Toy Chest (Campaign Setup)

A one-time reactive palette the GM draws from indefinitely. Not a timeline — a pressure system.

**Target:**

- 6–10 NPCs
- 3–5 Factions (each with an active off-screen action)
- 2–3 Locations
- 3–5 clocks already running

**Lore Sheets:** evocative jargon and thematic flavour per toy. Mile wide, inch deep.

**Layering:** connect toys lightly via shared NPCs, conflicting goals, overlapping locations. When toys intersect, emergent story generates itself.

## Session Zero

Ask every player:

1. What is your character trying to *become* by the end of this campaign?
2. What are they *afraid* of losing?
3. Who do they love, or who did they love?
4. What does failure look like for them personally?
5. What "red button" makes them throw caution to the wind?

Wire campaign tentpoles to these answers. The rails exist — but players *want* to follow them because they built the rails themselves.

## Theatrical Mechanics (on request)

**Degrees of Success** — 3-tier DC (10 / 15 / 20). Failure must move the story forward, never stall it.

**Mechanical Shenanigans** — one character-specific gamble per session: *"Offer [Character] advantage / reroll / automatic success if they [pay a meaningful character cost]."*

**Box of Doom** — flag climactic rolls. Declare DC and both outcomes publicly before dice fall.

---

## Stakes-First Narration

Always lead with what characters stand to lose. Not room dimensions. Not lore. Not atmosphere for its own sake.

**Default structure:**

1. What is at risk *right now*
2. What the characters can perceive that makes the stakes legible
3. Environment only if it directly affects the stakes

- GM script: 2–4 sentences. Stakes first, environment second. No invented emotional beats.
- Player prompts: two per scene max. Types: Self-Discovery / Environmental Gap / Mechanical Interpretation.

❌ *"You enter a large stone chamber. Torches line the walls."*
✅ *"The chanting has already started. You can see the altar from here — and the figure bound to it. The chamber is large enough that splitting up might be your only option. And your only mistake."*
