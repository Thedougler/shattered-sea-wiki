## At a Glance

41-year-old silver fox Admiral in a red coat with gold epaulettes. Youngest Admiral in living memory—bought the title with charm before anyone official handed it to him. Smiles with his eyes. Built by the sea, held together by charm. The five captains he lost to Umberlee are pressed into his skull as voices—he is never alone in his own head.

## Mechanical Snapshot

Swashbuckler Rogue with a twisted Mortis: multiple personality disorder. Five captains' souls pressed into him by Umberlee activate as personalities, each bringing skill proficiencies and borrowed mastery in exchange for never being fully alone in his own head. Quick assessment and action, prefers talking his way out (better coin in it). Red coat functions as Cloak of the Manta Ray (Assassin's Creed Black Flag flavor).

**Return trigger:** A brass flagon snaps Delmar back to the surface from any captain surfacing. Tied to the night after the mutiny — stranded with nothing, five people who stayed, one brass flagon passed around. The moment the fleet was born. His anchor to who he is before the captains.

## Personality & Drives

**What He Wants:** To find a way to resurrect his five lost captains—the souls Umberlee pressed into him as the fleet sank. If resurrection is impossible, to release them so they can find peace in their afterlives. Full resurrection is where his guardrails come off.

**What He Wants to Become:** Stable. Better than he was. Forgiven (though he knows it has to be earned). He hasn't let himself get far enough ahead to think about who he'll be.

**Core Belief:** His mother taught him—everyone has value regardless of station. He extends this as "everyone has use even if they don't see it in themselves." He's been taken advantage of, but he still extends generosity. He's developed an eye for reading people to protect himself.

**Red Line:** Loyalty to found family is absolute. Their trust is the most important thing. Do not say his crew are backstabbers—the smile stays but the eyes don't. Violence is on the table.

**Phobia:** Ferrets are insanely unlucky. Abject terror. Will exit the room immediately. Not weasels. Ferrets specifically. Learned as a boy.

## Current Status

Active in the Shattered Sea campaign. Carrying the voices of five captains every moment. Umberlee drowns his fleet, took five souls. The five captains whose souls reside in him were the original crew members who stayed loyal through every rank—the five he could have saved. The wound everything orbits.

**The Secret He Carries:** He was the one who made the theft that triggered it all. The crime that led to the fleet sinking. He didn't mean for it to end that way, but he made the call and five people paid with their souls.

**Items of Weight:**

- Pocket watch with mother's picture inside (only relic his father left; mother pickpocketed it)
- The Duchess (musket)

## Hardest Thing He Survived

As a kid: shipwreck during a pirate attack. Stranded on an island with a disgruntled, dangerous group of survivors. With help from one kind crew member, he built a raft and escaped. When he returned years later with a proper boat and crew to help the remaining survivors, he found only corpses. It hardened something in him—but he never lost the smile.

## Crisis Response

Group assessment, self-assessment, then a choice: fight, escape, or in what order those two happen. Unless there's an obvious way to talk his way out of it—he prefers verbal conflict resolution (better coin in it).

**When Genuinely Scared:** Almost nothing scares him. Ferrets do. Abject terror. Clear on his face.

## Relationships

**Whose Opinion Matters Most:** Crissdalynn. The sole other survivor of the wreck. She flew him out of the Maw when everything was going under. She is by his side.

**Who He'd Trust with His Life:** Crissdalynn, for the same reason—she saved him.

**Someone from His Past:** Margaret "Maggie" Porcelain Pots—the first love of his life. A dragonborn with glittering peach and cream scales and vibrant pink eyes. Takes no shit from anyone but an absolute sweetheart. They were together for a couple years but his wanderlust wasn't what she wanted. It ended in an argument—she punched him hard enough to leave a shiner for a week. He has avoided her on every return trip to his home island and hasn't seen her in years. The reunion would feel like both gift and threat. Would get a definite reaction out of the Admiral despite being happily married. His wife (The Duchess) would likely dislike Maggie at first—but once she hears the shiner story, she'd befriend her quickly.

**Likely Enemies:** Most likely the families of sailors who were lost, and the island council who funded the ships.
