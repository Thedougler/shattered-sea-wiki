## Overview

Old Faas is a rigger of uncertain age who has outlasted several captains, a number of ships, and at least two situations that should have killed him outright. He answers questions with short, accurate sentences. He does not raise his voice. Very little surprises him, and the things that do surprise him do not seem to bother him either.

He went up into the rigging immediately after Grigori gave the nod. He prefers to be useful in ways that keep him off the deck. The lines are always in want of something.

He was the one who told the party about Murrat, flatly, between courses: *"He took us to Murrat. Wanted a live one. Went ashore with six crew. Four came back needing the surgeon."* He reported it the way you report a weather event you survived.

He has recently returned from shore leave in [[content/shattered-sea/places/calveno/Calveno|Calveno]] with two peg legs.

---

## The Head

When Faas was seven years old, he fell from a hay loft and landed on his head on the stone floor of a barn. He was unconscious for three days.

When he woke up, something was different. Not wrong, exactly. Just gone.

He does not experience fear. Not suppressed fear, not managed fear — simply absent, the way a man born without a sense of smell does not smell things. He will tell you this if you ask. He does not think it is interesting.

> [!DM]
> This is not a death wish. He is not trying to die; he simply cannot feel the thing that would stop him from doing dangerous things. He has no anxiety about heights, no dread when outnumbered, no instinct to flinch when something is swinging at his head. He is not brave. Bravery requires something to overcome. He just goes ahead and does it.
>
> He is not impulsive either. He thinks things through. He concludes that the rigging needs climbing, so he climbs it. He concludes the scaffold might not hold, so he climbs it anyway because the view is better. The calculation happens. The feeling that would change the outcome does not.
>
> He has never mentioned this to a captain. It has never come up.

## The Legs

He returned from shore leave in [[content/shattered-sea/places/calveno/Calveno|Calveno]] with two well-fitted peg legs — good timber, brass ferrules, proper socket work, clearly the product of someone who knew what they were doing. He is slightly shorter than he was. He is slightly less stable standing still on a flat surface. He is faster on the rigging.

When asked about the legs, he has given three accounts. They are as follows:

**Account One:** *"The bull. Part of the festival. They were running it through the Mercatura and it got loose in the crowd near the Bridge. The right leg. I stopped it. Eventually."* He nods once. That is the end of that account.

**Account Two:** *"The scaffolding on the south side of the Bridge. They were doing repair work during the festival. The view from the top was better for watching the Beffa in Le Paludi. I could see it was not stable before I climbed. The left leg. I was correct about the view."* He says this with mild satisfaction.

**Account Three:** *"Both legs, actually. The canal under the Mercatura dock. I misjudged the depth. Current took me under the moorings and pinned me between the hull and the stone. Both legs. That was Tuesday."*

He has offered all three accounts to different people with identical conviction. He does not appear to notice they cannot all be true. He has not been asked to reconcile them and does not volunteer to do so.

> [!DM]
> The party will never learn which story is real, if any of them are. He is not being evasive — he answers exactly what he is asked. He simply does not seem to track the contradiction. If directly confronted with all three stories at once, he will listen carefully, nod, and say: *"Ja. It was a busy week."*
>
> The peg legs do not slow him on the rigging. Do not explain it.

## Aboard Ship

He manages rigging, sails, and on-deck crew. He is the kind of bosun who doesn't raise his voice because the crew learned early that quiet from Faas means worse things than loud. He knows every line on this ship, what it should feel like under tension, and how long it has left.

He will go aloft in any weather. Not because he is showing off — the lines need checking and he is the one who checks them. He has done this in conditions that made younger crew grip the rail and look away. He does not comment on this. Neither should they.

He has no strong opinion about staying or leaving. He is a sailor. There is a ship. That is sufficient.

---

## Recruitment

Ask for his assessment of the rigging and listen to the full answer. He will give a detailed, quietly passionate account — every line, block, and splice and what each needs. Twelve minutes, maybe more. Anyone who walks away partway through gets an evaluating look at their back.

*"Most people leave before the fore-topgallant."*

That's the bar. Stay through the fore-topgallant and he signs on.

> [!DM]
> He has no ideological objection to working for privateers. He simply wants to know if the new management is serious about the ship or just serious about what the ship can do for them. The distinction matters to him. The rigging speech is a test, not a lecture.

## Session History

### Session 03 — The Murrat Account

Told the party about the Murrat expedition flatly, between courses. Six crew went ashore with Rook to capture a live Moucheron. Four came back needing the surgeon. Faas was not among those four. He offered this without prompting and did not elaborate.

### Shore Leave — Calveno

Returned to the ship with two peg legs. Gave three contradictory accounts of how he lost his legs over the course of one week. Went back up the rigging approximately forty minutes after returning aboard.
