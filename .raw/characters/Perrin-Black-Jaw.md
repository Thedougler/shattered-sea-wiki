## At a Glance

Ten-year-old lean fancy rat in an oversized olive-drab hood — black head, white body, longsword that looks too big for him and a bodhran on his back. Shortest person in the party by a significant margin; compensates entirely with personality. Built a ship to fish and support his family. Lost everything to something massive from below. Now hunting vengeance while his family doesn't know the ship is gone. Recently discovered a patron god thinks he matters.

## Mechanical Snapshot

Charisma-based Bard/Warlock multiclass compensating for the Mortis ("The Small"): disadvantage on Strength checks/saves, +2 Charisma. Small frame makes him unsuitable for ship work, so he leads instead—reads people like water, identifies currents, finds what's useful. Not built for combat; relies on charm, manipulation, and knowing where the exits are.

## Personality & Drives

**What He Wants:** Vengeance against the creature that destroyed his ship. Also: to fish again (genuinely misses it). Also: to support his family and eventually become an elder of the Tangle, teaching young Rattkin how to move through the Dry without getting killed.

**What He Doesn't See:** The chase has become the point. The version of himself that comes out the other side may not be the version the Run hierarchy will recognize as one of their own. He will probably put his family in the path of vengeance to get it.

**Core Beliefs:**

- Loyalty to his Run specifically, not Rattkin broadly. Proud but not supremacist.
- Will not traffic people. Everything else—manipulation, bribery, blackmail—has flex.
- Does not mistreat those who cannot fight back. Just stops paying attention to them.
- Sleazy operator who sees people as useful or not useful. Accepts this about himself.

**The Heavy Thing:** He has gone quiet on his family since the ship went down. He knows it. He has not fixed it. The vengeance is easier to stay focused on.

## Current Status

Active in the Shattered Sea campaign at level 4. Recently acquired a patron—something large and otherworldly spoke to him after the sinking. He was not a believer before; now he is trying to understand what it means that a god apparently thinks he is worth something.

**The Secret:**
Family does not know the ship is gone. They know he is alive but not the scale of the loss. He is trying to fix it before he has to tell them. The Vestra was built and captained by Nona Black-Jaw's late husband — Perrin's grandfather — a family heirloom passed down when he died. Losing her is not just a financial blow. It is the last thing his grandfather left.

**What He Carries:**

- Cloak of the Manta Ray (family heirloom, origin murky, earned through service to the Vestra)
- Longsword
- Bodhran drum
- Clyde's Bestiary of Oceanic Creatures
- Guilt about crew members he thinks died

**Unresolved Threads:**

- A surviving crewmember might walk through a door (guilt dressed up as relief)
- Family does not know about the ship
- Does not know what destroyed it; only that it was large and from somewhere deep
- Dravosi bounties on Rattkin are a background pressure (real, rarely enforced, always present)

## Combat Notes

**Strengths:**

- Read people and situations before acting
- Social manipulation, charm, bribery
- Finding exits and escape routes
- Patron abilities (Warlock) + Bard spellcasting

**Weaknesses:**

- Prey response when genuinely scared (might squeak, cower, hide)
- Disadvantage on physical tasks (Strength checks/saves)
- Crissdalynn (large predatory bird) complicates his fear response
- Does not trust dry-landers the way he trusts family

**Crisis Response:** Looks for the nearest exit first—not necessarily to use it, just to know it is there.
