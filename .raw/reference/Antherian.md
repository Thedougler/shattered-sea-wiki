# The Antheri

> *No sounding line has ever found the bottom of where they went. The ruins do not tell you what happened. They tell you it was fast.*

**See also:** [[Drowned-Maw]] for the Shelfworks' current state and the planar mechanics of the Maw. [[Auralis]] for the last surviving Antheri. [[Catarina-DaVirelli]] for the foremost living expert on Antheri materials.

---

## Overview

The Antheri were an aquatic civilization centered on the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]. They disappeared centuries ago (minimum), and only [[Auralis]] survives; his soul-pledge transformation erased his pre-ritual memory. Their city is built into the Maw's western wall from ~60 ft down to unreached depths. Upper tiers have been salvaged for decades; lower tiers remain unexplored. Their engineering and materials still exceed anything in active use by current powers, including [[Halythion]].

The Antheri did not originate as a deep-ocean species. They were surface nomads who were driven into the abyss by a climate collapse and remade themselves there across generations.

---

## Origin History

### The Pre-Descent: Surface Nomads

Millennia before their apex, the Antheri were a nomadic, pelagic species — physiologically similar to early tritons or sea elves — hunting the sunlit epipelagic zones of the Scatter. They were vulnerable to surface weather, reliant on migratory fish patterns, and hunted by the ancestors of the modern [[Whip-Shark|Whip Shark]] and surface leviathans.

Their turning point was a localized extinction event: an ancient, prolonged shift in the Scatter Current collapsed surface temperatures and with them the shallow-water food chain. Facing starvation, the early Antheri followed the only remaining heat source — deep-ocean hydrothermal vents along the continental drop-off.

### The Mesopelagic Era: Chemosynthetic Shift

Driven into the twilight zone, traditional hunting and gathering failed. The earliest Antheri Biomancers invented deep-sea agriculture — selectively breeding and magically stimulating vent bacteria and deep-sea tube worms, then creating the first magically sustained kelp gardens. Generations in the twilight zone triggered magically accelerated evolution: enlarged eyes, hydrodynamic dermal denticles, and the replacement of gas-filled swim bladders with pressure-resistant lipid deposits.

### Discovery of the Fissure

As their population rebounded, they followed the vent lines deeper, eventually discovering the lip of [[Drowned-Maw]]. Their burgeoning electroreceptors sensed something impossible: an endless torrent of raw elemental energy bleeding up from the trench floor — the planar boundary to the Elemental Plane of Water. The early attempts to harness this energy and build the first tiers of [[Shelfworks]] were catastrophic; shifting planar pressures caused sudden implosions and elemental surges wiped out entire early settlements.

### The Technocratic Meritocracy Forms

Traditional leaders — kings, chieftains, priests — were entirely useless against the physics of the deep ocean. Survival demanded absolute, flawless engineering. The society reorganized around those who could keep them alive. The individuals who developed the first bio-metallurgic dark alloy — capable of withstanding both saltwater corrosion and abyssal pressure — were elevated by universal necessity. By the time they began building down the trench wall, the Antheri had completely shed their old structures. They were a civilization of architects, biomancers, and planar engineers.

## Physical Form

Aquatic humanoids. No preserved record confirms exact appearance. Ruin evidence:

- Doorframes and furniture built for a humanoid approximately 5–6 feet tall with a narrower shoulder profile than a standard [[Human]]
- Hand-scale markings on tools and instruments consistent with five-fingered hands
- Sleeping alcoves dimensioned for a body with no significant limb variation from human proportion
- **No pressure-management systems in architecture; no air pockets designed in; no provision for surfacing** — they lived in open water without equipment

> [!secret] Exact physical form is not established by the record. Options consistent with the evidence: gilled humanoids with a fish-like secondary colour layer, deep-adapted humanoids with cartilaginous skeletal features, or something close to human in shape with physiological differences that don't show in architecture. The ruins do not resolve it either way.

## Society

### Structure

The record is architectural, not documentary.

- **Upper shelf structures**: large-hall civic buildings, storage facilities, organized workshop spaces with fixed equipment stations — collective, not private
- **Residential tier** (mid-depth): small by individual standards, densely arranged, stacked rows cut into the trench wall. No significant size variation between units — no obvious palaces, no obviously subordinate housing
- **Deep structures**: singular, large, isolated, purpose-built — one facility served the whole civilization per function

Residential uniformity may indicate social equality, or hierarchy expressed outside housing. Not established.

Authority belonged exclusively to those who could solve the cascading problems of deep-ocean survival — Chief Architects, Biomancers, and Planar Engineers. Status was fluid, granted to those whose designs successfully stabilized a trench-wall section or harnessed a new sliver of the planar boundary's energy. A brilliant engineer whose edge dulled with age would step down, passing the mantle to a sharper mind. Inefficiency or flawed design was the only true crime — a single structural failure at depth meant catastrophic implosion for thousands.

### The Soul-Pledge Tradition

The ritual that transformed [[Auralis]] predates him. The deep city includes a permanent soul-pledge chamber sized and marked for repeated use, with symbolic language repeated across deeper tiers. This indicates a formalized soul-binding service tradition with established ritual vocabulary. What other participants were bound to, and where they are now, is not established.

**The Immortal Framework** — the ultimate expression of Antheri bio-adaptation. As they pushed deeper toward the fissure, the raw planar pressure and elemental radiation exceeded what any biological form could withstand. To study and manage the boundary, the Antheri technocrats combined biomancy, soul-binding, and planar mechanics to extract an individual's consciousness from their physical body and fuse it into an artificial, immortal framework. The result was less a creature than a manufactured demigod, engineered to survive the absolute bottom of the trench and bound irrevocably to a single specific purpose.

[[Auralis]] is not a natural deity or a chosen champion. He is the pinnacle of Antheri technology — the final iteration of a technocratic society that calculated they needed a god to guard a broken planar door, and possessed the brutal brilliance to simply build one.

> [!secret] The soul-pledge chamber may show evidence of multiple uses — markers on the floor or walls for different bindings. If some bindings are still active, what they were bound to protect may still be operating, or may have failed when the Antheri fell, with unknown consequences.

### Relationship with the Maw

The city was deliberately built on the Maw's western wall and extends *toward* the planar boundary. Deeper tiers become increasingly purpose-built, indicating intentional deep-city development rather than overflow. Evidence does not support a sealing attempt; deep structures are oriented at the fissure. The surviving record does not establish whether the Antheri were studying, exploiting, or transforming boundary interaction with the Elemental Plane. [[Halythion]] Remembrancers identify the Antheri as the civilization that operated closest to the Maw and report that their final deep-city project was never completed.

> [!secret] A party that reaches [[Halythion]] can access Remembrancer accounts of the Antheri. The sea elves watched them fall — they know the sequence from the outside: accelerating activity in the deep city, then silence, then nothing coming up from below. It is the most complete external record of the Antheri's final period.

### The Great Architectural Inversion

Every colonial scholar, salvager, and Tessarine engineer studying the [[Antheri-Ruins]] assumes the same thing: that the Antheri started on the safe upper shelf and built downward over centuries. They are wrong.

When the early Antheri discovered the planar fissure at the trench floor, they drove the first anchor of their civilization directly into the planar boundary. They built the ruins **from the bottom up.** The structures on the Shelfworks at 60–200 feet are not the foundation of the city — they are the newest additions, the last things built before the civilization vanished.

Physical evidence of the inversion for anyone who understands engineering:

- **Age of the alloy:** Dark-alloy in the upper Shelfworks is the most advanced material by modern standards but the newest in the city. As depth increases, bio-metallurgy becomes older, denser, and far more complex.
- **Inverted load-bearing:** The structural geometry of the mid-tiers does not hang from the continental shelf. The massive arches and dark-alloy struts are oriented to support weight pushing *up* from below. The city is a colossal spire stacked upward from the trench floor, leaning against the trench wall for stability.
- **The exhaust manifold:** The sprawling geometric lattices of the Shelfworks that salvagers currently plunder were never foundations. They are **exhaust vents** — a civilization-scale heat sink bleeding off catastrophic thermodynamic and magical pressure generated by the engine built on the planar fissure below.

> [!secret] The salvagers at the Shelfworks are blindly dismantling the exhaust valves of a pressurized planar engine that is still running. When the Antheri realized their civilization could no longer contain the widening planar boundary, they did not flee to the surface — they sealed the exhaust vents, abandoned the upper city, and triggered a final failsafe at the very bottom, leaving Auralis to hold the door shut from the inside.

### Bio-Adaptation Systems

- **Illumination via seeding:** The dark-alloy geometry of the Shelfworks and deeper ruins was carved with precise capillary-like channels, seeded with genetically modified hyper-bioluminescent algae. The algae fed on ambient magical radiation from the planar fissure, pulsing in synchronized waves that provided both eternal illumination and city-wide data transmission.
- **Accelerated aquaculture:** Vertically suspended kelp gardens anchored to the trench walls and bathed in bioluminescent arrays grew impossibly fast — a harvested grove could regenerate its mass within days, providing the caloric and material foundation for relentless expansion into the nutrient-scarce twilight and midnight zones.
- **Arcane biomimicry:** Pressure-regulators were grown from modified deep-sea sponges. Transit currents within the city were driven by massive magically stimulated cilia networks rather than mechanical pumps. Tools and infrastructure blurred the line between organism and architecture.

## Architecture and Materials

### Design Philosophy

The Antheri were a fully pelagic species — there are no stairs, no flat walkways, no pressure-sealed bulkheads, and no air pockets designed into any structure. Their architecture is composed of massive vertical drop-shafts, spherical chambers, and sweeping current-funneling archways built for beings moving effortlessly in three dimensions. The Antheri did not build walls to hold the ocean back; they engineered their city to let the ocean flow through it. The colossal machinery is entirely flooded by design, utilizing crushing pressure differentials and freezing upwellings of the trench as kinetic and thermal power sources. The overall structure reads less like a city and more like a planetary-scale bore, angled entirely downward.

### Materials

Antheri construction uses a stone composite that has not been identified by any current materials expert.

- Does not corrode. Does not foul. Biological growth does not adhere to it — salvaged pieces brought to the surface are as clean as the day they were set.
- Current metallurgy cannot reproduce the alloy used in their instruments and fittings.
- **Salvaged Antheri material is valuable.** The [[Tessarine-Concordat]] has standing purchase orders for intact structural pieces. [[Cats-Curios|Cat's Curios]] holds more Antheri instruments than any other private collection in the Scatter.

### The Vertical City — Depth Tiers

The Antheri city is a **vertical trench-wall structure**, not a seafloor sprawl. It descends from ~60 ft at the shelf edge toward unknown trench depth. Each tier has distinct function and condition.

---

#### Tier 1 — The Shelf (60–200 ft)

Outermost tier; reachable with standard diving and used by active salvage crews. Most damage is from long-term salvage plus recent entity incursion, not original collapse. Portable components are mostly stripped; fixed machinery, integrated structures, and some sealed storage remain. This tier preserves symbolic wall reliefs and the civic/administrative face of the city. Entity activity now makes routine Tier 1 routes unsafe.

---

#### Tier 2 — The Mid-Works (200–500 ft)

Below safe diving range without magic. Instrument reliability degrades here at the boundary-zone edge, so access is limited and preservation is higher than Tier 1. This is the primary residential and research zone: trench-wall housing clusters, workshops with still-functioning fixed machinery, and intact research spaces with instruments, materials, and Antheri script records. Soul-pledge symbolic language is integrated into structural design and appears **load-bearing**, not decorative.

---

#### Tier 3 — The Deep Works (500 Ft and below)

Boundary-zone conditions dominate here: active elemental diffusion, unstable pressure behavior, fluctuating visibility, distorted sound propagation, failed magical instruments, and inconsistent standard spell behavior. Deep Works are **pristine** and undisturbed by modern salvage. Structures are intact, larger, and singular-purpose rather than clustered. The soul-pledge chamber is in this tier and shows repeated formal use. The deepest known terminus structure is fissure-oriented, but its function is not externally legible.

> [!mechanic] Reaching Tier 3 requires significant magical resource investment. Water breathing alone is insufficient — elemental diffusion damages mortal physiology. Mechanically: ongoing CON saves against elemental pressure, spell failure chances, exhaustion from environment.

## Knowledge and Magic

Antheri planar understanding of the Maw exceeds current scholarship. Modern arcane practice treats the Maw mainly as hazard/navigation problem; Antheri practice treated it as a study domain and engineering medium.

**Capabilities established by the physical record:**

| Capability | Evidence |
|---|---|
| **Soul-binding** | Formal ritual architecture, stable symbolic vocabulary, evidence of repeated successful use. [[Auralis]] is one instance of a broader practice. |
| **Elemental integration** | Deep-works construction incorporates elemental water into structural function rather than working around it. How they managed this is not recoverable from the exterior. |
| **Material science** | Composite materials with no mortal equivalent. Instruments more precise than current equivalents. Mid-works machinery still functional after centuries without maintenance. |

> [!secret] Intact Antheri research materials in Tier 2 could provide significant arcane advancement to a party that can read the script or find a translation. [[Catarina-DaVirelli]] has reverse-engineered components from salvaged pieces and is the best current living non-Antheri expert on their material culture.

## The Fall

The Antheri are gone. Ruin evidence of the collapse exists but no current expedition has fully interpreted it.

**What the physical record shows:**

- No evidence of external attack on the shelf structures — the civic tier was abandoned, not destroyed
- No mass casualty event in the residential tier — sleeping alcoves and workspaces are empty, not occupied by remains
- Activity in the deep works was **ongoing at the time of the fall** — mid-process work present in mid-tier research spaces, tools set down rather than stored
- The fissure widened at some point — the current fissure width is larger than what the city was originally built around

The available record supports rapid civilizational failure, not gradual decline: mid-works appear abandoned mid-process.

## What Current Civilizations Know

- **Sailors and civilians:** ruins are old and underwater; material is valuable; diving there is dangerous right now. Do not know the civilization's name, history, or its relationship to the Maw.
- **[[Tessarine-Concordat]] salvage operation:** knows the Antheri name; has basic records of shelf tier layout. Does not know the depth or extent of what's below the shelf edge.
- **No colonial scholarly institution** has a working translation of the Antheri symbolic language.
