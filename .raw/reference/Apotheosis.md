## Lore

A cleric who achieved undeath through the belief of a religious following. The Apotheosis positions itself as the sole conduit to a god, builds a devoted cult, then ritually sacrifices itself — the cult's collective belief in its immortality becomes the phylactery. As long as members convene to affirm the Apotheosis's deathlessness, it reforms.

The mechanism requires active maintenance: a cult that stops believing, or is destroyed, severs the anchor. Apotheoses invest heavily in keeping their congregations loyal, terrified, or dependent.
