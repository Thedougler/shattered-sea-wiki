# Blight

![[Blight.webp|The Blight grown from the Death Bloom at the heart of Aruhe]]

## What a Blight Is

A Blight begins as an Archdruid who performed a ritual to fuse their soul and body to a piece of land they were trying to protect — killing themselves in the process. The result is a druid lich whose phylactery is not an object or a living being but **a place**: the Death Bloom, a location at the heart of the protected land where the transformation ritual was performed.

Unlike other liches, a Blight cannot simply be destroyed by finding and smashing a phylactery object. The Death Bloom is a location — a dungeon to be cleared, not an item to be stolen. While the Death Bloom remains intact, a destroyed Blight will reform.

The land the Blight was created to protect is now its hunting ground. The ritual's byproducts — acid drainage, corrupted soil, spreading decay — are the Blight's passive presence working on the environment around the Death Bloom.

## Combat Notes

The Blight is a tiered encounter. Its stat block changes in stages tied to the Death Bloom's health. Damaging the Death Bloom weakens the Blight; destroying it ends the Blight permanently.

- **Stage 1 (Death Bloom intact):** Full power — high CR, full action economy.
- **Stage 2 (Death Bloom damaged):** Degraded — reduced HP, weakened abilities.
- **Stage 3 (Death Bloom nearly destroyed):** Desperate — erratic, may use environmental hazards.
- **Death Bloom destroyed:** Blight cannot reform; final death.

## In [[Shattered-Sea|The Shattered Sea]]

**[[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/Aruhe|Aruhe]]** hosts an active Blight. The abandoned island interior is its dungeon. The Death Bloom is the encounter at the island's interior center. The acid drainage dissolving the old agricultural terraces is the Blight's byproduct working on the soil.

The [[Grung-Clans|Grung]] patrol the surrounding reefs but refuse to land. Their name for Aruhe translates roughly as "the island that is already eaten." They know what is there.

The [[Ancient-Blue-Dragon|ancient blue dragon]] that claims the Redwind Isles lies east of Karath, which is east of Aruhe. Grung awareness of both threats shapes their patrol behavior. The two do not interact directly — the dragon has no interest in Aruhe, and the Blight has no interest in the water.

The Blight is an **optional encounter**. It enters play if the party shelters at Aruhe, follows a drifting wreck there, needs to avoid Karath patrols, or investigates a chart note. It is not connected to the Sorn arc, Jean-Claude's thread, or the Grung escalation.
