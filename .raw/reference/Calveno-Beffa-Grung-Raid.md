# Calveno — Beffa Grung Raid

## Situation

Simone Tabarnack's western expansion culminates in an operation that will be remembered as a watershed: a mass slave raid on [[content/shattered-sea/places/calveno/Calveno|Calveno]] during [[content/shattered-sea/lore/Il-Gioco-delle-Beffe|Il Gioco delle Beffe]] that takes hundreds of the city's civic population — workers, sailors, dockside families, festival labourers — in a single coordinated strike.

This is not an ambitious coastal raid scaled up. It is a purpose-built urban operation, years of expansion converted into one proof-of-concept: the Grung can take people from the heart of a Central Strait commercial city in broad daylight, and the powers who were supposed to stop it either could not or chose not to.

The target reveal is late in the festival, ideally Day 4 of the three-day prank window. If the party catches enough of the preparation early, this becomes an attempted raid they can blunt rather than a fixed cutscene. But even a partial raid landing changes the world.

---

## Current State

Calveno does not expect Grung raiders. The city is a northern commercial hub across the [[content/shattered-sea/places/Central-Strait|Central Strait]], not a Midchain coastal camp near the [[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]]. The working assumption is that a [[Vethka|vethka]] can terrorize reef country and nearby island shores but cannot quietly project a disciplined capture operation into Calveno's festival crowds.

That assumption is about to fail — and Simone is the reason it fails now rather than in five years. She has been building toward this. The Dravosi toxin revenue funded long-range staging. The scout circuit's overextension bought her tactical lessons. The Rook channel burning did not stop her; it accelerated the timeline. The Calveno raid is the operation that proves westward reach to Gold-caste leadership and fills the slave-soldier machine with the calibre of captive it needs: urban workers, trained sailors, men who can be put to work or broken into compliance.

**The Dravosi Role**

The watch gap is not luck and not inference. Members of the Dravosi Crown provided Simone with the intelligence that made this operation possible: patrol schedules showing when naval presence in the Calven Roads would be minimal, structural information about gaps in Calveno's canal-watch routine, and detailed maps of the city's sewer network below the festival districts.

Who exactly made this exchange, and whether it was quid pro quo for the toxin supply, a faction play inside the Dravosi, or a deliberate policy of non-interference, remains unresolved. The operational fact is settled: Simone knew where Calveno's defenders would not be and how to move hundreds of people out of the city without surfacing on a canal. She did not guess that.

The [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concord]] is caught completely off-guard. Their commercial intelligence network in Calveno — factors, brokers, ledger watchers — is built to track money, not military operations. They have no warning, no response, and no explanation in the aftermath for how an unprecedented mass abduction happened inside a city they consider part of their commercial sphere.

## Narrative Devices

- **Loaded Gun:** Festival panic is plausible and funny right up until it is not. The city trains itself to wait a breath before reacting. Simone's teams get that breath.
- **The Slaad Problem:** Summoned Chaos creatures do not discriminate. The Slaad distraction is spectacular and uncontrollable — it pins the city's watch and Canal Commission response across multiple districts simultaneously. By the time defenders understand they are not dealing with an escaped monster, the sewer extraction is already running.
- **Foreshadowing:** Distant raid rumours, trade-cover Grung in places they have no commercial reason to be, a Dravosi patrol gap that looks scheduled rather than random, and workers being asked about crew shifts and which men sleep aboard their vessels.
- **Clock:** Each festival day advances the handlers from infiltration to route testing to synchronized strike.
- **Reversal Potential:** The Dravosi collaboration is the buried charge under the whole event. It does not explode on session five. It becomes legible later — when the party has leverage, or when the wrong person puts the pieces together first.

## The Operational Plan

Simone runs four interlocking elements. Each one fails if separated from the others; together they create the impossible operation.

**1. Dravosi Intelligence**
Before any handler enters Calveno, Simone has three things from her Dravosi source: the patrol rotation showing the three-day naval minimum in the Calven Roads coinciding with peak festival crowd density; a summary of the Canal Commission's festival-week staffing gap (half the inspectors rotate to crowd-management duty); and infrastructure maps of the sewer collector system below the Mercatura, Le Paludi, and the Bridge approaches — the routes that connect captive drop points underground to outer-harbour egress points.

**2. Blue-Caste Handler Infiltration**
Simone's elite handlers enter Calveno over Days 1–3 under festival trade, labour, and audience cover. The Beffa makes their reconnaissance invisible: strangers asking about crowd flow, boat shifts, and bridge timing read as prank preparation. Handlers map the precise sewer access points — maintenance hatches, flood overflow vents, the old tannery drain that exits below the outer quay — and time the surface-to-water transfer.

**3. Synchronized Strikes with Slaad Distraction**
On Day 4 of the Beffa, purple-caste raid teams execute five simultaneous snatch points across the festival districts: one in the Mercatura crowd, one near the Bridge, two in Le Paludi's working yards, and one at the harbour approach. The strikes are spaced far enough apart that initial witness accounts cannot agree on where the raid began.

Simultaneously, Simone's red-caste operatives release summoned Slaad into the open Mercatura and on the Bridge approach. The Slaad are not targeting the captives; they are targeting the city's capacity to respond. Watch captains, Canal Commission officers, and any organised response have to split between visible Chaos creature attacks across the festival's busiest zones and the screaming coming from raid teams they cannot yet locate. By the time the city understands these are two separate events, the extraction window is closed.

**4. Sewer Extraction to Outer-Water Pickup**
Captives move underground within minutes of seizure. The sewer network runs from all five strike points to two egress vents on the outer quay below the Canal Commission's festival-week field of view. *Vethka* are waiting beyond the mouth of the Calven Roads — the position where the Dravosi naval patrol minimum leaves the water unwatched. The crossing happens in the dark hours before the city finishes accounting for the Slaad damage.

## Key Facts

- Hundreds of Calveno's civic population are taken if the raid lands cleanly: dock workers, sailors, yard hands, festival labourers, boatmen, and working-age men whose absence will be felt by name, not as an abstract count.
- The Slaad are a distraction, not shock troops. They occupy defenders and distribute response capacity. The raiders are the fast, quiet element.
- Sewer egress — not canal movement — is the extraction plan. Surface movement ends at the first maintenance hatch. Everything after is underground.
- The Dravosi patrol minimum is a scheduled gap, not a coincidence. Someone inside the Dravosi provided the window.
- The [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concord]] has no warning and no operational response prepared.
- Multiple simultaneous strikes ensure witnesses contradict each other on where it started and what they saw.
- The first screams are plausibly mistaken for Beffa beats; the city has been conditioned by three days of false alarms to wait before reacting.
- This is the first Grung operation of this scale ever conducted in the Central Strait. It proves westward reach, Gold-caste ambition, and the operational gap between what the Grung threat was assumed to be and what it is.

- The Beffa execution window reaches Day 4 with handler prep unbroken.
- The party follows suspicious festival logistics, trade-cover Grung movement, or a worker disappearance before the strike.
- Jean-Claude identifies blue-caste handler logic in a crowd or extraction position.

## Blocks

- Expose a handler cell early enough that the raid loses synchronization — one missing cell means one strike point fails, not all five.
- Identify and seal a sewer egress point before the extraction runs; captives surface on a blocked route and the raid teams improvise in the open.
- Warn Warren or Passage contacts who can close the specific maintenance hatch accesses without triggering a city-wide crush.
- Pursue the *vethka* before they clear the Calven Roads; the patrol minimum is three hours, and that window can be shortened.
- Force the red-caste operatives to release the Slaad early (party disrupts their timing), reducing the distraction duration during the actual strike.

## Consequences

- **Raid lands cleanly:** hundreds taken. Tessarine Concord in crisis. Dravosi officially surprised but privately aware of their own role. Jean-Claude confronts an operation that only Simone could have built. Grung raids are now a Central Strait problem permanently.
- **Raid lands partially:** the party saves one strike cluster or blocks one egress route. The impossible crossing still happens; the scale is smaller. Simone has still proven westward reach.
- **Raid is broken early:** Calveno sees the preparation for a mass Grung slave raid during its own festival. Simone loses handlers, Slaad materials, or sewer intel. The Dravosi gap becomes a public question.
- **Party ignores the aftermath:** resentment, anti-Grung backlash, Tessarine insurance crisis, and competing narratives about Dravosi complicity harden while the collaborator chain remains useful leverage.

## DM Notes

### Historic Weight

This is the event the Shattered Sea will date from. "Before the Calveno raid" and "after it" will mean different things to different factions. The Tessarine Concord will remember it as the moment their commercial intelligence failed catastrophically. Dravosi officers will remember it as an event they officially condemn and privately understand. Midchain ports will remember it as the moment the range question was settled: the Grung can reach anywhere.

Jean-Claude will remember it as the moment he understood what Simone built while he was running.

### Reveal Frame

Do not open with the city announcing a raid. Open with a festival crowd trying to decide whether the scream is part of the act.

The most useful immediate image is absence after motion: a dropped tool roll, a boat left tied wrong, festival masks in trampled water, a family shouting a working man's name into a canal crowd that has already moved on. Then, one block away, the sound of something large and wrong in a confined space — not human, not festival, the Slaad confusion already separating the watch from the raid.

### The Slaad at the Table

The Slaad are not the encounter. They are the sound of the operation already running. If the party is near a Slaad when it appears, that is a real fight, and it should feel chaotic and dangerous and wrong — because it is deliberately wrong, planted here. If they try to understand the Slaad first, they lose extraction time. If they ignore the Slaad to chase the raid, the Slaad may follow or spread.

Do not resolve the Slaad as a clean set-piece. They are distractions playing as monsters.

### Table Function

This is a Simone escalation for Jean-Claude and a Calveno scar for the whole party. It should not replace the Beffa with forced tragedy if the party is actively enjoying the festival. Seed enough evidence that intervention changes how bad it gets. The party should feel they had the clues and could have acted earlier — but also that acting on partial information was genuinely hard to prioritise.

### Dravosi Read

Dravosi in Calveno are a common commercial and maritime sight. The unusual patrol absence should feel like a missing familiar pressure, not described as an occupation withdrawing from its own city.

The first clue is procedural: a patrol handoff, factor inspection habit, harbour observer, or scheduled escort presence is missing in a way another sailor can name.

The second clue is harder: someone had to know the gap would exist soon enough for Simone's handlers to plan around it. The sewer maps are the sharpest piece of evidence — the Grung extracted hundreds of people through Calveno's underground infrastructure with no wrong turns. That level of route knowledge does not come from reconnaissance alone.

### Tessarine Aftermath

Iacopo Fieschi is in Calveno during the festival. He is not taken. He is, however, present for the aftermath — and his job is to report to the Concordat's commercial ledger, not its military command. He will file an accurate account of what he witnessed and a deeply inaccurate account of what it means, because his frame is commercial intelligence and this was not a commercial event.

The Tessarine response will be slower, more bureaucratic, and more expensive than anyone expects — because the Concord has never had to respond to this before.
