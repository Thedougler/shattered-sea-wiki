# The Dravosi Crown

![[Dravosi-Crown-Flag.webp]]
The Dravosi Crown is the oldest colonial presence in the [[Shattered-Sea|Shattered Sea]]. It holds much of the [[content/shattered-sea/places/crown-islands/index]], controls the western mouth of the [[Central-Strait]], taxes traffic through its waters, and calls the whole arrangement administration.

In port, Crown power looks like navy-blue coats, brass buttons, dry stamps, inspection lines, sealed passes, and clerks who know exactly which form makes a problem worse. Its officers speak of safe passage, reliable charts, piracy suppression, ship standards, and the civilizing duty of law.

---

## Public Face

The Crown presents itself as lawful order at the edge of an unmapped sea. In the [[content/shattered-sea/places/crown-islands/index|Crown Islands]], that argument works on people who have cargo to move and cannot afford disorder. The Dravosi fleet keeps the western Strait navigable, the Harbourmaster's Office sells charts that are usually better than rumor, and a stamped certificate from [[content/shattered-sea/places/port-tidefall/index|Port Tidefall]] can open doors in every Crown-held anchorage.

The price is submission to the document. A ship is not safe because it is seaworthy; it is safe because its papers say so. A captain is not honest because the harbour knows them; they are honest because their manifest agrees with the rating stamp. The Crown's public cruelty is rarely theatrical. It is procedural, patient, and confident that the person objecting will tire before the paperwork does.

## Government and Law

The Dravosi colonial government in the Scatter is split between civil administration and Admiralty command. The Governor in [[content/shattered-sea/places/port-tidefall/index|Port Tidefall]] manages courts, fees, harbour law, seizure claims, and commercial permissions. The Admiralty controls forts, patrol cutters, convoy routes, and sealed military orders.

The two branches overlap constantly. If the civil office cannot seize a vessel, the Admiralty can inspect it. If the Admiralty cannot justify a detention, the Harbourmaster can discover an unresolved fee.

Tyr is the state faith of the Dravosi military and administrative apparatus. A Crown oath sworn before a Tyr priest is a legal instrument, not a spiritual comfort. Commission papers, courts martial, and high-value contracts carry Tyr's seal because the Crown wants law to feel older than the people enforcing it.

Common legal tools:

- **Rating inspection:** A vessel, crew, cargo, and destination are checked against Crown registry standards before passage.
- **Impound:** Ships disappear into fee disputes, seizure yards, and "temporary holds" that can last years.
- **Privateer commission:** Legal cover for approved private captains to inspect non-Crown vessels in contested waters.
- **Species relocation bounty:** Public notices classify [[Rattkin|Rattkin]] as subject to live-capture relocation in several Crown territories. [[Moucheron|Moucherons]] carry a separate, higher bounty filed under hazardous wildlife removal — the distinction reflects what happens to officers who try to collect it without adequate preparation.
- **Admiralty pass:** A named access document that opens fort gates, restricted piers, military archives, and sealed roads.

## Territory

The Crown's power is real but uneven. In the [[content/shattered-sea/places/crown-islands/index]], Dravosi flags fly at every major anchorage, and ships treat Crown papers as the cost of doing business. Harwick stages the regional fleet. Calven holds [[content/shattered-sea/places/calveno/index]], the oldest commercial centre in the Scatter. [[content/shattered-sea/places/calders-tooth/index|Calder's Tooth]] controls the western mouth of the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]].

On [[content/shattered-sea/places/calders-tooth/index|Calder's Tooth]], Crown authority is loudest at [[content/shattered-sea/places/port-tidefall/index|Port Tidefall]] and [[Fort-Crestwall]] but thins rapidly away from the coastline. The Crown treats the island as a fortress, tollbooth, timber reserve, and legal instrument. The interior remains too forested, cave-riddled, and locally navigated for colonial cartography to fully own.

Outside the major harbours, authority becomes geography-dependent. Cannon, piers, roads, and ledgers extend the Crown. Mudflats, cave systems, local pilots, and unwritten routes weaken it. [[South-Harbour]] survives because Tidefall's attention faces west; [[Passage|the Passage]] survives because the Crown cannot tax a route it cannot see.

## Fleet and Enforcement

Dravosi enforcement prefers inspection before battle. A patrol cutter comes alongside, a rating officer boards, and the crew is made to understand that noncompliance can become a military problem after the correct box is marked. Most crews pay. Most captains know that winning a fight against one cutter means becoming a problem for the next three.

Crown ships are built to be recognizable: naval grey hulls, a white stripe at the waterline, Crown pennant at the masthead, brass registry plate at the bow. A ship can strip the markings, but the absence of them becomes its own confession in Crown waters.

Typical Crown personnel include young patrol sailors, veteran boarding enforcers, and warrant officers who treat stores, medicine, and weaponized compounds as parts of the same logistics problem.

## Culture and Daily Texture

Dravosi authority has a sound: boots on plank, paper slapped flat, a brass stamp striking wet ink, the morning gun at [[Fort-Crestwall|Fort Crestwall]], the forced silence after an officer asks for papers. The uniform is meant to be legible at a distance. Navy-blue coats and brass buttons tell a harbour which conversations must become quieter.

The Crown's working class is less monolithic than its officers pretend. Deckhands are underpaid, clerks are exhausted, dock guards know which fees are nonsense, and quartermasters sell tomorrow's paperwork tonight at [[Hook-and-Ledger]]. The system is oppressive because it is large enough to absorb individual decency. One kind guard can misplace a form; the archive still remembers the debt.

Dravosi speech in the islands leans formal in office and practical on deck. Officers call extortion "expedited irregularity resolution." Sailors call it "getting the ink wet." Locals call it the Tidefall tithe, whether or not the fee has a legal name.

## What Everyone Knows

- The Crown controls the western mouth of the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]] through [[content/shattered-sea/places/port-tidefall/index|Port Tidefall]].
- Tidefall's inspection pier is unavoidable for vessels entering under Crown law.
- Crown papers matter. Missing papers matter more.
- Rattkin bounties exist, are rarely enforced consistently, and become dangerous whenever an ambitious officer wants leverage. Moucheron bounties exist, are never enforced in Midchain waters, and are universally ignored by officers who have read the incident reports.
- The Crown and [[Tessarine-Concordat|the Tessarine Concordat]] are rivals, but both recognize enough of each other's commercial law to make life difficult for everyone else.
- The Crown's grip is strongest at forts, harbours, and roads; it weakens sharply in caves, mudflats, local villages, and [[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/index|Midchain]] waters.
