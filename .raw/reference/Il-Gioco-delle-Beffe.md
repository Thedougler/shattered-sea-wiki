# Il Gioco delle Beffe

> *Every year, Calveno proves that the finest skill in a city of contracts and witnessed deals is the ability to make someone believe something that is not true — beautifully, publicly, and without lasting harm.*

## Overview

**Il Gioco delle Beffe** — the Game of Pranks — is Calveno's most beloved festival. Five days. Anyone can enter. The rules are simple: design an elaborate public hoax, execute it on a willing mark, and leave your team's symbol at the scene to claim the prize.

The catch is timing. Marks sign the Beffa Accord before the festival — a formal acknowledgment that they may be targeted at any point during the three-day execution window. They know it's coming. They don't know when. That three-day window is considered the finest part of the whole affair by everyone except the marks.

The festival is old enough that its legendary pranks are commemorated on a bronze plaque in the Velo Quarter. The current record — a hoax that kept an entire canal district in a spontaneous six-hour street wake for a privateer who wasn't dead — has stood for forty years. The team responsible has never confirmed who they were.

---

## The Five Days

**Day One — La Scelta (The Choosing)**

Registration opens at dawn on the Mercatura bridge. Teams declare their mark, their concept category, and their scale. They also register their **team symbol** — a unique sigil, stamp, or seal design that is recorded by the committee and belongs to that team for the duration of the festival. The symbol must appear at the scene of every prank to make it official. No symbol, no score.

Marks on the Accord list are notified that morning. Their nervousness begins immediately and does not stop for three days.

**Days Two, Three, and Four — La Finestra (The Window)**

The execution window. Teams may strike on any of these three days, at any hour, in any order. This is the heart of the festival. Marks cannot know when they will be hit, which means they cannot reliably prepare — and a suspicious mark is a worse mark. Teams use the window strategically: some strike early to deny rivals a chance to sabotage. Others wait, watching marks relax, and hit on Day Four just as everyone assumes nothing more is coming.

Teams may also use the window for preparation, sabotage, and misdirection — including false-alarming their own mark on Day Two so they drop their guard by Day Four.

**Day Five — Il Giudizio (The Judgment)**

All executed pranks are presented to the committee. Marks give their score. Committee scores craft and recovery. The prize is announced at sundown from the Mercatura bridge, and the winner's team symbol is added to the plaque.

Teams who have not executed by end of Day Four forfeit their entry stake.

---

## The Team Symbol

Every entering team registers a unique symbol with the committee on Day One. The symbol may be anything: a sigil, a wax seal design, a color combination, a carved stamp. The committee records it. No two teams may share a symbol.

The symbol serves as proof of authorship. To claim a score, the team must have left their symbol at the scene — embedded in the prank, pinned to the mark, sealed to the final reveal, or dropped where the mark will find it last. How it gets there is the team's problem.

The symbol is the last thing the mark finds. Finding it is how they know who got them.

**Important:** a team's symbol can be stolen. Another team presenting your symbol on their prank is a recognized (if rarely achieved) gambit called a *beffa doppia* — a double prank. The committee has rules about this. The rules are complicated. Nobody fully agrees on them.

## The Prize

Entry stakes pool together. Standard entry is **25 gp**. Teams declaring a Legendary-scale prank pay **50 gp** and compete for a larger share.

| Placing | Share |
|---|---|
| First | 60% of pool |
| Second | 25% of pool |
| Third | Remainder |

The **Beffa Medallion** — cast bronze, a laughing mask — goes to the winning team leader. It is the only award in Calveno that the Council families cannot buy, commission, or inherit. Past winners are listed on the Velo Quarter plaque by team name and symbol.

**The mark bonus:** A mark who was genuinely, completely, and beautifully fooled will typically pay their team a goodwill gift — 10 to 50 gp depending on how impressed they are. This is not required. It is almost always paid.

## Scoring

The committee scores each prank across three criteria, with the mark contributing their own score:

| Element | Maximum | Who Scores |
|---|---|---|
| Craft | 10 | Committee — quality of the deception's construction |
| Surprise | 5 | The mark — how completely they fell for it |
| Recovery | 5 | Committee — how cleanly the prank was walked back |
| **Total** | **20** | |

## Entering

Registration opens at dawn on Day One at the Mercatura bridge. You need:

- Your entry stake (25 gp standard; 50 gp if declaring Legendary)
- A team name
- A mark selected from the Accord list — available from the committee on request
- A category: Social, Environmental, Theatrical, or Compound
- A scale: Minor, Notable, or Legendary
- Your team symbol design

Teams may be any size, including one person. The committee recommends not doing it alone. They will let you try.

The Accord list this year includes merchants, factors, harbour officials, two members of the Council families, a prominent Tessarine factor signing for the first time, and Giacomo Moretti — who has signed every year for twenty years and has never been successfully pranked. The committee considers this an open civic challenge.

## Current Hooks

- Registration is tomorrow morning. Several Legendary declarations are expected; the prize pool will be large.
- The committee is quietly hoping someone finally challenges the Six-Hour Wake's record. They have been hoping this for forty years.
- Giacomo Moretti has signed the Accord every year for twenty years. He considers his unblemished record a point of professional pride. The committee considers it an embarrassment they intend to resolve.
- A Tessarine factor on the list for the first time. Teams who successfully prank Tessarine factors tend to receive interesting correspondence afterward.
- Prospero Morsani is on the plaque. He will not confirm which year or what the prank was. The entry reads only "Environmental / Notable" with no description — the only entry on the plaque without one.
