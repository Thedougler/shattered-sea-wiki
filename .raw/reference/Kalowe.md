# Kalowe — Takowan and Ship Disguise

## Situation

The *Takowan*, a Dravosi intelligence vessel, has overstayed Kalowe's primary dry dock for eleven days on a three-day repair claim. It is not being repaired; its shore parties are mapping Passage contact names on the causeways. Kalowe is also the best Midchain option for disguising the stolen [[HCS-Surety]], but the only safe work route runs through Perrin's Passage node while the *Takowan* is watching.

---

## Current State

The *Takowan* is about two-thirds through its survey. If it finishes, Kalowe's unregistered traffic becomes legible to the Crown and courtesy stops begin within the season. Noa Fifita can repaint and de-identify the *Surety* in a back-channel slip between the second and third limestone islands, but he will not work while the *Takowan* is in port.

The party's practical choice is not "Kalowe or no Kalowe"; it is whether to burn risk into Perrin's contact network now, delay until the *Takowan* leaves, force the ship to depart, or use Calveno instead.

## Narrative Devices

- **Loaded Gun:** The reef-gap bell is a worker warning system, but the *Takowan*'s watch has learned which sounds are normal.
- **Foreshadowing:** Two-person shore parties ask about names, not cargo; one talks while the other counts who listens.
- **Clock:** The *Takowan* completes its Passage survey if left alone; the *Surety* has roughly one month before Crown courtesy-stop watch lists harden.
- **Reversal Potential:** A nonviolent reason for the *Takowan* to leave may save the Passage node and reduce Noa's price.

## Key Facts

- The *Takowan* is an intelligence ship, not a damaged vessel.
- Noa Fifita can change the *Surety*'s affiliation markers, not its cutter silhouette.
- Perrin's Passage contact is currently unidentified; using the contact while the *Takowan* is mapping risks burning it within a day.
- Delmar Fisk walking the causeways is the highest-value sighting the *Takowan*'s captain has had in years.

## Involved Parties

- [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Perrin-Black-Jaw|Perrin Black-Jaw]] — has the Passage node; pays the network cost.
- [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Delmar-Fisk|Delmar Fisk]] — former Crown admiral in a stolen cutter; major exposure risk.
- [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] — needs ports like Kalowe to remain outside Crown and Grung pressure.
- Noa Fifita — back-channel repair operator; practical, quiet, not a paper forger.
- *Takowan* captain — has enough names to make Kalowe uncomfortable, not enough to act yet.

## Locations

- [[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/Kalowe|Kalowe]] — three limestone islands inside a reef lagoon.
- Primary dry dock — *Takowan* berth; watched by shore parties.
- Reef-gap bell — harmless worker warning that can draw intelligence attention at the wrong hour.
- Back-channel slip — hidden lagoon-side berth reached through Perrin's Passage node.
- Causeways — public survey ground; the Passage contact operates through fish-oil and rigging supply cover.

## Timeline

- Day -11: *Takowan* arrives on a stated three-day repair stay.
- Now: survey is roughly two-thirds complete; shore parties focus on the second causeway.
- Within a day of Perrin using the node: the *Takowan* may notice the exchange if still in port.
- Within the season if unopposed: courtesy-stop notices begin circulating from Kalowe-derived contact maps.

## Triggers

- The party enters Kalowe by day with the *Surety* visible from the primary dry dock.
- Perrin uses the Passage contact while *Takowan* shore parties are active.
- Delmar appears openly on the causeways.
- The party creates a credible reason for the *Takowan* to leave, chase, or become uncomfortable in port.

## Blocks

- Wait until the *Takowan* leaves, accepting contact-network damage while it remains.
- Force or lure the *Takowan* away before using the Passage node.
- Approach at night through the reef gap and avoid the main lagoon.
- Choose Calveno's Warren-routed option instead.

## Consequences

- **Fast and careful:** Noa works the hidden slip; the *Surety* leaves de-marked but still cutter-shaped.
- **Use the node under watch:** contact likely burns; the Tangle notices the cost.
- **Delay too long:** Kalowe's Passage infrastructure becomes mapped enough for Crown pressure.
- **Botch public exposure:** the *Takowan* identifies Delmar, the *Surety*, or both.

## Open Questions

- What will make the *Takowan* leave without turning Kalowe into a battlefield?
- What false vessel name and home port can the party defend under a courtesy stop?
- Does Perrin spend Passage credit here or save it for Calveno/Warren pressure?

## DM Notes

### Strong Start: Night Approach

> [!read-aloud]
> The reef breaks the swell before you hear it — a low white roar in the dark that softens as you come through the gap into still water. The lagoon smells of salt and something warmer: woodsmoke, the sharp wet smell of caulking iron still cooling somewhere on the second island. Three limestone islands ahead, connected by stone causeways. The dry-dock cranes are dark shapes above the treeline. At the primary berth, below those cranes, a vessel is lit — lanterns at the rail, a Crown pennant hanging motionless in air you cannot feel moving. Her crew is awake. Someone on that deck is watching the gap you just came through.

Night reef approach is navigable but narrow. Hunter sharks work the current; no encounter unless someone enters the water. If the party passes the bell rope too fast, it rings. That does not wake anyone important, but the *Takowan*'s watch notices.

### Noa Fifita

Short, still, salt-bleached canvas trousers. He does not greet customers, does not negotiate, and looks at the hull instead of the speaker. If the party explains, he lets them finish and prices the job as if they had said nothing.

Sample lines:
- *"Two hundred. Three days, maybe five."*
- *"She's a cutter. Paint her any colour you want. She's still a cutter."*
- *"The plate comes off. What you do with it after — not my business."*

First meeting beats:
1. Perrin makes contact through the second-causeway Passage stall.
2. Noa names the back of the second-island chandlery after dark.
3. First price: 200 gp; 3 days, maybe 5.
4. Papers are not his work; he can point toward brokers for 50 gp and 1 extra day.

If the party has a credible plan to move the *Takowan*, Noa's price drops to 150 gp. He does not offer this; he responds to it.

### The Work

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| Location | Back-channel slip, second limestone island, lagoon side |
| Access | Perrin's Passage contact only; cold approach finds nothing |
| Timeline | 3 days minimum; 5 for thorough work and caulking |
| Cost | 200 gp for labour, materials, and silence |
| Window | Night arrival keeps the *Surety* off the main lagoon |

Changes: hull colour, bow registry plate, nameboards, pennant. Does not change: patrol-cutter silhouette, age, worn caulking, or lifting copper. Noa throws the registry plate into the lagoon; he has done this before.

False papers cost 50 gp and take 1 additional day through the Passage broker network. The party must provide a vessel name and defensible home port. Using repair and papers on the same visit concentrates exposure on Perrin's node.
