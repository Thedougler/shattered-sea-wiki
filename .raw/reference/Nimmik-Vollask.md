# Nimmik Vollask

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> The ship is unmistakable before it is even close: a boxy two-masted brig painted the colour of a construction hazard, wide enough to look wrong on the water, tall enough that the freeboard seems like a design error. On both sides, in lettering two feet high, painted black against the orange hull, are two words in a script you don't recognise. They do not need translation. A cheerful figure waves from the rail — not a gnome, though gnomes are visible through every porthole and hatch opening and at least one appears to be dangling by a rope from the rigging with a clipboard.

---

## Overview

*Nimmik Vollask* is a Tier 2 cargo brig operated by a company of some sixty gnomish staff who live and work in a ship built to their scale. The decks, holds, passageways, and ladders are sized for gnomes. The hull, however, is not sized for anything in particular. It is sized for cargo. Anyone who reads Gnomish has not yet mentioned what the name translates to in mixed company.

The ship is wide, boxy, and tall for its class, with a centre of gravity that maritime engineers have described as "hypothetical." It teeters alarmingly at the slightest shift in wind or ballast, which it does on a rolling schedule as gnomes move between tasks below decks. In a calm this is merely unsettling. In a swell it is genuinely impressive.

The service model is simple. Pull alongside — or flag the *Depot* down on the water, which it will accommodate — and find [[Cotter-Foss|Cotter Foss]] at the rail. Tell Cotter what you need. Pay Cotter. Accept complimentary coffee and a plate of gnomish meatballs, which are small, dense, and improbably good. Wait. Below decks, sixty gnomes locate, pack, wrap, and stage your order with a logistical efficiency that is difficult to reconcile with what the ship looks like from the outside. When ready, the bundle clears the hold hatch with a yell, sails across the gap, and lands in your hold. If it does not land in your hold, that is a missed shot, which Cotter will note in the log and apologise for in writing.

The *Nimmik Vollask* carries ship stores, chandlery goods, provisions, common alchemical supplies, rope, canvas, basic tools, and a rotating selection of mildly unusual items that gnome buyers have negotiated in bulk from sources Cotter does not fully understand but has stopped questioning.

## Layout

**Weather deck** — scaled to gnomes, so roughly shin-height railings by human measure. Hatch covers, coil-rope staging areas, and two cargo cranes (gnome-operated, surprisingly capable). The bundle-delivery yeet originates from the main cargo hatch. Cotter operates out of a small folding table near the starboard rail with a ledger, a coffee pot, and a stool.

**Between-decks (gnome scale)** — full working floor, 4.5 ft clearance. Gnome-width corridors, gnome-height berths, gnome-sized equipment. Non-gnomes who have been below report it as an experience they have not sought to repeat. The organisation is categorical and dense: rope by type and diameter, canvas by weight and bolt, provisions in labelled casks by date and content. The gnomes know where everything is at all times. Asking them takes longer than watching.

**Lower hold** — the deepest and largest space, full headroom by gnome standards, entirely useless as standing room for anyone else. This is where bulk orders stage for delivery. The access hatch is large enough to pass the bundles through.

## History

The company was founded by the current gnomish staff collectively, under circumstances Cotter describes as "a business arrangement I was brought in to facilitate and have since become load-bearing for." The ship was purpose-built or heavily modified — the hull is clearly older than the interior fitout, and the orange paint is applied over at least two previous colours. The *Nimmik Vollask* has been operating the Central Strait circuit for approximately four years. It has never sunk, which the gnomes cite as evidence of competence and Cotter cites as evidence of luck and adequate ballast.
