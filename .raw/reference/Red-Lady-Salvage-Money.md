# Red Lady — Salvage Money

## Island Premise

Someone is quietly assembling a salvage commission for a Maw wreck, and the money moving through Calveno's broker network is unusual enough that Savia Brentino has turned away three enquiries and the Tangle has taken note. The accessible thread is Batta Zusto, a mid-tier broker on the Bridge who is not the funder but knows where the work orders are going. Delmar and Crissdalynn are the PCs for whom this matters most. The question underneath the money is whether the party finds the commission before it finds them.

---

## Strong Start

One of two entry points — use the first that applies.

**If Delmar asks about the Maw or the Red Lady:**
> [!read-aloud]
> The chandler lowers his voice without being asked. "You're not the first person to ask about Maw salvage this month. There's money moving. Serious money. Three separate buyers, or one buyer with three hands — nobody's sure. Whoever it is, they're not advertising a target."

**If Crissdalynn asks about Red Lady survivors, charts, or who's been in the Maw:**
> [!read-aloud]
> The broker pauses his ledger work. "I had someone in here last week asking about historical cargo on a wreck in the Maw. Didn't name the wreck. Paid for the search in advance and didn't come back for the report." He pushes a folded sheet across the counter. "I kept a copy."

## The Broker Network

The money is moving through at least three separate Calveno broker contacts. Nobody has a complete picture. These are the accessible threads.

**Savia Brentino — Carpenters' Slip.** Has turned away three suspicious enquiries this month. She will mention this to anyone she trusts — which means a Warren introduction or an existing relationship. She noted vessel class, crew size estimates, and one name she heard twice: Zusto. She did not write it down. She will say it once.

**Canal district gossip.** Le Paludi tavern talk, ferry workers, dock crews: unusual cargo moving out of two different factoring houses toward an outer basin anchorage. Specific: diving apparatus (a rental order, three pieces, unusual for Calveno), a quantity of compressed-air bladders, and a sealed chart package that a courier refused to let anyone else carry. DC 12 to gather two of these three details. DC 8 if Delmar asks specifically about Maw salvage equipment.

**Batta Zusto — The Bridge.** Zusto operates from the upper tier of the Bridge exchange — a Calveno institution where cargo futures, commissions, and high-volume logistics contracts are brokered. He is not the funder. He is the person who connects funders to operational suppliers. He knows the shape of the commission, who has been sourced, and roughly what the target is (a Maw wreck, no name confirmed). He will share what he knows for a price or a favour.

---

## Batta Zusto

Human, late fifties, stocky, always dressed slightly too formally for the weather. He brokers large commissions and does not pretend to care about their morality, only their completion. He has a reputation for clean information — accurate, priced, no embellishment.

He is not hostile to the party. He is also not protective of his clients.

**What he knows:**
- The commission is for a Maw wreck, deep access, cargo recovery. He has not been told the wreck name.
- Three separate supply chains: diving apparatus (hired, not bought), a chart service (sealed package, premium rate), crew contacts in two port cities.
- The money came through a factor in Port Tidefall he has worked with before. Good for the credit. Not previously interested in salvage.
- Someone else has also been asking Calveno brokers about Maw wreck access this month — not the same buyer. He noticed the coincidence. He finds it interesting.

**Price for the above:** 40 gp, or a piece of information he doesn't have. He will take current Maw navigation intelligence as equivalent to 80 gp. He will take the name of the wreck target if the party has confirmed it — because then he knows something the funder didn't tell him, which has value.

**What he won't say:** the funder's identity, which he does not know directly, and the Port Tidefall factor's name, which he will not give without the party demonstrating they can do something with it.

## What the Commission Looks Like

When assembled:
- Crew: 8–10 people, mixed skills, at least two with documented deep-diving experience
- Vessel: a mid-size cargo lugger with extended hull storage for recovered goods
- Navigation: a sealed chart package that someone paid premium for — recent data, Maw-specific
- Equipment: diving apparatus, air bladders, recovery rigging
- Timeline: 10–14 days from Calveno departure to Maw access

The commission is 8–10 days from departure when the party arrives in Calveno. Fast action by the party can get them to the Maw first, simultaneously, or shortly after. Delay puts the commission ahead of them with better equipment.

---

## The Parallel Enquiry

Zusto mentioned someone else is asking. This second enquiry is not the funder and not the party. The Tangle has also noticed it. Neither thread connects yet. Leave it unresolved — it is a loaded gun.

---

## Loaded Guns

- The sealed chart package is the commission's navigation advantage. If the party can intercept or copy it, they have what the salvage crew has. Zusto knows the chart service that produced it; he won't volunteer this without a second conversation.
- Delmar's Maw navigation experience is worth real money to the commission's funder — more than any broker fee. If Delmar becomes visible as a Maw expert in Calveno, someone will make him an offer. That offer is not obviously a trap.
- The Port Tidefall factor is the closest traceable link to the funder. A party that follows this thread will eventually find a name — but not in Calveno, and not fast.

---

## Clocks

- **Commission assembly:** 8–10 days to departure from Calveno. Tick when a day passes or when the party delays a Maw-relevant decision. Visible tick: Zusto mentions the supply chain is closing, or Savia sees a lugger she recognises taking on unusual cargo at the outer anchorage.
- **Parallel enquiry:** this second buyer is also moving. No visible tick until they arrive at the Maw or the party identifies them.

---

## Player Choices

- Follow the money to Zusto and learn the commission's shape (information; costs 40 gp or a trade)
- Intercept the chart package (operational; requires finding and reaching the chart service)
- Get to the Maw before the commission (race; requires fast departure and Maw navigation)
- Make Delmar visible as a Maw expert and wait for the offer (social; the offer arrives; the terms are not innocent)
- Misdirect the commission (deception; requires access to a supply chain or a broker contact willing to carry false information)
- Let the commission go and track it to the Maw (patient; the commission becomes a rival at the wreck site)

---

## Rewards and Costs

- **Rewards:** navigation intelligence, chart package, commission intelligence for future planning, Zusto as a standing Calveno contact
- **Costs:** 40 gp for Zusto's information; commission departing with better equipment if they delay; Delmar's identity becoming known to the funder if he's visible

---

## Exit States

- **Resolved:** the party has the commission's shape, has acted on it, and is positioned ahead of or alongside the salvage crew.
- **Complicated:** partial information; the commission departs; the parallel enquiry remains unidentified.
- **Ignored:** commission assembles and departs. It arrives at the Maw ahead of the party. The wreck is not empty when they get there.
