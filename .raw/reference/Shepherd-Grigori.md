# Shepherd Grigori

## Island Premise

The party is unknowingly sharing a ship with a Hierarch sorcerer-lich whose phylactery is not an object — it is a living network of noble heirs whose incurable conditions he cured. As long as any of them live, he cannot be permanently destroyed. He is not hiding. He passed his own test and decided the party met his threshold. He is being genuinely helpful. He is also counting down to a Calveno visit to a hemophiliac Tessarine heir whose life he intends to save — and use.

---

## True Nature

**Shepherd Grigori is a [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/undead/Hierarch|Hierarch]] sorcerer-lich.**

His phylactery is a living network: noble heirs across the Shattered Sea whose incurable illnesses he cured. Each living heir is an anchor for his continued existence. He reforms from death more slowly or quickly depending on how many anchors remain. He is always expanding the network.

**The cure is the trap.** The healing is real. The patient is genuinely better. They never know they have been bound.

**He considers himself a benefactor.** This is not a lie he tells others — he believes it. The horror is that he is not wrong about the results. Only the mechanism.

His CHA 21 is real. Play the warmth and the faith as absolutely genuine.

## The Khlysty

Grigori leads the [[content/shattered-sea/factions/Khlysty|Khlysty]], a sect built around his theology: that divine grace is demonstrated through miraculous acts, and that survival of death is proof of divine favor. The sect's outer face is legitimate religious community. The inner face is Grigori's court of devoted servants and living phylactery anchors. See the Khlysty faction page for full detail.

---

## Calveno Target

He is traveling to Calveno to attend to a **hemophiliac young heir from a powerful Tessarine family.** The condition is worsening; the family has tried conventional physicians and exhausted their options. Word reached Grigori through the noble networks he maintains. The family is expecting him.

The cure will bind the heir as a living phylactery without the family's knowledge. The heir's Tessarine connections make this addition particularly valuable — the Concordat's reach extends into courts where Grigori does not yet have anchors.

**The window:** approximately five days from Calveno arrival before the heir's condition becomes critical. The tropical storm has already compressed this margin. He is counting days. He is not alarmed — he has navigated tighter windows — but he is not relaxed either.

---

## Strong Start

Grigori passed his test. He decided the party met his threshold. He is now the most dangerous person aboard the Surety, in a way the party has no reason to suspect.

Session 03 opens mid-storm. He is in the galley. He has opinions about the weather. He does not seem worried.

## Loaded Guns

- **The heir is a child.** Interrupting the cure leaves a child seriously ill. Killing Grigori through his phylactery network eventually means the child dies too.
- **He likes the party.** This is not a performance. His warmth toward people who meet his threshold is genuine. He will tell them things nobles usually keep quiet.
- **He knows the courts.** More royal households than anyone the party can afford. He shares freely — fair exchange, in his mind.
- **Khlysty mark.** DC 18 Religion or History check, having seen Khlysty iconography elsewhere first: a small sect symbol, not displayed but not hidden.

## Clocks

- **Heir's window:** 5 days from Calveno arrival. If it closes, the child's condition becomes critical and the Tessarine family begins searching for Grigori through Concordat channels — which may reach the party.
- **Storm delay:** already cost 1–2 days. Grigori is tracking this silently.

## Player Choices

- **Let him off and think nothing of it:** cure happens, network expands, party never knows — until a pattern surfaces later.
- **Follow him in Calveno:** they find the Tessarine household, the sick child, a grateful family. They may walk in on the cure itself, which looks exactly like a holy man healing a suffering child.
- **Confront him after learning what he is:** he is honest. He does not consider himself a monster. He will ask what they think should be done about the child who is now breathing without pain.
- **Combat:** CR 19 at party level 4. This is a death scenario. Do not run it unless something very unexpected has happened.

## Rewards and Costs

- **Intelligence:** Grigori will freely share what he knows about Crown courts, noble politics, and the routes he travels. Better source than anyone the party can currently afford.
- **Tessarine contact:** if the party handles his Calveno visit without interference, the heir's grateful family becomes a useful future contact.
- **Phylactery revelation:** learning the network's scope is a campaign-level reframe — several healed-noble NPCs may retroactively become potential plot elements.
- **Intervention cost:** stopping the cure saves the heir from being bound but leaves a child seriously ill and a powerful Tessarine family hostile to the party.

## Exit States

- **Resolved:** Grigori cures the heir and departs Calveno. Party may or may not know what happened. Network expanded. Thread dormant until a phylactery NPC surfaces elsewhere.
- **Complicated:** party learns what he is but does not stop the cure. Grigori knows they know. He thanks them for their discretion and means it.
- **Confronted:** party interrupts the cure or exposes him publicly. Tessarine family hostile. Grigori departs without violence if possible — he does not fight people he likes unless forced. Heir remains ill.
- **Ignored:** cure happens off-screen. Grigori remembers the party warmly. Thread dormant.

## Running Notes

**The frame:** Rasputin as an undead sorcerer. The cult of personality is the weapon. The miracle is the mechanism. The wine is always poisoned for everyone else — his guests drink and his cup is fine. Don't signal this. Let it sit in the staging until someone notices.

**Play the warmth as real.** Grigori is not performing goodness. He heals people. He improves their lives. His theology is internally consistent and he lives by it. If you play him as secretly menacing, players will sense it. If you play him as genuinely warm, the revelation lands properly when it comes.

**He asks good questions.** CHA 21, decades of practice. He knows how to make people feel seen. Let him ask players about their characters' histories, fears, and goals — naturally, in conversation. He is actually curious.

**He is not a danger to the party right now.** He evaluated them and decided they met his threshold. He will not revise this unless they directly threaten him or his network. Do not play him as coiled.

**The phylactery problem is a campaign-level hook.** Don't force it. It surfaces naturally when the party meets an NPC who survived something unsurvivable, or when someone points at a Tessarine heir and says "he was dying three years ago."
