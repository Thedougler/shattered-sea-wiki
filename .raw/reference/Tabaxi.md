# Tabaxi

## Overview

*Medium Humanoid*

Created by the Cat Lord — a divine being of the Upper Planes — to blend the qualities of humanoids and cats. Tabaxi are felines given reason and language, yet retain the predatory instincts of their kind. They are curious, territorial, and deadly. Some live among other peoples and adopt their ways; most follow their nature — hunting, competing, surviving on their own terms.

In the [[Shattered-Sea|Shattered Sea]], Tabaxi presence is sparse but ancient. The tribes of the [[content/shattered-sea/places/sunken-crown/index|Sunken Crown]] islands have inhabited those waters for generations beyond counting, isolated from other tabaxi populations.

## Racial Traits

**Cat's Claws.** Your fangs and claws are natural weapons. You can use your Claws to make unarmed strikes. When you hit with your Claws, the strike deals 1d6 slashing damage instead of the normal unarmed strike damage. If you're missing a hand or your hand is unusable, you can't use that hand's claws.

**Darkvision.** You can see in dim light within 60 feet of you as if it were bright light and in darkness as if it were dim light. You discern colors in that darkness only as shades of grey.

**Feline Ferocity.** Your reflexes and agility allow you to move with a burst of speed. When you move on your turn in combat, you can double your Speed until the end of the turn. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you move 0 feet on one of your turns.

**Keen Senses.** You have Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on hearing or smell.

**Predatory Agility.** You have Proficiency in the Stealth skill.

## Tabaxi Type

Choose one of the following types at character creation. Your type reflects both your physical build and the hunting niche your people fill.

---

### Tiger

*Broader and more muscular build. Tigers have distinctive striped or blotched markings in warm tones — russet, orange, gold, black, cream. Larger fangs and more powerful jaws. A Tiger's roar carries weight.*

Tigers hunt the open water and exposed terrain. They are built for power and presence, ambush predators that strike from shallow waters or rocky ground. They are confident to the point of arrogance — tigers do not hide well, and they do not need to.

**Build.** You're noticeably larger and more heavily muscled than other tabaxi. Your claws are prominent, your shoulders broad.

**Tiger's Pounce.** When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike using your Claws, you can try to knock it prone. The target must succeed on a Strength saving throw (DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier, your choice) or fall prone. You can use this trait a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a Long Rest.

**Powerful Build.** You count as one size larger when determining your carrying capacity and the weight you can push, drag, or lift.

**Apex Predator (5th Level).** When you reach 5th level, your Claws deal an extra 1d6 slashing damage on a hit.

---

### Jaguar

*Lean, elegant build. Jaguars have spotted or rosette markings in warm colours — golden, copper, obsidian, cream. Smaller than Tigers but more graceful. A Jaguar moves like water.*

Jaguars hunt the dense forest and complex terrain. They are built for agility, silence, and precision. They excel in environments where sight lines are broken and movement is measured. They are creatures of shadow and patience.

**Build.** You're lean and graceful, with sharp angular features. Your movements are economical and precise.

**Jaguar's Stalk.** You can take the Hide action as a bonus action on your turn. Once you use this trait, you can't use it again until you finish a Short or Long Rest.

**Climber.** You have a Climb Speed equal to your Walk Speed.

**Deathstrike (5th Level).** When you reach 5th level, when you hit a creature with an unarmed strike using your Claws, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw (DC = 8 + your Proficiency Bonus + your Strength or Dexterity modifier, your choice) or be knocked prone.

## Tabaxi Instinct

Tabaxi do not form social bonds easily. They are creatures of territory and competition. In a party, a Tabaxi must choose whether to extend loyalty to their companions or follow solitary impulse. This is not a mechanical trait — it is the fundamental tension of being Cat Lord's children among other species. How your character resolves this tension shapes who they are.
