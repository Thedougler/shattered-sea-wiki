# Uncertainty

![[raw/assets/banners/Uncertainty.webp]]

> [!read-aloud]
> She rides level in the water — freshly caulked, brown-black paint from cap rail to copper, no flag flying. The bow carries a ram plate fitted flush with the waterline, iron-edged, and above it a carved hardwood figure: a woman mid-stride, hands empty, face forward. Two cannon sit amidships, swivel guns fore and aft. From a distance she reads as a working coastal vessel. That is the point.

---

## Overview

The *Uncertainty* is the former HCS Surety — a Dravosi Crown patrol cutter taken as a prize in Session 01, damaged by a whip shark in Session 03, and now fully refitted at La Vasca under Cobb's direction on Nona Black-Jaw's account. All Crown identity has been removed. She has been repainted, renamed, fitted with a ramming prow, equipped with a new figurehead, and stocked for extended operation at sea.

Nona's standing instruction was explicit: the ship leaves La Vasca complete or it does not leave. After her grandson nearly died on the last run, she is not interested in moderation.

She is fast for her size, nimble in reef passages, and can operate in four feet of water — shallower than any Crown vessel of her class. She carries enough armament to discourage interference without advertising herself as a threat. The ram is not subtle. Everything else is.

## Refit Modifications

### Identity Erasure

All Crown and Dravosi identity removed or destroyed:

- **Registry plate:** Cut from the bow and melted down at La Vasca. Iron went to Cobb's tool stock.
- **Stern board:** Original name removed; *Uncertainty* carved in Calveno Roman script by a carpenter Cobb brought in for the purpose.
- **Paint:** Original naval grey with white waterline stripe completely stripped and repainted. New scheme: weathered brown-black, standard working coastal vessel. No markings. No waterline stripe. Nothing distinctive.
- **Pennant and signal flags:** Crown pennant and Dravosi identification flags locked away below. No flag flies.
- **Interior markings:** Crown Admiralty stamps on boarding equipment offloaded with the Weapons Locker restocking. Chart table markings scraped and the charts themselves redrawn over with Calveno notation — the navigation coverage is retained, the provenance is not.
- **Prisoner brig:** Decommissioned. The cage bolts were cut and the iron repurposed for the ram plate fitting. The space is now additional below-decks storage.

What remains and cannot be fully hidden: the hull silhouette. A Crown cutter's profile is distinctive to anyone who knows what to look for. The paint and the name handle port authority at a glance. They do not handle a Dravosi naval officer who has served on this class of vessel. See [[HCS-Surety-DM-Guide|DM Guide]] for Crown recognition timeline.

---

### Ram Bow

The forward hull below the waterline has been rebuilt with doubled planking, iron-pin cross-bracing, and an iron-capped ram plate running three feet along the keel. The plate is flush at waterline, angled forward, edge-blunted enough that it will not foul on barnacles but sharp enough to open a hull seam on contact.

> [!mechanic]
> **Ram Attack [HB].** Helmsman's action. The ship must move at least 20 feet in a straight line before the strike. Both ships take **2d10 bludgeoning damage**; the *Uncertainty* takes half (rounded down) due to the reinforced bow. A target of Tier 1 or smaller must succeed on a **DC 13 Strength save** or be pushed 15 feet and have its next turn's movement halved. The forward hull has **AC 13** against head-on strikes only.

> [!dm]
> The ram works best against stationary or slower targets. Against a maneuvering vessel of equal tier, call for an opposed helmsman Dexterity check first — the party wins on a tie. The ram is not subtle. Anyone watching the approach sees the intent.

---

### Shallow Keel

The keel depth has been reduced by 18 inches — ballast stones redistributed, garboard strakes faired back in. Cobb's La Vasca yard contact did the work in three days.

> [!mechanic]
> **Shallow Draft [HB].** The *Uncertainty* can operate in water as shallow as **4 feet** (minimum depth for a standard patrol cutter is ~8 feet). This opens river mouths, tidal flats, lagoon approaches, and Calveno's inner canal network to the ship at speed. The helmsman has **advantage** on Dexterity checks to navigate reef channels and shallow coastal passages. **Trade-off:** Reduced lateral resistance costs windward performance in poor conditions — Speed (poor wind) is 30 miles/day instead of 35.

> [!dm]
> The main value is access, not speed. A patrol that would have to anchor offshore and row in can now sail directly to a shallow-water destination. Crown vessels of this class can't follow — they ground at 8 ft. In rough open water (storm or heavy swells), apply disadvantage on helmsman checks to hold course, as the reduced keel depth means less lateral grip off the wind.

---

### Figurehead

The old figurehead was lost to the whip shark in Session 03. Nona's contact in Le Paludi — a retired canal-ship carver — replaced it in four days. He charged for the wood and nothing else. He did not ask the ship's name until he was finished.

The figurehead is a woman, mid-stride, hands open at her sides. The carver gave her Calveno features and no expression that reads as hope or fear. She is simply going. She has no crown, no weapon, no attribute. She is not named.

**Surgeon's Berth:** A proper medical station forward of the officer cabin. Two folding cots, suture kit, splinting materials, fever treatments. Nona included 3 Potions of Healing from Passage medical supply — they are not restockable from a standard chandlery; the next supply comes from Warren contacts. Needs a hireling Surgeon to unlock full Hospital benefits, but a PC with Medicine proficiency can use it for DC 10 treatment checks.

**Weapons Locker:** Crown boarding pikes and Crown-stamped short swords offloaded. Replaced with: 8 boarding axes, 4 crossbows (20 bolts each), 6 grapple lines, 3 smoke grenades, incendiary flasks × 2, and a lockable chest with the party's arms inventory. Cobb contributed a set of caulking hammers he notes will also break a knee cleanly if needed.

**Provisions Store:** La Vasca's canal supply has good Passage contacts. Stocked for 30 days at full crew (10 persons): salt fish, rice, dried beans, fresh citrus, fresh water casks × 4, small beer × 2 barrels, cooking oil. One sealed emergency ration chest (7 days for 10, not to be opened except at Nona's say-so — Cobb enforces this).

## Layout

Unchanged from the Surety. Full dimensions and deck-by-deck: [[HCS-Surety-Layout]].

**Summary:** 80 ft × 22 ft beam. Three levels.

- **Weather Deck:** Open. Cannon amidships (port and starboard), swivel guns fore and aft. Ram plate at the bow. New figurehead above the waterline forward.
- **Gun Deck (interior):** Officer cabin aft converted to crew mess — bench seating for 6, a small folding table, a crate of cards and dominoes Cobb found in the tool chest and left without comment. A hatch in the aft deck with an iron ladder drops to the galley below. The captain bunks forward with the crew. Crew hammocks forward. Weapons Locker amidships. Surgeon's Berth forward of the officer cabin.
- **Hold:** Galley forward, directly below the crew mess — cast-iron stove on a sand bed, cook's tools, bread locker, connected to the mess above by iron ladder through the hatch. Provisions Store occupying former prisoner brig space and adjacent stores. Powder magazine aft. Fresh water casks and emergency ration chest secured to deck.

> [!dm]
> Cobb converted the officer cabin without being directly asked by the party. He received word via Mr. Thunk the previous day — a message from "the captain." He tells anyone who asks and does not editorialize. If pressed on why Thunk specifically: *"He came by yesterday. Said the captain sent him."* Cobb keeps no opinion about command philosophy.

## Crew Changes Since Acquisition

### Departures

**Grigori** — Departed in Calveno as planned. His business here was always his own; the party was transport, not company. He left no note. Within a day of his departure, two rumours are circulating in Calveno:

> *A dying Tessarine heir — bedridden three weeks, the family already measuring for the funeral — recovered overnight. Completely. The servants say a man came to the house after dark, drunk enough that the door guard almost turned him away, and was with the patient for twenty minutes. When he left, the patient was sitting up asking for food. Nobody got a clear look at the man. Someone says he smelled like wine and something else. Someone else says he was singing under his breath the whole time.*

The family is not publicizing this. The rumours are coming from the servants. Both versions agree the man left without asking for anything.

> [!dm]
> Grigori's target was the hemophiliac Tessarine heir — this was his Calveno business from the moment he came aboard. The healing is real; the drunk monk act is performance. His whereabouts after departure are unknown. He is operating on his own timeline. See [[content/shattered-sea/private|GM-only Grigori material]] for what he is actually doing.

**Alys Kuiper** (Ship's Surgeon) — Did not return from shore leave. No note, no message. Her berth has been cleared of personal effects; whoever took the time to do that was not Alys. The Surgeon's Berth she helped install is stocked and functional. The role is vacant.

> [!dm]
> Alys's departure is clean — no drama, no confrontation, simply gone. If the party investigates in Calveno, they can learn she took a room at a respectable Le Paludi boarding house for three days and then checked out. The boarding house owner mentions a man came to meet her on the second evening and they talked for some time. She left the next morning. This is the full extent of what is findable. Don't invent more unless you have a prepared thread for it.

## History

Launched as **HCS Surety** under Crown registry CS-1147. Commanded by Lieutenant Barnaby Rook on eastern Crown Islands patrol commission. Taken as a prize by the party in Session 01 following a boarding action. Sustained significant hull damage from a whip shark encounter in Session 03. Renamed *Uncertainty* by party decision. Dry-docked at La Vasca, Calveno, under Cobb's supervision on Nona Black-Jaw's account. Refit completed in 5 days.
