# Warren — Grung in the Sewers

## Island Premise

Three Calveno residents have reported sighting what each described, independently, as a large green humanoid in the sewer access tunnels and canal maintenance corridors beneath Le Paludi and the Bridge district. One report is from a Warren runner who works the sub-level drainage routes — she is not given to exaggeration, and she is not Dry. The other two are from Dry residents who might be mistaken about most things, but their descriptions match the runner's.

Grung in Calveno is not a thing that happens. Calveno is a northern commercial city across the Central Strait. The Verdant Teeth are two days south of Kalowe, which is another three days south of the Strait. The gap between Grung territory and Calveno's sewer network is enormous. That is what makes this worth investigating, and also what makes Nona reluctant to mention it to anyone who would dismiss it on those grounds alone.

She does not have anyone who knows Grung. Then Perrin walks into her kitchen and tells her Jean-Claude is crew.

> *"Before the other things. I need to know what is in the tunnels under my city."*

---

## Strong Start

> [!read-aloud]
> The Warren runner's sketch is four junctions and an X — maintenance tunnels below the Le Paludi canal street, an access hatch marked in charcoal. The ladder drops fifteen feet to water and limestone. The lantern Enzo handed you at the top smells like fish oil. Down here it smells like low tide and old stone and something else you haven't placed yet.

Do not describe what the party finds before they look. Ask what they do first.

---

## What Is Actually Down There (DM Only)

Simone's blue-caste handlers have been moving through this network at night, mapping the sewer system below the festival districts as an extraction route for the Beffa raid. They work at night and are gone before dawn. They leave minimal sign. They are very good at this.

The party will not learn any of that from this investigation. What they find is evidence that something was here — not what it was doing, not why it came this far north, not what comes next. The raid, the extraction plan, Simone's involvement: none of this surfaces here. This is the first strange mark on a wall that takes three sessions to understand.

---

## The Tunnel Network

Collector tunnels, 5–6 feet high, ankle-deep slow water, branching off the main canal access corridor below Le Paludi and the Bridge. Iron hatches set flush. The kind of infrastructure the city maintains but does not think about.

| Location | Notes |
|---|---|
| Le Paludi access hatch | Where the runner saw the sighting; ladder descends 15 ft to the main collector |
| The Y-junction | Main collector splits; the left branch has heavier foot disturbance in the silt |
| The Bridge connector | Links Le Paludi collector to the Bridge district; narrower, 4 ft high |
| The far hatch | At the Bridge end; the silt around the base is disturbed on both sides — something has used this from below |

## What the Party Finds

**Physical signs:** Boot prints in the silt — or what reads as boot prints, except the toe is too wide and the weight distribution is wrong for anything Dry. At least two different sets. The left-branch disturbance is heavier. Something moved through here more than once.

> [!read-aloud]
> The left branch has been used more than the right — the silt is disturbed from here to the next junction. When you look closer, the impressions are wrong: the weight is off-centre, the toe too wide for anything Dry.

**The smell:** Whatever the party couldn't name at the ladder. Wet vegetation, something mineral, something organic. Not canal smell. Not rat smell. Not anything that belongs in a Calveno sewer. Jean-Claude places it immediately if he makes no check at all: it is Grung. Ambient, faint, a day or two old. Not one individual — the smell of a space that has had Grung in it repeatedly.

**The markings:** Small incisions on the limestone, low on the wall, easy to miss. At the Y-junction, three marks on the left branch. At the Bridge connector, two marks near the far hatch. They are not maintenance notation — Calveno's canal commission uses chalk and paint, not cuts. They are not vandalism. They are too precise and too consistent in placement.

> [!read-aloud]
> Low on the wall, knee-height. Three cuts into the limestone — small, clean, exactly the same depth. Not chalk marks. Not paint. Whoever made these had time, had a tool, and did it deliberately.

> [!dm]
> Jean-Claude recognises the marks as Grung in origin — the spacing, the depth, the placement logic. That is all he can say with confidence. What they mean is opaque to him. Grung trail notation varies by caste and context; without knowing the specific system, the marks read as *intentional* and *recent* and *Grung,* full stop. That is already more than anyone else in Calveno knows.

**What the party does not find:** Whoever made these marks. No active presence. No encounter. The tunnels are empty. Whatever was here worked at night and left before dawn. The absence is its own information — this was disciplined, not wandering.

## Reporting to Nona

The party comes back with: confirmed prints, confirmed smell (Jean-Claude), confirmed markings in Grung notation, no active presence, no explanation.

Nona listens without interrupting. Then she is quiet for a moment.

> *"Grung. In my tunnels. During the Beffa."*

She does not say what she makes of this. She asks one question:

> *"Did they see you?"*

If no: she nods. If yes or unknown: she nods differently, and sends Enzo somewhere immediately after the party leaves.

**What Nona does with the report:** She does not know what this means either. She has more context than the party — she knows the scale of Grung expansion over the last few years, she knows the Beffa draws crowds — but she cannot construct a complete picture from boot prints and a smell. She takes it to the Tangle. The Tangle tells her to watch and report. They do not sound surprised in a way that reassures her.

> [!dm]
> Nona suspects something is building. She does not know what. This is accurate — she is not withholding from the party; she is working with the same incomplete information. The investigation confirms the reports are real and raises a question none of them can yet answer. That is the correct exit state for a first hint.

---

## Jean-Claude — The Unease

Jean-Claude can confirm the Grung presence. He cannot explain it. That gap — knowing what was here but not why — is the thing that sits with him after the tunnels.

Grung do not wander this far north. That is not how Grung work. Grung expansion is deliberate and directed. Whatever sent scouts this far into a Central Strait city during a festival had a reason. Jean-Claude knows enough to know that, and not enough to know what the reason is.

Do not push this as a revelation scene. If the player goes somewhere with it, follow. If they do not, the unease is in his pocket until the Beffa makes it legible.

## Optional Encounters

Drop either of these if the party is moving through the tunnels and the scene needs teeth. Neither reveals anything about the Grung presence — they are sewer hazards, not plot.

---

### Canal Crocodiles

Calveno's outer canal water has always had crocodiles working the tidal margins. Two have pushed up through the Bridge connector's flood overflow into the collector tunnels and settled in the standing water near the Y-junction. They have been here long enough that their slide marks are in the silt alongside the boot prints. In the dark they read as logs until they move.

> [!mechanic]
> **2 Crocodiles.** AC 12, HP 19 each. Bite: +4 to hit, 4d6+2 piercing; grappled target (DC 12 Strength to escape). In a 5-ft tunnel, a grappled creature cannot be pulled clear without the rescuer also making a DC 10 Athletics check against the crocodile's resistance. The crocodile has advantage on Stealth in ankle-deep water. Passive Perception 10 — they detect movement by vibration, not sight.

> [!dm]
> The crocodiles are not related to the Grung presence. They are simply a bad tunnel. If the party tries to push past them quietly: DC 14 Dexterity (Stealth), with disadvantage for anyone in armour heavier than leather — the tunnel amplifies sound. The crocodiles do not pursue past the Y-junction into the Le Paludi side; they are settled in the darker, quieter stretch.

---

### A Grung Who Hasn't Left

The handlers work at night and are gone before dawn. If the party enters at an awkward hour — or pushes deep and fast — one is still in the Bridge connector, finishing a route check. She is not there to fight. She is there to leave without being seen.

She is also very good at her job and hears the party before they hear her.

> [!mechanic]
> **1 Grung Elite Warrior.** AC 13, HP 49. Multiattack (2 strikes): +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing + 2d4 poison. **Grung Poison:** DC 12 Constitution save or poisoned until end of next turn. **Standing Leap:** jump up to 25 ft without running start. **Amphibious.** She carries a hand crossbow (20 bolts) and will switch to it if the party blocks the tunnel.

> [!read-aloud]
> Movement in the connector ahead — away from you, fast. A shape in the dark already moving toward the far hatch. It heard you before you saw it.

> [!dm]
> Her objective is the far hatch, not the fight. On her first turn she moves toward the exit; she only attacks if the party blocks or grabs her. She will not speak. If Jean-Claude addresses her in Grung she stops for one beat — not because she will answer, but because she is recalibrating. Then she continues toward the exit. Do not have her reveal anything about the operation: she runs, she escapes, or she dies. No names, no purpose, no information beyond the fact that she was very clearly there to do a job. The party reports a dead or escaped Grung to Nona. Nona adds it to the picture she cannot yet complete.

## Triggers

- Nona hears Jean-Claude is with Perrin (automatic; she offers this first)
- The party asks what Nona needs before she offers
- A Warren runner brings a new sighting while the party is at the table

## Blocks

- The party reports to someone outside the Warren before reporting to Nona
- Jean-Claude refuses to enter the tunnels (Nona sends a runner instead; it goes worse)

## Rewards and Costs

- **Rewards:** Tangle trust; confirmed intelligence that the reports are real; Jean-Claude's Grung knowledge earns immediate Passage goodwill
- **Costs:** An unanswered question now sits on the table, and none of them know what it is counting down to

---

## Exit States

- **Investigation complete, reported to Nona:** The reports are confirmed real. Nona has Grung boot prints, Grung smell, and Grung notation under her city during the Beffa. She does not know what it means. The party does not know what it means. The DM does.
- **Investigation incomplete:** Nona gets partial confirmation. She treats it as real anyway.
- **Party investigates and says nothing:** Nona finds out they were in the tunnels. She asks what they found. The credit does not appear until they answer honestly.
