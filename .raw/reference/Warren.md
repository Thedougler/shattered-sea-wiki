# The Warren — Active Hub

## Situation

The Warren is the only fixed Rattkin settlement in the Shattered Sea: 200-250 permanent residents, the largest single Run in the Passage, and the anchor point for the network's memory. Perrin can reach it, but doing so brings the party into a guarded intelligence culture with three active pressure points already in motion.

---

## Current State

The Tangle has reasons to engage Perrin if he returns: Nona and Anzolo are already searching for him, the secret Below project makes Perrin's silence a liability, they can route the *Surety* disguise work, and they have three Calveno problems suited to capable outsiders. The party should experience the Warren as useful, wary, and transactional without becoming cold. Its trust starts dry-but-watched.

## Narrative Devices

- **Loaded Gun:** The Tangle keeps copies, caches, and route shards; any favour can become a future door or future demand.
- **Foreshadowing:** Rattkin do not panic visibly; urgent problems arrive as quiet, precise requests.
- **Clock:** Bela has 2-3 days left; Venn's next informant check-in is 9 days after inquiry begins; Anzolo has stalled without a new Perrin lead.
- **Reversal Potential:** The Passage's "criminal" network is more ethically reliable than several lawful institutions in Calveno.

## Key Facts

- The Tangle is seven senior elders holding the Passage's most sensitive routing intelligence.
- Perrin Black-Jaw's family is the Tangle; he knows routes, protocols, and people travelling-Run Rattkin do not.
- The Tangle has received four non-Rattkin guests in the last century.
- Dravosi Crown does not know the Warren exists as a fixed settlement.
- The Seven Houses know Rattkin exist in Calveno but not the Warren's location or scale.
- Andriana Trevisan knows roughly where it is but will not name a contact or lead anyone there.
- The Below is a secret Tier 4 habitat carrack under construction at Kalowe, commissioned as the dry hull *Saltmarsh Trader*.
- Perrin knows the Below exists and where it is; he does not know whether the Tangle will let him aboard if it matters.

## Perrin Family Clock

Nona has activated a circuit-wide Passage inquiry because Perrin's silence has gone on too long. She knows he is alive somewhere in the Central Strait corridor, based on a fragmentary six-week-old Crown Island sighting. She does not know the ship, crew, or why he has not answered.

Anzolo has worked the Passage contacts between Calveno and the Central Strait and found nothing. The only person who can confirm Perrin's current location is [[Beaumont-Sel|Beaumont Sel]], who spoke with Perrin after the *Surety* taking and is now heading toward Tidefall.

Outcomes:
- Perrin contacts Nona first: the *Vestra* conversation happens on his terms.
- Anzolo finds him first: it happens on Nona's terms, with a witness.
- No contact next interval: Nona escalates; Anzolo is not her last card.

## The Below

The Below is the Tangle's evacuation strategy: a Tier 4 habitat carrack under construction at Kalowe. Kin name: the Below. Dry commission name: *Saltmarsh Trader*. The Chain Council authorized the commission without knowing the true purpose; three representatives signed, none understanding what they bought. The Kalowe dock master knows only that he is well paid and the crew is entirely Kin.

Purpose: carry 400 Rattkin, the Tangle Chamber, and the Memory Hold archive to open water if Calveno ever exposes the Warren. Perrin's silence since the *Vestra* sank makes the elders ask whether he can still be trusted to carry knowledge of it.

Triggers:
- Perrin makes contact and establishes whether he is still Kin in their eyes.
- Crown genealogical records or Calveno politics move closer to the Run.
- Chain Council discovers the true purpose of the commission.
- The party reaches Kalowe and sees the project without context.

## Active Leads

### Bela Silt-Paw — Sub-Level Survey

**Hook:** Bela Silt-Paw, 22, first solo deep-route assignment, is trapped in flooded drainage tunnels below the Warren after a Water Weird claimed the central chamber. She has a broken forearm, her message shard, emergency rations, and about 2-3 days left.

**Site:** 40 ft flooded chamber, 8 ft ceiling at highest, 5 ft approach tunnels. Water is 3 ft at shelves, 8 ft in main channel. One Water Weird claims the central channel and attacks only when something enters the main water. Optional second Weird on west approach if escalation is needed.

**Reward:** Warren access review, 200 gp on shard recovery, plus operational cache: 340 gp, 2 potions of water breathing, and a sealed two-year-old Dravosi dispatch exposing Commendant Ulrik Faste's fraudulent supply contract. Dispatch is worth about 500 gp to the right buyer or major Passage goodwill.

**Outcomes:**
- Bela alive: major Tangle goodwill; her shard reveals a possible harbour-foundation route.
- Bela dead: trust goes cold; shard and route are lost.
- Weird destroyed: route clears; Tangle asks what the party wants for opening a blocked channel.

### Marrow's Crate — Dead Courier

**Hook:** A crate delivered to Marrow's by Dry runner Dren has gone unclaimed for three weeks. Dren is dead. The crate contains a sealed letter implicating House Vretek and Commendant Alessi Brol in selling Passage cargo timing to Tessarine merchants.

**Truth:** Serin Haut, Dravosi intelligence attaché under Tessarine trade-assessor cover, killed Dren and has been watching canal approaches for ten days. Use Spy stats, +1 Deception, concealed shortsword, 3 doses basic poison.

**Crate contents:** Vretek/Brol letter, 280 gp in Tessarine silver, and a secondary House Vretek signet ring worth 120 gp as jewelry or more as evidence.

**Investigation path:** canal inquiry DC 12; watch records DC 15 or 5 gp bribe; employers DC 13; canal broker identifies woman in green coat as Dren's suspicious contact.

**Outcomes:**
- Give letter to Tangle: 400 gp, major trust; Passage handles Brol/Vretek quietly.
- Sell to Vretek rivals: 600-750 gp; lasting Vretek enmity.
- Blackmail Brol: 800 gp now; attempted murder within two weeks.
- Destroy letter: House Vretek eventually owes an unnamed favour.
- Hand Haut to Tangle: major goodwill; canal district becomes quieter.

### Ferrin Locke — The Leak

**Hook:** Three Passage surface operations were intercepted over three months. The common channel leads to Ferrin Locke, a Dry canal broker coerced by Dravosi intelligence with an old smuggling conviction.

**Truth:** Ferrin is frightened, not loyal. He has been feeding technically accurate but slightly delayed route timing to Petra Venn, a Dravosi intelligence attaché posing as a Tessarine trade arbitration consultant. He wants out.

**Venn's room:** codebook worth 500 gp/major Passage value, 420 gp operational coin, list of three non-Passage informants, and Ferrin's signed cooperation agreement.

**Investigation path:** pattern DC 12 identifies broker Faro; Faro leads to Locke DC 10; Locke talks if offered a way out, read with Insight DC 13, or pressured DC 15. Next Venn check-in is 9 days after inquiry begins.

**Outcomes:**
- Expose Locke to Tangle: leak stops; Ferrin disappears non-lethally; 300 gp and trust.
- Help Locke escape: valuable protected contact; Passage owes a different kind of debt.
- Neutralize Venn: leak stops at source; codebook and coin available.
- Sell codebook: 500 gp; Venn notices within a week and the Passage later asks questions.

**Jean-Claude hook:** Ferrin noticed Venn had a months-old standing query for a Grung passenger travelling with false documentation. He did not report it. He mentions this if treated kindly.

## Consequences

- **Warren helped cleanly:** Perrin gains real Tangle credit, usable later when the party needs routes, papers, or protection.
- **Warren treated carelessly:** access remains possible but cold, expensive, and watched.
- **Intelligence sold outward:** short-term coin, long-term Passage suspicion.
- **Jean-Claude trusted:** Rattkin anti-slavery policy gives him a rare non-Grung ally base.
- **Perrin delays too long:** the Tangle treats the *Vestra* loss as a liability and reassesses his standing around the Below.

## DM Notes

### Access Mechanics

Plausible paths to entry: service to a Run in danger, a threat neutralized against the Calveno Hold, or Fancy Rat relationship built over multiple sessions. Il Velo makes Rattkin movement easier because a Fancy Rat in a crowd reads as costume.

### Tone

The Warren is not a thieves' guild quest board. It is a kin-state with memory. Offers are phrased as reviews, routing help, cache rights, or future access. Coin appears, but the real currency is whether the Tangle changes what doors open for Perrin.

### Nona — Table Frame

Use the public Nona page for what the world can know about her. Keep the table-facing read here: she has been at war her whole life and stayed anyway. The food is what all of this is for, not a way to work the room. She sees guests clearly from the first moment and will tell them what she sees, but only after they have eaten.

Voice cues:

> *"Eat. You look like a starved little mouse. Ruk dear, more stew for the cleric."*

> *"Never snatch. Snatching is for children and amateurs. Smile. Sit. Eat. Make them decide to be generous."*

> *"Enzo, if they reach for steel before bread, break only one hand. We are not animals."*

### Nona — Pressure Points

She does not yet know the *Vestra* is gone. When she learns, she will feel the family grief and the Passage logistics at the same time — the ship was both.

What she is really afraid of is not that something happened to Perrin. It is that something happened *to* him — that the silence means he's drifting from what she built him to be. She raised him. She chose him. She knows what he's capable of, and she doesn't know which version of the silence this is. Both are bad.

Her instructions to Anzolo, if it comes up at the table:

> *"Find my boy. If he is hiding, bring him home. If he is hurt, bring me the one who hurt him. If he is dead, bring me the truth, and do not soften it. I am old, not fragile."*

She does not panic. That is how everyone around her knows she is afraid.

### Nona's Inner Circle

Nona keeps two bodyguards at her table in the Tangle Chambers:

**Enzo** — black jaguar tabaxi, obsidian-furred with rosettes visible in the light and a pale scar along the left side of his jaw. Tailored suit, immaculate shoes. Rarely speaks. Communicates through a lit cigar, a held door, and the slow unsheathing of one claw. Pulls chairs, pours wine, blocks exits. His sentence is *"Nona asked you a question."*

**Ruk** — massive scarred lizardfolk. Heavy muscle and occasional sous-chef. Literal, observant, terrible at pretending not to notice things. Will snap someone's neck and ask for a second helping of risotto. Useful as a barometer — if Ruk has gone quiet and is watching someone specifically, that is not a good sign for them.

### The Rattle

The Warren's alarm system — pan-striking that spreads through kitchens, vents, and stone until it fills the district. Legally unactionable: cooking noise, nothing more. To Kin it means Dravosi are in La Paludi; to fighters it is a call to arms. Two quick taps after silence means the threat is clear. If the party hears the Rattle while inside the Warren, treat it as a timed pressure: Kin are moving to positions, routes are closing, and the window for civilian behavior is narrowing fast.
