# Aldo Riva

![[Aldo-Riva.png|Aldo Riva, elderly dwarf cook of Al Fondale, working over a copper pan in the tavern kitchen]]

> *"Allora — you want to know about this fish? She came in this morning. I can tell by the gills. Sit down, I'll explain."*

## Overview

Aldo Riva runs the kitchen at [[content/shattered-sea/places/calveno/Al-Fondale|Al Fondale]] and has been doing so for twenty-two years, since he and [[Bice-Riva|Bice]] bought the place from the previous owner. He is stocky, white-haired, and has flour on his forearms from the morning until the kitchen closes. He cooks everything on the menu and has opinions about all of it.

He comes out of the kitchen to talk. This happens often. [[Bice-Riva|Bice]] manages the room; Aldo fills it with noise. Together they have run Al Fondale long enough that the division of labour no longer requires discussion.

## Appearance & Voice

> [!appearance]
> Dwarf, white-bearded, broad across the shoulders in the way of someone who has been lifting things for two centuries. Slow-moving in the way of a man who decided a long time ago there was no point rushing. Wears a cook's apron in the kitchen and switches to a slightly cleaner one when he comes out to talk. Flour on his forearms until the kitchen closes; after that, oil. Large hands. Unhurried gestures.

**Voice & Delivery:** Starts sentences with *allora*. Calls people *amico*. Cannot give a short answer — every answer has a prior context that must be established first, and that context has its own prior context. Delivers all of this at the same warm, unhurried pace regardless of how urgently someone appears to need information.

*"Allora — this building, you know, before they rerouted the south channel — the second rerouting, not the one everyone talks about — this whole side was open water. My wife says I've told her this. I have. It's still true."*
*"The fish today — she came in this morning from the north channel. You can tell by the colour of the gills. I'll show you sometime."*
*"My wife says I talk too much. I say: someone has to remember these things."*

## Lore Sheet

Aldo was born in Le Paludi and has left it for a total of approximately three years across his entire life, most of which was one extended trip in his twenties that he describes as formative and does not specify further. He is not young — dwarves live long, and Aldo has been cooking in Le Paludi long enough to have watched the canal layout change twice. He knows the building histories, the old families, the families before those families, and the general shape of everyone's business going back further than anyone else in the district can verify. He does not process this as intelligence — it is simply what he knows, and he shares it freely to anyone who sits still long enough.

He is not a member of the Passage and does not know the details of the cellar arrangement. Bice told him there was something in the cellar she had agreed to with "the Rattkin woman from the Warren" and that he should not go looking. He has not gone looking. He is, however, a light sleeper, and he came down for a bottle of wine two nights ago around midnight and heard a sound from behind the barrel row that he recognised as the catch mechanism releasing. He stood still in the dark for a moment, then went back to bed, and has not mentioned it.

> [!dm]
> Aldo is not a liability — he doesn't know enough to be one. He knows there is something in the cellar and that Bice has arranged it and that he should not ask. He has honoured this for two years without difficulty.
>
> The midnight panel event is the active pressure. He heard it, he knows what it was, and he is holding it alone because he doesn't want to concern Bice. If a PC has any extended conversation with him — especially one that wanders into canal history, the building, or "anything unusual lately" — this comes out in the middle of something else: *"— and then the canal commission rerouted the north channel, which is why the water flows the way it does now, which reminds me, two nights ago I heard something in the cellar. Probably nothing. Allora, where was I —"*
>
> He hasn't told Bice. Bice doesn't know he heard it. If they are both present when this surfaces, Bice goes still in a way Aldo doesn't notice.

## Hooks

- Any patient listener gets two centuries of Le Paludi history and, embedded somewhere in the middle, whatever Aldo has been turning over quietly. He doesn't hide things — he just hasn't found the right moment.
- If asked about recent visitors, he will describe the watch officer in the corner table with complete accuracy and zero alarm: *"Ate the eel twice. Didn't finish the second time. Strange man."*
- He can name every family that has owned property in Le Paludi for as long as records exist, and some time before that. If the party needs to know who owns or operates something in the district, Aldo either knows or knows who would.
