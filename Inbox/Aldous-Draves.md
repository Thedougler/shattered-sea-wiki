# Aldous Draves

|                 |                                                          |

## Overview

Aldous Draves is a Crown-licensed import factor operating out of [[Port-Tidefall|Port Tidefall]] — which means he has spent thirty years reading shipping manifests, filing inspection appeals, attending maritime court proceedings, and learning the names of every clerk worth knowing. He is not a naval man. He is a man who has spent his life adjacent to the naval world, which is a different thing, and which he has never fully admitted is not the same thing.

He arranged his son's berth on the [[HCS-Surety]] through the same mechanism he arranges everything: patient cultivation of the right connections over years of being exactly useful enough to be remembered when something comes up. A patrol cutter posting is not a commission. It is not an officer's berth. It was what those connections could produce, and Aldous presented it to Geoffrey as the opening move in a plan rather than the ceiling of his reach.

His son has been somewhere on the eastern patrol routes and has not written in some time. He has filed formal inquiries with the Harbourmaster's Office. He is waiting.

---

## Role

Aldous is the administrative pressure behind Geoffrey's original berth: not an officer, not a sailor, but the kind of Crown-adjacent civilian whose favors can put a son on a patrol cutter and then call that success.

## Appearance & Manner

When you meet him, the first thing you notice is the clothes. The cut is correct; the insignia on his lapel is accurate to Crown administrative rank; the whole presentation reads as respectable Dravosi gentleman from a distance. Up close, the cut is a decade out of date. He has either not noticed or decided that noticing would require admitting something he prefers not to admit.

He is formal the way people are formal who were taught that informality is how family names become forgettable. He uses titles correctly and consistently. He asks questions with the precision of a man who has spent years asking questions whose answers he already needed. He does not smile easily, and when he does, it looks like a decision he made after weighing the alternatives.

## Wants

To close the gap.

The Draves family has Crown access, the right connections, and a name that carries weight in the administrative tier where he operates. But the line between *access* and *inside* has never been crossed, and the distance between the Draves branch and branches of the family that went further is a lesson Aldous absorbed early and has never managed to stop measuring against. His son becoming a man whose name reaches the ears of important Crown officers without being attached to a problem: that would be something.

He does not say this plainly. He does not say most things plainly.

---

## Voice

Formal, economical, precise. He refers to Geoffrey as "Geoffrey" in every context — no warmth or distance attached to it, just designation. When he says something kind, it arrives structured as a factual assessment. When he is displeased, he goes quieter rather than louder.

> *"Geoffrey is a capable young man. Whether capable becomes something more is a question of whether he applies it in the right direction."*

> *"The berth on the Surety was not a small thing to arrange. The Crown does not distribute patrol postings casually. I want to be clear about that."*

## Known Leverage

Aldous knows the public Crown wage tables, the limitations of his own connections, and enough factor gossip to understand that a missing patrol cutter is not a small administrative inconvenience.

## The Wage Situation

He has been a licensed factor for thirty years. Crown privateer marine ratings and their baseline wage schedule are standard knowledge in the Port Tidefall commercial tier. He knows that a rated marine earns considerably more than a carpenter-plus-supplement for the same boarding work. The math is not hidden. It is in the public Crown wage tables he has read hundreds of times.

Whether he explained the difference to Geoffrey is the question to hold. He may have assumed the boy would look it up. He may have believed that carpenter-plus-supplement was still a decent start — which is technically true, it is just not what Geoffrey thought it was. He may have known the distinction was not in Geoffrey's favor and said nothing because saying something would have required explaining that the berth was the ceiling, not the floor, of what his connections could produce.

If pressed directly, he will cite the contract language. The contract is accurate. He knows this. He is counting on it.

---

## The Dancing

He found out through a watch officer's notation in the Surety's duty logs — a minor observation, meant to amuse, filed because watch officers file things. He did not confront Geoffrey about it. He did not mention it. The moment when he could have said something without it becoming a larger conversation passed, and then enough time had gone by that starting it would have required explaining why he had said nothing.

What bothers him is not the dancing itself. It is the choice. Geoffrey chose it deliberately, over years, in secret — chose it over the things Aldous could understand and work with. He cannot explain why that specific choice produces the particular quality of frustration it does. He has stopped trying to understand it and started treating it as a closed file.

## Cedric Hollowell

He has met [[Cedric-Hollowell|Cedric Hollowell]] at Tidefall factor association meetings. They are not friends; they are two men who exist in the same professional tier and maintain civil working relations. When Aldous learned that Geoffrey kept returning to a dock near Hollowell property, he made no direct move — he did not know Verity Hollowell was involved, and by the time he understood the full picture, Cedric had already handled it.

He is not sure how he feels about the Hollowell terms. Professionally, they are reasonable. Personally, they underscore the Draves branch's standing in a way he finds uncomfortable.

## The Knighton Question

If Geoffrey's activities reach [[Rupert-Knighton|Knighton's]] desk — a defecting Crown sailor working for foreign privateers — whether Aldous distances himself from his son or finds a way to frame the whole sequence as bold initiative will depend entirely on how things land. He is not a disloyal man. He is a careful one.

---

## If Geoffrey Returns

**Successful, with money and connections:** He arranged the berth. He always believed the sea was the right path. He may have believed some version of this even before he knew how the story ended.

**Disgraced, wanted by the Crown:** He has no record of the arrangement. The connections he used were informal. Informal connections leave no paper trail.

**Dead:** He will have the Harbourmaster's inquiry letter framed and kept. He has already drafted it in his head.
