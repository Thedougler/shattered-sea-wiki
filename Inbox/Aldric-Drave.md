# Aldric Drave

> [!read-aloud]
> The man standing at the far end of the room wears a Dravosi dress uniform that is not quite right — the cut is correct, the insignia accurate, but the standard it matches has not been in use for two hundred years. He is tall, gaunt, and utterly still. When he turns to look at you, it is with the unhurried attention of someone who has been waiting in this room for a very long time and considers your arrival a minor event in a long sequence of minor events.

## Overview

Aldric Drave is the hidden founder of the Dravosi Crown and the undead architect behind its bloodline bureaucracy. He is a late-game pressure point, not a casual contact: his existence explains why lineage, archives, and Crown administration have become instruments of power.

## Role

Aldric is a campaign antagonist and the owner of the Draves bloodline mystery. Use him when the party starts pulling on deep Crown records, the Pearl of Souls, or the question of why Dravosi genealogy has been maintained with religious precision.

## Appearance & Manner

Tall and gaunt, with the bearing of a man who has never once doubted that the room belongs to him. His dress uniform is Dravosi colonial — epaulettes, insignia, cut — maintained to a standard that was retired two centuries ago. The fabric is immaculate. His skin is the colour of old vellum. His eyes are the same pale grey as the cliff face of [[Port-Tidefall|Calder's Tooth]], and they do not catch light the way living eyes do.

He looks like a portrait of a man rather than the man himself. The resemblance to certain faces in the oldest Dravosi genealogical records, if the party thinks to look, is exact.

**Roleplay Concept:** A dead king who hasn't told anyone yet. The Dravosi Crown in human form — cold, certain, and three hundred years past the point where it could have chosen differently.

## Voice & Delivery

- Formal, clipped Dravosi register. Old-fashioned phrasing that sounds like documents rather than conversation.
- Refers to current Crown officials by childhood nicknames or family diminutives they have never heard anyone use. He is the only living person — the only person — who knew them that way.
- **Floor:** Entirely at ease. Patient in the way of something that measures time in decades. Will hear you out completely before responding.
- **Crack:** When his Heir candidacy is threatened — when the strongest bloodline descendant is in danger or acting against his interests — the patience evaporates and something much older and colder surfaces. He has not needed to act directly in a long time. He remembers how.

> *"The Governor is a capable administrator. His grandfather was better. His great-grandmother was exceptional. The blood thins, as it always does. That is why I am here."*
>
> *"I have read every genealogical record the Crown has produced in this sea. I compiled the first ones myself. You will understand why I find your situation interesting."*

## Deep Records

Aldric Drave was the founder of the colonial project that became the Dravosi Crown — a sorcerer of considerable power who achieved Hierarch lichdom before the first Dravosi ship entered the Scatter, then seeded his bloodline deliberately into every noble family the Crown would later produce. The Crown is not named after his house by coincidence. He named it. He designed the administrative apparatus — the ledgers, the genealogical records, the documentation theology — because he needed a system that would track every living phylactery across three centuries of colonial expansion.

It worked. For two hundred years the Dravosi Crown was, without knowing it, a self-maintaining map of his bloodline.

The problem is time. The Scatter's mixed populations, intermarriage across colonial and indigenous lines, and three centuries of dilution have weakened the blood. His phylactery network is fraying at the edges. He is beginning the process of selecting an Heir — the most powerful sorcerous descendant currently living — to perform the Renewal ritual and begin the cycle again.

The [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] believes Aldric has identified the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Pearl-of-Souls-Item|Pearl of Souls]] as a way out of that bloodline bottleneck. If the Pearl can collect, hold, and transfer drowned souls without regard for ancestry, Aldric may be able to graft its function onto his Hierarch blood-binding: external anchors, non-descendant phylacteries, or a reservoir of souls that lets him stabilize himself without waiting for a suitable Heir. The Concordat does not know which version is possible. It considers any version unacceptable.

He lives beneath [[Port-Tidefall|Port Tidefall]], in the original [[Drave-Vaults|Drave family vaults]] sealed into the cliff face below Fort Crestwall. The current Dravosi administration does not know the vaults exist except as sealed architectural errors. The fort was named after his family. Nobody thought to ask why.

> [!SECRET]
> Aldric Drave is a Hierarch — a sorcerer lich whose soul is anchored to his own bloodline. Every living descendant carrying Dravosi noble blood is a phylactery. The Dravosi Crown's genealogical archives, maintained with religious devotion by the Crown's administrators, are an inadvertent map of his phylactery network.
>
> **The encounter problem:** Destroying Aldric permanently requires eliminating every living blood-descendant — which includes the Governor, senior naval officers, and dozens of Dravosi noble families who have no idea what they are. The party cannot simply kill him. He will reform within weeks or months depending on how many descendants remain. The only permanent solutions require either breaking the blood-binding ritual at its source, or a campaign of elimination that makes the party complicit in something that looks a great deal like a purge.
>
> **The Heir:** The strongest sorcerous blood currently living in the Dravosi lineage is someone the party will already know by the time this reveal lands. The identity of the Heir is a campaign decision — but whoever it is, Aldric has been watching them.
>
> **Late-game reveal.** He surfaces when the party starts pulling on Dravosi genealogical threads, when someone accesses the deep records at Port Tidefall, or when the Heir's sorcerous power attracts his attention to the party.
