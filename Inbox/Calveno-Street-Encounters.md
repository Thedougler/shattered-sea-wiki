# Calveno — Street Encounters

Drop these when the scene needs texture, the party is restless, or you want to press a specific PC. None are mandatory. All can be played social-first and allowed to escalate, or bypassed entirely with no consequence.

Festival context: these are set during or just after Il Gioco delle Beffe, which means crowds are large, watch attention is diffuse, and unusual behaviour reads as prank or performance until it clearly isn't.

---

## 1. The Wrong Mark

**Setup:** A Beffa team — three young Calveno dockworkers with a bucket of fish oil, a rope, and a signed permission slip from someone they have clearly coerced — has targeted one of the party as their mark. They have been following for two blocks. They have the wrong person, and they are about to commit.

> [!read-aloud]
> The oil lands before the sound reaches you — a wide cold splash from above, and a cheer from the balcony. Three young Calvenans lean over the railing, rope still swinging. The crowd turns. Someone laughs. The smell arrives a moment later: fish, and not the fresh kind.

**What happens:** The Beffa team springs their prank (bucket drop from a balcony above a narrow calle). DC 12 Dexterity save or the PC is drenched in fish oil and loses the contents of one jacket pocket to the splash. The team cheers and runs.

**If pursued:** Three Commoners and one Spy (the team captain, who is actually fast and knows the district). Catch check: DC 13 Athletics or Acrobatics through festival crowd. If cornered: they fold immediately. They have a written Beffa permit, a team name (*La Trota Gloriosa*), and a completely wrong name for their intended target. They are mortified.

**If escalated past comedy:** The Beffa captain has enough festival legal cover that the watch will not intervene unless weapons come out. If the party makes a scene, they draw a crowd. Dravosi watch officers find large crowds interesting.

**Flavour:** The permit is real. The name they were given as the mark belongs to a Tessarine factor who has been running a Beffa team for six years and apparently has enemies. The factor's address is on the permit.

> [!dm]
> Pull this when the Beffa needs to feel real and ambient rather than plot-adjacent. The Tessarine factor whose name is on the permit is a loose thread you can develop or ignore — he is not a prepared character, just a name on a piece of paper.

## 2. Ponte Muscle

**Setup:** A broad man in a dockworker's coat is blocking a canal walkway on one of the Bridge district footbridges, collecting a "festival toll" from anyone trying to cross. Two friends wait on the far side. The man they are currently collecting from is clearly not going to pay and is about to do something he will regret.

> [!read-aloud]
> A broad man in a dockworker's coat stands in the middle of the walkway. Not moving through it — standing in it. His two friends wait on the far side of the bridge. There is a man between him and you who is nearly out of options.

**What's actually happening:** The three are hired muscle for a canal freight broker who has been using festival crowd management as an excuse to slow competitors' deliveries through this bridge. This is marginally legal and entirely ordinary for the Bridge district.

**What the party can do:**
- Pay (2 cp each, the toll is nominal, this is about the delay)
- Push past without paying: the broad man puts a hand on the nearest PC's chest — DC 10 Persuasion or Intimidation to make him reconsider; failure means his two friends come off the far side
- Intervene on behalf of the man being held: the broker's man becomes the problem; the man being held turns out to be a Passage runner for a Warren contact, and Nona hears about it within a day

**If it goes to combat:** 1 Thug (leader, AC 11, HP 32, club +4, 2d4+2) and 2 Bandits (AC 12, HP 11, scimitar +3, 1d6+1). They do not fight to the death — at half HP the leader calls it off.

> [!dm]
> The Passage runner is named Orso. He is not a significant character. If the party helps him, Nona knows before they reach the Warren. She does not mention it first; she waits to see if they do.

## 3. The Chase

**Setup:** Someone has just lifted a purse — either from a party member (if no one is watching their pockets in the festival crowd, this happens automatically) or from a well-dressed merchant who immediately starts screaming for help. The thief is already moving, weaving into the Beffa crowd.

> [!read-aloud]
> A hand lifts clean from a coat pocket and disappears into the crowd. The merchant reaches for his purse and finds air. He spins in a full circle. Ten feet ahead of you, a small shape is already three bodies deep and not slowing down.

**The thief:** Fen, a teenage halfling, fast and small, who has been working the festival crowds for three days. Not violent. Not part of any operation. Just good at this. Use **Spy** stats, +5 Stealth, +5 Acrobatics.

**Chase structure:** Three checks, each DC 12. Acrobatics for cutting through the crowd, Athletics for straightaways, Perception to keep eyes on. Two successes: cornered in a dead-end calle off Le Paludi. One success: Fen makes it to a canal and goes in the water (swim check, then either resurface in the Rialto or duck into a maintenance hatch). Zero successes: gone.

**If cornered:** Fen has the purse, a folding knife she will not use, and a sob story that is about 40% true. DC 14 Insight to pick the true parts. The true part: she is paying off a debt to a Bridge district broker who found her working his territory. The false part: she claims she was going to return the purse anyway.

**What she has:** Whatever was in the lifted purse, plus 11 sp, a Beffa team's claimed sigil (stolen from a dead-drop, which is its own problem if the team finds out), and a maintenance hatch key she will not explain.

> [!dm]
> The maintenance hatch key fits the sewer network below the Bridge connector. She does not know this — she stole it thinking it was worth something. It is worth something. Leave it in her possession unless the party is specifically looking for it.

## 4. A Man in the Canal

**Setup:** Late evening, narrow calle beside a Le Paludi canal. A man goes in — tripped by festival crowd surge, pushed by persons unknown, or stepped off a gangplank he misjudged. He is not swimming. The canal current here runs under a low stone arch twelve feet away; past the arch it is dark and fast and another hundred feet to the next opening.

> [!read-aloud]
> The sound is wrong — not a splash, a drop. A man surfaces in the canal beside the walkway, already turned the wrong direction. He is not swimming. Twelve feet ahead, the canal narrows under a stone arch into the dark. The current is pulling.

**The window:** Two rounds before he goes under the arch. DC 10 Athletics to swim out to him in round one; DC 12 in round two (current pulling). A rope thrown from the bank: DC 10 Dexterity to land it on him, DC 12 Strength to haul against the current. A boat pole, a Beffa banner pole, an awning rope — the canal side has options if someone looks (DC 8 Perception).

**Who he is (roll or choose):**

| d4 | Identity | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | A Tessarine factor's ledger clerk, drunk on festival wine | His employer gives the party a minor factor discount as thanks — 10% off one Calveno merchant purchase |
| 2 | A Dravosi junior watch officer, off duty and embarrassed | He owes the party a favour he cannot officially acknowledge; the party has his name |
| 3 | A Warren Kin runner, Passage courier | Nona hears. The party's Tangle credit goes up without them knowing why |
| 4 | The thief from encounter 3, if she ran into the canal during the chase | She still has the purse |

**If the party does not intervene:** He goes under the arch. Roll d6: 1–2 he surfaces further down and is pulled out by a gondolier; 3–6 he is recovered from the canal the next morning. Either way, someone saw the party standing there.

> [!dm]
> The canal current under the arch is dangerous but not immediately fatal — this is a tense skill moment, not a death sentence. Use the watch-and-do-nothing outcome only if it creates interesting character pressure, not as punishment.

## 5. Papers at the Gate

**Setup:** A Dravosi canal-gate checkpoint at the entry to the Arsenal district, staffed by one watch sergeant (Veteran) and four guards. The party has reached this gate going somewhere specific. The sergeant is doing his job.

> [!read-aloud]
> The canal gate is a stone arch with a portcullis drawn halfway. A sergeant in Dravosi grey leans against the frame with a ledger. He looks at the party the way men who have been standing in one place for six hours look at things that require effort.

**He wants:** Ship registry or travelling papers. For the *Uncertainty*: the new Calveno private-vessel papers (if Perrin arranged them through the Warren) or an explanation. For PCs on foot: guild affiliation, residential address, or a credible reason to be at the Arsenal end during a festival.

**Delmar:** Any Dravosi officer who has served on this class of cutter will clock the *Uncertainty*'s silhouette. The sergeant has not — he is Canal Commission, not Navy. But DC 14 Charisma (Deception) to maintain the Fisk persona under direct questioning; the sergeant has a habit of asking the same question twice, slightly differently.

**Jean-Claude:** DC 12 Deception with working papers. DC 15 without. If the sergeant has received a flag notice for a deserter matching JC's description: DC 17, disadvantage.

**If it goes wrong:** The sergeant calls for two more guards (now 6 total) and asks the party to step to the side. He is not aggressive — he is thorough. The window for talking out of this closes after three rounds. After that he is sending for a lieutenant and this becomes a significant problem.

> [!mechanic]
> Sergeant: Veteran stats (AC 17, HP 58, multiattack, +5 to hit). Guards: AC 16, HP 11, spear +3. The sergeant will not draw first; he will call for backup. If the party runs: he does not chase personally but the incident is logged and Dravosi presence around the party increases for 48 hours.

> [!dm]
> This works best as a papers check the party can pass with preparation, not a fight they stumble into. Use it to put pressure on identities and remind the table that Calveno has a Dravosi administrative layer. If Perrin handled the Warren ship papers, this is where that investment pays off cleanly.

## 6. The Festival Drunk

**Setup:** The Mercatura, mid-afternoon, festival crowd. A very large half-orc in a sellsword's rig is at the centre of a growing circle of space. He has knocked over a wine stall, is currently singing something in what might be Orcish, and is holding a bottle in one hand and his sword still sheathed in the other. The stall owner is bleeding from a split lip and looking at the party.

> [!read-aloud]
> A ring of empty space has opened in the Mercatura crowd. At the centre of it, a half-orc in a sellsword's rig holds a wine bottle in one hand and his sword — still sheathed — in the other. A wooden stall is on its side behind him. The stall owner is looking at you.

**The situation:** Gost (use **Berserker** stats, CR 2, AC 13, HP 67, greataxe +5, 2d12+3) is not violent yet. He is drunk enough that the line between celebration and violence is thin and moving. The watch is four minutes away — someone ran for them. The crowd wants to see what happens.

**What the party can do:**
- Talk him down: DC 13 Charisma (Persuasion) or DC 10 Intimidation. He responds to direct honesty and disrespects anything that reads as management. Jean-Claude telling him the watch is coming and this is going to be a bad four minutes: automatic success.
- Get him moving: Persuasion DC 10 if someone offers to buy him another drink somewhere quieter. He goes. No fight, no watch.
- Wait for the watch: the watch sergeant (see encounter 5) is not gentle with drunk sellswords during festival week. Gost ends up in a holding cell; the party may have opinions about that.
- Let it escalate: he throws the bottle, hits a passing Tessarine merchant's hat, and now there are two problems.

**If he fights:** Reckless Attack, no Danger Sense (too drunk). First two rounds he is at disadvantage on saves; after that the adrenaline is burning the alcohol and he is simply dangerous.

**After:** Gost is looking for work. If the party handled this without bloodshed and without the watch, he finds them the next morning and offers his sword at the going rate. He is not subtle, not quiet, and extremely reliable. Use or discard.

> [!dm]
> Gost is a loose hiring hook, not a required NPC. If the party needs a seventh crew member and finds him charming, he is available at ordinary sellsword rates (4–6 gp/week). If they never follow up, he disappears into the festival and is not mentioned again.

## Combat Escalations — Jean-Claude Arc

These three encounters connect to the handler cell plot. Unlike the general encounters above, they require setup: Jean-Claude active in the city, or the party already moving on the Marrow's Crate / Ferrin Locke investigation. Pull from here when those beats heat up — do not drop them cold.

---

### Handler Cell

**Setup:** Jean-Claude spots what looks like handler reconnaissance — a pair of Grung in nondescript trade clothes asking systematic questions about boat shifts, bridge crush timing, or which yard workers sleep aboard. The questions are wrong for tourists. They are exactly right for a blue-caste handler mapping a captive extraction route.

He is the only one in the party who recognises it.

> [!read-aloud]
> Two figures in trade clothes at the same Bridge pillar Jean-Claude clocked earlier — different position now, same posture. They are not watching the performer. They are watching the crowd move around the performer. The hour is wrong for the spot.

**Trigger:** Jean-Claude's player acts on the suspicion (confirms with Insight DC 13, or just acts on it). If the party engages, the handlers try to withdraw and break contact. If cornered, they fight.

**Location:** Le Paludi — narrow canal, 5 ft alleys, two boats tied at the nearest mooring. Poor light. Festival crowd two streets over and getting no closer.

**Enemies:**

| Stat block | HP | AC | Key |
|---|---|---|---|
| Blue-Caste Handler × 2 | 16 | 13 | Scout (MM p.349). Shortbow +4, 1d8+3 piercing + Grung Poison (DC 11 Con or Poisoned 1 min). Standing Leap 20 ft as part of movement. Breaks and runs at half HP. |

One handler carries binding rope and a sealed wax message cylinder — blank on inspection, but the wax is pressed with a mark Jean-Claude recognises from Botukuri shipping labels.

**Stakes:**
- **Capture alive:** the handler knows arrival timing and the extraction canal route. Telling the Warren closes one of the raid's lanes.
- **Kill both:** Simone does not learn the cell was made. One extraction lane goes dark without explanation.
- **Let them go:** Simone learns the party is in Calveno. Jean-Claude's face is now a report.

---

### Serin Haut at Bay

**Setup:** The party has moved on the Marrow's Crate investigation and Haut has been made. She does not run to the Warren (she doesn't know where it is). She has a backup — a local hired thug watching her door — and she will fight to protect her room and her codebook.

> [!read-aloud]
> The corridor outside her room is three feet wide. Haut is at the far end of it, facing you. The door behind her is closed. A broad man with a club stands between you and her. There is no comfortable angle in this hallway.

**Trigger:** Party approaches Haut's lodgings without neutralising her first, or she has identified them and moved to intercept.

**Location:** The narrow corridor outside her rented room above a canal-facing notary's office. Or an ambush on the party's route back through Le Paludi.

**Enemies:**

| Stat block | HP | AC | Key |
|---|---|---|---|
| Serin Haut | 27 | 12 | Spy (MM p.349). Concealed shortsword. 3 doses basic poison (ingestion DC 11, 2d6 poison on fail). +1 Deception. Uses Cunning Action to disengage and break line of sight. |
| Hired backup | 32 | 11 | Thug (MM p.350). Club +4, 2d4+2 bludgeoning. Stays between Haut and the exit. |

Haut does not die to protect her handler. Below 10 HP she offers the codebook and cooperation in exchange for her life. This is genuine — she is a professional, not a martyr.

**Stakes:**
- **Capture or turn over to the Tangle:** canal district gets quieter; Dravosi lose an active intelligence asset in Calveno.
- **Killed:** codebook and coin in her room; Dravosi will notice the gap within a week.
- **Escaped:** she flags the party's description and departs Calveno; no longer a Calveno threat but someone has been told about them.

### Le Paludi Hired Muscle

**Setup:** The party has been asking loud questions, making deals that offended someone, or become conspicuous enough that someone in Calveno wants them to stop. Four thugs are waiting on the mooring where the party tied their shore boat. A fifth does the talking.

> [!read-aloud]
> Four people on the mooring where you tied your boat. They are already facing your direction. Your shore boat is behind them. The one in front has not uncrossed his arms. The other three have.

**Trigger:** DM discretion. Use when the party has been visibly active in one district for too long, or when they've crossed a named Calveno figure (Tessarine factor, Vretek-adjacent broker, anyone with hired capacity).

**Location:** Canal-side mooring, night. Water on one side, wall on the other, a 10 ft walkway. The party's shore boat is behind the muscle.

**Enemies:**

| Stat block | HP | AC | Key |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thug × 4 | 32 | 11 | Thug (MM p.350). Pack Tactics (advantage if ally adjacent to target). Heavy crossbow on one. |
| Bandit Captain (optional) | 65 | 15 | Bandit Captain (MM p.344). Multiattack. Parry reaction. Present if the sender wanted this done right. |

**Who sent them:** Leave unresolved. The thugs don't know or won't say. One of: Iacopo Fieschi (Tessarine envoy, embarrassed by something the party made public), a broker whose client is the Red Lady salvage commission, or a Dravosi commercial factor who noticed them at the wrong time.

**Stakes:**
- **Take a prisoner:** learn who's watching the party and why.
- **Win without a prisoner:** someone spent money on this. The party is now a known quantity to somebody.
- **Flee:** the muscle doesn't follow past the canal bridge. The boat is still there, with the muscle standing on it.
