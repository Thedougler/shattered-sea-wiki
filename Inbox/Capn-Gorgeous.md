## Overview

He was the Dravosi enforcer Barnaby Rook sent down the ladder first. Heavy crossbow, tight hold, five strangers in the dark — standard inspection procedure. He'd probably done it a hundred times.

The party put him down before he realized he stepped into the trap.

One poison-tipped arrow through his shoulder, one musket blast to the chest at point blank range, and his shattered kneecap. Simultaneous, then a moment later the evidence of which was swept within the illusion hiding the party. He never acted. He never shouted. He hit the deck.

His crewmates had taken to calling him Cap'n Gorgeous. They shouted it when he went down. Whether that was grief, dark humor, or just the only name anyone had for him by that point is hard to say.

---

## Appearance & Manner

The nickname wasn't ironic. He was a genuinely striking man — athletic build that made the hold feel smaller when he walked into it, long golden hair that moved like he was perpetually in a sea breeze, a jawline that belonged on a coin, and pale blue eyes sharp enough that you noticed them even in poor light.

He looked like the kind of man who wins fights just by showing up. He did not get to demonstrate that today.

---

## Noble House

He wore a House Knighton crest on his coat. His crewmates called him Cap'n Gorgeous and probably had no idea what that meant. [[Shepherd-Grigori|Shepherd Grigori]] recognized it immediately.

House Knighton has served the Dravosi Crown since its founding and has accrued corresponding leverage. His father will hear that his son died aboard a Crown patrol vessel that is now missing from Tidefall. He will send people — not the Crown navy, his own people — to find out what happened and to whom.

The party does not know his real name. It will surface eventually.
