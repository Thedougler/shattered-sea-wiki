## Overview

Cedric Hollowell runs a mid-tier import agency out of offices near the Tidefall inspection pier. Thirty years in Crown commercial licensing has made him fluent in the language of fees, delays, and expedited resolution. He is not corrupt in any dramatic sense — he is simply a man who has spent his career understanding that every transaction has a price and every relationship has a use, and who applies this logic to his family with the same unsentimental efficiency he applies to cargo.

When a sailor kept returning to a dock near his property, he made inquiries, assessed the situation, and set terms. He was not wrong that Geoffrey Draves had no money and no prospects. The terms were calibrated to seem fair while being practically unachievable on patrol cutter wages. He does not think about this distinction.

His daughter runs his secondary accounts better than anyone he has hired. He considers this settled. What is not settled is her refusal to conclude any of the introductions he arranges, which is becoming a logistical problem as much as a personal one.

## Terms & Manner

He speaks the way someone speaks when they have been negotiating with Crown inspectors for thirty years: precise, patient, and never the first to name what something is actually worth. He does not raise his voice. He restates his position more clearly when challenged rather than backing down.

He is not hostile to the party by default — he is a factor, and factors are useful to people moving cargo through Tidefall. He will help if there is a reason to help and a reasonable accounting for it.

**The thing he does not know:** [[Verity-Hollowell|Verity]] has found a gap in his primary accounts — bonded cargo moved through Hollowell licensing without a manifest entry. Whether he put it there or someone used his credentials is unclear. If the party knows about it before he does, that is leverage. If he knows about it and is managing it quietly, he is in a worse position than he looks.

> *"My daughter is an excellent account manager. I'm sure whatever brought you here can be addressed through the appropriate channel."*

> *"The terms I set were reasonable. A man who cannot meet them in three years cannot meet them in ten. That is arithmetic, not cruelty."*
