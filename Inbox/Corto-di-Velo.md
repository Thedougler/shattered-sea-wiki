# Corto di Velo

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Weapon (shortbow) |
| **Rarity** | Uncommon |
| **Attunement** | No |

A Calveno shortbow on a short yew stave, barely four feet, with a recurve at both limbs for draw-to-power ratio. The grip is wrapped in oiled linen, not leather — practical in wet conditions. No decoration, no maker's mark. The string is dock-grade fiber. This is a work tool.

---

## Properties

**[HB]** You gain a **+1 bonus** to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon.

**[HB] Point Blank.** Being within 5 feet of a hostile creature doesn't impose disadvantage on attack rolls made with this weapon.

---

## Balance Notes

*For DM reference. Do not share.*

- +1 shortbow is uncommon baseline.
- Point Blank removes a situational penalty rather than adding a new bonus — no new damage, no additional resources. It rewards tactical positioning rather than rewarding passive ownership.
- Jean-Claude with Archery Fighting Style (+2) and this bow attacks at +5 in melee range (where normal shortbow use would be at disadvantage). This is equivalent to the hand crossbow's close-range advantage without the loading limitation. The shortbow's superior range (80/320 vs. 30/120) compensates.
- No attunement — Jean-Claude can carry this alongside other gear without an attunement slot cost.
- Verdict: the Point Blank property is a focused, situationally powerful quality-of-life fix for an urban ranger. Uncommon is correct.

## Cost

**Purchase: 30 gp** (Velo Quarter dealer price, unidentified).

The dealer knows this bow is better-made than his festival stock. He does not know it is magical. He bought it in an estate lot from a Calveno dockmaster's sale eight months ago and has been asking 30 gp because it looks expensive. He is not a bowyer. He has not tried it.

**Market value (identified): 200 gp.**

> [!mechanic]
> The bow carries no visual enchantment — no glow, no inscription. DC 15 Arcana or *Identify* reveals both the +1 bonus and the Point Blank property. Until identified, the +1 registers as unusually consistent groupings, not as magic. Jean-Claude will notice the Point Blank property the first time he fires from within 5 feet of a target and the expected penalty does not appear.

## History

The Velo Quarter dealers supply Beffa teams. Most of the stock is generic; the Corto di Velo pattern is the exception — a Calveno dock design built for sailors and festival operatives who might need to shoot over cargo stacks, through hatch openings, or while someone is already inside their reach. The Velo dealer has two in stock at 30 gp. Jean-Claude is the first to ask the right question.

The name is vernacular: *Corto di Velo* — "Velo Short." Not a proper name. The bow's maker has been dead for fifteen years; the pattern stayed.
