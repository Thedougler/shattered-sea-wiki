# The Crown Islands

![[1778025470700~2.png]]
![[Crown-Islands.webp|Mountainous green Crown Islands ringed by bright reefs under clearing storm clouds]]

## Overview

Five large islands running east to west across the northern arc — broad-shouldered land with forested highlands, deep natural harbours, and pale stone fortworks placed by engineers with cannon range in mind. The Dravosi flag flies from every major anchorage. The southern coasts face the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]], the northern coasts take open ocean swell, and the reef systems between islands — called the Greyteeth — have contradicted three generations of charts without apology. Local pilots are worth what they charge.

The Crown Islands are safer than the [[Midchain|Midchain]] in roughly the way a locked strongroom is safer than an alley. Patrols answer distress signals. Ports keep records. Cargo moves under recognized law, which merchants value and sailors tolerate, because recognized law tends to arrive with fees attached and harbourmasters who remember what stamp you were supposed to have.

The patrols keep the big harbours clean, not the whole sea. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/humanoid/pirate|Pirates]] still work the blind water between reefs, usually under captains who know exactly how long a distress signal takes to reach a fort. The natural threats are less theatrical but more constant: [[hunter-shark|hunter sharks]] off the southern coasts, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-crab|giant crabs]] around wreck-strewn reefs, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-boar|giant boars]] in the highland forest, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-axe-beak|giant axe beaks]] on open ridge roads and scrubby uplands, and [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wolf-spider|giant wolf spiders]] in old drains and sinkholes. Crown law makes those problems documented, not solved.

## Geography

The Crown Islands form the northern arc of the [[Verdant-Scatter|Verdant Scatter]]. Their southern coasts face the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]], while their northern coasts take open ocean swell and cold upwelling. The islands are larger and more mountainous than most of the [[Midchain|Midchain]], with forested highlands, deep-water approaches, productive fishing grounds, and fortified harbours.

The Greyteeth reefs between islands have contradicted three generations of charts, and not all losses in those waters are reef casualties. An [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/dragons/adult-blue-dragon|adult blue dragon]] laired in the northern Greyteeth limestone uses the reef's reputation as cover for a second category of loss. Certain captains who know the northern passage fly specific colours and keep an unsealed chest on the aft deck. Captains who don't know about the arrangement take their chances with what harbourmasters file as navigational error. The Dravosi Crown does not officially acknowledge this situation. The Crown's pragmatism and the dragon's lawfulness have produced an arrangement that works well enough that neither side has broken it. ^[inferred]

## Routes & Access

Ships enter the Crown Islands through controlled harbours, charted convoy lanes, and reef passages that reward local pilots. The southern approaches connect directly to the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]], while the northern routes cross colder fishing grounds and Greyteeth reef systems where charts are treated as suggestions rather than guarantees.

Most pirate ships avoid the Crown Islands unless they have a very good exit. Small raiders such as [[Knifes-Wake|the Knife's Wake]] can still work fog, false colours, and Greyteeth confusion against soft targets before a patrol arrives. A larger threat like the [[The-Glass-Debt|Glass Debt]] does not belong in Crown harbour waters for long, but even a credible report of her near a convoy route is enough to make HCS captains start counting escorts.

## People & Power

The Crown Islands are the [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi Crown]]'s strongest regional foothold: forts, harbourmasters, inspection ledgers, ratings, patrol cutters, and trade houses with authority behind them. Local pilots and harbour families still matter because control on shore does not make the Greyteeth honest, but the first face a visitor usually meets is uniformed, licensed, or keeping a ledger.

## Trade & Resources

The cold upwelling off the northern Crown coast is the most productive fishing water in the Scatter: bluefin, yellowtail, and large reef fish in volume. Fishing fleets out of Tidefall's South Harbour work these grounds year-round. The Greyteeth reef systems produce shellfish in quantity — spiny lobster, sea urchin, oyster beds in the shallower channels.

## Flora

The broad-shouldered islands carry forested highlands and older interior forest away from logging roads and fort tracks. Specific Crown Island flora is not yet documented beyond productive highland forest and managed settlement land.

## Fauna

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/sea-life/hunter-shark|Hunter sharks]] are the consistent offshore threat across all five islands — the fishing grounds off the northern Crown coast and the Strait approaches to the south are both productive enough to concentrate predators year-round. The Greyteeth reef systems add structure that small reef sharks and larger hunters use as ambush geometry, which makes capsized-dinghy recoveries more complicated than they should be.

The highland interiors carry [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-boar|giant boars]] in the older forest sections away from logging roads and fort tracks. They are a documented bounty animal on Harwick and a livestock hazard on Aldenmere, and they are treated with the mild seriousness that a chronic problem earns — reported, managed, not solved. The Crown's interior roads run through boar country as a matter of geography.

The northern Greyteeth is a different category of concern. An [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/dragons/adult-blue-dragon|adult blue dragon]] lairs in the limestone reef structures and uses the Greyteeth's existing reputation for unreliable charts as operational cover. The Dravosi Crown does not officially acknowledge this. The captains who fly the right colours and keep an unsealed chest on the aft deck understand it practically.

## Hazards

The Crown Islands' hazards are documented rather than solved: Greyteeth reef errors, pirate false colours, hunter sharks, giant crabs, highland boars, axe beaks on exposed routes, wolf spiders in old drains and sinkholes, and the unacknowledged blue dragon arrangement in the northern reefs.

## Current Hooks

- The Greyteeth dragon arrangement remains officially unacknowledged, but captains who know the colours and chest protocol keep surviving routes that other ships lose to "navigational error." ^[inferred]
- Reports of the [[The-Glass-Debt|Glass Debt]] near convoy routes force Crown captains to consolidate escorts and expose delays elsewhere.
