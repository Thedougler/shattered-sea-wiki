# Dath-Mar

> *A hard place kept for hard hours.*

The smallest of the five [[Sunken-Crown|Sunken Crown]] islands. Its name preserves the local tabaxi sense of strengthened stone — a place of endurance rather than comfort. A few individuals live there year-round by choice. Most use it only when they need to.

[[Keth-Naar]] hunters retreat here when a storm splits boats from home, when someone needs distance from the central settlement, or when a decision is too heavy to bring to the elders without sitting with it first. The island's value is not what it offers — it can hold a person above the water when the rest of the Blue Hole turns against them.

## Geography

Dath-Mar is a hard, exposed rock with leeward approaches, a sheltered cut, black stone, higher cracks, and tide-dependent access. It is not comfortable and is not meant to be.

## Shelter Cairns

Cairns mark the leeward approaches and the sheltered cut in the rock, accessible only at certain tide angles. They're maintained by whoever last used them. Leaving one worse than you found it is a serious breach — the next arrival may not have the luxury of doing without it.

The current reversals around the Blue Hole have made the approach less predictable lately. Skiffs strand on the wrong side of the rock more often than they used to.

The island is too exposed for deep jungle, but [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-lizard|giant lizards]] sun against black stone after storms, and [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-bat|giant bats]] use the higher cracks when weather grounds them. Keth-Naar hunters treat both as one more reason to check a cairn before trusting it.

## Roads & Routes

Approach depends on tide angle, leeward shelter, and cairn knowledge. The island is small and exposed, so route choice is less about roads than about finding the correct rock cut before weather changes.

## People & Power

Dath-Mar is held by Keth-Naar custom rather than occupation. The rule is maintenance: leave a cairn worse than you found it and the next arrival may pay for it.

## Trade & Resources

The island does not trade. Its resource is survival space: a place to wait out weather, separation, or a decision that cannot yet be brought home.

## Flora

Dath-Mar is too exposed for deep jungle. Vegetation is sparse and not yet separately documented beyond the hard stone refuge environment.

## Fauna

The island is too exposed for deep growth, which makes its animal population visible in ways the larger islands are not. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-lizard|Giant lizards]] use the black stone after storms — the rock absorbs heat faster than the surrounding air, and the animals move across it in numbers large enough that fresh arrivals at the cairns should check the paths before using them. They do not threaten people directly but will hold ground and display aggressively when surprised on warming stone.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-bat|Giant bats]] use the higher cracks as storm shelter. The crevice system through the rock runs deeper than the surface suggests, and a colony displaced from their normal roost by weather may fill the sheltered cut in numbers that make using the cairns uncomfortable until the weather moves on. Keth-Naar hunters treat both as a reason to check any cairn before committing weight to it — not because the animals are necessarily dangerous, but because a refuge that requires dealing with an occupied crack is not a refuge that arrived at a good time.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-crab|Giant crabs]] work the tidal zone at the island's base. The rock pools on the leeward side are productive enough to draw them from the reef, and at low water the cairn approaches pass through crab territory. Large individuals at the water's edge are assertive enough to contest passage on the stepping paths. Keth-Naar hunters harvest them selectively, which manages the count without eliminating the population.

## Hazards

Exposure, current reversals, tide windows, giant lizards, giant bats, giant crabs, and failing predictability around the Blue Hole.

## Current Hooks

- Current reversals have made skiffs strand on the wrong side of the rock more often.
- If the refuge stops functioning, Keth-Naar loses its safest fallback during storms or hunting failures.
