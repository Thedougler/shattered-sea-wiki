# The *Dead Lady*

> *She is always upwind, regardless of actual wind direction. She does not pursue. She is already where you are going.*

**Type:** Brigantine (spectral), Tier 2 | **Owner:** raw/ingested/Umberlee | **Status:** Dormant until triggered — see Deployment below

The [[content/shattered-sea/ships/Red-Lady|*Red Lady*]], raised by raw/ingested/Umberlee as a punishment instrument. She exists to compel [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk|Fisk]] to retrieve the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]], not to kill him. Umberlee cannot kill Fisk without losing the five captains' souls into the Maw a second time. She knows this. The *Dead Lady* is pressure, not execution.

---
---

## Appearance

Her hull matches the *[[Red-Lady|Red Lady]]*'s outline exactly: same length, same rigging configuration, same dark red paint — now the colour of old blood in water. The figurehead is the same carved woman, eyes still forward. Her wake does not foam. She makes no sound at any distance. Other ships that sight her know immediately what she is.

**Damage Immunity:** Poison, psychic, non-magical bludgeoning/piercing/slashing.

**Damage Vulnerability:** Radiant, force.

**Destroying her:** Reducing to 0 HP disperses her for 1d4 weeks, after which Umberlee reconstitutes her. Permanent destruction requires a specific ritual — a campaign-significant event, not a standard encounter resolution.

---

## Crew

Crewed by the rank-and-file sailors of Fisk's original five-ship fleet (not the five captains, who are in Fisk's mind and unavailable to Umberlee), plus additional drowned souls from Umberlee's general collection. Approximately 30 crew. Recognisable as individuals to anyone who sailed with the fleet. They do not speak. They go about their work with complete mechanical competence.

If Fisk sees them close enough to identify faces, he will recognise them. The five captains in his mind will also recognise them.

**Type:** Shadow (standard statblock, ship-appropriate). Can move through solid hull material — locked hatches do not stop them. Do not initiate boarding unless Stage 3 or later.

**She has no captain.** Umberlee drives her directly. There is no intermediary to bargain with, deceive, or remove.

---

## Special Properties

**Dead Reckoning.** She always knows where Fisk is. Cannot be misdirected, lost, or misled. Navigation tricks, false trails, fog, and weather do not affect her course.

**Forbidding Tide.** Ships within 1 mile experience reversed currents and unnatural headwinds. Tier 1 ships: −20 miles/day. Tier 2 ships: −10 miles/day. Passive and constant — not a combat action.

**Spectral Boarding.** Her crew can pass through solid hull material. Boarding actions do not require grapples or entry points.

**The Voice Below Deck.** When within half a mile of Fisk, he can hear voices from below her waterline — his crew, calling up through the hull. The five captains in his head hear it too and recognise the voices. Not a mechanical effect; information delivered without a save. The DM decides what, if anything, the voices say.

**Umberlee's Wake.** Any creature that falls overboard within 300 feet must make a DC 16 Strength save at the start of each turn or be pulled 30 feet toward her hull. A creature pulled into contact with the hull is subject to Spectral Boarding crew response.

---

## Spectral Cannons

Fire bolts of compressed cold saltwater. Silent at the moment of firing — the shot arrives with only the sound of water displaced.

- **vs. crew:** 4d10 cold damage; DC 14 Con save or gain 1 level of exhaustion
- **vs. hull:** Double damage against Tier 1 vessel hull material

## Deployment

### Trigger

[[Waveservants]] deliver Umberlee's terms to Fisk once she becomes aware he has survived and is operating again. She sets a deadline (suggest 8–12 weeks from first contact) to demonstrate movement toward retrieving the Pearl. If the deadline passes without meaningful action, the *Dead Lady* is dispatched.

---

### Stage 1 — Contact

A Waveservant delivers Umberlee's terms directly to Fisk: retrieve the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]] from the Maw and return it. The Waveservant names the deadline. She does not describe the consequence. She does not need to. The deadline does not extend if Fisk negotiates, stalls, or ignores it.

---

### Stage 2 — Sighting

The *Dead Lady* appears on the horizon. She matches the party's heading but does not close. Visible at dawn and dusk, gone by midday, visible again by nightfall.

Other ships that see her give the party distance. Ports where she has been spotted receive the party with visible unease. Not a Reputation loss yet — a warning that one is coming. The five captains in Fisk's mind are aware she is there.

---

### Stage 3 — Approach

The *Dead Lady* closes to within weapon range of the party's ship but does not fire on Fisk's vessel directly. She attacks other ships in the party's company: supply ships, ally vessels, any ship that moves to assist against her. She circles rather than closes.

Her crew begin boarding other vessels — not Fisk's ship. They take crew from those ships overboard. Harbours with Waveservant presence close to the party rather than host the confrontation.

**Reputation loss:** 1 step with any faction or port aware of the situation, unless the party frames it in favourable terms.

---

### Stage 4 — Escalation

The *Dead Lady* begins actively targeting Fisk's ship's hull. She still will not kill Fisk — but she will destroy his ship, strand his crew, and take anyone who enters the water. Her crew board through the hull and take crew members below deck, through the keel, into the water.

At this stage the party cannot sail, cannot port, cannot operate. The only resolutions: compliance, temporary destruction, or a deal through the Waveservants.

---

### Resolution

| Path | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Compliance** | Fisk demonstrates genuine movement toward the Maw with intent to retrieve the Pearl. Umberlee stands the *Dead Lady* down to the horizon. She returns if progress stops. |
| **Temporary appeasement** | Significant offering (real treasure + deed of service to [[Waveservants]]) buys a deadline extension. Extends Stage 2 window. Does not buy a Stage 4 reversal. |
| **Destruction** | Disperses for 1d4 weeks. Umberlee reconstitutes her. Next escalation may move faster. Does not resolve the underlying situation. |
| **Negotiation** | If Fisk understands his leverage — Umberlee *needs* him alive and cannot retrieve the Pearl herself — he has a genuine negotiating position. The Waveservants will not volunteer this. A player who works it out and pushes has real leverage with a goddess. See [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]] for the full shape of the leverage. |
