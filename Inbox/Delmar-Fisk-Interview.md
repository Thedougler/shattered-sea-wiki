# Delmar Fisk

## Summary

Delmar Atticus Fisk is forty-one years old, the youngest Admiral in living memory, and he was using that title before anyone official handed it to him. Sandy red hair in a messy topknot, thick beard and curled mustache both shot through with grey, a long red admiral's coat over tall worn boots — he walks like every deck he stands on belongs to him. The charm lands before he speaks. He is magnetic, readable, and dangerous in that order, and he knows all three.

He has not had a quiet moment in his own head since the day raw/ingested/Umberlee swallowed his fleet. Five ships went down over [[Drowned-Maw|the Maw]], his five captains with them, and she pressed their souls into him as the hulls sank. The voices are always there — each surfaces when triggered, bringing borrowed skills and someone else's instincts. The people who admire the Admiral do not know the Admiral is never just one person.

His goal is singular: resurrect his five captains, or release them so they can find peace. He would compromise who he is to get there. He knows this about himself. Underneath it — stability, and forgiveness he has not yet earned, and a version of himself he has not yet had time to think about becoming.

## Mortis

**Mark:** He is never fully alone in his own head. The five captains' voices are always present — surfacing at need or at provocation, bringing someone else's instincts into his own decision-making.

**Gift:** Activating a captain's personality grants their skill proficiencies as borrowed mastery. Each captain can be triggered; each trigger has a cost in presence.

**Nature:** He is fully aware of the shape of it. Five people paid with their souls because of a call he made. The voices are not punishment in any formal sense — but he experiences them as one.

## History

Delmar grew up on the islands, raised by his mother — a woman considered second-class by the society around her, who nonetheless treated every person as having value regardless of station. He never knew his father. He heard stories from sailors and old tales. What he did know about the man he learned second-hand, and what his father left behind — a pocket watch with his mother's picture inside — his mother had pickpocketed off the man herself.

He came to the sea early. Fishing boats, deckhand work whenever he could get it, everything nautical absorbed from the ground up. When he assembled his first crew he had no business succeeding, and he failed often enough — but he failed his way into loyalty. Five members of that first crew stayed with him through every ship and every rank. They each eventually captained one of the five vessels he commanded as Admiral.

Before the fleet went down, the last ordinary stretch was a week-and-a-half smuggling run for a noble — expensive goods, clean payoff. All crews from all five ships gathered afterward at a favourite spot to rest, resupply, and spend the coin. Nobody saw the end coming.

The theft triggered the sinking. He made the call; five captains paid with their souls. The storm came as fog first — so thick the water was invisible from the deck — surrounding all five ships simultaneously. A rogue trench opened in the fleet's path and drove all five ships 80 feet down into a valley of water. [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss]] dove from the crow's nest and grabbed him; she had only risen 50 feet when the walls of water closed in. Her momentum and the buoyancy of his coat — the [[Delmars-Cloak-of-the-Manta-Ray]] — carried them both to the surface. He heard raw/ingested/Umberlee's laughter on the wind, then her rage, as the fleet was swallowed whole. The five captains entered his mind in that moment. They drifted for a day. A passing fisherman named [[Beaumont-Sel]], aboard [[Saltwright]], found them by sheer luck.

Earlier in his life, as a boy, a pirate attack sank the ship he was aboard. He washed ashore with a dangerous, disgruntled group of survivors. One kind crew member helped him piece together a raft and escape. When he later returned with a proper boat and crew to help the others, they found only corpses. It hardened something in him. He never lost the smile.

## Personality & Drives

- **The charm hits first.** Before a word is spoken. It is not an act — it is who he is — but he also knows exactly what it does and has never stopped using it.
- **Reads people well.** Developed as self-protection; the instinct to extend generosity is still there underneath and he knows it is exploitable.
- **Prefers verbal conflict resolution.** "Better coin in it." Fighting is available; talking is cheaper.
- **His mother's ethic runs deep.** Everyone has value, even if they can't see it themselves. Not naive — he has been taken advantage of — but the default is still generosity.
- **Near-immune to fear** in most situations. Tactical first three seconds: group assessment, self-assessment, then fight or escape or some order of the two.
- **Ferrets are the exception.** Abject terror. Clear on his face. He will exit the room. Not weasels — ferrets. Ferrets are insanely unlucky. He learned this as a boy and has never revisited the belief.
- **Red button:** Call his crew backstabbers. The smile stays. The eyes do not. Violence is on the table.
- **Red line:** Loyalty to found family. He will not betray his own. The only time he came close was a crew member on his first boat who murdered another in a drunken rage; he nearly killed the man and stopped himself.
- **Full resurrection is where the guardrails come off.** The souls are the stated mission. If it becomes possible to restore them entirely, he would compromise who he is to get there and he knows it.

## Active Situations

- **The five captains** — their souls were pressed into him by raw/ingested/Umberlee as the fleet went down; their voices are always present. The crews of all five ships remain in the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]] at the wreck site: the *Red Lady*, *The Narrow*, *Heft*, *Fernen*, and *Loud Argument*. His sole mission: resurrect the captains, or release them for peace. The guardrails come off if full resurrection becomes possible.
- **[[Dead-Lady]]** — raw/ingested/Umberlee's pressure instrument; the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Red-Lady|Red Lady]] raised spectral, following him. She needs him alive to retrieve the Pearl.
- **raw/ingested/Umberlee's interest** — a dangerous symbiosis. She took everything from him, needs him functional, and he cannot afford to ignore her. He believes in her now. It would be difficult not to.
- **Home island** — he has been quietly avoiding it. His mother is there. So is Margaret "Maggie" Porcelain Pots, his first love. He has not returned since the fleet sank.
- **Enemies made** — the families of his lost sailors and the island council that funded the ships most likely consider him an enemy. He has not reckoned with this directly.
- **[[Serafina-Windfall-Fisk|Serafina "The Duchess" Windfall Fisk]]** — his wife; Aasimar fortune-teller and compelled wanderer; genuinely accurate precognitive pulls; they married knowing it would be mostly long-distance; the arrangement is open (physical encounters with others are acceptable; denying the other's existence is the line); he has never tested it; she was not on the ship; she doesn't know about the souls; he named his musket after her private nickname from their wedding night.
