# Peoples of the Shattered Sea

The Shattered Sea sits at the edge of the colonial world. West of the [[Galewall|Galewall]] is the known: kingdoms, records, recognized law. East of it is the Scatter, and the Scatter does not ask where you came from.

The further east you travel, the less the colonial map means. The [[Crown-Islands|Crown Islands]] look like the western homelands transplanted to warmer water. [[Kalowe|Kalowe]] and the [[Midchain|Midchain]] look like what happens when those homelands stop being able to enforce anything. The [[content/shattered-sea/places/tail/index|Tail]] and the [[Outer-Reach|Outer Reach]] look like what was here before the homelands arrived.

This page describes who is where and why. It is not a census. The sea moves people too quickly for counts to hold. Think of it as the pattern a careful observer would notice after a year of making port.

---

## Reading the Tables

**Dominant** — The majority of any given town or crew. The face of the port.

**Common** — Reliably present. You will see them every day. No one comments on it.

**Present** — Visible in any large port. You might go a week without one in a small settlement.

**Rare** — Occasional. Noticed. Not remarkable enough to cause trouble.

**Isolated** — A specific community exists. Everywhere else: almost never.

## The Crown Islands

*Dravosi colonial core. The western homelands with a tropical accent.*

The Crown Islands are the oldest colonial foothold in the Scatter, and they look it. The majority population is [[Human|human]] and came with the Dravosi flag. The administrative, military, and merchant classes arrived first; the support trades and labour followed. Three generations of that cycle has produced cities where diversity is more visible in the ports than in the interior highlands.

[[Rattkin|Rattkin]] are present in every Crown port even though the Dravosi Crown's relationship with [[Passage|The Passage]] is adversarial. The Warren prefers it that way.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Human\|Human]] | Dominant | Dravosi colonial stock; also Tessarine merchants and independent families. |
| Halfling | Common | Chandlers, bookkeepers, small merchants. The most reliable clerks in any Crown port. |
| Dwarf | Common | Naval engineering, fortification work, armaments manufacture. Preferred by the Crown for anything structural. |
| [[Rattkin\|Rattkin]] | Common | Port infrastructure, message networks, The Passage. Officially discouraged; practically irreplaceable. |
| Gnome | Present | Instrument makers, cartographers, Tessarine ledger work. Most are attached to a trade house. |
| Tiefling | Present | Mixed colonial heritage. More visible in Calveno than in Tidefall. |
| Orc | Present | Dock crews, ship labour, garrison soldiers. Often underranked relative to competence. |
| Dragonborn | Present | Scattered across military and merchant roles. No significant community; most are here on contract. |
| Aasimar | Rare | Occasional pilgrims, Tyr-aligned advocates, and one or two officers whose commanding presence nobody questions too closely. |
| [[Aarakocra\|Aarakocra]] | Rare | Mostly [[Sentinels-Of-The-Eyrie\|Sentinels]]. High Eyrie maintains a small permanent population near the eastern Crown approaches. |
| Other | Rare | The Galewall crossing drops all kinds onto Crown docks. Most do not stay long. |

## The Central Strait

*The main highway. The most transient, and therefore the most everything.*

The Strait is not a place people live — it is a place people pass through. Port Tidefall processes hundreds of ships a year. Calveno turns over its dockside population on the tide. What this produces is not a stable population but a rolling average of the entire Scatter: any given dockside inn on any given night will have more species at the bar than most Crown interior towns see in a month.

Calveno specifically skews more diverse than the rest of the Crown Islands. The [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] does not care about your heritage. It cares about your credit.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Human\|Human]] | Dominant | Still the majority, but less so than the interior Crown Islands. |
| Halfling | Common | Merchants. Calveno's counting houses run heavy halfling. |
| Dwarf | Common | Port engineering and armaments. Tidefall's fortifications are largely their work. |
| [[Rattkin\|Rattkin]] | Common | Higher density than the Crown interior. Every major port in the Strait has a Run. |
| Orc | Common | Crew and dock work. A mixed crew on the Strait is more common than a human-only one. |
| Tortle | Common | Island-adapted, long-lived, calm under pressure. Excellent captains and pilots. [[Beaumont-Sel\|Beaumont Sel]] is the most visible example. |
| Tiefling | Present | Tessarine merchant families in particular. |
| Gnome | Present | Navigation instruments, trade tools. |
| Dragonborn | Present | Military and merchant both. Dragonborn make good officers and decent creditors know it. |
| Water Genasi | Present | Natural affinity for this work. Unremarkable on a working ship. |
| [[Aarakocra\|Aarakocra]] | Present | Sentinels and a small number of independent messengers and scouts. |
| Kenku | Present | Port scavengers, couriers, and the kind of fast verbal relay that ships in harbour need. |
| Aasimar | Rare | |
| Air Genasi | Rare | Valued as weather-sense sailors. |
| [[Tabaxi\|Tabaxi]] | Rare | Far from home. Usually working. |
| [[Grung\|Grung]] | Rare | Blue-caste traders and the occasional scout. The Dravosi Crown does not love them in Tidefall, but it tolerates them. |

## The Midchain

*Free ports and local councils. The colonial map stops meaning much here.*

The Midchain has been occupied by too many different peoples for too long for any one of them to claim it. The island councils are plural for a reason: Kalowe's council looks different from the council at Morava, and neither looks like what the Crown would install. The free ports, back channels, and reef-knowledge economy all select for people who can work outside recognized law — which selects against the species most comfortable inside it.

The result is the most diverse region in the Scatter. Humans remain common but are not dominant. Island-adapted species thrive here. Interior tropical islands support communities that have little contact with the shipping lanes.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Human\|Human]] | Common | Still common everywhere, but no longer the default assumption. |
| Tortle | Common | Island-adapted, excellent sailors, found on independent crews across the region. |

| Halfling | Common | Free-port merchants, chandlers, small trade operators. |
| [[Rattkin\|Rattkin]] | Common | Every major port has a Run. Kalowe's Run is large. |
| Lizardfolk | Common | Warm water, mangrove edges, interior waterways. Ancient presence here. Mostly independent of port economics. Some trade, most don't. |
| Water Genasi | Present | Natural sailors. More concentrated than in Crown waters. |
| Tiefling | Present | Independent operators, away from Tessarine family pressure. |
| [[Grung\|Grung]] | Present | Blue-caste traders at designated Verdant Teeth beach sites. Occasionally further north on independent business. |
| Gnome | Present | Instrument repair, small-craft fitting. Less attached to trade houses than in Crown ports. |
| Firbolg | Present | Interior island communities. Don't use the port unless they need to. Remarkable pilots when they do. |
| Dragonborn | Present | |
| Air Genasi | Present | Valued as weather-sense. |
| [[Tabaxi\|Tabaxi]] | Present | More common here than in Crown waters. A few family groups have permanent Midchain footing. |
| Kenku | Present | |

## The Verdant Teeth

*Grung territory. The beach is as far as outsiders go.*

The Verdant Teeth interior belongs to the [[Grung-Clans|Grung clans]]. The coastline operates a strict beach-trade system: outsiders come to the waterline, goods change hands, outsiders leave. The clans do not negotiate on this.

The species breakdown here is not a demographic picture — it is a boundary.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Grung\|Grung]] | Dominant | Interior and island communities. All castes present. |
| Visiting traders | Present | At designated beaches only. No overnight presence. All species permitted at the waterline. |
| [[Tabaxi\|Tabaxi]] | Rare | Southeastern approaches. The Grung and the Tabaxi have a history neither translates clearly for outsiders. |

## The Tail

*Fishing communities and salvage crews at the edge of the charts.*

The Tail does not have colonial administration. It has pilot families. The distinction matters: pilot families pick who they share knowledge with, and that knowledge is the only thing that makes the Tail navigable. What this produces is a working meritocracy defined by capability rather than heritage.

The Maw's proximity draws the [[Sentinels-Of-The-Eyrie|Sentinels of the Eyrie]] and anyone with elemental affinity who has felt the Maw's pull and cannot explain it but can't stay away either. [[Halythion|Halythion]] interfaces with the Tail at the deep-water approaches.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Human\|Human]] | Common | Longline crews, salvage camp labour, staging island residents. |
| Orc | Common | Physical work in hard conditions. Well-represented in longline crews. |
| Tortle | Common | Long-lived, cold-resistant, comfortable at depth. Ideal salvage crew. |
| Goliath | Common | Cliff-work, heavy salvage, cold-water diving. Tail communities have had Goliath pilot families for generations. |
| [[Sea-Elf\|Sea Elf]] | Present | Halythion contact. Surface occasionally near the Shelfworks approaches. |
| [[Aarakocra\|Aarakocra]] | Present | Sentinels of the Eyrie maintain watch presence here. High Eyrie is on the northeastern Crown approaches but its watchers range the Tail. |
| Water Genasi | Present | Drawn to the Maw-adjacent water. Reliable in conditions that ground other sailors. |
| Air Genasi | Present | Navigation in fog and bad weather. The Tail has enough of both to make them valuable. |
| Fire Genasi | Rare | Occasionally drawn here by the Maw's elemental bleed. Usually have no explanation that satisfies a practical sailor. |
| Earth Genasi | Rare | |
| Halfling | Rare | Provisioning. The staging islands need someone to run the stores. |
| [[Rattkin\|Rattkin]] | Rare | No major Run. Individual operators working salvage information routes. |
| Triton | Rare | Deep-water presence near the Shelfworks. Encounter rather than community. |
| Lizardfolk | Rare | |

## The Sunken Crown and the Outer Reach

*Past the Maw. The oldest maps and the newest ignorance.*

The Sunken Crown is [[Tabaxi|Tabaxi]] territory. The [[Keth-Naar|Keth-Naar]] community has been here longer than any colonial record acknowledges. They do not seek outside contact; they manage it when it arrives.

Beyond the Blue Hole, the Outer Reach belongs to whatever was here before anyone started naming things. Outsider presence is not a demographic pattern — it is an event.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[Tabaxi\|Tabaxi]] | Dominant | Keth-Naar and its island network. Isolated from other Tabaxi populations for generations. |
| [[Sea-Elf\|Sea Elf]] | Rare | Halythion trade contact. Respectful, careful, usually carrying specific purpose. |
| Triton | Rare | Deep-water beyond the Blue Hole. The Tabaxi know their names. Outsiders do not. |
| Water Genasi | Rare | Drawn to the Blue Hole's elemental behaviour. |
| Other outsiders | Rare | Maw-crossers, pilgrims, salvagers, the lost. Keth-Naar has protocols for all of them. |

## On the Water

Ships are their own demographic. A working crew in the Scatter does not look like any port's population — it looks like whoever the captain could hire and keep.

A Midchain free-trade crew might be half orc and tortle with a Rattkin pilot and a Grung surgeon who does not discuss what caste they left. A Crown naval vessel runs formally human with dwarf engineers and a Rattkin aboard in an unofficial capacity everyone pretends not to notice. A [[Passage|Passage]] runner has no species requirement and one rule.

The ship is the smallest unit of real integration in the Scatter. It works because it has to.
