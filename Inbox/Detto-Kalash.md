## Overview

Detto captained the *[[Heft]]*, the fleet's heavy ship — broad in the beam, built to take punishment and hold ground. He used Mold Earth to open the sub-surface passage into Umberlee's shrine during the operation that ended the fleet. He was outside the cave when the water closed.

He drowned in the Maw with the rest of them. His soul is pressed into Delmar now, along with the other four captains.

---

## Appearance

Broad and low to the ground. The kind of build that looks immovable because it is. Stone-grey skin, darker and rougher at the knuckles and forearms. His face is wide and doesn't give much away — not closed, not cold, just still. He came to expressions the same way he came to the sea: late, and with the sense that other people needed them more than he did.

---

## The Voice

Detto does not perform. He is not interested in what a thing sounds like — only in what it is.

He pauses where you don't expect: mid-phrase, between a subject and its verb, at the end of a sentence that should feel finished but isn't. His sentences are short. Then sometimes longer, with commas holding weight. Then short again. He says strange things with complete sincerity and does not explain them. He never raises his voice. If he's angry, it sounds like he's noticing the weather.

**At table — say it slowly, with the pauses in:**

> *"You're being held. Yes. Here's what I know about being held. Eventually... you stop being held. The question is what state you're in when that happens."*

> *"Hit him. I'd say say something first, but... no. Just hit him."*

> *"I had a horse. White. Almost completely white. I never named it anything I'd repeat. It worked hard. I think about it sometimes, out here. I know that's strange."*

> *"She took all five ships at once. I'll give her that. She was... efficient. I respect efficiency. Not in this case. But in general."*

> *"This is a bad plan. I want to be clear that it's a bad plan. I'm not saying don't do it. I'm saying... know what it is."*

> *"You talk to people very well. I was never good at that. I found that if you just said the thing, people got confused. You make the confusion work for you. That's a skill."*

> *"The stone knows where to open. I just... ask."*

## Fleet Role

Detto is one of the five souls inside [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Delmar-Fisk|Delmar Fisk]]. He surfaces through the Mortis system when Delmar is physically blocked, grappled, or knocked down — or when something working and white crosses the frame and Delmar can't explain why he stops.

His connection to the fleet is the *Heft*, the shrine passage, and the fleet's doctrine of holding position when everything else was breaking.

---

## Backstory

He came to the sea late. Earth Genasi, from the interior — the specifics of where are not something he offered, and nobody pressed him on it. His manner made clear that the question would land as an intrusion, so people stopped asking.

What surfaced once: he had a horse before the sea. White, almost completely. A working animal, not bred for show, and not named anything he'd say out loud. He mentioned it to Fisk the year they laid the keel of the *Heft*, in the way of someone who said a thing before they decided to. Fisk didn't press. Detto didn't elaborate.

He never explained what he left behind when he came to the water, and nobody who knew him well enough to ask thought it was worth the asking.
