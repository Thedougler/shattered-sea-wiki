# The Doldrums

![[The-Doldrums.webp|Calm glassy sea with scattered low islands under a hazy bright sky]]

## Overview

The Doldrums are a windless band of flat water running east-west below the [[Midchain|Midchain]]'s southern edge. The surface goes mirror-still. Sails hang slack. The absence of wind is total, and sound crosses the flat water in ways that unsettle crews — voices from a quarter mile away arrive clearly, and the ship's own sounds seem louder than they should. The boundary is not marked on most charts. One watch you're in trade winds; the next, nothing. Frigate birds circling overhead in numbers are the standard warning. Jellyfish blooms collect in the still water, sometimes dense enough to foul a hull.

The band drifts 50 to 100 miles north or south by season. Experienced captains track its position and route around it through the [[Midchain|Midchain]] back channels. Ships that don't know where it is sail into it and wait. Becalmed vessels have limited options: tow with boats, jettison weight, or sit. Most carry enough water to do this without disaster. Most.

The Doldrums do not need apex monsters to be dangerous. Still water gathers blooms, carrion, and anything that hunts by patience. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wasp|Giant wasps]] and biting insects make any attempt to tow through mangrove shallows worse than the charts imply. A crew becalmed here learns quickly that silence is not safety.

The worst thing the Doldrums hold is not a weather problem. The mangrove channels south of [[Midchain]]'s edge have their own apex predator — an [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/dragons/adult-black-dragon|adult black dragon]] that has used the still water and the trapped quiet as hunting cover long enough that Midchain pilots treat the Doldrums' deeper southern channels as a category of hazard distinct from the calm itself. A becalmed ship cannot sail, cannot maneuver, and cannot outrun anything that chooses to surface alongside it. The dragon does not always attack. Sometimes it circles below and leaves. Pilots who have had this experience agree that the leaving is not reassuring. ^[inferred]

That silence helps people too. Becalmed ships cannot choose distance, and rowed cutters can move where sail cannot. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/humanoid/pirate|Pirates]] use the Doldrums less as home waters than as a trap already set by the weather. A low raider like [[Knifes-Wake|the Knife's Wake]] is exactly the wrong shape to see moving by oar through flat water at dusk.

## People & Power

The Doldrums do not have formal governance. They are a weather trap, a pilot problem, a pirate opportunity, and the northern edge of a deeper southern-channel predator territory.

## Trade & Resources

The region has little regular trade of its own. Its economic effect is negative space: delays, towing labour, water consumption, route changes, pirate exposure, and the value of pilots who know where the calm has drifted.

## Flora

The documented plant life is mostly mangrove margins in the southern channels and jellyfish blooms in the still water rather than terrestrial flora.

## Fauna

The nuisance layer of the Doldrums is relentless without ever being dramatic. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wasp|Giant wasps]] nest in the mangrove margins and swarm any vessel anchored close to the southern channels — not in any organized way, simply in the way that things that fly and sting are unavoidable when the air is hot and still and a crew has nowhere to be. Any attempt to tow through mangrove shallows adds the wasps to an already unpleasant calculation.

The channels themselves carry [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-crocodile|giant crocodiles]] in numbers the calm water and low predation pressure have never brought down. Still water suits them. They wait in the mangrove root systems, on mud banks, and in the channel margins where a low-slung vessel creates shade. A tow crew working the shallows in boats is working in crocodile water and should know that before entering it.

The mangrove channels' most significant occupant is the reason that Midchain pilots mark the Doldrums' southern approaches as a distinct category of hazard. An [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/dragons/adult-black-dragon|adult black dragon]] has held this territory long enough to make the flat calm part of its method. A becalmed ship cannot sail, cannot manoeuvre, and cannot outrun anything that surfaces alongside it unhurried.

## Hazards

The Doldrums' hazards are windless water, sound carrying too clearly, jellyfish blooms, water depletion, towing exposure, giant wasps, giant crocodiles, pirates moving by oar, and an adult black dragon using still water as hunting cover. ^[inferred]
