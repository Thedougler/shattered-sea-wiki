# Elder Anton

**Roleplay Concept:** *Jaguar grandfather who ran black ops* — patient, deliberate, reads a room the way he once read water. Menacing only because he is never surprised.

---

> [!read-aloud]
> The elder is seated at the cave entrance when you arrive, hands resting on his knees, watching the channel. He does not turn until you are close. One eye is pale and still; the other finds you immediately, takes the measure of you in a single pass, and decides something you are not told. "You came from outside," he says. It is not a question.

> [!appearance]
> A broad-shouldered old tiger tabaxi, heavy through the chest even now. His muzzle has gone grey and the fur around his right eye is scarred in a ragged line from an old hunting wound — the eye itself clouded white and useless. The left eye is still sharp and dark. His hands are large, knuckled, deliberate. He sits like someone who stopped needing to prove things a long time ago.

> [!secret]
> Anton knows the smell that came up from the deep. He smelled it once before — sixty years ago, when he was young and the elders then called it *the opening smell*. That time it closed on its own after two seasons. Twelve boats were lost before it did. He has not told Stripes this. He sent Stripes moving toward the answer, not toward the thing itself. He is not certain those are different destinations.

---

## Overview

Elder Anton is the memory-keeper of the Keth-Naar tribe and the person holding the line while the shrine crisis worsens. He sent Stripes west because waiting at home would only let the problem close around them.

**Voice & Delivery**

Short sentences. No wasted words. He asks questions that already contain the answer he's waiting for you to confirm. When something surprises him, he goes quieter rather than louder — and that's when you feel it.

*"You came from outside."* (Not a question.)
*"How long since you left the Midchain?"*
*"Tell me what the water smelled like when you passed the Maw."*

Hands stay still. He doesn't gesture. Eye contact is steady and patient — the kind that makes you want to fill the silence.

The crack: if someone tells him Stripes is dead, he doesn't react. He closes his good eye for a moment. Then he asks how it happened.

--- right ---

**The Opening Smell**

Elder Anton is the memory-keeper of the Keth-Naar tribe — the one who holds the oral histories, the seasonal patterns, the record of what the sea has done before. He has run the tribe's decisions for thirty years through a combination of patience and the willingness to make calls other people won't. When the keth-fin scattered and the deep water started smelling wrong, he read the signs and sent the tribe's strongest hunter west. He has received no word back.

He knows more than he has shared. The smell from depth is not new to him — he encountered it once before, sixty years ago, young, when the elders called it *the opening smell*. That season cost twelve boats before it closed. He sent Stripes toward the answer rather than toward the thing itself. He is not certain those are different destinations.

The tribe does not know how close the line is between harder and failing. Anton does. He is managing the distance.
