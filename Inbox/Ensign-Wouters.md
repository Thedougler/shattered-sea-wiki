> [!warning] Deceased
> Killed during the boarding of the [[HCS-Surety]] — shot in the groin by [[Delmar-Fisk|Delmar Fisk]]'s musket. Did not survive. His body remained on deck long enough for [[Ket]] to feed on it before the cage was opened. Never recruited.

## Overview

Barely twenty, uniform almost still correct, quill already out before he's fully through the door. He had the boarding manifests sorted by date before anyone asked him to. He hadn't noticed doing it yet.

He is a Crown-trained navigator — chart work, reef avoidance, Maw crossings. Competent without being exceptional. He has nowhere particular to go when the ship makes port and is quietly aware of it.

## Aboard Ship

He gravitates to the chart table. This is where he is useful and he knows it. He will produce organized, legible logs. He will update manifests. He will flag navigation problems before they become navigation crises, in a tone of mild apology, as if the reef is his fault.

Under pressure he does paperwork. This is not a coping mechanism — it is simply how his mind organizes chaos. The paperwork is always correct.

## Recruitment

He defaults to leaving at the next port. The argument that closes it: give him a formal title. **Navigator.** Something he can write down. He produces a small journal from somewhere on his person and writes it down immediately. That's the whole thing. He just needed to be asked, and asked specifically.

> [!DM]
> The easiest recruit in this crew, and the most functionally important one — open-water voyages require a navigator. He does not negotiate on title, only on whether one exists. "First Mate" does not work. "Navigator" does. He needs the specificity.
