# Flask of Endless Water

![[raw/assets/portraits/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack-portrait.webp|Jean-Claude Tabarnack carrying his flask]]

---

## Description

A heavy ceramic flask — wide-bodied, short-necked, stoppered with dense cork that fits too well to be accidental. It has the look of a working vessel: no markings, no ornament, worn smooth at the grip from years of handling. The only tell is the stopper, which runs cold even in direct sun, and the fact that no matter how much water has come out of it, it never feels lighter.

[[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] has carried it since leaving the [[Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]]. He does not explain where he got it.

---

## Properties

While the stopper is removed, the holder can speak a command word as an action to produce water:

| Command | Output | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| *"Stream"* | 1 gallon per action | Pours steadily; useful for drinking, filling containers, wetting surfaces |
| *"Fountain"* | 5 gallons per action | A pressurized upwelling; enough to drench a target or flood a small space quickly |
| *"Geyser"* | 30 gallons in a 30-ft. jet | The holder makes a ranged attack or the jet targets a point; creatures in the jet make a **DC 13 Strength saving throw** or take **1d4 bludgeoning damage** and fall prone. Structures and objects take full damage automatically. |

The water produced is always fresh. It does not stop unless the stopper is replaced or the command word is not spoken.

---

## In Play

The geyser mode has already been used in combat — Jean-Claude blasted [[Barnaby-Rook|Barnaby Rook]] off the gangplank between the *[[Saltwright]]* and the *[[HCS-Surety]]* during the Session 01 boarding. Rook hauled himself back up. The jet worked; the problem was that Rook is difficult to keep down.

---

## Lore

Origin unestablished. Jean-Claude carried it out of the [[Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]] when he ran. He has never said where it came from, and the question does not appear to interest him — the flask works, it is useful, and a Grung who needs to keep his skin hydrated in salt-air ports has good reason to value it beyond its tactical applications.
