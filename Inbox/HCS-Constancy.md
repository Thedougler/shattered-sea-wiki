# HCS *Constancy*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A two-masted frigate on a grey hull, twelve cannon ports per side on the main gun deck and eight more above on the upper deck. The white stripe runs bow to stern at the upper deck rail. Crown pennant at the mainmast. A rating officer is already visible at the rail before the ship finishes its turn into your wind. She is not threatening you. She is waiting for you to make the next move.

---

## Overview

A two-masted heavy frigate, 140 feet on the waterline, built at the Harwick Naval Yard to the Crown Admiralty's standard patrol specification. The *Constancy* is the class the Crown uses when it needs a ship that can enforce, not merely inspect: enough guns to discourage resistance, enough cargo space to transport seized goods and prisoners, and enough range to operate in the Scatter for months without returning to Tidefall.

Hull is naval grey. White Admiralty stripe at the upper deck rail, narrower than the *Ordinance*'s but identically maintained. Brass registry plate at the bow. Crown pennant standard at the mainmast. The stern cabin is a working captain's office — two sealed document drawers, a rating ledger bolted to the desk, and a speaking tube that runs to the gun deck below.

The name is not incidental. The Crown names its patrol class after virtues because a patrol ship represents the steady, unremarkable presence of law. The *Constancy* is not the ship that arrives for a crisis. It is the ship that was already there.

---

## Purpose

Where the *Ordinance* settles matters, the *Constancy* creates the conditions that require settling. A patrol frigate of this class:

- Conducts **Rating Inspections** on vessels transiting Crown waters — checking hull registry, cargo manifest, crew papers, and charter documentation.
- Executes **Impound Orders** issued by civil or Admiralty authority, including seizure of vessels with outstanding Crown fees.
- Escorts **Crown convoys** through contested reef passages and Greyteeth approaches.
- Maintains **patrol presence** along assigned routes, logging every vessel encountered and reporting back to the Harbourmaster's Office at Tidefall.
- Suppresses **unlicensed privateering** in Crown waters by force if necessary.

The *Constancy* does not outrun threats. It discourages them.

## Special Facilities

### Inspection Office *(Special — 2 space units)*

A dedicated compartment off the main gun deck, fitted with a Crown registry desk, a scales and weights station, and a locked evidence cabinet. When a vessel is brought alongside for boarding, the Inspection Office processes it: cargo weighed and compared against manifest, crew papers checked against the Crown registry, hull markings verified against the Harwick shipyard rolls. Hireling: **Rating Officer** (specialist — counts as ship's legal officer, not a crew role).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Rating Officer may conduct a formal inspection of a named vessel or individual. On success, the officer produces a Crown-certified rating: a document establishing legal status, outstanding fees, or registered infractions. This document is admissible in any Crown civil or Admiralty proceeding.

*Passive (All):* Crown-flagged vessels in the same harbour as the *Constancy* are treated as inspected for the week. Non-Crown captains who have been formally rated by this ship's officer make Deception checks at disadvantage when presenting forged papers to any Crown official who has access to the registry network.

### Brig and Holding Deck *(Special — 2 space units)*

A reinforced below-decks section forward of the magazine, fitted with iron cell doors, a marine guard post, and four separate holding compartments capable of accommodating up to twelve prisoners in Crown custody. Used for seized crew, persons under Admiralty warrant, and passengers who made poor decisions at the inspection rail. Hireling: **Marine Sergeant** (double-role: serves as Bosun on crew roster).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Marine Sergeant may process one prisoner per holding compartment, producing sworn depositions, extracting a confession (with or without consent), or arranging a prisoner transfer to Crown civil authority at the next port of call.

*Passive (All):* The *Constancy* can hold and transport persons under Crown warrant without additional cost or logistics. Escape attempts from the Brig are made at disadvantage: the cells are below the waterline, and the Marine Sergeant has done this before.

## Layout

Three decks above the hold.

**Weather Deck** — Wheel, compass, signal flags, and a Crown-standard lamp array for fleet communication. Captain's cabin aft with rating ledger and sealed dispatch storage. Four swivel mounts amidships for close boarding suppression. Marine station at the rail.

**Upper Gun Deck** — Eight guns per side, 18-lb long cannon. Inspection Office aft, accessed by a short companion ladder from the weather deck. Officers' berths and mess forward of the Inspection Office.

**Main Gun Deck** — Twelve guns per side, 18-lb long cannon. Primary fighting deck. Crew berths and mess forward. Armorer's station amidships.

**Below Decks (Orlop)** — Brig and Holding Deck forward. Surgeon's berth. Sailcloth and rope stores. Carpenter's supplies.

**Hold** — Magazine and shot locker aft. Water casks, provisions, spare timber. 75 tons cargo capacity, typically occupied by seized goods in transit to Tidefall.
