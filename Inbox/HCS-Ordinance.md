## Overview

A three-deck Ship of the Line, 210 feet on the waterline, built at the Harwick Naval Yard in the Crown Islands. The *Ordinance* is the pinnacle of Dravosi shipbuilding — the largest vessel the Crown has ever commissioned, the only Tier 4 hull in the Shattered Sea, and the clearest possible statement that the Crown's authority is not an argument it intends to have twice.

The hull is naval grey from keel to rail. A white Admiralty stripe runs the full length at weather deck level, matching every Crown vessel in the fleet but wider and more precisely maintained. Cannon ports number forty-two per side across three gun decks, all fitted with cast-iron 24-pounder long guns. Six stern chasers and six bow chasers extend the battery further. Total mounted guns: 96.

The figurehead is Tyr — robed, blindfolded, scales extended. Carved in pale northern timber, gilded at the scales and sandals, unpainted at the face. A brass registry plate below the bowsprit reads the ship's name, commission date, and the Admiralty seal.

Built to be seen. Built to be unmistakable. Built so that the conversation about whether to comply happens before a cannon port opens.

---

## Purpose

The *Ordinance* does not patrol. It arrives. Its presence in a region means the Crown has decided a situation requires the full weight of Admiralty authority — not a cutter, not a boarding party, not a civil notice. When the *Ordinance* anchors in a harbour, it brings its own court, its own legal staff, its own impound capacity, and enough cannon to reduce a fortified position to rubble without significant risk to the hull.

In practice, the ship functions as:

- **Mobile Admiralty Court** — capable of hearing military tribunals, issuing commissions, revoking privateer licenses, and rendering Crown judgements under full Tyr's-seal authority.
- **Fleet Command** — the *Ordinance* carries an Admiral's staff and signals complement for coordinating squadron operations across the Shattered Sea.
- **Deterrent** — the ship's reputation travels faster than the ship. News of its transit through Crown waters changes harbor behavior for weeks before and after its visit.

## Special Facilities

### Admiralty Court *(Special — 2 space units)*

A sealed chamber on the upper stern, paneled in pale timber with a raised dais, Tyr's symbol set in brass above the judge's seat, and a witness rail bolted to the deck. Fully equipped for Crown military tribunal, commission hearings, and Admiralty proceedings. Hireling: **Judge-Advocate** (specialist — counts as ship's legal officer, not a crew role).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Court may issue one Crown legal instrument: a commission, a revocation, a warrant, an impound order, or a letters of marque. The instrument carries full Admiralty authority and is enforceable in all Crown-held ports.

*Passive (All):* Crown officers aboard gain advantage on Intimidation checks against parties subject to Crown law, and on Persuasion checks with Crown-aligned NPCs.

### Registry Vault *(Special — 2 space units)*

A steel-doored archive below the waterline, fitted with fireproof caskets, sealing wax stores, and a copying press. Maintains the ship's official records: officer commissions, crew ratings, cargo manifests, genealogy filings for noble-line officers, and sealed correspondence awaiting delivery. Hireling: **Ship's Registrar** (specialist — not a crew role).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Registrar may produce a certified copy of any document in the vault, forge a Crown certificate with DC 18 Investigation to detect, or locate a named individual's Crown registration within the fleet's genealogical records (if they have one).

*Passive (All):* The ship generates a legal paper trail for every port it visits, every vessel it inspects, and every order it issues. Attempts to conceal the *Ordinance*'s activities from Crown civil authority are made with disadvantage.

### Gunner's Magazine *(Special — 2 space units)*

A reinforced lower-deck compartment directly beneath the main gun battery, climate-controlled with alchemical desiccant, fitted with the Crown's standardized powder-charge casting equipment. Hireling: **Gunner-Warden** (double-role: serves as Gunner on crew roster).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Gunner-Warden may prepare a specialized shot order: grapeshot (area suppression), chain shot (rigging/mast targeting), or alchemical incendiary rounds. Prepared shot applies on the next combat encounter.

*Passive (All):* The *Ordinance*'s cannon attacks deal maximum damage on a confirmed critical hit rather than rolling. The magazine has never misfired.

## Layout

Four working decks plus hold.

**Weather Deck (Spar Deck)** — Quarterdeck aft with wheel, compass binnacle, signal station, Admiral's walk. Figurehead and bowsprit forward. Admiralty Court chamber takes up the full stern deckhouse. Open gun positions amidships, though the spar deck is not a primary gun deck.

**Upper Gun Deck** — 14 cannon ports per side, long 24-pounders. Officers' quarters aft, including Admiral's cabin with private chart room and sealed dispatch archive. Marine barracks forward.

**Main Gun Deck** — 14 cannon ports per side, long 24-pounders. The primary fighting deck. Registry Vault accessed here through a steel-strapped door aft. Crew berths and mess forward.

**Lower Gun Deck** — 14 cannon ports per side, long 24-pounders. Lowest fighting deck, operates at the waterline in rough conditions — ports can only open in fair weather. Gunner's Magazine directly below. Surgeon's berth aft.

**Orlop Deck** — Below the waterline, no guns. Carpenter's stores, chain locker, shot locker, bosun's stores, sailcloth. Junior officer berths.

**Hold** — Ballast, water casks, salt provisions, spare timber, bilge access. 160 tons of cargo capacity, though the *Ordinance* rarely carries commercial freight — the hold typically runs legal impound cargo, sealed dispatches, and stores for extended operations.
