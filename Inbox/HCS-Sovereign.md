# HCS *Sovereign*

![[raw/assets/banners/HCS-Sovereign.webp]]

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> Three gun decks rise above a grey hull painted the colour of deep water — fifteen cannon ports per side on the lower deck alone, all closed, brass fittings catching light at every seam. A white Admiralty stripe runs the full length of the ship at the spar deck rail, wider than any other Crown vessel in the Scatter. Three mastheads carry the Crown pennant. The figurehead is a robed figure holding a crown aloft, not offering it — claiming it. She moves slowly. She does not need to move quickly.

---

## Overview

A first-rate ship of the line, 215 feet on the waterline, built at the Harwick Naval Yard over six years and commissioned as the definitive expression of Dravosi naval power. The *Sovereign* is the only vessel of her class in the Shattered Sea — the largest hull afloat, the heaviest armed, and the clearest possible answer to the question of whether the Crown's authority in these waters is negotiable.

![[raw/assets/banners/HCS-Sovereign-Full-Sail.webp]]

The hull is naval grey from keel to rail, copper-sheathed below the waterline. Three gun decks stack below the spar deck: lower carries thirty 32-lb long cannon, main carries twenty-eight 24-lb long cannon, upper carries thirty 12-lb long cannon. Four heavy carronades sit on the spar deck, two at the quarterdeck rail and two at the forecastle — short-range weapons of last resort that no one in boarding distance has survived. Stern and bow chasers extend the battery further. Total mounted guns: 104, plus eight chasers.

The figurehead is not a god. It is a robed figure, indeterminate in feature, holding the Crown of Dravas aloft with both hands in a gesture that reads less as offering and more as evidence. The Admiralty had it carved in pale northern timber and gilt at the crown itself. The face was deliberately left unfinished.

Built to be the last word. The Crown does not expect to need a second.

---

## Purpose

The *Sovereign* does not patrol and does not escort. It is deployed when the Crown has determined that the full weight of naval authority must be physically present — not implied, not threatened, but moored in a harbour with 104 gun ports and a flag flying.

In practice, the ship functions as:

- **Fleet Flagship** — the *Sovereign* carries the Admiral of the Shattered Sea and a full staff complement. When operating as flagship, it coordinates all Crown naval assets across the Scatter through signal and dispatch.
- **Final Enforcement** — where the [[HCS-Constancy|Constancy]] enforces and the smaller patrol class deters, the *Sovereign* concludes. Its arrival ends disputes. Its presence in contested waters alters the behaviour of every vessel within visual range.
- **Strategic Deterrent** — the ship's reputation precedes it by weeks. Harbours that receive word of its transit make different decisions before it arrives.

## Special Facilities

### Grand Magazine *(Special — 2 space units)*

A reinforced double-chamber below the lower gun deck, climate-controlled with alchemical desiccant and sealed from the magazine proper by two fire-doors. The forward chamber holds prepared shot charges for all three gun decks; the aft chamber stores specialist rounds — chain, bar, heated, and grapeshot — in separate sealed lockers. Hireling: **Master Gunner** (double-role: serves as Gunner on crew roster).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Master Gunner may prepare a specialist shot order for any one deck: chain shot, grapeshot, bar shot, or heated shot. The prepared shot applies automatically in the next combat encounter — no action required to load.

*Passive (All):* The *Sovereign's* cannon attacks deal maximum damage on a confirmed critical hit rather than rolling. The Grand Magazine has never misfired. On a natural 1 on any cannon attack, the gun misfires but does not explode — it is simply cleared as an action.

### Admiral's War Room *(Special — 2 space units)*

The full stern cabin of the *Sovereign*, spanning the width of the spar deck at the quarterdeck level — glazed windows, a chart table bolted to the centre, a signals rack, and a dispatch archive. The room doubles as the flag captain's tactical headquarters and the Admiral's residence. Hireling: **Flag Lieutenant** (specialist — not a crew role).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Flag Lieutenant may transmit fleet orders via signal or dispatch, coordinate a Crown naval asset within the Scatter (repositioning an NPC Crown vessel), or prepare a tactical assessment of a named vessel, port, or sea route. The assessment provides advantage on the next navigation check or ship-to-ship encounter involving that target.

*Passive (All):* While the *Sovereign* is in a region, Crown naval assets in that region act with coordination — treat all friendly Crown ship crews as if the Captain role is filled by a skilled officer for purposes of morale and pursuit checks. Hostile captains within visual range of the ship must make a DC 14 Wisdom save at the start of each encounter or begin their first round shaken (disadvantage on attack rolls until they succeed on the save).

### Battle Surgeon's Deck *(Special — 2 space units)*

A full surgical deck on the orlop level, below the waterline, positioned for stability during combat. Two operating tables, a medicine cabinet stocked for mass casualties, amputation kit, and a recovery bay with ten cots. Hirelings: **Ship's Surgeon** + **Surgeon's Mate** (double-role: Surgeon's Mate serves as Cook on crew roster).

*Active (Director):* Once per Bastion Turn, the Battle Surgeon may perform emergency surgery on one downed crew member (stabilize + remove one level of exhaustion), produce a batch of three Potions of Healing, or administer a full treatment that removes disease or poison from up to four crew simultaneously.

*Passive (All):* When the party takes a short rest aboard, each PC may roll their Hit Dice twice and take the higher result. When the party takes a long rest aboard after combat, each PC recovers one additional Hit Die.

## Layout

Five decks from keel to spar.

**Spar Deck (Weather Deck)** — Wheel and compass binnacle aft at the quarterdeck rail. Signal station, pennant lockers, Admiral's walk. Two heavy carronades at the quarterdeck rail, two at the forecastle. Eight 12-lb guns per side amidships, exposed. Admiral's War Room takes up the full stern deckhouse. Bowsprit and figurehead forward.

**Upper Gun Deck** — Fifteen 12-lb guns per side. Officers' quarters aft, including flag captain's anteroom and access to War Room via companion ladder. Marine barracks and armoury forward.

**Main Gun Deck** — Fourteen 24-lb guns per side. The primary fighting deck. Crew berths and mess forward. Armourer's station amidships. Lower ports are sealed in heavy weather — fights this deck best in moderate conditions.

**Lower Gun Deck** — Fifteen 32-lb guns per side. The heaviest weight of shot in the fleet. Grand Magazine directly below, accessed through a locked hatch. This deck operates near the waterline; ports are sealed in any significant sea. Fights this deck only in calm water or in harbour.

**Orlop Deck** — Below the waterline, no guns. Battle Surgeon's Deck forward. Carpenter's stores, chain locker, bosun's supplies, spare canvas and rigging. Junior officer berths.

**Hold** — Ballast, water casks, salt provisions, spare timber, bilge access. 100 tons cargo capacity, typically running stores for extended deep-water operations.

## Crew

280 at full complement: flag captain and staff, three deck officers (one per gun deck), Master Gunner (Grand Magazine), Flag Lieutenant (War Room), Surgeon and Surgeon's Mate, three watches of ordinary sailors, and a marine company of forty. The ship requires 55 to move and steer; 55 cannot fight the guns effectively — full fighting capacity requires the gun deck crews.

## Tactical Notes

The *Sovereign* does not chase. It presents a broadside and lets the other vessel decide. Against anything Tier 3 or below, a single full broadside from the lower gun deck will end the engagement. Against a prepared fortification, the three-deck broadside takes down walls. The ship has no answer to shallow water, fast pursuit, or fire ships — these are the three things that a competent enemy uses against it.

For full broadside and multi-deck volley mechanics: see [[Ship-Combat]].
