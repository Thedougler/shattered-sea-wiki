## Article I — Vessel Particulars

His Crown Majesty's Cutter [[HCS-Surety|*Surety*]] is a Tier 1 patrol cutter, purpose-built for inspection, pursuit, and boarding. She is not a ship of the line and is not to be employed as one. Her two cannon are sufficient to discourage uncooperative merchant traffic and adequate for boarding-range combat; they are not adequate for sustained exchange with a rated vessel. She is fast, draws little water, and may be taken through reef passages that would strand anything larger. The Commanding Officer is encouraged to keep these qualities in mind.

### Vessel Statistics

| Particulars | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Classification** | Patrol cutter, Tier 1 |
| **Hull Integrity** | 120 points |
| **Hull Armour Class** | 11 |
| **Rate of Passage — favourable wind** | 70 miles/day |
| **Rate of Passage — poor wind** | 35 miles/day |
| **Rate of Passage — calm, oars deployed** | 15 miles/day |
| **Crew Complement — minimum / full** | 4 / 10 |
| **Cargo Capacity** | 16 tons rated (6 tons currently available) |
| **Ordnance** | 2 × 6-lb cannon (port and starboard); 2 × swivel gun (bow, stern) |
| **Weekly Upkeep** | ~28 gp |
| **Condition** | Worn |

**On Hull Integrity and Armour Class.** Hull Integrity functions as hit points for the vessel. When it reaches zero, the vessel is sinking. Hull Armour Class represents the difficulty of landing a meaningful strike against a ship-scale target.

**On Repairs.** At-sea repair requires a skilled Carpenter on board. During a short rest, the Carpenter makes an **Intelligence (Carpenter's Tools) DC 15** check; on a success, the vessel recovers **2d8 + proficiency bonus** Hull Integrity. In port, repairs run 1 gp per Hull Point at 25 Hull Points per workday.

## Article II — Deck Arrangement

**Weather Deck.** The open upper deck, running the full length of the vessel. The bow mounts a swivel gun with a 360-degree arc of fire. Two 6-lb cannon on rolling carts with securing ropes amidships, one per side. The quarterdeck is raised two feet at the stern and carries the ship's wheel; attacks from the quarterdeck against deck-level targets have advantage, and ascending to it costs an additional five feet of movement. Rigging may be climbed with a **DC 10 Athletics** check as an action. The mainmast boom may be swung as an improvised weapon by a crew member who makes a **DC 12 Strength** check; any creature struck must make a **DC 13 Dexterity** saving throw or take **1d8** bludgeoning damage and be pushed ten feet.

**Gun Deck.** The interior living space, with approximately 6'4" of overhead clearance. Crew quarters forward, galley, boarding locker amidships, chart table, officer's berth, and the captain's cabin aft. The boarding locker — stocked with cutlasses, boarding axes, flintlocks, and grappling hooks — may be accessed as a bonus action.

**Hold.** The lowest level. Provisions are stored forward; the prisoner brig is bolted to the keel amidships; the powder magazine occupies the aft section.

The Commanding Officer is directed to enforce strict fire discipline in the vicinity of the magazine. Open flame within ten feet of the magazine requires a **DC 12 Dexterity** saving throw or causes **8d6** fire damage in a twenty-foot radius and inflicts **30** points of structural damage on the vessel. This is often immediately and violently counterproductive to your survival.

## Article III — Ship's Company

Each of the following roles must be filled for the vessel to operate at regulation standard. A role may be filled by a member of the ship's complement or by a hired professional. When a member of the complement fills a role, they add their ability modifier to that role's checks. A hired professional performs competently at baseline. An unfilled role either cannot perform its function at all or does so at disadvantage.

**On minimum complement.** The *Surety* requires a minimum of four souls to operate. Below that number, all ship-related checks are made with disadvantage and rate of passage is reduced by twenty percent.

### Roles and Rates of Pay

| Role | Function | Key Ability | Rate | Actions While in Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Captain** | Strategic command — where to sail, what to target, and when to fight or run. Final authority in action. | Charisma | — | **Action:** *Rallying Order* — one creature within 60 ft gains advantage on their next check or attack before the start of your next turn. |
| **Quartermaster** | Crew welfare, discipline, stores management, and the ship's internal order. Senior to the Bosun; manages all matters below decks not directly related to seamanship or ordnance. Reports to the Captain. | Charisma | 10 gp/wk | **Action:** *Crew's Voice* — one ally aboard may reroll any failed check, attack roll, or saving throw and use the higher result. **Reaction:** *Fair Witness* — when a crew member within 60 ft would fail a death saving throw or a save against the Frightened condition, they roll with advantage instead. |
| **Navigator** | Course-plotting, reef avoidance, Maw crossing | Intelligence (Navigator's Tools) | 10 gp/wk | **Action:** Navigation check (Article V). **Reaction:** *Hazard Warning* — when a navigation check or save is called, declare it early; the vessel rolls with advantage. |
| **Bosun** | Rigging, sails, management of the deck crew | Strength or Dexterity | 8 gp/wk | **Action:** *Trim Sails* — **DC 10 Dexterity** check; on a success, the helmsman may move one additional band this turn. **Reaction:** *Brace!* — one creature aboard halves damage from a ram or collision. |
| **Gunner** | Ordnance direction and maintenance | Dexterity | 8 gp/wk | **Action:** Salvo, Aimed Shot, or Reload — see Article VII. |
| **Carpenter** | Hull repair, below-decks damage control | Intelligence (Carpenter's Tools) | 6 gp/wk | **Action:** Emergency Repair (Article I). **Bonus Action:** *Plug the Breach* — **DC 10 Intelligence (Carpenter's Tools)**; on a success, stop fire spread for one round. |
| **Cook** | Provisions management and crew sustenance | Wisdom | 4 gp/wk | No combat-specific action. Long rest benefit applies automatically. |
| **Surgeon** | Treatment of wounds and management of casualties | Wisdom (Medicine) | 8 gp/wk | **Action:** Stabilize one incapacitated creature (**DC 10 Wisdom/Medicine**) or restore **1d6 + 4** HP (expends one Healer's Kit use). **Bonus Action:** *Field Triage* — **DC 10 Wisdom (Medicine)**; on a success, one creature gains advantage on their next death saving throw. |
| **Marine** | Boarding actions, armed watch, vessel security | Strength or Dexterity | 14 gp/wk | **Action:** Attack, Grapple, or Shove (standard combat). **Bonus Action:** *Hold the Rail* — one enemy boarding the vessel must make a **DC 13 Athletics** check or be shoved back. |
| **Ordinary Sailor** | All other duties | — | 2 gp/wk | **Action:** Help (2024 rules) — grant one creature advantage on their next check or attack this round. |

**Required at all times:** Captain, Bosun.
**Required for open-water passages:** Navigator.
**Required when ordnance is to be employed:** Gunner.
**Required for at-sea repair:** Carpenter.
**Required on voyages exceeding three days:** Cook.
**Required to operate the Surgeon's Berth:** Surgeon.
**Required for boarding operations:** Marine.

The Crown Admiralty notes that a competent Cook is among the cheapest investments available to a commanding officer. Sailors who eat well recover an additional **1d6** hit points on a long rest. The Crown does not consider this a luxury.

## Article IV — Maintenance of the Vessel

Weekly upkeep is owed to the vessel whether she is underway or berthed. The rate of typical expenditure is as follows:

| Expenditure | Weekly Cost |
|---|---|
| Crew wages | Variable (see ship's muster) |
| Provisions | 5–10 gp |
| Maintenance and consumables | 5 gp |
| Berthing fees (when in port) | 2–5 gp |
| **Total (estimated, *Surety*)** | **~28 gp/week** |

Failure to meet upkeep obligations carries no immediate mechanical penalty. It carries a narrative one. Experienced hands have found other employers before. The Commanding Officer is advised to maintain accounts in good order.

## Article V — Passage and Navigation

The vessel's rate of passage varies with wind conditions.

- **Favourable wind:** 70 miles/day
- **Poor wind:** 35 miles/day
- **Calm — oars deployed:** 15 miles/day

### Navigation

When the vessel is transiting dangerous or unfamiliar waters, the Navigator makes an **Intelligence (Navigator's Tools)** check. Conditions determine the difficulty:

| Conditions | DC |
|---|---|
| Familiar or charted waters, clear weather | 10 |
| Unfamiliar waters, reef approaches, poor visibility | 14 |
| Maw approaches, active storm, uncharted shallows | 18 |

On a success, the passage proceeds without incident. On a failure, the voyage is delayed and complications arise — the severity depends on the waters.

Crown survey charts — available from the [[Harbourmasters-Office|Harbourmaster's Office]], [[Port-Tidefall]] — grant advantage on navigation checks in charted waters. Crown Islands and Central Strait coverage is reliable; Midchain charts are contradictory and grant no advantage. Within five miles of the [[Drowned-Maw|Maw]], standard compasses fail and magical navigation instruments produce false readings. Celestial navigation remains unaffected. The [[Compass-of-the-Drowned|Compass Rose]] (400 gp, Port Tidefall chandleries) corrects for this directly.

### Conduct During Extended Passages

On voyages of three days or longer, members of the ship's complement may pursue downtime activities per standard 2024 rules. Activities requiring a fixed location or specialist instructor cannot be undertaken at sea.

**On long rests at sea.** Standard rules apply. If the Cook role is filled, each character recovers an additional **1d6** hit points. If a storm strikes during the rest period, each character makes a **DC 12 Constitution** saving throw; failure means the rest confers no benefit.

## Article VI — Action Against Hostile Vessels

Ships act in initiative order as creatures do. All persons aboard act independently each round on their own initiative counts.

### Range Bands

Ship-to-ship combat is tracked in three range bands rather than exact distances. On their turn, the helmsman uses their **Action** to move the vessel one band toward or away from another vessel. **Dash** moves an additional band. Ships cannot move in reverse.

| Band | Description | What's Possible |
|---|---|---|
| **Boarding** | Ships are alongside, locked, or grappled | Crew can board. Cannon hit automatically — no attack roll. Swivels effective. |
| **Cannon** | Standard engagement distance | Normal cannon fire. Swivels cannot reach. |
| **Distant** | Extreme range | Cannon fire at disadvantage. Swivels cannot reach. |

### Chases and Pursuit

Each vessel moves on its initiative count. The faster vessel dictates the band — it may close to Boarding or open toward Distant. When both vessels have been Distant for **two consecutive rounds**, the quarry escapes.

Chases may be interrupted by conditions — fog, shallows, reef channels, wind shifts. When conditions arise, the relevant PC makes an appropriate check; consequences scale with the outcome.

### Ramming

A vessel may ram any vessel at Boarding range as part of the Helm action.

- The struck vessel takes **4d10** bludgeoning damage.
- **Ram angle determines self-damage:** The ramming vessel's bow is reinforced for impact. If striking the target's **port, starboard, or aft**, the ramming vessel takes **2d10**. If striking **fore-to-fore**, both vessels take **4d10**.
- The struck vessel's helmsman makes a **DC 15 Strength** saving throw; on a failure, it is pushed back to Cannon range.
- Unsecured crew on both vessels make a **DC 13 Dexterity** saving throw or fall prone and take **1d6** bludgeoning damage.

Both vessels halt upon impact. The ramming vessel may not use the Helm action until the start of its next turn.

## Article VII — Ordnance and Gunnery

The [[HCS-Surety|*Surety*]] carries two 6-lb cannon — one to port, one to starboard — and two swivel guns, mounted bow and stern. Swivel guns are anti-crew ordnance only; they cannot damage a hull.

### Serving the Guns

When the **Gunner role is filled**, both cannon may be directed as a Salvo or an Aimed Shot — the Gunner coordinates crew and timing. When **no one fills the Gunner role**, a PC may fire one gun as their Action, but no Salvo is possible and the gun must be reloaded on the following round.

### Gunner's Actions

The officer or hireling filling the **Gunner** role expends their **Action** each round to direct the guns:

**Salvo.** All guns bearing on the target fire simultaneously. The Gunner makes one attack roll (**Dexterity + proficiency**) against the target vessel's **Hull AC**. On a **hit**, deal full damage from all guns on that side. On a **miss**, deal half — rounds at sea rarely miss in any absolute sense; they simply strike nothing vital. All guns that fired must reload before the next Salvo.

**Aimed Shot.** One gun targets a specific component at **−2** to the attack roll. On a hit, the targeted component is disabled rather than dealing standard hull damage (see shot results below). That gun must reload before firing again.

**Reload — All Guns.** All guns reload in parallel. No attack is made this round.

### Ordnance Statistics — HCS *Surety*

| Ordnance | Damage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 6-lb cannon | d8 hull damage | Effective at Cannon and Boarding range |
| Swivel gun | d6 crew damage | Boarding range only; cannot damage hull |

Attack roll: **Dexterity + proficiency** versus target **Hull AC**. Hit: roll damage. Miss: half damage. Critical hit: maximum damage, no roll.

### Shot Types

The Gunner declares shot type before rolling. Standard shot is **round shot**. To load an alternative, the Gunner uses the Reload action; the alternate type discharges on the next order.

| Shot Type | Effect — Hit | Effect — Miss | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Round Shot** | Full hull damage | Half hull damage | Standard; always available |
| **Chain Shot** | Speed drops one band. Three reductions = dead in the water. | Half reduction | Use to slow a fleeing vessel or set up boarding |
| **Grapeshot** | All exposed deck crew: **DC 14 Dexterity** or incapacitated; PCs take **2d6** (save halves) | PCs save with advantage | Boarding range only; cannot damage hull |

**On chain shot.** Band reductions accumulate. A vessel at zero speed cannot maneuver — it may still fight. Repair: **DC 14 Intelligence (Carpenter's Tools)**, one hour.

**On grapeshot.** Any PC aware of incoming grapeshot may drop below deck as a Reaction before it lands.

## Article VIII — Ship's Facilities and Weekly Management

The vessel is formally classified under Crown Naval Code as a mobile station — the complement's shared operational base. Each week, the Officer Complement conducts a **Facility Review**. All special facilities aboard are issued an order for that week. The review occurs whether the vessel is underway or in port. Facilities operate normally at sea provided minimum complement is maintained.

Officers absent from the vessel may issue orders via *Sending* or equivalent means. An officer who issues no order defaults to Maintain for all their assigned facilities. Any facility that goes **three consecutive weeks** without orders goes dormant — hirelings have found other employment, supplies have been consumed or spoiled. Reactivating a dormant facility costs 100 gp and one week in port.

### Current Facility Status — HCS *Surety*

The *Surety* carries four facility berths at Tier 1. Two are currently commissioned; two remain available.

| Facility | Status | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Weapons Locker** *(Armory)* | **Commissioned** | Upgrades defender dice from d6 to d8 while stocked. Trade order: buy or sell arms at volume. |
| **Chart Archive** *(Library)* | **Commissioned** | Research order: yields three pieces of intelligence — navigation data, lore, faction records, or similar. |
| **Crew Berths** *(Barracks)* | Available — recommended | Recruit order: enlists and berths up to 12 Defenders. The most reliable protection against hostile action events. |
| **Provisions Store** *(Garden)* | Available — recommended | Harvest order: produces rations, Healer's Kits, Healing Potions, or antitoxin each week. |
| **Surgeon's Berth** *[Non-standard installation]* | Pre-installed; Surgeon role required | Once per weekly review: stabilize all incapacitated crew; remove one disease or poison over eight hours; or cast *Lesser Restoration* without expending a spell slot. |

*The Admiral-Commandant's office recommends commissioning the Crew Berths and Provisions Store to bring the vessel to full operational capacity.*

### Weekly Facility Orders

Each week, each special facility is assigned one of the following orders. Not every facility supports every order; the facility specification indicates which orders apply.

| Order | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Craft** | Produce items or magical items permitted by the facility. |
| **Empower** | Trigger the facility's active operational effect. |
| **Harvest** | Collect facility outputs — potions, rations, poisons, materials. |
| **Maintain** | No directed activity. A facility event may occur. |
| **Recruit** | Engage hirelings, Defenders, or specialist personnel. |
| **Research** | Gather intelligence, lore, or item identification. |
| **Trade** | Generate income via commerce. |

### On Defenders

Defenders are the vessel's hired security — enlisted via a Recruit order to the Crew Berths. They absorb hostile action events when the Officer Complement is absent. A single Recruit order enlists up to twelve. Keep them recruited.

## Article IX — Vessel Standing in Port Records and Faction Registers

The *Surety*'s name and flag are known quantities. Standing is maintained in each major port and with each significant faction. The following effects apply:

| Standing | Practical Effect |
|---|---|
| **Respected** | Reduced berthing fees; willing hirelings readily available; faction contacts accessible. |
| **Known** | Treated according to actual conduct. Neutral default. |
| **Wanted** | Port access restricted or denied; bounties registered. |
| **Notorious** | Capable crew seek the vessel out; some quarries surrender rather than engage. |

Operating without colours is grounds for suspicion in any Crown port. Operating under false colours is common practice in the Scatter. It also constitutes a criminal offense under Crown Naval Code, should the [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi]] apprehend the vessel in the act.

The *Surety*'s current Crown registration is a liability. The Commanding Officer is assumed to have already given this matter appropriate consideration.
