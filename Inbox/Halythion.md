# Halythion

## Overview

> An ancient sea elf city built into the Teikhinos Reef — invisible from the surface, impassable by ship, older than any colonial record. It measures civilization by what it remembers. Here, memory is law, and law is sung.

**Location:** Eastern Midchain — the Shearwall Reef, approximately 20 miles across

**Depth:** 15–60 feet; city centred on the Koilon

**Theme:** The Aoide — oral law, living memory, civilization as archive

**Patron Deity:** Deep Sashelas

## Naming & Language

Halythion's internal naming draws from a pre-colonial Aegean root — older than any Dravosi chart. The city names itself in the same tradition it governs by: spoken, not written.

## The City

Forty feet below a reef that looks like nothing, the Koilon opens. Bioluminescence first — amber high, blue-white deep — then arch doorways cut into coral at every tier, then the centre floor, kept clear by centuries of convention. A voice from the floor reaches the rim without loss. This is why the city was built here.

The Teikhinos — the outer reef wall — is not navigable by ship and is not marked on any colonial chart. Surface charts call the reef impassable and route around it; no vessel drawing more than 2 feet can navigate the passage. The route is known only to the sea elves and a small number of authorized outsiders. Halythion has never been found by those who came looking. It has been found, occasionally, by those who were not.

The Aoide is not a scripture. It is not stored anywhere. It lives in the mouths of those trained to carry it. The Aoidos — the Singer — is the office responsible for its continuity. The position is formal, hereditary in practice if not in law, and currently incomplete: [[Coralyra-Dranra]] left her training before the Council declared her ready.

Halythion measures civilization by what it remembers. Law lives in trained voices, and the Aoidos is the office responsible for continuity. [[Coralyra-Dranra|Coralyra]] left before the Council declared her ready.

## Trade & Services

Halythion is not a public market. Its value is guarded passage, memory, reef knowledge, oral law, and sea elf continuity older than any colonial record.

## Surface View

The name surfaces in port taverns south of the [[Midchain|Midchain]] — usually from someone who heard it from someone else. An underwater city, old, somewhere in the eastern reefs. The reef in question is impassable by ship and not on any chart worth trusting. Vessels that have gone looking for a passage through have not returned.

Whether there is a city there or not, the reef is real. Most pilots know to route around it.

## Fauna

The reef that surrounds the area pilots call Halythion is dense enough to stop a ship and deep enough at its inner edge to make rescue impractical. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-shark|Giant sharks]] work the outer reef wall, drawn by the current upwellings where the deep water hits the reef structure. They are large and numerous enough that a longboat crew assessing a grounded vessel has a secondary problem in addition to the primary one.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-octopus|Giant octopuses]] occupy the deeper reef structures — limestone overhangs, wreck debris, and any hollow structure large enough to hold them. The inner reef is known for wrecks. Wrecks accumulate octopus. Divers who have worked the outer edge of the reef's salvage opportunities note that some of the wrecks appear to have been moved.

Sailors who have passed the outer reef margin without grounding and returned describe sightings of organised shapes moving in formation through the water below the reef break — too large for reef fish, too consistent for current. The Midchain pilots who hear these accounts do not call it evidence of anything. They note the accounts and add the reef to the list of places worth routing around regardless of what is or is not down there.
