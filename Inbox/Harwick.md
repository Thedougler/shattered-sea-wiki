# Harwick
![[1778027612063~2.png]]
## Overview

Harwick is the second-largest island in the [[content/shattered-sea/places/crown-islands/index|Crown Islands]] and the military centre of Dravosi power in the Shattered Sea. Where [[content/shattered-sea/places/calders-tooth/index|Calder's Tooth]] controls the western entrance to the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]] and [[content/shattered-sea/places/calveno/index|Calveno]] handles commercial business, Harwick builds ships and stages fleets. The name is old Dravosi: *hær-wīc*, army place. It has not changed its purpose since.

Two days east of Calder's Tooth, Harwick sits near the middle of the northern arc — accessible from both ends of the Crown Island chain and close enough to open ocean to move the fleet quickly when it has to. The deep-water anchorage on the island's south face is the largest natural harbour in the Scatter that a Ship of the Line can enter, and the Crown has spent considerable money making sure that advantage is fortified rather than merely scenic.

## Geography

The island is broader and flatter than the limestone ridges of Calder's Tooth. The interior plateau drops slowly to marsh and tidal channels on the eastern side, and rises toward forested hills in the northwest. The southwestern harbour face is sheltered from the prevailing northeast winds by a curved headland the charts call the Bight. The bay inside it runs deep close to shore, which is why the Naval Yard is there.

The northern coast takes open ocean swell directly. It is uninhabited outside of a signal station and a few fishing hamlets that have learned not to be surprised when a Crown frigate requests water. The eastern marshes drain through a network of channels into a shallow bay used by small craft, fishing boats, and anyone who wants to avoid the Yard's official entry points.

## The Naval Yard

The Harwick Naval Yard occupies the entire western shore of the Bight — dry docks cut into the rock, covered slipways long enough to lay a three-deck hull, storehouses that smell of fresh timber, pitch, and cordage. The Yard produces every class the Crown Admiralty operates. The [[HCS-Constancy|HCS *Constancy*]] and the [[HCS-Ordinance|HCS *Ordinance*]] — the *Constancy* being the standard heavy-frigate patrol specification, the *Ordinance* the largest vessel the Crown has ever commissioned — were both laid down here, on slipways that have turned out warships for six generations of Dravosi governors.

The Yard is Crown military property. A civilian can observe the outer walls and smell the tar; anything past the gate requires Admiralty permission, which is not freely given.

Timber comes from the island's interior forests, supplemented by imports from the eastern islands. The northwestern hills have been worked for shipbuilding timber long enough that the forest there is managed rather than wild — logging roads cut through it, old stumps ringed by new growth, and stands of young hardwood set aside as future keels. ^[inferred]

## The Admiralty

Harwick's Admiralty office sits on the hill above the Naval Yard, overlooking the anchorage. This is where Crown naval operations across the Scatter are scheduled, commissioned, and sealed. Patrol rotations, commission papers, privateer letters, quarterly chart updates dispatched to every Crown vessel — it all routes through here.

The Admiralty maintains a sealed key protocol for every warship in the fleet. Powder magazines lock with two keys: one held aboard the vessel, one archived at Harwick. Captains carry orders they are not permitted to open until specified conditions are met. The building is a fortress in the administrative sense — not much to look at from outside, extremely difficult to navigate inside without authorization, and staffed by people who know exactly which form makes your problem worse.

## The Town

The civilian settlement, also called Harwick, clusters along the eastern edge of the Bight between the Yard wall and the market wharf. It exists because the Naval Yard needs labour, the fleet needs provisions, and sailors on liberty need somewhere to spend their pay. Chandlers, taverns, a ropemakers' district, ships' suppliers, and the usual run of lodging houses. The Admiralty does not govern it directly; a civil harbour office handles licences, disputes, and the ongoing effort to collect docking fees from vessels that believe anchoring two hundred feet offshore exempts them.

The naval rating pool that crews Crown ships on rotation lives mostly here, in barracks along the north edge of town. Harwick sailors are not the Scatter's most adventurous. They are, reliably, the Scatter's most sober when they need to be.

## Roads & Routes

The main access is by the deep-water anchorage inside the Bight. Interior logging roads connect the managed northwestern forests to the Yard, while eastern marsh channels support smaller craft that avoid the official entry points.

## People & Power

Crown military authority dominates the island through the Yard, Admiralty, rating pool, and harbour protocols. Civil harbour officials govern the town day to day, but the Admiralty sets the limits.

## Trade & Resources

Harwick consumes timber, pitch, cordage, food, labour, and paperwork, and produces warships, patrol schedules, naval orders, and military employment.

## Flora

The interior plateau and northwestern hills carry a broadleaf hardwood forest — stands of ironwood and dense timber the Yard has harvested for ship's knees, keel sections, and hull planking for generations. Away from the logging roads, undergrowth runs to fern, moss, and climbing vine. The eastern marshlands produce saltgrass, sedge, and mangrove-fringed channels thick with water reeds. The shoreline below the harbour walls hosts kelp beds in the shallower reaches, and ribbon sea-grass grows in the sheltered tidal flat behind the Bight headland. ^[inferred]

## Fauna

The interior forest holds [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-boar|giant boar]], particularly in the older growth sections the Yard has not yet reached. They are a periodic problem for timber crews; the Crown posts a bounty on them that keeps the worst incursions managed. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wolf-spider|Giant wolf spiders]] occupy old stump hollows, drainage cisterns, and the darker corners of storehouses. The Yard runs periodic clearances, with mixed results.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-axe-beak|Giant axe beaks]] range across the open plateau — visible from the Admiralty walls on clear days, moving in loose groups across the grassland. They occasionally push close to the town outskirts, which the locals treat as an inconvenience rather than a crisis. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-owl|Giant owls]] are common in the older unmanaged forest in the northwest, hunting at dusk along the treeline. The harbour waters hold [[hunter-shark|hunter sharks]] year-round, drawn by fish offal and waste the Yard puts into the Bight. The Admiralty posts shark warnings at the bathing steps. It does not do much else about it. ^[inferred]

## Hazards

Military restriction, shark-filled harbour water, managed but dangerous forests, giant boars, axe beaks, giant wolf spiders, and Yard security all shape visitor movement.
