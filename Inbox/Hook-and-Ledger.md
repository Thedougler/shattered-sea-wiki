# The Hook & Ledger

## Overview

The Hook & Ledger is a narrow Shelf tavern in [[Port-Tidefall|Port Tidefall]] where clerks, quartermasters, pursers, and captains drink close enough to the harbour paperwork to hear tomorrow's trouble early.

## Description

The Hook & Ledger is a narrow tavern halfway up the Shelf road in [[Port-Tidefall|Port Tidefall]], close enough to the Harbourmaster's Office for clerks to reach it on a short break and far enough from Dockfront noise to conduct business in a normal voice.

A brass ship's hook hangs over the bar. Ledgers sit on shelves behind the bottles like holy texts. Candlelight catches in dusty green glass, and the room smells of lamp oil, wet wool, damp paper, and wine gone a little sharp in the cask. Nobody stops talking when a stranger enters, but the careful tables get quieter.

## Function

The tavern is a paperwork-adjacent meeting room where harbour gossip, freight deals, berth changes, inspection rumours, and side arrangements move before becoming official record.

## People & Operations

The regular crowd is the paper side of port traffic: pursers, quartermasters, freight factors, impound runners, petty officers, and shipmasters too mean to drink at home.

**Owner: [[Hector-Podge|Hector Podge]]** — A heavyset human man in his fifties, grey and steady, who has been running this room long enough that Crown officers stop asking his name and start asking which table is free. Keeps the room respectable enough for uniforms and loose enough for side deals.

## Notable Areas

**Main Room** — Round tables are packed closer than comfort allows, every one marked by old cup rings and older ink stains. Shipping news, berth changes, inspection gossip, and pricing rumours pass through here before they harden into official record.

The loudest laughter usually comes from the worst liars. The useful conversations happen a table away.

**Card Alcove** — A curtained side nook holds one heavy table, a scarred deck box, and enough privacy for people who do not want privacy noticed. Freight factors and labour middlemen settle advances here after dark. Coins move slower than paper. Nobody cheats small.

**Back Bar** — The upper shelves hold ledgers; the lower shelves hold bottles. A brass rail keeps the books from sliding when the cliff wind hits the shutters wrong.

## Stock / Services

The public inventory is ordinary: common wine, better wine for officers and shipmasters, bread with salt butter when available, hard dock cheese, pepper stew, candles, lamps, and a few travel goods for next-day departures.

The useful thing is not the stock. It is that every ordinary item comes with someone willing to talk while handing it over.

## Access & Security

The room is open and respectable enough for uniforms, but the card alcove offers enough privacy for business that should not look too private.

## Current Hooks
