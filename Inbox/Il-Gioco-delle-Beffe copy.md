# Il Gioco delle Beffe — DM Notes

## Situation

The Beffa is a five-day festival structured as a multi-phase team skill challenge. Every party member contributes through their own best skills. The money is real. The social capital is better. The fun comes from asking each player what *their character* does — then assigning skills to match.

The multi-day execution window (Days 2–4) is the core design change from the older one-day format. Marks are nervous for three full days. Teams can strike early, wait, or false-alarm their mark first. That uncertainty is the game.

---

## The Team Symbol

On Day One, the party registers their symbol with the committee. Let the players design it — a sigil, a stamp, a wax seal shape, a color band, anything. The committee records it. It belongs to them.

Mechanically, the symbol is proof of authorship. The team must leave it at the scene for the prank to count. How they leave it is their problem and part of the fun of Phase 3. It should be the last thing the mark finds — not a tip-off, not a clue, but a signature delivered after the fall.

**Beffa doppia (optional complication):** a rival team can attempt to steal the party's registered symbol and place it on their own prank, claiming the score. This requires the rival to know the symbol (possible if they observed registration) and to execute a contested Stealth check against the party's Perception during Phase 2. If it works, the committee adjudicates. The rules on this are historically contested and the committee enjoys the chaos.

## Phase Structure

### Phase 1 — La Scelta: Design (Day 1)
*No checks. Pure roleplay.*

The party picks their mark, designs their concept, and registers their symbol. The key question is not "what is the prank" but **"what does each character do?"** Let every player answer. Write it down. Their answers become the skill assignments in Phase 2.

| What they say | Likely check |
|---|---|
| "I scout the mark's daily routine" | Investigation or Perception |
| "I create a distraction in the market" | Performance or Deception |
| "I acquire a prop without being noticed" | Sleight of Hand or Stealth |
| "I plant false information in advance" | Deception or Persuasion |
| "I build a physical element" | Tool check or Athletics |
| "I recruit a civilian accomplice" | Persuasion |
| "I research the mark's weaknesses" | History or Insight |
| "I use magic for preparation" | Arcana or direct spell |

**Declare scale:** Minor (DC 12), Notable (DC 14), Legendary (DC 17, 50 gp stake). The party decides. Legendary is hard and worth attempting.

---

### Phase 2 — La Preparazione: Setup (Days 2–4, any day chosen)
*Each party member makes one check based on Phase 1 commitments.*

The party decides when during the window to strike. Earlier means less risk of sabotage; later means more time to prep and a more relaxed mark. Either is valid.

Assign DCs from declared scale. Success = their part works. Failure = their part goes wrong in a specific way.

**Complications on failure:**
- A prop is wrong → disadvantage on one Phase 3 check
- The mark spotted something → raise Phase 3 DC by 2
- A rival team noticed → they attempt a counter move
- A committee adjudicator observed more than intended → pointed question on judgment day

**Rival interference:** one rival team may attempt to sabotage preparation. Contested Stealth vs. Perception. If the rival succeeds, assign a complication. If the party succeeds, they identify the rival — and earn a free sabotage action against them in Phase 3 (contested, no cost).

**False-alarm gambit (optional):** the party may deliberately tip off their mark on an early day to lower their guard before the real strike later. This is a Deception check (DC = mark's Wisdom + 2). Success: mark's Phase 3 save is made at disadvantage. Failure: mark is now actively suspicious — DC raised by 3.

---

### Phase 3 — L'Esecuzione: The Strike (chosen day)
*Each party member makes one live-pressure check. The mark makes a Wisdom save. The symbol must be placed.*

**The mark's save:** DC = 8 + number of party successes in Phase 2.
- Fails: completely fooled. Scores 4–5.
- Succeeds by 1–4: partially fooled, something felt off. Scores 2–3.
- Succeeds by 5+: spotted the trick. Scores 0–1. The prank still proceeds — craft and recovery can still win it.

**Party checks:** each player makes the execution version of their Phase 2 role. Same skills, live pressure. Failure means something goes visibly wrong — crowd notices, judges note it.

**Placing the symbol:** one party member must place the team symbol during or at the end of the prank, without tipping off the mark prematurely. Sleight of Hand or Deception, DC 12. Failure does not invalidate the prank — but the symbol is found too early, reducing Surprise by 1.

**Recovery check:** after the prank, one party member walks it back. Performance or Persuasion, with Deception or Medicine as alternates. Good recovery is worth up to 2 extra craft points from the committee.

## Rival Teams

Present their results at sundown on Day Five. You do not need to play them out — just describe what happened.

**The Candle Brothers** — two humans, twelve consecutive entries, four second-place finishes. Technically brilliant, emotionally inert. They will score 13–15 regardless of their prank.

**Serena Voss** — a halfling factor who entered on a dare and is going for the Tessarine factor as her mark. No team. No experience. One very good idea. Roll her score secretly: 2d6+4. If she rolls high, the party has real competition — and she wants to buy everyone drinks regardless of result.

**Party Unknown** — three people in matching grey coats who registered under a team name nobody recognizes, declared "Compound / Legendary," and have refused to state their mark or concept beyond the category. They are either brilliant or going to disqualify themselves spectacularly. Roll their score at Day Five: 1d20. On 1–8: disqualified (the crowd loves it). On 9–14: score 11. On 15–19: score 16. On 20: score 19 — the party needs to beat or tie this to win.

## Prize Table

| Party Score | Result | Prize |
|---|---|---|
| Highest | Win | 60% of pool + Beffa Medallion + mark bonus |
| Second | Second | 25% of pool + mark bonus (if applicable) |
| Third | Third | Remainder of pool |
| Below all rivals | Respectable loss | Mark bonus only (if mark was fooled) |
| Disqualified | Disqualified | Stake lost; crowd applauds anyway |

**Pool estimate:** 4 teams × 25 gp standard, plus any Legendary stakes. Typical pool: 125–200 gp. Party wins 60% = 75–120 gp + mark bonus + side bets.

**Side betting:** party may bet on themselves at registration, max 50 gp at 2:1 (Notable) or 4:1 (Legendary). Listen to where crowd money is moving to learn rival team intelligence.

## Key Facts

- Prospero Morsani's plaque entry is the only one without a prank description. He was significantly younger when he won. He will not explain. The entry reads "Environmental / Notable."
- Giacomo Moretti signs the Accord every year and enjoys the festival. He finds the attempts flattering. He will never say this.
- Committee adjudicators are genuinely bad at not being noticed. DC 10 Perception identifies one during Phase 2. Performing for them is legal and effective.
- The beffa doppia has succeeded exactly twice in the festival's history. Both times the committee's ruling took three days. One ruling is still disputed.

## Running Notes

**Ask what each character does, not what the plan is.** The party's plan is their business. Your job is to assign a skill to whatever they commit to in Phase 1 and hold them to it in Phase 2. A prank that uses five different skills from five different party members is always better than one you designed yourself.

**Play the mark.** When the prank lands, embody whoever they picked. Be genuinely fooled by good ideas. Be appropriately suspicious of weak setup. The mark's reaction is the scene.

**The window creates tension for free.** On Days 2 and 3, when the party hasn't struck yet, describe marks being jumpy, rivals being seen in unexpected places, and committee adjudicators moving through the district. The uncertainty is doing the work; you don't need to add pressure manually.

**The medallion matters more than the money.** In Calveno, the Beffa Medallion is social proof that you are clever enough to deceive people in public and charming enough to get away with it. The Council cannot buy it. Iacopo Fieschi will mention it to Cosimo. Morsani will nod at the party across the Mercatura, and the nod will mean something, though he will not explain what.

- **Win:** Calveno social capital, medallion on the ship, Cosimo and Fieschi aware, Morsani nods
- **Lose honourably:** mark pays the bonus if the prank was good; Serena Voss wants to buy drinks
