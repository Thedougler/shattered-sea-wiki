## Description

The Inspection Pier is the first stop for any vessel entering [[Port-Tidefall|Port Tidefall]]. It runs out into the harbour mouth before the docks proper: wide planks, cleats along both sides, and a covered station at the far end where Crown clerks work through rating, manifests, and fees.

During inspection hours, the deck is all movement: sailors waiting with paperwork, petty officers looking irritated on principle, and clerks with ink-stained fingers checking cargo against what captains swear they meant to declare. The whole place has the practical, unpleasant importance of a door everyone has to pass through.

At high tide, the water sits just below the deck. At low tide, mud and barnacle-crusted pilings show beneath it, and the harbour smell gets exactly as honest as you would expect.

## Function

The pier processes arriving vessels through Crown rating, manifest checks, fees, and questions before they reach the docks proper.

## People & Operations

Crown clerks and petty officers operate the covered station, check cargo against manifests, assess fees, and keep arriving captains waiting with official patience.

## Notable Areas

**Deck** — The deck is long, exposed, and built for processing rather than comfort. Vessels tie up on either side while their papers are checked, fees are assessed, and questions are asked with official patience.

Nobody comes here for pleasure. They come here because Tidefall does not let ships simply arrive.

**Below the Pier** — At low tide, there is enough exposed mud under the pier for someone to walk below the deck, hunched among the pilings with voices and footsteps overhead. At high tide, the same space belongs to the harbour.

The underside is mostly old wood, barnacles, soft clay, and the smell of brine. Dockhands know the tides. Strangers learn them quickly or ruin their boots.

## Access & Security

The pier is a required processing point for arriving vessels. At low tide, the underside can be walked by someone willing to move hunched among pilings; at high tide, the harbour owns that space.

## Current Hooks

- The underside of the pier offers a tide-dependent route beneath voices, footsteps, and official business.
