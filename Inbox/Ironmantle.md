# Ironmantle

## Appearance

A Valcoran-built remnant sloop, single-masted, its hull plated with iron below the waterline. The iron sits visibly heavy in the water, dark and surface-corroded where it emerges at the waterline, running into cleaner metal below. The timber hull above is old. The join between iron and wood is uneven in a way that no current shipwright would produce. Four iron-tipped sweep oars rack along the inner hull. In choppy water, the hull makes a low resonance that carries up through the deck planking.

Below decks, iron ribbing runs the full length of the hull interior, making the space cramped, low-ceilinged, and loud underway. A small stern cabin with iron-braced walls fits one occupant. Five hammock hooks forward. No galley. The hold is minimal — the iron framing consumes most of the space a standard sloop would carry as cargo.

## Current State

**Hull:** Worn. Iron plating below the waterline is intact but shows significant surface corrosion in several sections. Timber frame beneath is sound. Repairs to the iron require Valcoran methods no current carpenter knows — standard repairs apply to the timber; iron sections are treated with pitch and rust inhibitor (included in upkeep, effective but not permanent).

**Weapons:** Both cannons mounted and operational. Gun mounts are iron-reinforced and more stable than standard — no accuracy penalty in rough weather.

**Below Decks:**

- Small stern cabin, iron-braced walls. Lockable. Fits one occupant.
- 5 hammock hooks forward in a low-ceilinged space. The iron ribbing makes the below-decks cramped and loud underway.
- Minimal hold — iron framing eats into cargo space significantly.
- No galley fitted.

**Identifying features:** Iron plating visible below the waterline when she heels. Immediately recognisable to anyone who knows Valcoran construction. The hull makes a distinctive low resonance sound in choppy water.

---

## What She's Good At

- Highest HP and AC of any Tier 1 vessel. She absorbs punishment that would sink a standard sloop.
- Gun mounts are more stable in rough weather — no accuracy penalty.
- The iron construction is a psychological deterrent: most captains know better than to try to board her.

## What She's Not Good At

- Speed. Slowest Tier 1 in standard conditions.
- Maneuverability. She does not respond quickly.
- Maintenance. 40gp/week upkeep is nearly double a standard sloop.
- Structural modification. Adding or expanding interior spaces requires tools capable of working iron.
