# Jean-Claude Tabarnack

## Summary

Jean-Claude Tabarnack is a three-foot-tall Blue Grung Gloomstalker Ranger who carries the weight of two lives — his own, and the one that was taken to teach him a lesson. He is twelve years old, early middle-age for his kind, and his body tells that story plainly: a compact, scarred frame, bright toxic-blue skin, and eyes that miss nothing. His people know him as names like "Jumpy Don't Touch" and "Hopstradamus." He knows himself as something harder to name — a deserter, a survivor, and maybe, if he lives long enough, a liberator.

He cannot lie. Not well, anyway. Whatever mechanism allows a person to smooth over the truth simply isn't present in him — his face, his posture, his blunt amphibian stare give him away before he opens his mouth. It cost him everything when it mattered most, and it may cost him again. But that same rawness is what makes him extraordinary in the dark: nothing escapes his attention, no trail goes cold, no ambush catches him off guard. He is as readable as he is undeceivable, and the world has learned to leave him alone for it.

The worst day of his life was also the best day of his life. He walked to his parents' hut full of hope — he had a plan to fake the death of his gnome friend [[Pell]], smuggle him to freedom, give him the world he'd only ever heard described. He walked in and found his sister, his parents, and [[Pell]] in chains. Simone had told them everything. What followed was a month of torture conducted specifically for his benefit, a lesson meant to harden him. It did the opposite. When he finally ran, he freed every slave he could reach — and watched them be slaughtered in the chaos, their screams buying him the seconds he needed to disappear into the dark. He has been disappearing ever since.

He carries a Flask of Endless Water. He has been moving since the night he fled, and he intends to keep moving until he is ready to stop.

What he wants — what he *needs* — is to go back. Not to escape, not to survive, but to return as the one who ends it. [[Pell]] once told him, in fragments of conversation stolen between chores, that every people has something the others lack, and that the only way to grow is to reach across that gap. Jean-Claude took that lesson and made it the spine of his entire existence. He is living the life his friend never got to live, and he intends to make it count for both of them.

**Mark:** Permanent disadvantage on Persuasion and Deception checks.

**Gift:** Advantage on Perception and Survival checks.

**Nature:** He cannot mask himself — his intentions, his nature, what he is. He's not sure if he's just bad at talking to people, or if something deeper is at work.

## History

Jean-Claude grew up in an insular Grung clan built on slavery. In his early years as a scout, he was not passive — he acquired slaves for his clan with fervor: scouting settlements, developing ambush tactics, making the Grung a more effective threat. He did it chasing glory, blind to the suffering he caused. He was good at it. He has never told anyone.

Somewhere in that period, a gnome slave entered his life. Conversations stolen between chores. Fragments of a broader world. A lesson that every people has something the others lack, and that the only way to grow is to reach across that gap. That gnome rewrote him.

He conceived a plan: petition his parents to make the gnome his personal slave, take him hunting a few times, then fake his death and let him escape. He walked to his parents' hut on what felt like the best day of his life. He walked in and found his sister [[Simone-Tabarnack|Simone]], his parents, and the gnome in chains. His younger sister had told them everything.

What followed was a month of torture — [[Pell]] killed slowly, specifically for Jean-Claude's benefit, as a lesson in hardness. It had the opposite effect. When he finally ran, he freed every slave he could reach. They were overwhelmed and slaughtered in the chaos. Their screams covered his exit. He has carried that sound since.

He fled into the world. He has not seen a Grung since.

## Personality & Drives

- **Cannot lie well.** His face, posture, and blunt amphibian stare give him away. He has accepted this.
- **Reads the dark.** In ambiguity or danger he observes first, acts second — unless someone is actively being harmed, in which case thought doesn't enter into it.
- **Skin contact is a mating ritual.** To a Grung, non-combat skin-to-skin contact in a social setting initiates a mating sequence. Watching landwalkers hug each other and walk away genuinely baffles and mildly horrifies him.
- **Afraid of birds** — a grung thing, not a paralyzing phobia. Avian creatures make him uneasy and wary. If they're calm and non-threatening he holds it together fine. Fast movement or a perceived threat tips it into visible fear.
- **Respects Umberlee** — not because she is good, but because the sea is indifferent to caste and species. She is equally a threat to everyone. He finds that oddly progressive, and weirdly comforting.
- **Firm moral absolute:** He will never enslave another living being. Witnessing active abuse or torture of a slave ends rational thought entirely. Violence follows immediately.
- **Long game:** Return home. Not to escape — to end it. He is living for himself and for the gnome.

## Active Situations

- **His clan** ([[Grung]], [[Verdant-Teeth]]) — knows what he did. He has not seen a Grung since leaving. The threat is real; it has not arrived.
- **[[Pell]]** — dead. Jean-Claude's gnome friend, tortured to death over one month as a lesson. Present in every dream, every decision, every nightmare. The relationship that changed everything and that Jean-Claude has never dealt with.
- **[[Simone-Tabarnack|Simone]]** — his younger sister. She betrayed him. He tried to get her out. A reunion would be both a grief and a threat.
- **His history as a slave-catcher** — blue caste duty, performed with skill and fervor before his conscience woke. He has told no one. It follows him.
