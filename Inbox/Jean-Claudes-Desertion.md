# Jean-Claude — Grung Expansion

## Situation

Simone Tabarnack is actively looking for Jean-Claude as part of a broader Grung expansion she is architecting. Jean-Claude knows the Grung slave system from the handler side, ran from it, and does not yet understand how far Simone's reach has extended.

---

## Current State

Blue-caste scouts have reached outer Midchain waters under trade cover, farther than the Grung have operated in years. One raid hit a small coastal settlement; a second overextended and failed. Rumour of unusually distant Grung raids is moving through fishing communities.

Simone does not know Jean-Claude's location. She knows he is alive and outside the Verdant Teeth. Rook was her buyer for Grung hunting toxin into the Central Strait; with Rook gone silent, her operative asked the wrong questions in Tidefall and exposed the supply channel at one end.

## Narrative Devices

- **Loaded Gun:** Simone's Rook toxin channel is burned at Tidefall; Verity Hollowell is positioned to hear the wrong-shaped inquiry.
- **Foreshadowing:** Sideways looks at Felix Aho's stall, distant raid rumours, blue-caste scouts under trade cover.
- **Clock:** Each world tick can move the scout circuit closer or force Simone to accelerate because the slave-soldier math is failing.
- **Reversal Potential:** The Vaka know Gold-caste divisions; the Grung order is less unified than it looks from below.

## Key Facts

- Simone is gold-marked and commands as expansion warlord under pure-gold leadership.
- She is looking for Jean-Claude as an operational loose end, not family sentiment.
- Karath has six confirmed *vethka* hulls on the expanded raid circuit, each carrying fourteen crew and four tons of captive cargo.
- Slave soldiers are breaking down and sometimes turning on handlers mid-raid.
- Jean-Claude understands the program's failure point because he ran slave-handling operations on Sorn before deserting.
- The Calveno raid is the defining western escalation: hundreds of civic captives taken from a Central Strait city using a Dravosi-sourced patrol gap, sewer extraction routes, and summoned Slaad as a distraction. This is what Simone built the western supply chain to fund.

## DM Notes

### Verdant Teeth Structure

The Teeth have run a slave economy for at least four generations. Blue-caste intermediaries handle outsider contact so gold leadership remains unseen. Veth is the political face; Sorn holds the largest enslaved population and Botukuri history; Karath stages raids; Dreth and Orak support agriculture/freshwater and lower-intensity capture.

Veth outranks Simone and can order action on Jean-Claude but has not done so. Either he is not worth the risk yet, or his return serves a future Gold-caste purpose.

### Pell Flashback Use

Play Pell warm first: she made Jean-Claude feel seen, not useful. The crack is when she talks about slaves who will never get out. She does not cry; she gets very still.

Sample lines:
- *"Where I'm from, it's different. Not better, just — different. That's enough, isn't it?"*
- *"You already knew. You just needed someone to say it out loud."*
- *"I'm not telling you what to do. I'm just telling you what I've seen."*

Death fact: Pell died in a cell beneath Sorn after 31 days of torture for names. She did not know whether Jean-Claude made it out.

### Fare I'a Pressure

Felix Aho has been in Tidefall four years. His stall is his livelihood and a findable same-culture support point for Grung passing through. As raid rumours reach Tidefall, the sideways looks become less sideways.

> [!read-aloud]
> The dock market moves the way water moves — around obstacles, filling gaps. A smell pulls attention before you see the stall: sweet and sharp and wrong. Behind the counter a green grung barely three feet tall wraps a parcel for a harbour porter with quick, unhurried hands. He looks up; wide amber eyes move across the group the way a man reads a document. He smiles warmly. His eyes do not stop working. "Well. You look hungry."
