# Ket

![[Ket.webp|Ket, a hungry mosquito-like Moucheron, reaching through the bars of a brass birdcage in the HCS Surety brig]]

A [[Moucheron|Moucheron]] from [[Murrat|Murrat]]. Not integrated. Not a mercenary. Rook went ashore on Murrat and took him — trapped, caged, brought back as a specimen before the village could respond. That was three weeks ago.

He is not the [[Five-Blades|Five Blades]]. He has not learned patience or contract discipline or the social math of operating in mixed company. He is a feral island Moucheron who treats every warm-blooded creature as potential prey, and three weeks in a cage has not changed that. It has just made him hungrier.

---

## Current State

**Hunger:** Difficult, trending Desperate. The crew fed him on rotation — a donor would approach the cage, arm through the bars, and had to be fast pulling back or he'd latch and not let go voluntarily. Nobody has been down since the Saltwright engagement. He knows exactly how long it's been.

**The cage:** A brass birdcage, roughly two feet across, hanging from a bent ship nail in the keel timber. The bars are close enough to keep him in. His proboscis reaches through them. The crew learned this the hard way.

---

## Behavior

He is actively hostile to anyone who comes within range. Not performing hostility — he will attempt to feed the moment a limb, a hand, a face gets close enough to the bars. He is Small and the cage is small; the proboscis extends further than looks comfortable. The crew has maintained a strict don't-get-within-arm's-reach policy since the first week.

He uses Common when it suits him — usually to tell whoever is nearby exactly what he intends to do to them, or to invite them closer. The tone is not threatening in a dramatic way. It's matter-of-fact. He's hungry, you have blood, this is obvious. He does not understand why this is a social problem.

He is tricksy in the fey sense: will say things calculated to get someone to step closer. *"I'm not going to do anything."* He absolutely is going to do something.

> [!DM]
> He speaks Common fluently — Moucherons aren't unintelligent, just predatory. He will answer questions, hold a conversation, even be briefly interesting. None of this is goodwill. It is bait. He wants whoever is talking to him to forget for one moment how close they're standing.
>
> The crew is visibly reluctant in the brig for this reason. Even Grigori only came once, looked at him, and left without getting near the cage. Ket found this funny. He told the crew afterward, in Common, that the *"big one smells wrong."* Nobody knows what that means and nobody wanted to ask.

**Sample lines:**
- *"Come closer. I won't bite."* (he will absolutely bite)
- *"You're bleeding a little. From the fight. I can smell it."*
- *"The other ones used to hold still better. You'll learn."*
- *"Let me out. I'll go straight to the water."* (he will not go straight to the water)
- *"Just your hand. Just for a moment."*

---

## Combat (if the cage is opened)

He has no blades and no mercenary training. What he has is a stinger/proboscis, fly speed 60 feet, and the Sanguivore trait. He will immediately attempt to feed on the closest creature, using the Stinger. He is not interested in a fight — he wants a quick feed and open air. If he gets one hit in, he disengages and flies for the nearest exit.

He is currently at Difficult on the hunger track, meaning he has disadvantage on Wisdom saves and advantage on attacks against any bloodied creature. Anyone who took damage in the Saltwright fight and hasn't been fully healed is effectively a beacon.

If he gets out: he feeds on whoever is closest and within reach — crew member, PC, doesn't matter — and then he's gone. Out a hatch, through a gun port, into the air, heading back toward open water. He is not sticking around to make a point.

---

## Resolution

**Freed:** He grabs whatever he can reach on the way out and flies. The party gets nothing from this except the problem being gone.

**Kept and fed:** He accepts the blood — there is no refusing blood — but remains hostile. He is not becoming an ally. He is becoming a fed predator in a cage, which is a marginally safer problem.

**Brought to Kalowe, introduced to the Five Blades:** Varet will recognize him as Murrat-born immediately. She will not be warm about it. She'll tell the party he needs to go back to the water and that keeping him is a bad idea, plainly, without elaborating on why it's their problem. She will not translate anything he says to her. Whatever passes between them is between Moucherons.

**Left alone:** At Desperate (Day 3) he stops trying to lure and starts making noise — not screaming, not pleading. A low continuous drone, the wing-sound Moucherons make when agitated, audible through the hull at night. The crew will not sleep well.

## Session 02 — Freed

[[Jean-Claude-Tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] offered a wine glass of his own blood through the bars; Ket drank, his abdomen went red, calmer but not safe. The party debated killing him versus release.

[[Perrin-Black-Jaw|Perrin]] drew his longsword and moved to execute Ket. [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss|Crissdalynn]] deflected the blade and put herself between him and the cage. In the rush she stepped past the safe distance from the bars. Ket drove his proboscis into her upper arm and fed. She pulled back. Perrin cast Tasha's Hideous Laughter; Ket went down incapacitated.

The party chose release. A corpse was placed on deck as a lure (Ensign Wouters); [[Mage-Hand|Mage Hand]] opened the cage from a safe distance. Ket fed for about a minute on the body, then lifted into the air and flew toward home.

[[Shepherd-Grigori|Grigori]] said he was glad. The crew had always hated what Rook did to him.

**Current status:** Free. Flying toward [[shattered-sea/places/midchain/Murrat|Murrat]].
