# The *Knife's Wake*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A low cutter comes on with patched tan canvas and a tired brown hull, the kind of working boat that should smell of fish and wet rope. Then the gunports open. Black-painted teeth show along the rail, boarding hooks come up from behind the bulwark, and the false trade pennant drops before the real flag rises: a [[Narrow]] black streamer cut like a knife wound.

---

## Overview

The *Knife's Wake* is a Tier 1 pirate cutter built for ambush, intimidation, and clean withdrawal rather than honest freight or naval battle. She passes at distance as a worn coastal trader: dull paint, patched canvas, no polished brass, no carved figurehead worth remembering. Up close, the disguise fails on purpose. Her gunports are too well-cut, her rail too clear of clutter, and her deck crew too ready to move.

She is a small-water predator. In reef channels, fog, harbour approaches, and the last hour before dusk, the *Knife's Wake* is exactly the wrong ship to let come alongside. In open water against a Crown patrol vessel or a properly crewed brigantine, she runs.

---

## Purpose

The *Knife's Wake* takes small valuable prizes: payroll chests, passenger jewels, locked dispatch boxes, contraband, spell components, officers worth ransoming, and cargo whose owner cannot safely report the loss. She is not built to haul bulk goods, and her captain does not want a hold full of wet grain when a sealed courier satchel pays better and sails lighter.

Her best attack is a short deception. She approaches under false colours, lets the target read her as poor and harmless, then fires one disabling volley and boards before the other crew finds its discipline.

## Layout

The *Knife's Wake* is organized around the first minute of a boarding action. Everything visible is either part of the disguise or part of the violence that follows.

**Weather Deck** — Kept clear for rapid boarding. Four light cannons sit amidships behind removable false cargo screens. Swivel positions at bow and stern cover the approach and escape. Boarding hooks, weighted lines, and short ladders are stowed under the rail where a deckhand can pull them free without crossing the deck.

**Aft Cabin** — More command locker than comfort. One narrow bunk, a bolted chart shelf, a locked strongbox, and a rack of false pennants. The captain's real papers are kept in oilcloth below a removable sole plank.

**Below Decks** — Cramped crew hammocks forward, powder and shot amidships, plunder cages aft. The cargo space is usable but awkward; prize goods are meant to be sorted fast, hidden fast, and sold fast.

## Crew

The ship works best with ten hands and becomes unreliable below four. A typical crew includes a captain, navigator, bosun, gunner, carpenter, cook, and four ordinary sailors who double as boarders.

| Role | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Captain** | Chooses targets by legal silence: smugglers, isolated couriers, and merchants too compromised to complain. |
| **Navigator** | Knows reef turns, false channels, and which charts colonial patrols trust too much. |
| **Bosun** | Keeps the deck empty, the hooks ready, and the lies consistent. |
| **Gunner** | Fires to cripple rigging and frighten crew, not to sink prizes. |
| **Carpenter** | Handles quiet repairs and removes identifying marks from stolen fittings. |
| **Cook** | Keeps food, grog, gossip, and morale in roughly equal measure. |

The *Knife's Wake* has 4 bastion space units available and no special facility slots. Her natural installed configuration is practical and violent.

| Existing Feature | Bastion Mapping | Space | Notes |

| Deck brazier and mess stores | Ship's Galley | 1 | Cramped but functional on voyages longer than three days. |
| Aft command locker | Captain's Cabin | 1 | Prize ledger, false flags, ransom notes, and captured papers. |

Replacing the Captain's Cabin with an Expanded Cargo Hold is possible, but it changes the ship's role from raider to smuggler. Adding a Surgeon's Berth would require sacrificing either comfort or command space.

## Operational Notes

**Ambush posture:** The *Knife's Wake* prefers dusk, fog, port congestion, shoals, and damaged targets. Her first volley aims at sails, rudder, and morale.

**Boarding profile:** She is at her most dangerous when allowed to come alongside. Swivels and deck crew suppress resistance while boarders cross fast.

**Weakness:** She cannot win a sustained gun duel. A disciplined patrol cutter that keeps distance and refuses the boarding angle has the advantage.

**Legal risk:** Owning her as a prize is complicated. The hull is not registered cleanly, and several fittings likely came from ships whose owners still have friends in port offices.
