## Overview

The krakling is what sailors are calling the arm-thing that has started appearing in the outer Shattered Sea — the eight-armed, eyeless predator that comes up under ships in the dark and starts pulling people into the water. Whether it is actually a young kraken, or something else entirely that resembles one, is not established. The adult Kraken is a different creature: Gargantuan, ancient, and something close to intelligent. The krakling is Large, hungry, and operating on instinct.

It is the third known elemental from the Plane of Water. Like the [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/sea-life/Leviathan|Leviathan]] and the [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/sea-life/Ridgeback|Ridgeback]], it has no eyes, black skin, and breathes only water. Unlike either of them, it is a surface hunter. It does not stay deep. It targets the hull and the crew simultaneously.

At CR 6, it is designed as a solo encounter for a party of four 5th-level characters aboard a tier 1 ship. It is challenging but not a total party threat. It will disengage if reduced below 40 HP, diving and not resurfacing during the encounter.

---

## Appearance

Eight arms, each 12–15 feet long and matte black. The mantle — the main body — sits about ten feet across. No eyes anywhere on the head mass. The face, such as it is, is a circular disc of skin around a parrot-like beak, which is the only hard surface on the creature. The skin does not reflect light. Looking directly at it in dark water is like looking at a hole.

When only partially surfaced, the first things visible are the arm-tips breaking the water around the hull — eight points appearing in sequence as the creature positions itself beneath the keel.

---

## Behavior

It hunts by feel. The arms go up over the railing first, testing — then one locks onto something solid and the grapple begins. It can hold four crew members simultaneously, working them toward the water's edge with Wrenching Pull while continuing to grab more. This is the tactical shape of every krakling encounter: multiple crew being dragged toward the rail while the rest of the party tries to fight something that is both in and out of the water and can grab anyone who comes close.

The Thrashing Ink recharge is the reset button — when the krakling is taking concentrated fire, it can stun the deck and black out the fight simultaneously. In the ink cloud, the party is blinded and prone; the krakling is not. It uses this to reposition or establish new grapples.

Crew who go overboard are in the water with a creature that has blindsight 60 feet. Rescuing them becomes the secondary crisis. The krakling may drag a grappled crew member underwater entirely rather than continuing to fight.

**Morale:** At 40 HP or below, the krakling releases all grapples and dives. It does not chase ships. If it loses its grip and its arms are cut back from the hull, it will circle at a distance before re-engaging — once. If driven off twice, it retreats.

**Ship-side tactics:**
- The ballista is a real threat. A good roll can do 16+ damage and force a retreat check early.
- Crew members not controlled by the PCs can be grabbed. Use grabbed crew as leverage to draw PCs into range.
- If the ship is not moving (anchored, becalmed, or held by tentacles), the krakling can take its time. If the ship is under sail, it has to match speed — its 40 ft. swim speed is sufficient in most sea states.

## Lore

Nobody who sees one calls it a krakling to its face. The name started as shorthand on the Kalowe docks: *"heard there was a krakling off the Verdant Teeth approach."* Whether it is actually kin to the adult Kraken — that Gargantuan ancient thing from older accounts — is a question nobody has had the opportunity to ask it.

What the survivors report consistently: it comes up from below, not across the water. There is no surface approach. The ship just starts losing crew, one by one, to arms appearing over the rail. The ink cloud is usually what ends the account. Everything after the cloud is guesswork.

Three ships have reported arm-related attacks in the outer Midchain in the past month. One made port with a missing mast and four crew. One made port with eight missing and a hull breach in the starboard bow that the carpenter says was pushed inward, not struck. The third has not made port.

**PC gravity:** Perrin Black-Jaw's connection to the Plane of Water incursion runs directly through the Leviathan and the Vestra sinking. A third elemental creature appearing in the Shattered Sea is part of the same pattern the Pearl of Souls is driving. The party does not know this yet, but Perrin will recognize the flat-black skin and the absence of eyes if he gets a good look at it — they match Clyde's Bestiary notes on planar fauna.
