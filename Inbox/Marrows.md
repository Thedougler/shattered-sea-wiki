# Marrow's

![[Marrow.webp|Marrow, proprietor of Marrow's]]

## Overview

Marrow's is a cargo broker's stall carved into the rock off the Warren's north channel corridor — three steps down from the main passage, easy to miss if you don't know it's there. Marrow handles things that needed to move without appearing on a manifest: sealed documents, consignment packages, cargo that required a different kind of routing than the surface city offers. He does not ask where things came from. He does not explain where they go.

## Description

Three steps down from the north channel corridor is an alcove carved into the rock — old work, older than the current Warren layout. Two walls hold shelves, most of them empty in a way that suggests recent turnover rather than slow business. A brass scale sits on the central table, polished on the pan surfaces and scratched everywhere else. The single lamp is positioned to illuminate the table without lighting the back of the room.

Marrow is usually already seated when anyone enters and usually already watching the door. He is lean, with one ear notched at the tip, and wears clothes that settle into the stone colour without effort. He weighs everything twice on the brass scale. Nobody has asked him why, and nobody has caught him weighing something that shouldn't need weighing and not learning something from the result.

## Function

Marrow handles cargo and document transit for people who cannot use the surface city's official channels — and who have the right introduction to be standing in the Warren in the first place. He is not a fence for stolen goods in the ordinary sense. He is a logistics specialist for situations that require deniability.

His terms are simple: the consignment period is three weeks, no exceptions. After three weeks, unclaimed items become a problem he solves on his own terms.

## People & Operations

**[[Marrow]]** gives no other name and is not asked for one. He has operated from this alcove for at least fifteen years; the Tangle regards his work as a Passage-adjacent service and leaves him to it. He accepts Kin by default. Dry need a Kin introduction and Marrow's own read of the situation — he has turned away people with good introductions before and offered no explanation.

## Stock / Services

- 5 gp+ — [[Document-Transit|Document transit]] — *"Price reflects content weight. Not physical weight. No questions."*
- 10 gp + 5% — [[Cargo-Brokerage|Small cargo brokerage]] — *"Three-week hold. Stated value. Non-negotiable terms."*
- 1 gp/wk — [[Consignment-Storage|Consignment storage]] — *"Three weeks. Hard limit. After that — my concern, not yours."*
- 15 gp+ — [[Passage-Network-Introduction|Kin-network introduction (Dry)]] — *"I decide eligibility. I'll let you know."*

## Access & Security

The alcove is not marked. Finding it requires someone who already knows where it is. Marrow does not respond to knocking on the wrong stretch of rock wall.

## Current Hooks

- A crate has been sitting in Marrow's consignment corner for three weeks. The courier hasn't returned. Marrow cannot keep it past the end of the week — that is the rule — and he does not know who the intended recipient is or how to find them. He will pay 50gp to anyone who locates either the courier or the recipient. He will not say what is in the crate.
- Marrow is quietly interested in reliable Dry contacts with surface access. He will not say so directly, but the questions he asks during introductions tend in that direction.
