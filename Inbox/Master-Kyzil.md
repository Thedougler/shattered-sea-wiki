## Overview

Master Kyzil is a senior master of the [[Sentinels-Of-The-Eyrie|Sentinels of the Eyrie]], the aarakocra observation order out of [[High-Eyrie]]. He assigned [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss]] her pilgrimage — map the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]] from inside a storm — and staked his own reputation on her readiness when he did it. Before she left, he pressed his crystal dreidel into her talons.

He tracked her by current and weather patterns after the *Red Lady* went down, deduced where she would wash ashore, and came to [[content/shattered-sea/places/port-tidefall/index|Port Tidefall]] to find her. When she never arrived, he learned the ship had sunk and left Tidefall to search. His drift calculation sent him east into the Midchain first — reasonable model, wrong answer. At a wayport he found a maritime contact who had seen an aarakocra monk aboard a captured Crown patrol cutter heading west. He corrected course. He is now moving through the Central Strait, days behind the party, with a confirmed sighting but no ship name and no destination.

## Appearance & Manner

Tall and lean, built and coloured like a barn owl. His facial disc is pale and broad, the feathers around it tawny-brown fading to ash at the crown and mantle. He wears plain sentinel robes with loose cuffs that occasionally shift to reveal geometric markings on his forearms.

He stands with wings folded tight, feet set with the still grip of something perched rather than standing. When he turns, his head moves before his body follows.

He asks questions instead of giving answers. He does not raise his voice. He does not repeat himself. When he watches you, his head tilts slightly, and the precision of it lands before you work out why it unsettles you.

His formality can crack once, and only where Crissdalynn is concerned. Seeing her alive in Calveno breaks through the master-mask long enough for a sudden silent dive and a crushing hug. He straightens out of it quickly: robes smoothed, wings folded, dignity recovered by force of habit.

## Voice & Delivery

**Anchor**: Javier Bardem doing Kenobi — composed enough to be unnerving, cracked exactly once, covering the crack immediately with a question.

**Quote**: *"You may fight the current. The current doesn't notice."*

**Speech patterns:**
- Questions over statements. When he wants something, he asks. When he's teaching, he still asks. He doesn't inform — he waits for you to arrive at it.
- Stops before the emotional word. "I corrected course—" [beat] "—three times." The pause is the feeling. He never names the feeling.
- Agrees when he wants to apply pressure. Student says something self-critical. He says: "Yes." No elaboration. Let them sit with it.
- Wind and tide are his vocabulary for human relationships. He doesn't say *I was worried*. He says *I corrected course three times looking for you*.

**Physical tic**: After his composure breaks, he smooths his robes, refolds his wings — right over left — then adjusts his hood, in that order. The party sees it once: right after the hug in Calveno. It is a tell. He does not know it shows.

## Crissdalynn

He assigned her the hardest pilgrimage on record because he believed she was capable of it. The day before he gave it to her, she had been flying lazy circles with friends when she should have been at her post. He said it once, in the form of a task.

He came to Port-Tidefall to hear her account of what she saw at the Maw. He has told her the pilgrimage is not over. He has not elaborated on what finishing it looks like.

In Calveno, he asks for the course of her pilgrimage rather than a confession. He wants what she saw, what she recorded, and what the Maw did to wind, tide, current, cloud, compass, and courage. He believes she has grown stronger. He is also still enough of a master to be certain he can teach her something.

## Calveno Sparring Frame

Kyzil may invite Crissdalynn and her companions into a nonlethal four-on-one spar. The statblock below is his real combat profile, not a softened training-mode version. His restraint is narrative: he turns edges, catches falls on the wind, drops a student cleanly instead of maiming them, and stops before harm becomes permanent.

The level 4 party should not be able to beat him by trading actions. The victory condition is respect, not HP. Count clean moments: forcing Perfected Focus, making him spend Empty Wing Parry, landing a hit after baiting movement, protecting an ally from a gale line, disarming one dagger, or using terrain to deny a line. Four clean moments is extraordinary; Kyzil still ends standing.

The desired table feel is four people punching empty air: Kyzil steps through gaps, lets blades miss by inches, redirects momentum with wind pressure, and corrects footwork while his daggers return to his hands as if the air owes him favors.

For Crissdalynn, he is the positive example: flight used with discipline, wind used to protect as much as punish, and blades used only with purpose. If she impresses him, he frames **Read the Current** as a reward. If she struggles, he frames it as instruction. Either way, the lesson uses the monk's existing ki/focus point spend on a hit. It is not a new resource or tracker.

> [!dm] Spar Quick Card
> **Opening**: "I'd like to show you something. Please try to hit me." He does not raise his voice.
> **Routine**: Multiattack (4 attacks, pulling edges) → Patient Defense → Empty Wing Parry or Crosswind Slip on each hit attempt. By round 2: Updraft Step to high ground, full pressure.
> **Win condition**: Count clean moments — forcing Perfected Focus, landing a hit after baiting movement, protecting an ally from Cutting Gale, disarming a dagger, using terrain to deny a line. Four clean moments is extraordinary. Kyzil still ends standing.
> **Cutting Gale**: Use the first time two or more PCs stand in a line. Say "He exhales." That is the warning. Let them react or not.
> **Legendary routine**: Dagger Flash (1) for constant pressure, Silent Step (1) to reposition, Downburst (2) when PCs cluster.
> **Ending**: Kyzil stops, not the party. He catches a weapon or fist mid-swing, holds it one beat, says "There." Steps back. Done.

## Session 03 — Calveno Reunion

Kyzil tracked [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Crissdalynn-Khinriss|Crissdalynn]] to Calveno by a wayport sighting of an aarakocra monk aboard a captured Crown cutter. He arrived Day 1 of the party's shore leave.

He found the party in a Le Paludi street and dove at Crissdalynn from height, sun behind him — no warning, no sound. She was knocked twenty feet back, wind driven out, before she recognized him. He held her, then straightened up: wings folded, robes smoothed, dignity recovered by force of habit. "Oh my God, Priscilla, you're alive. Thank God." Then, with the others watching: "Hi, I'm — I'm Master Kyzil. Nice to meet you. What happened?"

He introduced himself to [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Delmar-Fisk|Delmar]] by name and shook hands. Met [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Perrin-Black-Jaw|Perrin]] when Perrin emerged from a barrel he'd been hiding in. Extended one finger. "It is a pleasure to make your acquaintance, Master Perrin."

**Current state:** In Calveno. The pilgrimage conversation — what she saw at the Maw, what finishing it now means — has not yet happened.
