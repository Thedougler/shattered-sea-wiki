# Meth-Var

> *The island that watches the water no one trusts.*

The easternmost of the five Sunken Crown islands, facing open Maw waters on an exposed, windswept approach with little shelter for larger vessels. Meth-Var is not inhabited permanently. The people of [[Keth-Naar|Keth-Naar]] rotate watchers through it because the island's value is attention — it faces the direction where weather and deep movement can reach the Blue Hole before anyone in the central settlement sees the sign.

Watchers leave marks in paired sets: one for what was seen, one for who must be told. A sighting without a carried warning is considered unfinished work.

Lately, the warning patterns have been arriving wrong.

Meth-Var has little cover for ground predators, which makes the threats that do land there more obvious and less comforting. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-vulture|Giant vultures]] wait out bad weather on the lee rocks, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-bat|giant bats]] crowd the signal caves when storms push them off their usual routes, and watchers still mark possible [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/monstrosity/harpy|harpy]] calls separately from wind noise. A warning post that stops listening is just a rock.

## Geography

Meth-Var is exposed, windswept, and shelter-poor, facing open Maw waters at the eastern edge of the Sunken Crown. It has lee rocks, signal caves, and limited places to survive bad weather.

## The Scalded Ropes

The recent dragon turtle wyrmling story begins here because Meth-Var is where the evidence survived. A Keth-Naar skiff did not return cleanly from the eastern watch run. The next rotation found the landing ropes bleached pale and scalded stiff, the signal stones reset in the emergency pattern, and one watcher alive in the lee crack with burned hands and no boat.

Their account is treated as a warning, not a tale. The water warmed before the attack. Steam moved low over the reef. A dark shell-ridge, too small for the old island-sized stories but large enough to break a skiff, rose from the Blue Hole edge. The skiff turned sideways under a tail strike before anyone could row clear. Whether the wyrmling lairs in the Crown or passed through from the [[Outer-Reach|Outer Reach]] is not established; Meth-Var only records that it was close enough to reach the watch route. ^[inferred]

## Roads & Routes

Meth-Var is reached by watch rotation rather than ordinary travel. Landing is exposed, shelter is limited, and watchers move between marks, caves, and lee cracks as weather allows.

## People & Power

Keth-Naar maintains Meth-Var through rotating watchers. Authority here is the obligation to see, mark, and carry warning back.

## Trade & Resources

The island produces no goods. Its value is early warning from the open Maw direction.

## Flora

Meth-Var has little cover for ground predators and no documented useful plant resources.

## Fauna

Meth-Var's exposed position concentrates certain animals during storms and along the prevailing currents. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-vulture|Giant vultures]] wait out bad weather on the lee rocks — they use the island as a windbreak and thermal launch point, and their presence in numbers is a reliable indicator that something recently died in the surrounding water or washed up on the reef. Watch rotations treat a vulture count above baseline as a secondary observation worth logging alongside the primary marine signs.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-bat|Giant bats]] crowd the signal caves when storms push them off their usual routes from Keth-Naar and Ral-Arn. The caves are the only deep shelter on the island, which means bats and watchers compete for the same dry space during the worst weather. Watchers who have made this rotation describe the bats as loud, territorial, and preferable to being outside.

Watchers still mark possible [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/monstrosity/harpy|harpy]] calls separately from wind noise in the observation record. The eastern exposure puts the island in the path of whatever travels the current between the Maw and the Blue Hole. A watch post that misidentifies a harpy call as wind is a watch post that stops being useful.

## Hazards

Maw weather, exposed landing, giant vultures, giant bats, possible harpy calls, heat events, and dragon turtle wyrmling signs.
