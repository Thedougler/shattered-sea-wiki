# The Midchain

![[1778025598810~2.png]]
![[The-Midchain.webp|Cluster of lush Midchain islands surrounded by bright reef water and deep channels]]

## Overview

The Midchain is the southern arc of the [[Shattered-Sea|Shattered Sea]]'s [[Verdant-Scatter|Verdant Scatter]]: dozens of low limestone and coral islands packed close, most a half-day's sail from the next. The treeline often appears before the land does. Dense rainforest runs to the water's edge on many coasts; elsewhere, pale limestone cliffs drop straight to reef. The water shifts from turquoise over the shallows to blue-black where the shelf falls away, and the line between them is abrupt.

The air is warm, wet, and salt-heavy, with the sweetness of rainforest rot carrying from the interior. At night the stars are dense, the treeline is loud, and channels between islands narrow into green corridors where the current slows under the canopy.

Fresh water and shelter are rarely far away. Certainty is harder to find.

## Geography

The Midchain is lower, closer, wetter country than the [[Crown-Islands|Crown Islands]]. Reefs shift. Channels silt. Back passages open after storms and close again without asking permission. Dense islands, mangrove cuts, blue holes, reef corridors, rainforest interiors, and sheltered lagoons make it useful to crews that know it and dangerous to crews that only bought a chart.

Prevailing winds turn more westerly near the [[Doldrums|Doldrums]], creating back channels useful for westbound ships trying to avoid Strait inspection. Dravosi patrol cutters run intercept routes through the more predictable lanes, which is one reason experienced captains prefer the less predictable ones.

## Routes & Access

Charts for the Midchain contradict each other because the Midchain keeps changing the question. Local knowledge is worth more than paper in most of the inner channels. The same back channels that hide a ship also hide teeth. Deep blue holes are avoided unless a pilot knows them by name, because [[Sawek|Sawek]] lairs look like useful shelter until something moves below the keel. Mangrove cuts and river mouths add their own hazards: [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-crocodile|giant crocodiles]], [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-constrictor-snake|giant constrictor snakes]] waiting under root mats and flooded banks, and enough lesser reef predators that local pilots talk about water color the way farmers talk about weather.

The Midchain is also where small piracy becomes geography. A cutter like [[Knifes-Wake|the Knife's Wake]] can disappear through channels a frigate captain refuses to enter, and ordinary [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/humanoid/pirate|pirate]] crews survive by knowing which reef turn is real and which one exists only on a Crown chart. The [[The-Bad-Receipt|Bad Receipt]] is a more organized version of the same problem, working Kalowe repairs, false papers, and southern Strait traffic into one business. When sailors talk about the [[The-Glass-Debt|Glass Debt]] in the Midchain, they are usually talking about convoy routes getting expensive.

## People & Power

The Midchain is difficult to govern from outside. Colonial powers hold strategic points and run patrol cutters through the lanes, but three centuries of trying has not produced full administration. Most of the region belongs to island councils, free ports, pilot families, market bosses, and whoever knows the reef better than whoever is chasing them.

Island councils are stronger here than anywhere else in the Scatter. They do not all agree with each other, and they do not need to. Each island has its own arrangements, its own welcome, its own price, and its own reef. When a problem reaches beyond any one island, affected representatives may gather in [[Chain-Council|the Chain Council]] at [[Kalowe|Kalowe]] and vote on a shared response.

## Trade & Resources

[[Kalowe|Kalowe]] is the primary Midchain settlement: a free port built across three reef islands over a natural lagoon. It has the only Tier 2 dry dock in the Midchain outside colonial administration, which makes it the usual place for unregistered vessels, hired crews, cargo that skipped the ledger, and people working hard to be difficult to locate.

Two days south of Kalowe, the [[Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]] rise from low reef-fringed coasts into dense rainforest. The interiors belong to the [[Grung-Clans|Grung clans]]. Outsiders trade at designated beaches and stay at the waterline. The coastal settlements of the Midchain know the raids have worsened in recent years. Everyone has heard enough to avoid the interior. Some merchants still go to the beach.

Reef fish are everywhere: parrotfish, snapper, and grouper through the coral systems. Lagoons support net fishing and trap lines. Sea turtles nest on the outer islands from late spring through summer. Whale sharks pass through the western Midchain seasonally, following warm current pulses south from Crown waters.

The Midchain can feed a careful crew and swallow a careless one. Most routes are not hidden. They are just local, and that is not the same thing.

## Flora

Dense rainforest runs to the water's edge on many coasts, with mangrove cuts, river mouths, wet margins, and larger southern interiors that can support agriculture, fruiting canopy, and inland routes. Specific crop lists are not yet fully documented outside the Verdant Teeth trade goods and Rakoru pepper.

## Fauna

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/sea-life/Sawek|Sawek]] lairs in the deep blue holes — the sudden shelf drops that appear between reef systems where the bottom vanishes and the water goes dark. Blue holes look like shelter and anchorage to captains who do not know them by name. Pilots who do know them by name do not anchor in them. Whether it is a single animal with a range across several holes or a population occupying distinct territories is something the pilot community has declined to investigate systematically.

The mangrove cuts and river mouths concentrate [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-crocodile|giant crocodiles]] in numbers that make wading crew any flooded interior channel a decision worth making deliberately. Midchain crocodiles are larger than the Crown Island population — the interior waterways run wide and warm, and the food supply is reliable enough to support animals of the size that local guides describe. Most small-boat groundings in mangrove water include crocodile awareness as a factor in the rescue timeline.

The larger southern island interiors produce [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-ape|giant ape]] sign: stripped canopy trails, boulders relocated to positions no storm explains, and fruit trees worked from the crown down. The range clusters near the [[Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]] but is wide enough to make the southern Midchain interior a category rather than an isolated sighting. Pilots treat it as a coastal problem — the apes rarely reach the beach — but landing parties that push past the first treeline on unknown southern islands do so with that in mind. ^[inferred]

## Hazards

The Midchain's practical dangers are shifting reefs, uncertain channels, Sawek blue holes, crocodile water, constrictor-snake root mats, interior giant ape sign, Grung boundary violations, and piracy that turns local route knowledge into a weapon.
