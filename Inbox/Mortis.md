# The Mortis

> *A Mortis is never a punishment. It is a fact about who the character is, expressed through mechanics.*

A session zero option pairing a permanent mechanical disadvantage (the **Mark**) with a targeted mechanical benefit (the **Gift**). The two sides always describe the same person — not a character who is also cursed, but a character whose strengths and limits are two sides of the same coin.

**Source:** `Sources/reference/2026-04-13-mortis-rule.md`

---

## Design Rules

**Mark and Gift describe the same person.** Read only the Mark — you should still be able to sketch the character. Both halves name the same truth from opposite ends.

**The Mark must have real teeth.** If a player can easily ignore it, it does not qualify. Must apply consistently in situations that come up at this table.

**The Gift must target the core fantasy.** Not a consolation prize — the thing that makes the character feel more like themselves.

**The Gift always exceeds the Mark in value.** The Mark is the price of leaning harder into a concept, not the cost of entry. At maximum punishing Mark, the Gift reaches roughly the value of a free feat.

---

## Creating a Mortis (Session Zero)

1. Player names the character flaw — a fact that limits them in a specific, consistent way
2. DM translates it into a specific mechanical condition
3. DM proposes a Gift that exceeds the Mark and targets the player fantasy
4. Both agree. It locks in. DM has final say on both sides.

**One Mortis per character. Does not change as the character levels.**

---

## Running It

The Mark goes on the character sheet as a standard mechanical note. Disadvantage/proficiency/etc, integrated into your character sheet math for easy quick look ups, you shouldn't need to think about it.

---

## Constraints

**Mark must:**

- Apply to a defined category of rolls or actions that will come up at this table
- Be enforceable without case-by-case adjudication
- Affect something outside the character's core class fantasy

**Mark must not:**

- Remove a core class feature or subclass ability
- Prevent the player from attempting an action — they roll with disadvantage, they do not lose the option
- Require special tracking beyond a sheet note

**Gift must:**

- Be mechanically concrete (named feat, flat bonus, proficiency, expertise, or cleanly defined passive)
- Directly serve the player's stated character fantasy
- Be readable from the character sheet without referencing this document

## Example Mortises

| Name                  | Concept                                    | Mark                                         | Gift                                                                              |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The Reckless**      | All offence, no self-preservation          | Cannot take the Dodge action                 | +2 to all melee attack rolls                                                      |
| **The Loud**          | Subtlety is not in them                    | Disadv. on Stealth checks                    | Inspiring Leader feat                                                             |
| **The Impulsive**     | Acts before they think                     | Disadv. on Insight checks                    | +5 Initiative; cannot be surprised while conscious                                |
| **The Obsessive**     | Tunnelled into their craft                 | Disadv. on Perception checks                 | Expertise in one skill (player's choice)                                          |
| **The Frail**         | Not built to absorb punishment             | Max HP –1 per character level (scales)       | +2 to one ability score (player's choice)                                         |
| **The Scholar-Blade** | Every hour on the blade, none on the books | Disadv. on History, Arcana, Nature, Religion | One combat feat (Dual Wielder / Polearm Master / Shield Master / Crossbow Expert) |

---

## What Makes a Bad Mortis

- A Mark that barely triggers (free feat in disguise)
- A Gift that misses the player fantasy
- A Mark punishing a core class feature (Rogue + Stealth disadv., Wizard + Arcana disadv.)
- A Mark requiring case-by-case adjudication ("when distracted" — must be unambiguous)

## The Party's Mortises

Three members of the Surety crew took a Mortis at session zero.

---

### The Small ([[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Perrin-Black-Jaw\|Perrin Black-Jaw]])

*Homebrew feat. Session zero only, DM approval required. Not repeatable.*

Not built to haul rope or take a hit in a scrum — so he leads instead. Reads people like water. Finds the current, finds what's useful.

- **Mark:** Disadvantage on all Strength-based checks and saving throws.
- **Gift:** Charisma score increases by 2.

---

### The Unmasked ([[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack\|Jean-Claude Tabarnack]])

*Homebrew feat. Session zero only, DM approval required. Not repeatable.*

What he is reads clearly — in his face, in his stillness, in the way he positions before a fight. That same unfiltered attention makes him a sharp reader of terrain and intent.

- **Mark:** Disadvantage on Charisma (Persuasion) and Charisma (Deception) checks.
- **Gift:** Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Wisdom (Survival) checks.

---

### The Crowded ([[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Delmar-Fisk\|Delmar Fisk]])

*Homebrew feat. Session zero only, DM approval required. Not repeatable.*

Five captains' souls pressed into him by Umberlee as the fleet sank. He is never fully alone in his own head — they surface when their triggers fire, borrowing his body and bringing their skills with them.

- **Mark:** Disadvantage on all Wisdom saving throws. When a captain's trigger fires, that captain surfaces involuntarily.
- **Gift:** Once per short rest (bonus action), surface one captain. While surfaced, gain that captain's skill proficiencies as borrowed mastery; if already proficient in a granted skill, gain Expertise instead. The brass flagon returns Delmar to the surface from any captain.
