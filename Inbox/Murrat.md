# Murrat

![[Murrat.webp|Murrat seen from the reef, a dense uninhabited tropical island with low haze over the canopy]]

## Overview

Murrat sits roughly midway between [[Kalowe|Kalowe]] and the western edge of the [[Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]], a limestone reef island low enough that the canopy is the first thing you see above the horizon. From the approach it looks like every other uninhabited Midchain island: dense forest, pale cliff faces above the waterline, reef running out ahead of the hull. The water inside the reef gap is calm and deep and looks like a reasonable anchorage.

It isn't on most working charts. Pilots avoid it. The island is [[Moucheron|Moucheron]] territory and they attack anything that lands — people included, no differently than the fauna they feed on. Unlike the Grung-held islands of the [[Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]], where a careful approach to Veth can open trade, there is no protocol on Murrat and no one to negotiate with. Any party that comes ashore is treated as prey.

## Geography

The island is a single irregular limestone mass, larger than it looks from a distance. The reef extends further east than the charts that do show it suggest, which has caught at least two vessels that tried to give it a wide berth and didn't give it wide enough. The interior rises to a low central ridge before dropping back down to mangrove cuts on the southern coast. There is fresh water — springs in the ridge face and a small interior lake the Moucherons use — which explains why the island can support multiple permanent villages rather than a single small settlement.

The canopy is thick. Landing parties who got ashore and survived long enough to leave accounts described not being able to see more than twenty feet in any direction on the ground. The Moucherons, who fly at sixty feet, see everything.

The cliff faces above the waterline hold the villages — stone platforms built into the rock, rope bridges between them, structures tucked into the natural hollows of the limestone so that nothing is visible from the water. A ship that comes in close enough to see the platforms has already been seen by everyone in them.

## Roads & Routes

There are no roads. The Moucherons move through the canopy and in the air. Ground-level movement through the interior is dense bush and root systems — manageable for a Small creature who knows the paths, very slow for anyone larger. The reef gap on the northwest face is the only vessel entry point with enough depth for a working boat. It is also the approach every Moucheron village can see from their platforms.

---

## People & Power

Several Moucheron villages occupy the cliff faces and canopy above the interior. The villages operate as separate kin-groups but share a common position on outsiders: they are hostile to any non-Moucheron on the island and will attack. They are fey predators that feed on blood — on Murrat, that means the island's fauna and anyone who lands. x

The [[Moucheron|Moucherons]] who leave the island and work as mercenaries in the wider Midchain are the same creature with the same appetite. What leaving taught them is that fighting other people's wars delivers better blood on a better schedule than hunting. They are not less dangerous. They are more patient.

## Trade & Resources

None. There is nothing to trade for and no party on Murrat who would trade. The island produces what it consumes.

---

## Flora

Tropical Midchain canopy: dense hardwood forest on the ridge, mangrove on the southern coast margins, fig trees and palms in the interior. The Moucherons have no agricultural tradition — the island feeds them through its fauna.

---

## Fauna

Murrat's interior fauna is dense relative to comparable Midchain islands, because the Moucherons take blood rather than meat and the carcasses they leave behind feed the scavenger chain, which keeps prey populations high. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-constrictor-snake|Giant constrictor snakes]] work the mangrove cuts and river mouths. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-spider|Giant spiders]] hold the high canopy. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-frog|Giant frogs]] breed heavily in the interior lake margins. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-ape|Giant apes]] range the ridge face. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wasp|Giant wasps]] nest in the cliff hollows between the Moucheron platforms — they are a nuisance, not a pet, but the Moucherons tolerate them because anything that stings an intruder first is doing them a favor.

The net effect for a landing party: the Moucherons are the primary threat. The fauna is the secondary threat. Surviving one does not resolve the other.

## Hazards

**[[Moucheron|Moucherons]].** Villages can scramble a response within minutes of a landing. They fly 60 feet and spot movement through the canopy from above. Multiple groups will respond — killing the first wave does not end the encounter. Expect 3–6 per squad, multiple squads.

**Fauna.** The interior has giant constrictor snakes, giant spiders, giant apes, and giant frogs. A party that breaks away from the Moucherons moves directly into their hunting grounds.

**Eastern reef shelf.** Extends further east than most charts show. Sharp limestone, outgoing tide pulls toward it, no assistance from the island.

**Visibility on approach.** The Moucheron platforms are built into the cliff faces and invisible from the water until a ship is already close enough to be seen from them. Assume any daylight approach within half a mile is observed.
