# Musket

*Martial ranged weapon · 500 gp · 10 lb*

---

## Properties

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Damage | 1d12 piercing |
| Range | 40 / 120 ft |
| Properties | Ammunition, Loading, Two-Handed |
| Ammo cost | 1 gp per 20 shots (powder and ball) |

**Loading.** You can fire once per attack action without additional class features. The Attack action fires once; Extra Attack does not grant additional shots from this weapon unless cleared between each.

---

## Special Rules

**Misfire.** On a natural 1 to hit, the weapon misfires and cannot be fired again until cleared. Clearing requires an action (no attack).

**Loud.** The report carries up to 300 feet. Any Stealth advantage from hiding before attacking is lost, even on a miss. Creatures in the vicinity are alerted.

**Powder and Ball.** The powder must stay dry. If the weapon is fully submerged, it misfires automatically on the next firing attempt (and must be cleared and re-loaded). In steady rain, the shooter makes a DC 14 Dexterity check before each shot — failure is a misfire.

---

## Niche

**Best at:** peak burst damage. 1d12 is the highest single-shot damage of any ranged weapon — above the heavy crossbow's 1d10 and the longbow's 1d8. That one shot is the reason to carry it.

**Worst at:** range and reliability. At 40/120 it has shorter effective range than every other two-handed ranged weapon. Loading prevents extra attacks. Misfire, Loud, and Powder-and-Ball are costs bows and crossbows never pay. In steady rain it is a liability.

The higher wet-weather DC (14 vs pistol's 12) reflects the longer barrel and larger powder charge.
