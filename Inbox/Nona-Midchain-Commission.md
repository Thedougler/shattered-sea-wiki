# Nona's Midchain Commission — Aruhe Survey

## The Ask

This is the second thing Nona offers Perrin, not the first. The sewer investigation ([[content/shattered-sea/situations/islands/Warren-Grung-Sewers|Warren — Grung in the Sewers]]) is the immediate ask at the table — the job that lands the moment she hears Jean-Claude is crew. Aruhe comes later, privately, once the meal is done and the Tangle leads have been distributed. She does not mention it to anyone else at the table.

Nona found a reference to Aruhe in forty-year-old Passage route notes — a waypoint entry: *good water, good stone, one approach, don't stay past dark.* No explanation of that last line. She cross-checked the chart against three other old sources. Every one describes the same thing: high cliffsides, a single navigable harbour entrance, a deep-water anchorage inside. The island sits east of Karath, inside the reef shadow of the Verdant Teeth, and has been deserted for as long as anyone currently alive can remember.

She chose it specifically because the Grung avoid it. An island Grung patrols will not land on is an island no Crown vessel will find evidence of Grung near. She has worked out that the avoidance creates a blind spot. She has not worked out why the Grung avoid it.

Her exact words to Perrin, paraphrased: *the Passage needs a fixed place in the Midchain, not just routes. The Below is an exit; she wants a door that opens both ways. Aruhe might be it. She wants to know if it can be held. She wants Perrin to tell her that, not a report.*

She is aware of the Grung proximity. She is not aware of what is on the island. She is asking Perrin to confirm the site is safe and suitable. She will contact the *Uncertainty* via signal lantern each evening at dusk. Perrin answers. She asks three questions. He answers in twenty-five words or fewer.

---

## What Nona Does Not Know

The Grung call Aruhe *"the island that is already eaten."* They patrol the surrounding reefs but have not landed on it in living memory. The coastal strip is abnormally crowded — too many giant frogs, scorpions, snakes, and spiders packed against the shoreline, pressed there as if something worse holds the interior.

Something does. The Blight has established a Memorial in the abandoned interior. The Death Bloom is at the island's centre. The corruption has pushed through every flooded ditch and terrace structure outward from the Death Bloom. What is left in the interior is not wilderness — it is a dungeon.

None of this is visible from the sea. A passing ship sees cliff and jungle. A ship anchoring in the harbour sees a narrow beach and a lot of fauna near the waterline. A landing party finds out.

Full Blight situation: [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Aruhe|Aruhe — DM Notes]].

## Narrative Devices

- **Loaded Gun:** The old route note ends with *don't stay past dark.* Nona passed this to Perrin as a navigation note — the harbour entrance is a technical approach in low light. It was not a navigation note.
- **Foreshadowing:** The fauna crowding on the coastal strip. Too many animals, too close together, too quiet. Something is pressing them outward from the interior. A DC 12 Perception check on approach from the water picks up that the shoreline behaviour is wrong.
- **Clock:** The Grung run regular patrol loops through the reef east of Karath. The *Uncertainty* can observe for two to three days before a patrol boat closes to identification range. After that, someone in the Verdant Teeth knows a ship was anchored at Aruhe. The Grung response to a ship at Aruhe will not be routine.
- **Reversal Potential:** The harbour's single entrance is excellent for defence. It is identical for containment. If the Blight's outer pressure has reached the coastal strip, anything that enters the harbour may find the entrance harder to leave than to arrive through.

## Commission Objectives

Nona wants answers to five questions. She does not need them all at once.

| # | Question | What It Determines |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Is the harbour deep enough for a Tier 2 vessel at high tide? | Can the *Saltmarsh Trader* use it as a base |
| 2 | Is there freshwater on the island? | Settlement viability without resupply infrastructure |
| 3 | Is the entrance defensible by a small crew? | Whether five fighters can hold access during construction |
| 4 | Is there sign of current occupation or frequent visitation? | Whether anything has found the island in the years since the charts were drawn |
| 5 | What is the Grung patrol frequency and closest approach? | Whether the location can remain clandestine for the 18–24 months a shipyard takes |

Question 4 is the one that answers itself the moment the party lands.

## Grung Proximity

Aruhe's surrounding reefs are part of the Verdant Teeth patrol circuit. The Grung work the channel east of Karath and turn back at Aruhe's beach line — they do not land.

**Operational parameters:**
- Patrol boats are flat-bottomed, 2–3 crew, darts and boarding hooks
- Circuit frequency: every 36–48 hours past the eastern reef
- A ship anchored inside the harbour is not visible from the patrol route
- A ship anchored outside on the approach is visible within 48 hours
- Any light or fire on the island at night is visible from the Karath side

**What escalation looks like:** The Grung response to a ship at Aruhe is not a territorial challenge. It is retreat followed by a larger force. They do not want to know what is there. They want whatever is there to stay there.

## Daily Check-In — Signal Lantern Protocol

The *Uncertainty* carries paired signal lanterns: one rail-mounted aft, partner kept below. Range 30 miles, 25-word message limit.

Nona's format each evening:

> *[Condition check] — [Objective update] — [One question]*

The condition check is always first: she wants to know the party is alive before she asks about the island. If there is no reply by two hours past dusk, she waits one more evening. She has not told Perrin what acting looks like if contact stops.

**What Perrin sends back:** short, factual, in order. Nona is reading these in the dark. She is not looking for reassurance.

**If the lantern is destroyed or lost:** no contact. Nona will not know what happened. The party is operating without a net.

## Running the Survey

The survey is a three-phase sequence with escalating danger. The party can complete phases 1–2 without triggering the Blight. Phase 3 is the dungeon.

### Phase 1 — Approach and Harbour (Safe)

The harbour entrance is technical but navigable. Correct bearing (east-northeast on the old chart) is required; the gap is only visible on direct approach. A helmsman with Nona's route note makes the approach at DC 10. Without it: DC 14.

Inside the harbour: deep water (~40 ft at centre), limestone cliffs on three sides, a narrow shingle beach at the back. The cliffs are 150–200 ft. One stone staircase, cut into the cliff face above the beach, leads to the plateau.

| Task | Check | DC | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Navigate reef entrance, first pass | Dexterity (Water Vehicles) | 10 / 14 | With/without bearing note. Failure: grounded on outer reef, 2d6 hull HP damage, no structural damage. |
| Assess harbour depth | Intelligence (Navigation) | 13 | Success: reliable depth estimate. Failure: rough estimate, Nona will ask for confirmation. |
| Read the shoreline fauna behaviour | Wisdom (Perception) | 12 | Success: something is pressing the animals outward from the interior. This is identifiable as displacement, not natural crowding. |

### Phase 2 — Clifftop Survey (Dangerous, Not Blight)

The plateau above the harbour is overgrown. Old stonework — terrace walls and collapsed structures — is visible under the growth. The stonework has been dissolving at the base: the acid drainage from the interior has reached the terrace footings.

A PC who investigates the drainage channels detects the acid smell. This is the same mineral-sharp smell that Aruhe's eastern drainage produces at low tide. DC 12 Investigation to trace it inward.

The fauna on the coastal strip is present in abnormal density. At night, anything on the plateau will hear sounds from the interior — not animal noise, something underneath it. A DC 10 Perception check at night picks this up.

| Task | Check | DC | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Assess clifftop defensibility | Intelligence (any military background) | 10 | Clear yes — sight lines cover the approach, high ground holds the entrance. |
| Identify acid drainage source | Intelligence (Investigation) | 12 | Traces toward the interior. PC knows it is coming from inside the island, not from the sea. |
| Investigate terrace stonework | Wisdom (Survival) or Intelligence (Nature) | 13 | The terraces dissolved from below, not from neglect. Something in the interior is producing this. |
| Survey freshwater source | Wisdom (Survival) | 11 | A freshwater spring feeds the drainage channel. It runs year-round. |

### Phase 3 — Interior (Blight)

The interior is the dungeon. The Death Bloom is at the centre. The corruption has spread through every flooded ditch and terrace structure. Giant fauna that were not subsumed have been driven to the coastal strip.

A party that moves inland triggers Blight encounter content. Use [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Aruhe|Aruhe — DM Notes]] for full encounter structure. The interior reads visibly wrong before the first encounter: the web density is wrong, the ground cover is wrong, the colour of the water in the ditches is wrong.

**Do not require the party to clear the interior to complete the survey.** Phases 1–2 are enough for Nona's five questions. The interior is a choice — and a loaded one, because the site cannot be used as a settlement while the Blight holds the interior. Perrin will know this when he sees it.

## DM Notes

### Emotional Core

Nona sent Perrin to find a home for his people. He is going to find out why the island has been empty for forty years. The question the scene is actually asking is not *can this site be used* — it is *what does Perrin tell Nona, and what does he leave out.*

He can tell her the harbour is perfect, the entrance is defensible, the freshwater runs year-round, and the Grung stay away — all of which is true. He can leave out why the Grung stay away. That is a choice with a long tail.

### What Nona Will Do With an Honest Report

She will be quiet for a moment. Then she will ask how bad. Then she will ask if Perrin thinks it can be cleared. She is not asking the party to do it immediately. She is asking if it is possible. The Below gives the Tangle time; this site gives them permanence. She will wait for permanence.

She will not punish an honest assessment. She will punish a report that she later discovers was incomplete.

### The Route Note Warning

*Don't stay past dark.* The original Passage runner who wrote this note landed in the harbour, climbed to the plateau, noted the freshwater and the cliff approach, and then heard the interior at dusk. He left. He never elaborated. He also never went back.

If the party is on the plateau at dusk and makes a DC 10 Perception check, they hear it too. Describe it as: sound that should be wind moving through the old terrace walls, except the air is still.

## Triggers

- Perrin agrees to the commission at Nona's table
- Nona mentions "the work" she referenced earlier and Perrin follows up
- The party is departing Calveno and Perrin asks what Nona needs

## Blocks

- The party publicises the island's location or purpose
- Perrin engages the Grung patrols directly or leaves visible evidence
- The party overstays the patrol window and is identified

## Outcomes

| Outcome | Result |
|---|---|
| Survey completed, Blight reported honestly | Nona takes the full picture to the Tangle. The commission becomes a longer-arc project: clear the island, then build. |
| Survey completed, Blight omitted | Nona proceeds. The Tangle sends a construction team. They find out. Nona finds out Perrin knew. |
| Interior cleared as part of the survey | The site is viable immediately. Nona's standing in the Tangle shifts significantly. Perrin's standing with Nona shifts more. |
| Party driven off by Blight or Grung | Partial information. Nona gets enough to know the site is complicated. She asks Perrin to go back. |
| Grung discover the operation | Verdant Teeth knows someone was at Aruhe. Their response is unusual — not territorial but alarmed. The site is now a political problem, not just a logistical one. |
