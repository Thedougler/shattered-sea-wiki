# Osha's Slip

## Overview

A fishing village built on stilt-platforms over the southeastern mudflats of [[Calders-Tooth-Overview|Calder's Tooth]]. The villagers trap mud-crabs, spear river-eels, and maintain a rope-and-plank bridge network that shifts with wind and tide. There are no solid roads. Overland approach from Tidefall means crossing the mudflats, which is slow and unpleasant at any tide and impossible in armour at the wrong one.

The village is independent in the way small places far from the fort tend to be. The Crown's authority here is largely theoretical.

The same mudflats that feed Osha's Slip give large constrictors room to hunt. Villagers watch the eel channels for water moving against the tide, crab traps dragged sideways, and reed beds flattened in a single smooth line. Nobody calls every missing animal a monster, but people who work barefoot over tidal mud learn which pools are too quiet. ^[inferred]

## Districts & Landmarks

**Stilt Platforms** — Homes and work decks raised over the mud, connected by rope-and-plank bridges that shift with wind and tide.

**Outer Boardwalks** — Work routes into the reed-bed margins and tidal pools; children are kept off them after the light goes.

## People & Power

Osha's Slip is governed by the practical authority of the people who live and work the platforms. Crown authority is distant enough to be theoretical unless a patrol chooses to make a point.

## Trade & Services

The village can provide mud-crab, river-eel, local mudflat knowledge, and help crossing the platforms. It does not offer formal port services.

## Access & Approach

Overland approach from Tidefall means crossing mudflats that are slow at any tide and dangerous in armour at the wrong one. Boat approach depends on shallow channels and local knowledge.

## Fauna

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-crab|Giant crabs]] are the settlement's primary harvest and its baseline hazard — the mud-crab trapping industry depends on their presence, and the larger specimens in the deeper channels push back against trappers who do not respect the difference between a crab you can carry and a crab that carries you. The trap lines in the outer channels are sized accordingly.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-frog|Giant frogs]] occupy the reed-bed margins and the shallower tidal pools, hunting at dusk. They are not consistently dangerous to adults but are large enough to take a child or a wading dog, and their camouflage against mud and reed is good enough that the first sign of one is often the displacement it makes when it strikes. Villagers do not let young children work the outer boardwalks after the light goes.

The mudflats' apex predator is the [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-constrictor-snake|giant constrictor snake]] in the eel channels. Villagers watch for crab traps dragged sideways, reed beds flattened in a single smooth line, and water moving against the tide. A missing trap is ambiguous. A trap with the line still taut but nothing on the other end is not.
