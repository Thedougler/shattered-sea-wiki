# The Overland Track

## Overview

The Overland Track is the muddy southern switchback road connecting [[South-Harbour|South Harbour]] to [[Port-Tidefall|Port Tidefall]] across [[Calders-Tooth-Overview|Calder's Tooth]]. The crossing takes roughly a day and a half on foot or by mount, assuming the weather has not recently made a point.

Wagons break axles daily on corduroy logs laid over the worst mud. The road climbs through wet interior ridge country, then drops again toward the opposite coast, never quite deciding whether it wants to be a road or a tolerated wound in the island.

Locals call it the toll road because every legitimate crossing gives the Crown another chance to count bodies, cargo, and coin.

## Description

The road crosses wet interior ridge country, climbs through switchbacks, runs over corduroy logs laid through the worst mud, and drops toward the opposite coast. It is official enough for tolls and rough enough that carts, mounts, and tired crews all pay for the crossing in more than coin.

## Function

The Track is the dry connection between South Harbour and Port Tidefall. It moves bodies, cargo, tolls, official attention, and anyone trying to cross Calder's Tooth without going around by water.

## Access & Approach

The crossing takes roughly a day and a half on foot or by mount. Weather can slow or damage the route. Wagons break axles daily, and the switchbacks create predictable choke points for toll collection and predators.

## People & Power

The [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi Crown]] controls legitimate crossings by counting bodies, cargo, and coin. Track crews flag fresh wallow sign near the road when they find it, but the road remains a compromise with the ridge country rather than control over it.

## Notable Features

**Southern Switchbacks** — Muddy climbs where pack animals slow and wagons become predictable targets.

**Corduroy Mud Sections** — Log-laid stretches over the worst mud; useful until a wagon breaks an axle on them.

**Drainage Culverts** — Old stone catchment boxes and culverts that create warm, dark spider habitat.

## Flora

The Track crosses wet ridge sections, interior scrub, muddy road margins, and wallow ground. Specific plant life is not yet documented.

## Fauna

The road concentrates the kind of prey a giant axe beak can use. Pack animals slow in the mud, wagons stop where the switchback pinches, and the ridge above the road gives a running bird room to pace a caravan before dropping onto it. Drivers watch for three-toed tracks in fresh mud and loose stones skittering downhill before the bird itself appears. ^[inferred]

The ridge country the Track crosses is [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-axe-beak|giant axe beak]] territory — the open switchbacks give them room to pace a slowed caravan before committing, and pack animals stuck in the mud are exactly the kind of prey the birds prefer. The warning usually arrives before the bird does, but not by much.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-boar|Giant boars]] work the wetter ridge sections where the road runs close to interior scrub. They are not road predators specifically, but a sounder pushed by one disturbance will push through whatever is between them and open ground, which is sometimes a wagon and sometimes a toll-collector. The Crown's track crews flag fresh wallow sign near the road when they find it.

The drainage culverts and old stone catchment boxes along the lower switchbacks are [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wolf-spider|giant wolf spider]] habitat — warm, dark, and rarely disturbed by anything that checks before reaching in. Track maintenance crews carry poles for this reason.

## Hazards

The Overland Track's hazards are mud, broken axles, toll delays, switchback choke points, giant axe beak ambushes, boar sounders, and wolf spiders in drainage structures.
