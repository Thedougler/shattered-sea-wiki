# Maw Pearl Crisis

## Situation

The [[Pearl-of-Souls|Pearl of Souls]] is intact inside the *Red Lady* wreck at the Shelfworks eastern edge, below Umberlee's domain boundary. It broadcasts a soul-collection signal across the planar fissure, drawing entities from the Elemental Plane of Water into the mortal sea. Removing it does not close the fissure, but it removes the attractor.

---

## Current State

The fissure is geological and widening slowly. Under normal conditions it would leak elemental water and occasional passive drift. The Pearl changed the behaviour: the Leviathan followed the signal, a Ridgeback followed the signal, and the next incursion will do the same unless the Pearl is moved or silenced.

Auralis has shifted from passive watch to active containment. His old wards are failing under the Pearl's beacon and the elemental surge. Umberlee cannot retrieve the Pearl because the wreck sits below her jurisdiction, so she is keeping Delmar Fisk alive and pressured as her proxy.

## Narrative Devices

- **Loaded Gun:** The Fleet Intelligence Room aboard the *Red Lady* may reveal who arranged the Pearl theft and why the wreck ended up beyond Umberlee's reach.
- **Foreshadowing:** Warm water, dead-fish smell, colour-shifted light below 80 ft, missing Sentinels, and salvage crews abandoning lower tiers.
- **Clock:** Each world tick can bring another entity through or push the Leviathan higher into salvage zones.
- **Reversal Potential:** Returning the Pearl to Umberlee may solve the attractor while empowering a hostile goddess; keeping it may save leverage while sustaining the crisis.

## Key Facts

- The Pearl is the active attractor; the fissure is the underlying geology.
- The Leviathan and Ridgeback are confirmed directed incursions, not random sea monsters.
- The *Red Lady* wreck and Pearl sit below Umberlee's domain boundary.
- Auralis predates Umberlee's claim and guards the deeper machine around the fissure.
- Salvagers are dismantling upper exhaust/pressure architecture, not harmless ruins.
- Recovering the Pearl is the most consequential Season 1 action currently available.

## Consequences

- **Pearl recovered and removed:** directed incursions stop; fissure remains a passive geological hazard.
- **Pearl returned to Umberlee:** crisis quiets, but Umberlee regains a major soul artifact.
- **Pearl kept from Umberlee:** party gains leverage and danger; Umberlee escalates.
- **Pearl left in place:** more entities follow the signal; lower Shelfworks and Red Lady access deteriorate.
- **Auralis line fails:** upper tiers flood, the gold rush collapses, and the Antheri machine is tested openly.

## DM Notes

### Auralis and the Antheri Machine

Auralis is a manufactured demigod, not a natural deity: the last Antheri consciousness fused into an immortal framework engineered to survive the trench bottom. The visible Shelfworks are the newest layer of a bottom-up machine: vents, pressure bleeds, and safety architecture. Salvagers are pulling valves off a pressurized planar system.

Checks at the Shelfworks:
- Perception DC 12: temperature drops and light changes colour below 80 ft.
- Arcana/History DC 14: lower channels are too regular for habitation and too large for plumbing.
- Investigation DC 15: salvage damage concentrates on flow/pressure-regulation structures.
- Nature DC 13 + Sentinel records: identify safer approach windows to the *Red Lady*.

Party level 4-5 should work around or time the danger, not fight the Leviathan.

### Umberlee Pressure

Branca stays polite until Fisk stalls too long. The Dead Lady exists to remind him what is at stake, not to kill him. Umberlee needs Fisk functional; killing him risks losing the five captains and the Pearl permanently.

Waveservants will not discuss the theological problem: Umberlee does not own the deepest water in the known world. Pressing them produces subject changes, not answers.

### What Is in the Maw?

Current answer: the Pearl's signal is the attractor. The fissure remains after removal. The unknown is what the Antheri machine was built to contain before the Pearl amplified the problem.
