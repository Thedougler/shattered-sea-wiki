# Perrin Black-Jaw

*Statblock pending — create `Perrin-Black-Jaw-Statblock.md` from character sheet.*

## Summary

Perrin Black-Jaw — PBJ to the people who know him — is ten years old and squarely middle-aged by Rattkin reckoning. He spent those years building something: contacts first, then capital, then crew, then a ship. The ship was the point. It meant he could fish, which he loves, and provide for his family in the Tangle, which matters more to him than most things he would admit out loud. He is a sleazy operator in the way that a person with charm and no illusions about people tends to become: manipulation and bribery sit fine with him, blackmail has happened, and he does not lose much sleep over any of it. But he does not traffic people, and he does not mistreat those who cannot fight back. That line is not negotiable and never has been.

The ship is gone. Something came up from below and took it — the ocean around him went hot before the strike, the wood and metal burning to the touch, and then the hull was just gone. He survived because of the Cloak of the Manta Ray: a mildewy, well-worn family heirloom he earned through service to the Vestra, old enough that no one in the Run is entirely sure where it came from first. He does not know what destroyed the ship. He knows it was large and from somewhere deep, and he has pointed himself at vengeance with the focused energy of someone who has decided that fixing the problem is easier than sitting with the loss. He has not told his family. He is avoiding that conversation by staying in motion.

His patron found him after. He was not a believer before — the gods were not especially relevant to a Rattkin with fish to catch and a family to support. But something large and otherworldly spoke to him and apparently decided he was worth something, and that is harder to dismiss than theology. He is still working out what it means. For now it sits alongside the drum, the sword, and Clyde's Bestiary of Oceanic Creatures as things he carries that have weight he has not fully reckoned with.

He travels cloaked. The hood comes down eventually and people register that they are looking at an upright rat — black-headed, white-bodied with brown patches, lean and slight across the shoulder, the kind of frame that makes the longsword at his hip look genuinely too large for him. He carries it anyway, along with a bodhran on his back, which together read simultaneously like a diplomat and something that just climbed out of a ship's hold. He reads people the way he reads water: for what is underneath, for where the current is going, for what will be useful. He likes the Dry (non-Rattkin) more than he lets on. He does not trust any of them the way he trusts his Kin.

What Perrin wants, at the bottom of it, is to go home. To run a fishing operation, earn the respect of the elders, and eventually become one himself — the old Rattkin who teaches the young ones how to move through the Dry without getting killed. The vengeance is what he is telling himself gets him there. What he has not noticed yet is that the chase has become the point, and that the version of himself who comes out the other side of it may not be the version the Run hierarchy will recognise as one of their own.

## History

Perrin grew up in [[Warren]], the family network at the heart of [[Warren]] — the Rattkin community he still thinks of as home. He spent his formative years learning what the Run taught: read people, build capital, stay useful. He was good at it. Contacts became capital, capital became crew, crew became a ship. The ship was a family heirloom he had upgraded and crewed himself, and it represented everything he had built.

He ran a fishing operation. He loved fishing. He was also a sleazy operator — manipulation, bribery, blackmail where necessary — and he does not think of those things as sins. He drew one line: people are not cargo. Everything else has flex to it.

To earn the Cloak of the Manta Ray — an old, mildewy family heirloom, origin murky even to [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Vestra]] — he served [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Vestra]] directly. The cloak passed to him through that service.

The *Vestra* went down on an ordinary fishing day. The water around the hull began to bubble. He touched the rail and burned his hand. Then something enormous hit from below — [[Leviathan]], a displaced apex predator from the [[Elemental-Plane-of-Water|Elemental Plane of Water]] that entered the mortal ocean through the fissure at the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]. The hull was gone. The Cloak of the Manta Ray kept him alive. He surfaced alone. He assumed the crew was dead.

[[Auralis]] found him in the aftermath — the last Antheri, transformed into a demigod guardian bound to the Maw, woken by the same incursion that sank the *Vestra*. Perrin was not a believer before; he is starting to become one now, reluctantly. He has not told his family any of it. He is staying in motion because the alternative is the conversation he is not ready for.

The first leg of that motion: after the Leviathan shipwrecked him on [[Keth-Naar]], he limped along the beach and stowed away in the cargo hold of [[Saltwright]], a shrimping vessel captained by [[Beaumont-Sel]]. He sheltered in a secluded corner near a gap in the hull panels above the waterline, fresh air and a sliver of sky. [[Beaumont-Sel]]'s capuchin Bisou nearly caught him. He made no contact with the crew. The ship later took on two passengers found floating on driftwood after the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Red-Lady|Red Lady]] wreck — [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]] and [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss]], sole survivors of Umberlee's storm. Perrin was in the hold. He did not see them board and does not know who they are.

## Personality & Drives

- **Travels cloaked.** What registers first is a lean, slight figure in an oversized olive-drab hood — shortest in any group by a visible margin, longsword at the hip that looks too large for him. Then the hood comes down and the rat is visible. The surprise is deliberate.
- **Reads people like water.** He looks for what is underneath, where the current is going, what will be useful. It is not cruel — it is just how he processes the world.
- **Transactional by nature.** People who can do nothing for him and do their job fine get ignored. People who affect him get managed. He does not mistreat anyone who cannot fight back — he just stops paying attention to them.
- **Genuinely warm toward the Dry** (non-Rattkin), more than he lets on. Trust at the deep level is reserved for Kin.
- **Prey response when frightened.** Might squeak. Might cower. Finds the nearest small dark space. Is embarrassed about it afterward. [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss]] — a large predatory bird — is going to complicate this.
- **No single red button.** Fish, coin, goodwill, vengeance all pull at him, but nothing would make him abandon his family without thought. He would be torn. He would feel it. He would not just go.
- **Firm moral absolute:** He will not traffic people. Cultural prohibition and personal conviction both. Everything else has flex.
- **Long game:** Go home. Run the operation. Earn the elders' respect. Become one. What he has not yet seen is that the vengeance may cost him the version of himself the Run hierarchy would accept.

## Active Situations

- **The ship** — gone. A family heirloom he had upgraded and crewed. He has not told [[Warren]] the full scale of the loss. He is trying to fix it before the conversation happens.
- **[[Leviathan]]** — destroyed the *Vestra*. Large, heat-ambient, from the [[Elemental-Plane-of-Water|Elemental Plane of Water]] via the Maw fissure. Perrin has been studying it in Clyde's Bestiary since the sinking. He will recognise the Metal Fever tell before anyone else does.
- **[[Auralis]]** — his patron. Found him in the water after the *Vestra* went down. The last Antheri; guardian of the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]; woke when the Leviathan entered. Perrin is working out what the relationship means.
- **His family** — in [[Warren]], governing the Calveno Run as the Tangle. He has gone quiet since the sinking. They know he is alive. They do not know the rest. When this conversation happens, it will happen in front of the people who run the Passage's anchor point.
- **The crew** — he assumed they all died. A surviving crewmember is possible. Names TBD.
- **Dravosi standing bounty** — species-level, rarely enforced, always present. Background pressure.
