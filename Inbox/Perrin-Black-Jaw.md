# Perrin Black-Jaw

## Overview

**Perrin Black-Jaw** — PBJ to the people who know him — is a Rattkin sailor, negotiator, and the only known survivor of whatever destroyed the *Vestra* three days before the campaign begins. He is ten years old, squarely middle-aged by Rattkin reckoning, and by most measures he had built exactly the life he wanted: the ship, the fishing operation, the family supplied and respected. Then something came up from below the waterline on what had been an ordinary day, and now he is hiding in the hold of [[Saltwright|the *Saltwright*]] with a family heirloom on his back and a conversation he is not ready to have.

He is a sleazy operator in the way a charming person with no illusions about people tends to become: manipulation, bribery, and leverage are tools he reaches for without much apology. He draws one line, firm and old: people are not cargo. Everything else has flex to it.

---

## Appearance

He travels with the hood up. What registers first is a cloaked figure barely three feet tall, moving with the unhurried confidence of someone who has already noted all the exits. The hood is olive-drab and genuinely large on him — it drapes past his shoulders in a way that reads less like fashion and more like deliberate cover. Then it comes down.

Perrin is a fancy rat Rattkin — black head, clean white body, a colouring people remember. He is lean and slight, narrow across the shoulder, and small even by Rattkin standards; in any lineup he is the shortest person present by a significant margin. The longsword at his hip looks genuinely outsized on him. He carries it anyway. Add the bodhran drum across his back and the whole impression lands somewhere between Robin Hood and something that just climbed out of a ship's hold — which is more or less accurate.

He compensates for the frame with personality, and has done so for as long as he can remember.

## Backstory

Perrin grew up in the Tangle — the family network at the heart of [[Warren]], the only fixed Rattkin settlement in the [[Shattered-Sea|Shattered Sea]], built beneath the city of [[content/shattered-sea/places/calveno/index]]. He was raised on the practical lessons of the Run: read people, build capital, stay useful, know which doors to use and which ledgers to stay out of. [[Dravosi-Crown|The Dravosi Crown]] keeps standing bounties on Rattkin that are rarely enforced and always present. He learned early what that background pressure means for how you move through the world.

He was good at the Run's lessons. Contacts became capital, capital became crew, crew became a ship. The ship had belonged to his grandfather — [[Nona-Black-Jaw|Nona Black-Jaw's]] late husband built and captained her, then left her to the family when he died. When the *Vestra* passed to Perrin, it came with the weight of that inheritance and the responsibility of the route. He upgraded her, added gun mounts, crewed her himself, and ran a fishing operation.

He genuinely loved the fishing. That tends to surprise people who meet the diplomat first, but it was always the point. The contacts and the capital were how you got to the part where you could drop a line into deep water and not need to be anywhere.

## The Vestra

The *Vestra* was a small two-masted brig, older than most [[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/index|Midchain]] traffic, her topsides painted a deep green that had been reapplied so many times it no longer matched from plank to plank. A rat figurehead at the prow with one hand extended, palm up. Compact, practical, built for a small crew travelling light. She was not remarkable. She was his.

She went down three days before the campaign begins, on what had been an ordinary fishing day. The water around the hull began to bubble. Perrin touched the rail and the wood burned his hand. Then something enormous struck from below — not a reef, not a storm, something deliberate — and the hull was simply gone. The *Vestra* went down too fast for anyone aboard to understand what was happening.

The Cloak of the Manta Ray kept him alive. He surfaced alone among wreckage. He assumed his crew was dead.

He hasn't told his family the full extent of what happened. They know he is alive. They don't know the ship is gone. The *Vestra* was not just a vessel; she was the last thing his grandfather left behind, and losing her is not only a financial disaster. Perrin is staying in motion because the alternative is that conversation, and he is not ready for it yet.

## Keth-Naar and the Saltwright

The Cloak brought him to the surface. He washed up on the beach of [[Keth-Naar|Keth-Naar]], and what he woke to — besides the sand and the shock — was the unfamiliar weight of a blade in his hand and the echo of something vast and distant in his head.

Perrin was not a believer before. The gods were not especially relevant to a Rattkin with fish to catch and a family to support. But something large and otherworldly found him in the water after the sinking and apparently decided he was worth speaking to. He is still working out what that means. For now it sits alongside the drum and the bestiary and the cloak as things he carries that have weight he hasn't fully reckoned.

He found [[Saltwright|the *Saltwright*]] in the [[Keth-Naar]] harbour and tucked himself into the hold before anyone asked questions. He has made no contact with the crew. The captain doesn't know he is aboard.

## Character

Perrin reads people the way he reads water — for what's underneath, where the current is moving, what will be useful. People who affect him get managed. People who can't do anything for him and do their job fine get left alone. He doesn't mistreat anyone who can't fight back. He just stops paying attention to them.

He is genuinely warm toward the Dry — non-Rattkin — more than he lets on. Trust at the deep level is reserved for Kin. Everyone else gets charm and read, which is not nothing, and sometimes becomes something more over time.

His one absolute is old and firm: he will not traffic people. Cultural prohibition and personal conviction both. That line doesn't flex. Everything else does.

When genuinely frightened — and the Dry has enough in it to frighten a Rattkin who knows what to look for — his prey response comes out. He might squeak. He might cower. He finds the nearest small dark space. He is embarrassed about it afterward. A large predatory bird in the party is going to complicate this, and he already knows it.

He is not a religious man by nature, or was not. The entity that spoke to him after the sinking has complicated that. He is still figuring out what it means that a god apparently thinks he matters.

---

## The Long Game

What Perrin wants, at the bottom of it, is to go home. Run the fishing operation. Earn the respect of the elders. Become one — the old Rattkin who teaches the young ones how to move through the Dry without getting killed. The vengeance against whatever destroyed the *Vestra* is what he tells himself gets him there.

What he hasn't noticed yet is that the chase has become the point, and the version of himself who comes out the other side may not be the version the Run hierarchy will recognise as one of their own.

## Known Possessions

- **Cloak of the Manta Ray** — old, mildewy, a Black-Jaw family heirloom. It kept him alive when the ship went down.
- **Longsword** — carried at the hip; not his original tool of trade.
- **Bodhran drum** — carried across the back.
- **Shield** — worn alongside the sword.
- *[[Clydes-Bestiary-of-Oceanic-Creatures|Clyde's Bestiary of Oceanic Creatures]]* — heavily annotated since the sinking. He is learning everything he can about what lives below the waterline.

## Key Relationships

**[[Nona-Black-Jaw|Nona Black-Jaw]]** is his grandmother, a Rattkin matriarch and senior elder of the Tangle. She is the person he is not ready to call. The *Vestra* was her late husband's ship. Perrin knows exactly what losing it means in terms of family standing and family grief, and he is trying to fix the problem before he has to explain the loss.

**The *Vestra*** was his ship, his grandfather's inheritance, and his whole working life in a single hull. Gone.

**[[Saltwright|The *Saltwright*]]** is where he is now, in the hold, hidden, listening to the ship work around him.

## Session History

### Session 01 — The Saltwright Boarding

Perrin cast Minor Illusion across the hold doorway after [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss|Crissdalynn]] dragged Cap'n Gorgeous's body back through it — the sound of the musket shot had raised the alarm, but the illusion meant the two Dravosi deckhands came down blind. Both were killed in the hold without a shout reaching the deck.

On the weather deck, Perrin and [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] came up through a gap in the decking at a second angle while Crissdalynn took the two nearest Dravosi prone. The deck fight was over quickly.

On the gangplank he was caught in the Dravosi Alchemist's gas canister alongside Jean-Claude. Both resisted. He is unaware that Jean-Claude recognized the compound's origin. See [[Session-01-Recap]].

### Session 03 — The Vision, the Shark, and Nona

On night watch, Perrin's eyes went white at the rail. He experienced an extended vision: the bottom of the sea, worked stone, bioluminescent life forms, the sensation of being a [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/sea-life/Whip-Shark|whip shark]] — sixty feet of muscle and instinct — diving to hunt a two-hundred-foot whale. The hunt succeeded. At the end, one word burned into his mind from somewhere below the stone floor: *"Grow."* He woke as his knees finished buckling.

He fell from the rigging shortly after (dex check, rolled 1). Jean-Claude's tongue caught him; [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Crissdalynn-Khinriss|Crissdalynn]] caught them both. He was the one who identified the shark and called the fight — bardic inspiration to Delmar, Jean-Claude, and Crissdalynn before the first round. In combat he held Mirror Image in the rigging and used Cutting Words to reduce the shark's tail attack against Jean-Claude (still hit, but Jean-Claude held on). Healing Word brought Jean-Claude back up mid-fight.

At sea he worked the rigging with [[content/shattered-sea/characters/crew/Old-Faas|Old Faas]] (acrobatics 16; Faas was impressed). Faas told stories about running cargo with Rook when he was just a pirate. Some of it was almost certainly bullshit.

In Calveno, Perrin ran to [[content/shattered-sea/characters/npcs/Nona-Black-Jaw|Nona]]'s kitchen in Le Paludi and told her the Vestra is gone. Nona said she didn't care about the ship. Perrin gave her the full account — the water bubbling, the heat, the lightning, the speed. Nona has a favor to ask. She gave him a sending stone before he left. [[content/shattered-sea/characters/minor/Enzo|Enzo]] and [[content/shattered-sea/characters/minor/Ruk|Ruk]] watched without warmth.

When [[content/shattered-sea/characters/npcs/Master-Kyzil|Master Kyzil]] dive-bombed Crissdalynn in the street, Perrin had arrived in time to see it and chose a barrel. He introduced himself from inside it, by name only, with no extended hand — one finger-touch when Kyzil knelt down and offered one.

## Public Standing

Perrin's reputation is small outside Rattkin circles, and inside those circles it's complicated. He was a competent operator with a good family name and a working ship. He still has the family name. He is also a man hiding in a cargo hold aboard someone else's boat, carrying the wreck of his grandfather's legacy and a new debt to something vast and divine that he didn't ask for.

The version of Perrin Black-Jaw who comes out of what is coming will be a different thing than the one who went into the water over the Maw. He doesn't yet know how different.
