## Description

A flat brass-and-darkwood case, roughly the size of a ledger, fitted across its face with rows of small buttons. Each button is stamped with a tiny symbol and a Common glyph. Press one and the box produces a clear, slightly tinny tone — not quite a voice, not quite a bell — that resolves unmistakably into a word or short phrase any sailor would recognize.

The mechanism inside is a dense coil of tuned reeds and a small bellows driven by a winding key on the side. No magic. It runs until the spring runs down, which takes about four hours of active use, and is rewound with thirty seconds of work.

Most Phrase Boxes on the market are built to the Maritime Standard layout — a format that emerged from Midchain gnome workshops and became the default because the alternative was every box being different. Customized variants exist: negotiating editions, fishing-crew editions, one famously misguided tavern edition that replaced every navigation command with a drink order. The Maritime Standard is what a Kenku working a ship will reach for.

---

## Mechanics

The Phrase Box is a mundane clockwork tool. It requires no attunement, no spell slots, and no magical ability to operate. Any creature can press a button.

**Range.** The tones are audible to 60 feet in calm conditions; 30 feet in moderate noise such as a working deck; difficult to hear at all in high winds or active combat.

**Clarity.** Any creature that understands Common and can hear the tone understands its meaning without a check. The tones are unambiguous by design.

**Rewinding.** The Phrase Box requires rewinding after approximately 4 hours of active use (roughly 200 button presses). Rewinding takes a bonus action.

**Durability.** The mechanism is robust. Saltwater immersion requires 1 hour of drying and cleaning before it functions again reliably. Dropping it from height into hard stone has a 1-in-6 chance of jamming a button cluster until a tinker spends 10 minutes on it.

## Notes

The Phrase Box does not replace language — it augments the absence of it. A Kenku using one can communicate operational intent precisely and quickly; anything more complex still requires pantomime, written notes, or the mimicry a Kenku brings to every interaction regardless.

Experienced Phrase Box users develop a rhythm with the buttons fast enough to hold a genuine conversation at the pace of slow speech, provided the subject stays within the layout's vocabulary. Kenku working long runs on the same crew often develop shorthand with crewmates — a single button press paired with a look meaning something the button technically doesn't say.

The "Write it down" button sees heavy use.

Some veteran Kenku carry a second, personal box built to a custom layout that reflects the specifics of their trade or regular crew — the Maritime Standard handles the ship, the personal box handles the rest.

## In the Campaign

Phrase Boxes are common across [[content/shattered-sea/lore/Demographics|Midchain free ports and Central Strait docks]] wherever [[Kenku]] work port courier and runner roles. Most chandleries in [[Kalowe|Kalowe]] and [[Calveno|Calveno]] stock them alongside rope and lamp oil. They are unremarkable enough that a crew would notice their absence before they'd notice their presence.

A used Phrase Box often shows wear in clusters — the emergency buttons bright from testing, the navigation cluster worn from a decade of deckhands learning the layout by feel in the dark. A box with the "Man overboard" button polished smooth from use tells its own kind of story.
