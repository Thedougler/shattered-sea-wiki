# The Ponte Bassa

![[Oleandro-Fuschi.webp|Oleandro Fuschi, proprietor of the Ponte Bassa]]

## Overview

The Ponte Bassa is a canal-side tavern built into the bridge-level masonry at the main Calveno crossing, where the arch of the three-tier bridge comes down to the waterline. It is the kind of place people go to eat cheaply in public view of the canal, which in Calveno means in public view of everyone.

## Description

The low ceiling on the canal side is where the bridge arch comes down; the back half of the room has clearance. Seating is open to the water — anyone on a passing boat can see who is eating and with whom, which most Calveno regulars treat as a feature rather than a flaw. The kitchen smells of fish stock and cheap wine and something always frying. Upstairs: beds, basic, available by the night.

## People & Operations

**Proprietor: [[Oleandro-Fuschi|Oleandro Fuschi]]** — Wide-shouldered, green-grey, soft-spoken in the way that makes people unsure whether they should be louder. Keeps a mental ledger of every vessel that has moored at the bridge canal in twenty years; he has never been caught wrong about one. He does not volunteer this information unprompted. If you're looking for someone who came through Calveno recently, Oleandro either knows or knows who would.

## Access & Security

Ground floor open to the canal and the street. No door on the water side between midday and last light — boats tie up at the ring bolts and orders are taken from the waterline. The private rooms upstairs require asking Oleandro directly.
