# Port Tidefall
![[1778025103752~2 1.png]]
![[Port-Tidefall.webp|Fortified cliffside port city built in tiers above a busy harbor]]

## Overview

Port Tidefall is carved directly into the cliff face of [[content/shattered-sea/places/calders-tooth/index|Calder's Tooth]] in three tiers: Dockfront at the waterline, the Shelf mid-cliff, and [[Fort-Crestwall|Fort Crestwall]] at the crown with cannon pointed seaward. The harbour mouth is narrow, the inspection pier extends to starboard before any ship reaches the dock, and three nooses hang from the gate arch above the entrance — empty, freshly painted black. Nobody in port remembers when they went up. Every ship entering the western Strait is expected to stop, answer questions, and produce the right paper in the right order. Most do.

This is where the Crown's authority is loudest, which also makes it where that authority is most negotiable. The Harbourmaster's Office sells charts and vessel ratings. The inspection process sells delay. The taverns on the Shelf sell the composure to discuss paperwork without raising your voice near an officer. Paper can save you here, and paper can sink you, and paper can become a different kind of problem if the clerk on duty has time and initiative. Keep the cargo story short and the copies dry.

## Districts & Landmarks

**The Dockfront** sits at the waterline, where arrival becomes procedure. The [[Inspection-Pier|Inspection Pier]] catches ships before they reach the docks proper, and the [[Harbourmasters-Office|Harbourmaster's Office]] turns that delay into ratings, fees, impound paperwork, and official charts. Crown patrols are constant here. The pace is fast and the paperwork is immediate.

**The Shelf** is the mid-cliff commercial district, where the port's paperwork becomes conversation. Trade licensing, navigational instruments, Crown petitions, officer business, and side deals all pass through this tier. The Dravosi Admiralty has offices here. Most of what passes for negotiation in Tidefall happens on the Shelf.

**[[Fort-Crestwall|Fort Crestwall]]** at the crown is pass-only: barracks, command post, armoury, court, and cannon battery above the harbour approach. What goes on inside it is not discussed on the Shelf, and the people on the Shelf who would normally know have been finding that fact mildly concerning for several weeks.

## People & Power

The [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi Crown]] controls the port through inspection, garrison presence, permits, and the practical force of Fort Crestwall. The [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] operates inside that system where contracts and salvage work need commercial leverage as much as legal standing.

## Trade & Services

Naval gear, weapons, and Dravosi charts are abundant. Tessarine goods and arcane specialist items are scarce and priced accordingly. The [[Harbourmasters-Office|Harbourmaster's Office]] handles charts and commission postings, the [[Tidefall-Chandlery|Tidefall Chandlery]] covers provisions, gear, and common equipment, and the [[Shelf-Instrument-Dealer|Shelf Instrument Dealer]] carries navigational tools. Contract work and salvage opportunities move through the [[Tessarine-Trade-House|Tessarine Trade House]] when the Concordat wants the terms to be enforceable under Crown law.

Unsanctioned cargo moves through intermediaries. The formal scrutiny is concentrated on the western dockface; [[South-Harbour|South Harbour]] on the eastern side handles what the western ledgers prefer not to see.

## Access & Approach

The harbour mouth is narrow, and incoming vessels are expected to stop at the [[Inspection-Pier|Inspection Pier]] before reaching the dock proper. Business below the Shelf is public and procedural; business above the Shelf depends on appointments, passes, rank, or someone willing to make the paperwork move.
