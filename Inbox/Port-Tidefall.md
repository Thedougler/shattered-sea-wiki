# Port Tidefall — Dockfront Opportunities

## Situation

Port Tidefall is overfull, resentful, and full of small useful doors. The party can find ship options, impound schedules, a buried cache, a Tessarine salvage job, Waveservant pressure on Delmar and Stripes, and personal/social hooks without treating each building as a separate situation.

---

## Current State

Garrison capacity is strained. Rook's recent impound behavior has left resentment in dockfront bars. Tessarine salvage work has produced an unspoken diving accident. Grung raid rumours are making Felix Aho's stall less safe. Delmar's name, Perrin's silhouette, Jean-Claude's identity, and Stripes' divine role all have local pressure points.

## Key Opportunities

### Anchor & Line

- Front-room intel: garrison over capacity; Rook impounded a catch on a technicality; Tessarine Trade House had a quiet diving accident.
- Perrin can shelter in the canvas-separated back section; Crown patrols only check the front by informal six-year arrangement.
- Vrtek has pocket contents from a Crown coat: four coins, brass button, candle stub, carved fish, Dravosi rating stamp, and a sealed note he cannot read.
- Sosse tells Perrin after the third drink that Vrtek has something official that does not belong to him.
- Jean-Claude hook: six dockfront labourers drink here from sixth bell; two know Maret is held in the Trade House back room. DC 11 Persuasion/Insight gets them talking.

### Hook & Ledger

- Impound clerk is selling tomorrow's berth adjustments and release notes to cover gambling debt.
- DC 13 Insight spots him before he names a price; DC 13 Perception notices three tables listening without looking.
- Card alcove hosts freight factors and labour middlemen. DC 11 Persuasion joins the next negotiation; DC 16 Insight identifies real authority.
- Locked back drawer: copied impound schedule, unpaid Fort Crestwall sergeant marker, half-finished list of tomorrow's secondary inspections.

### Inspection Pier Cache

- Vrtek's note identifies a cache: 3rd piling from harbour mouth, south face, 2 ft below mussel line at spring low.
- Low-tide window: about 2 hours starting at third bell.
- Contents: 740 gp old Dravosi gold, 60 gp Tessarine silver, 40 gp jade fish, and an oilskin letter proving a Crown inspector took bribes 15 years ago.
- Rook periodically checks the waterline. Perrin's silhouette matches a nearby bounty notice; he should coordinate from outside or approach from water side.
- DC sequence: Pier 4 Investigation 11; mussel line Nature/Survival 10; tide board automatic; approach Stealth 13; quiet Stealth 12; clay seal Athletics 12; chest free Athletics 14; exit Athletics/Stealth 13/12.

### Harbourmaster Board

- Greyteeth Runner for sale: 1,400 gp.
- Lasting Insult clears impound for 374 gp plus about 3 gp/day accrual.
- Salvor's Claim: 40% of about 800 gp from reef wreck; prior team failed; lapses in 10 days.
- Handwritten "the Runner, Kalowe" card is below official notices and will be pulled once noticed.
- Charts: Crown Islands/Central Strait 25 gp; Midchain partial survey 75 gp if asking chart clerk specifically.

### Lasting Insult

- Third berth in South Harbour Impound; hull sound, serviceable.
- Legal acquisition: pay 374 gp plus accrual.
- Night acquisition: single chain padlock, DC 18 Thieves' Tools; only likely witness is the clerk if awake.
- Clerk accepts full payment, Admiral's commission, or Harbourmaster's writ. No partial payment, barter, charm, or corruption. The ledger is what he protects.

### Tessarine Trade House

- Maret, human dockworker and sole survivor of previous dive, is held in back office on Concordat debt-recovery hold.
- He is not frightened of the creature; he is frightened of going back alone. DC 12 Insight.
- Salvage terms: 40% recovered cargo, about 320 gp if manifest accurate; 10-day deadline; 30-40 ft depth; hold at 35 ft; one large unknown creature using wreck as den; cargo includes diplomatic pouches, one strongbox, five luxury crates.
- Jean-Claude likely acts if he understands a dockworker is being held under debt law.

### Tidefall Chandlery

- Garrison-sanctioned stock. Common stock open sale; uncommon magic requires asking; rare depends on private seller.
- Scarce: Tessarine goods, inland produce, arcane specialist items. Abundant: naval gear, Crown-standard weapons.
- Key prices: pistol 250 gp; musket 500 gp; powder horn 35 gp; bullets x10 3 gp; antitoxin 50 gp; alchemist's fire 50 gp; thieves' tools 25 gp; navigator's tools 25 gp; forgery kit 15 gp; paired Sending Stones 300 gp.
- Magic: healing potion 50 gp; water breathing 100 gp; spell scroll cantrip 30 gp; 1st level 50 gp; greater healing 150 gp; Bag of Holding/Cloak of the Manta Ray/Eyes/Helm/+1 weapon 500 gp; Mariner's Armor 600 gp.

### Waveservant Shrine

- Senior Waveservant knows Delmar is in port and his 15 gp tithe appointment is overdue.
- Senior knows Stripes by what he is, not just who he is; cannot be rushed or deflected.
- Recent offering hook: compass needle wrapped in oilcloth, left within two days, petitioner unknown.

### Maggie Porcelain Pots

- Maggie does not know Delmar is married. She heard his fleet went down and did not reach out; no bad blood, she just does not chase.
- Her pottery business can organically cross the party's path across islands; she always returns home.

## Consequences

- **Party leans into dockfront:** gets ship options, cash, salvage, and intelligence but creates witnesses.
- **Jean-Claude notices coercion:** Maret's debt hold or labour deals become immediate moral pressure.
- **Delmar delays Waveservants:** polite shrine pressure becomes tied to the broader Pearl crisis.
- **Perrin uses Anchor refuge:** safe for now, but Vrtek's note and bounty silhouette pull him back into risk.
