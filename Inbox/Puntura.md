# Puntura

| | |
|---|---|
| **Type** | Weapon (shortsword) |
| **Rarity** | Uncommon |
| **Attunement** | No |

The blade is the forward third of a whip shark's secondary barb — fourteen inches of calcified spine ground to a point and set into an ironwood grip bound with tarred cord. The barb has yellowed to old bone. Along the spine, in shallow relief, are the natural ridges of the barb's structure. They look like decoration. They are not.

---

## Properties

**[HB]** You gain a **+1 bonus** to attack and damage rolls made with this weapon.

**[HB] Venom Trace (1/day).** As a bonus action, you draw on the barb's residual venom. Until the start of your next turn, the first creature you hit with this weapon takes an additional **1d4 poison damage** and must succeed on a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw** or be **Poisoned** until the end of its next turn.

> Poisoned: disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks.

---

## Balance Notes

*For DM reference. Do not share.*

- +1 shortsword is uncommon baseline.
- Venom Trace costs a bonus action, limits to first hit, and is once per day — comparable to a Dagger of Venom (rare, 2d10, DC 15) but strictly weaker. Sits correctly at uncommon.
- DC 13 Con save at level 4 is meaningful against most humanoid enemies. Monsters with high Con will resist it regularly — the effect won't trivialize encounters.
- Poisoned for 1 turn (not a full minute) keeps the debuff decisive without being dominant.
- No attunement — Delmar can hold this without giving up an attunement slot.

## Cost

**Commission: 150 gp** (party provides the barb; Catarina charges labor only).

Catarina prices the labor at 150 gp — 3–5 days of precision bladework, plus the ironwood grip, tarred cord, and finish work. She waives the material premium because the grinding byproduct alone is worth 20–30 gp in venom-precursor compounds, and she keeps it. She will not say this unless pressed.

> [!mechanic]
> DC 15 Persuasion: 120 gp — she accepts the fraction without comment; she was keeping the byproduct regardless. Offer to let her examine the remaining whip shark eggs before the party sells them: 100 gp, eggs returned undamaged within two hours.

---

## History

The barb recovered from the sea off Calveno is 4 ft of intact specimen — Catarina DaVirelli's assessment confirmed it viable for bladework. She needs 3–5 days to finish the commission: the calcified structure is dense and will crack if rushed. The grip tape, ironwood pommel, and finish are her craft; the blade is the creature's.

The name is hers. Calveno dialect: *puntura* — a sting, a puncture wound, the moment of contact.
