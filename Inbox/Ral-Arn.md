# Ral-Arn

> *The island where memory is kept against salt and forgetting.*

The record island of the Sunken Crown — larger than Dath-Mar or [[Meth-Var]], visited when a question must be answered by memory rather than appetite. The oldest family lines maintain carved marks here: tide accounts, storm signs, records of prior seasons. Ral-Arn does not replace oral history. It anchors it. A mark here is only useful if someone living can read the story around it.

The records survive because people tend them, not because the island is gentle. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-lizard|Giant lizards]] bask across the stone paths, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wolf-spider|giant wolf spiders]] take abandoned shelters, and [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/swarm-of-ravens|raven swarms]] gather where offerings or storm-killed fish are left too long. A visitor who cannot read the local marks can still understand the fresh claw scores.

## Geography

Ral-Arn is larger than Dath-Mar or [[Meth-Var]], with exposed carved record surfaces, stone path networks, abandoned shelters, and offering places where storm-killed fish may gather scavengers.

## Roads & Routes

Visitors move by stone path networks across exposed record surfaces. Some panels may require waiting for animals to clear, and unused shelters must be checked before trust.

## People & Power

Ral-Arn belongs to Keth-Naar memory practice. Elders and family lines preserve the marks, but the marks only matter because living people know how to read the stories around them.

## Trade & Resources

Ral-Arn does not trade in ordinary goods. Its resource is inherited guidance: records of past seasons, blessing failures, storm signs, and what restored balance before.

## Flora

No distinct flora is documented beyond the exposed stone, offering places, and shelter margins.

## Fauna

The stone path network across the carved record surfaces is [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-lizard|giant lizard]] territory — the flat, exposed rock holds heat well into the evening, and the lizard population is large enough that visitors to the record panels routinely wait for an animal to clear a section before working it. They do not attack unless cornered, but they are large enough to cause serious problems if surprised on a narrow path above the waterline.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-wolf-spider|Giant wolf spiders]] have taken the abandoned shelters — the structures that Keth-Naar families no longer maintain are warm, dark, and undisturbed, which suits wolf spiders precisely. Any shelter that has gone unvisited for more than a season should be checked before it is used. The shelters that have been unvisited for years are checked with a pole.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/swarm-of-ravens|Swarms of ravens]] gather wherever offerings or storm-killed fish have been left too long. The birds are aggressive in numbers and have learned that arrival of people on the island correlates with food. They do not distinguish between offerings, stored provisions, and things left in the open during a work break.

## Hazards

Giant lizards on warm paths, giant wolf spiders in abandoned shelters, raven swarms around offerings, and the risk of reading marks without the story that gives them meaning.
