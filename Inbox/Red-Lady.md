# The *Red Lady*

![[Red-Lady.webp]]
> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A two-masted brigantine rides low in the water, her hull stained a deep, even red from waterline to rail. Seven cannon ports line each side beneath the gun deck, and swivel mounts sit at bow and stern. At the prow, a pale wooden figurehead of a cloaked woman leans forward with one hand open at her side. The ship is long, clean-lined, and built with the hard, practical look of a vessel meant to chase, fight, and keep going.

---

## Overview

A two-masted brigantine, built by the [[Chain-Council]], 96 feet on the waterline, privateer-rigged. The hull ran dark red from waterline to rail — not painted, stained deep into the wood, uniform and deliberate. The figurehead was a cloaked woman carved in pale wood, eyes forward, one hand open at her side. Two gun decks, seven cannon ports per side, swivel mounts fore and aft.

Built for extended independent operations: fast enough to disengage, armed enough to press, fitted for months without port resupply. She was in prime condition when she sank.

---

## Crew and Command

[[Rael-Corven|Rael Corven]] served as flag captain. [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Delmar-Fisk|Delmar Fisk]] commanded as Admiral. [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss|Crissdalynn Khinriss]] served as navigator and crow for nearly two years.

Every new hand on the *Red Lady* went through Rael's training program before touching a line in action. He knew every name aboard and built each sailor's duties around their specific capabilities. The ship's reputation as the best-run vessel in the fleet was not incidental — it was a direct result of how he ran it. He worked closely with any natural spellcasters in the fleet alongside his own crew, helping them develop what they had. Several of the *Red Lady*'s more useful fixtures — a self-clearing galley table, a navigation stool that moved with the ship's pitch — were his own quiet craftsmanship.

---

## The Sinking

Umberlee's retribution came as fog — thick enough to swallow the water from the deck, surrounding all five ships at once. A rogue trench opened in the fleet's path and drove them 80 feet down. The walls closed and swallowed the fleet whole.

Crissdalynn dove from the crow's nest, grabbed Fisk, and flew them clear. They heard Umberlee's laughter on the wind, then her rage, as the ships went under. The *Red Lady* went down with the [[content/shattered-sea/items/Pearl-of-Souls|Pearl of Souls]] still aboard.

The five ships lost: *Red Lady*, *[[Narrow|The Narrow]]*, *[[Heft]]*, *[[Fernen]]*, *[[Loud-Argument|Loud Argument]]*. Two survivors.

## Layout

Three decks, organized for long independent operations.

**Weather Deck** — Wheel, compass, signal station with flags and lamps for fleet coordination. Captain's quarters and a fleet intelligence room at the stern. Open working space forward around the mainmast.

**Cannon Deck** — Seven guns per side, with a central passage kept clear for powder runners and damage control. Navigator's station in a chart room aft, with Midchain routes, Strait approaches, and partial Maw bathymetry. Officers' berths and mess at the stern end; crew hammocks and mess forward.

**Lower Belowdecks** — Surgeon's berth, sailcloth and rope stores, water casks, lamp room, carpenter's stores.

**Hold** — Powder magazine and weapons locker aft, behind locked doors. Cook's galley with fresh-water cistern amidships. Ballast, spare timber, bilge access forward.
