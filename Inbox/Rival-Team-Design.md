# Rival Team Design

Pointy Hat's method for creating rival parties as foils to player characters. Requires PCs to be finalized first.

## What Is a Foil?

A foil highlights another character's traits by contrasting with them. Two methods:

- **Opposite foil** — every key trait is inverted. Simpler to create; high immediate impact.
- **Mirror foil** — shares most traits with the PC, but one or more key aspects are flipped to maximize contrast. More nuanced; better for rivals who feel genuinely parallel.

## The Four Steps

### Step I: Examine the Hero

Boil each PC down to three categories:

- **Character Traits** — personality attributes
- **Backstory Information** — formative history
- **Want** — the core driving desire

### Step II: Choose Foil Type

Pick opposite or mirror (see above). Mirror foils are recommended for rival teams — they feel more personal.

### Step III: Create the Rival

Apply the same three categories to the rival, flipping only the elements that create the most friction or contrast. Keep shared elements to make the parallel legible.

*Example: Alois wants to live up to his mother's impossible expectations (anxiety, warmth, scrappiness). His mirror rival Fernanda has the same drive but nobody expects anything from her at all — she's determined and cold, fighting for recognition while he fights to survive expectation.*

### Step IV: Build the Team

Rival teams as a group:

- Match the party's rough power level.
- Give the rival team a **similar motivation** (same goal, or same type of conflict), not the same objective — creates natural collision.
- Rivals begin antagonistic; can evolve into allies or full villains based on play.
- Don't require rivals to be villains — they can simply be competing toward the same end by incompatible means.
