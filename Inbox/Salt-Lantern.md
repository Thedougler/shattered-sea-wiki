# Salt Lantern

A well-made brass ship's lantern. The reservoir is empty and has never held oil. The flame, when present, burns a steady pale white.

---

## Properties

[HB] While within one mile of open saltwater coastline or aboard a vessel at sea, the lantern burns continuously without fuel, producing light equivalent to a standard lamp. When taken inland or into an enclosed interior space with no sea air, the flame dims and extinguishes within one minute. It relights automatically when the bearer returns to a salt-air environment.

## History

Morsani describes it as "a practical enchantment — someone made this for a specific purpose, and that purpose was not decorative." He does not know who enchanted it or where it originated.
