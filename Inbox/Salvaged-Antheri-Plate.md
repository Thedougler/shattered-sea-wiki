# Salvaged Antheri Plate

*Wondrous item, uncommon, requires attunement*

> The gap in his shell doesn't look like a gap anymore.

## Description

A single shoulder pauldron of Antheri construction — salvaged from the Shelfworks ruins, worked centuries before any colonial presence in the Scatter — fitted to cover the breach in [[Beaumont-Sel|Beaumont's]] shell where a cannonball punched through. The plate is not a match for the shell in colour or texture and makes no attempt to be. It sits flush, bonded during attunement, and does not shift.

The Antheri alloy has a persistent mirror-bright finish that cannot be dulled — no oil, no salt, no corrosion takes hold. In direct light it throws a hard glare. Archers have learned not to aim at it.

## Properties

While attuned, the wearer gains **+2 AC**. Ranged weapon attacks against the wearer have **disadvantage** — the plate's reflective surface catches the light and throws a glare at anyone trying to draw a bead on it.

*Tortle-specific — the plate bonds to the shell breach during attunement and cannot be transferred to a non-tortle wearer.*

## History

Sourced by [[Catarina-DaVirelli]] through her Antheri metalwork contacts. She assessed the breach in Beaumont's shell, identified a salvaged plate with compatible geometry, and fitted it in her Calveno workshop. She did not elaborate on where it came from. Beaumont did not ask.

## Current Status

Seated in the shell breach, attuned. The glare has caused more than one crossbowman to flinch wide.
