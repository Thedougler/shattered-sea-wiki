# Ship Operations

> Travel, upkeep, acquisition, and life aboard ship.

---

## Upkeep

Paid weekly whether sailing or docked.

| Cost Component | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crew wages | 8–20 gp | 40–80 gp | 150–250 gp |
| Provisions | 5–10 gp | 20–40 gp | 70–120 gp |
| Maintenance | 5 gp | 15 gp | 40 gp |
| Berthing fees | 2–5 gp | 5–10 gp | 10–20 gp |
| **Total (approx)** | **20–40 gp/wk** | **80–145 gp/wk** | **270–430 gp/wk** |

Missing upkeep is handled narratively. Officers have other options. The DM determines when patience runs out.

## Navigation Checks

When conditions are uncertain — reef channels, Maw approaches, storms, unfamiliar waters — the Navigator rolls Intelligence (Navigator's Tools).

**Success:** route proceeds normally.  
**Failure:** add time and apply a complication.

| DC | Failure |
|---|---|
| 10 | Off course. +1 day. |
| 14 | Significantly off course. +2 days, minor encounter likely. |
| 18 | Lost. +1d4 days, serious complication. |

- Tidewardens' charts grant **advantage** in charted waters.
- Midchain charts are contradictory — no advantage.
- **The Maw:** Compasses fail within 5 miles of the edge. Magical navigation gives false readings. Star navigation is unaffected. A [[Compass-Rose]] solves the compass problem directly.

## Downtime at Sea

On voyages of 3+ days, characters can perform **downtime activities** (2024 rules). Activities requiring a specific location or instructor can't be performed at sea. Bastion facility benefits still apply if the facility is aboard.

**Long rests at sea:**
- Standard 2024 rules.
- If the Cook role is filled: characters recover an additional **1d6 HP** on a long rest.
- If a storm hits during the rest: DC 12 Constitution save or the rest grants no benefits.

## Acquiring a Ship

### Buy
Available at any port with a shipyard. Tidefall has a broker on the Dockfront. Asking price runs 10–15% high — a DC 12 Charisma (Persuasion) check with a reasonable counter-offer closes the gap. On a secret d6 roll of 1, there's a meaningful defect the seller knew about.

### Prize
Take a ship in combat. A sinking vessel can be saved if the winning crew boards and controls the flooding fast enough. The prize needs crew to sail her to port. Stripping a Tier 2 ship for parts instead yields 2,000–4,000 gp at the right buyer.

### Steal
Tidefall's main harbour is hard — the garrison watches the piers and logs everything. South Harbour (Calder's Tooth's eastern side) is loosely administered. Ships in the outer bay waiting for a berth are accessible by rowboat and can simply sail once crewed.

### Mutiny
Built on crew relationships. Approach potential allies privately first. The outcome reflects the trust built beforehand.

### Build
Requires a shipyard. Same cost as buying new, but custom specs are possible.

| Tier | Build Time |
|---|---|
| 1 | 2–4 months |
| 2 | 6–12 months |
| 3 | 18–36 months |

[[Catarina-DaVirelli|Catarina Da'Virelli]] can advise on unusual specifications and install modifications during construction at standard rates.

## Living Quarters by Tier

**Tier 1** — Captain's bunk in the stern, hammocks forward. Party and crew share close quarters.

**Tier 2** — Captain's cabin (private, lockable). Two shared officer berths. Forward crew hammocks. Galley. Cargo hold.

**Tier 3** — Officer cabins, wardroom, captain's suite. Full privacy for the party.

**Captain's Locker** (Tier 2+) — Lockable iron box bolted to the cabin floor. DC 18 to break or pick.

## Ship Reputation

The ship's name and flag are known. Track reputation with each major faction and in key ports.

| Level | Effect |
|---|---|
| Respected | Reduced berth fees. Willing hires. Faction contacts available. |
| Known | Treated on actual deeds. Neutral default. |
| Wanted | Port access restricted or denied. Bounties posted. |
| Notorious | Capable crew seek them out. Some targets surrender rather than fight. |

Flying no flag is suspicious. Flying a false flag is common and carries legal risk if caught by the Dravosi.
