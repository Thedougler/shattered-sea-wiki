# Sports Match Rules

Pointy Hat's 5e-based rules for running a sports match as a combat encounter. The sport itself is left intentionally vague — flavor it to fit your setting (underwater, aerial, magitech, blood-sport, etc.). Share these rules with players the session before the match.

## Core Rules

- Goal: score more points than the opposing team within the time limit.
- A match lasts **10 rounds** (or 20 for a longer match).
- Score by shooting the ball into the enemy team's goal.
- A **15-foot-radius Goal Zone** surrounds each goal. Only the goalie may enter it.
- Each team designates one **goalie** who cannot leave their Goal Zone.
- After a goal: the defending goalie takes control of the ball, all players return to starting positions, and the match continues. Repositioning time doesn't count as match time; initiative order is unchanged.
- **Tie-breaker:** Three penalty shots per team against the enemy goalie. If still tied after penalty shots, the match ends in a draw.

## Actions

**Pass** *(Action)*
Pass the ball to a teammate up to 30 feet away. Roll Dexterity attack + proficiency bonus. DC 10 to succeed.
*Example — level 6, Dex 20: d20 + 5 + 3 = result vs. DC 10*

**Shoot** *(Action)*
Launch the ball toward the goal (max 30 feet). Roll Strength attack + proficiency bonus.

| Roll | Distance Traveled |
|------|-------------------|
| 10 or lower | 10 feet |
| 11–15 | 15 feet |
| 16–20 | 20 feet |
| 21–25 | 25 feet |
| 26+ | 30 feet |

**Tackle** *(Action)*
Force a ball-carrier to drop the ball and push them 10 feet. Roll Strength (Athletics) contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics). On success: target drops the ball (it occupies their previous space) and is pushed 10 feet.

**Steal** *(Action)*
Take the ball from a carrier without physical force. Roll Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) contested by the target's Wisdom (Insight) or Dexterity (Acrobatics). On success: you take possession of the ball.

## Bonus Actions

**Pick Up** *(Bonus Action)*
Pick up a ball within 5 feet of you. Only usable when the ball is not in any creature's possession.

## Reactions

**Intercept** *(Reaction)*
Stop a pass or shot passing through your reach. When an uncontrolled ball enters your range during a pass or shot, roll a Dexterity saving throw against the passer's/shooter's roll. On success: you gain possession.

**Lunge** *(Reaction)*
Move up to 10 feet in any direction. Can be used at any time except during your own turn — useful for positioning to receive passes.

## Concentration Rule

If a ball-carrier takes any damage, they must immediately make a Concentration saving throw (same as concentrating on a spell). On failure, they drop the ball; it lands in an empty space within 5 feet of them (GM's choice).

> **Note:** Class features or items that interact with objects as a bonus action or free action do not apply to the ball.

## Tactical Tips

| Role | Best Build |
|------|-----------|
| Goalie | High Dexterity; ranged combatant (archer or caster) — will be away from the action most of the match |
| Defender / Backline | Casters; stay near the goalie to maximize spell range |
| Midfielder | Dexterity-based or ranged builds — facilitate passes to attackers |
| Attacker | Strength-based builds — the only way to score is a Shoot action |

- Track ball possession with a ring (in person) or stamp (online) on the map.
- Using your reaction for an opportunity attack means you cannot Intercept or Lunge that round — weigh the trade-off.
- Ways to force a turnover: Tackle, Steal, or deal damage to trigger a Concentration save.

## World-Building Hooks

- How popular is this sport? Does everyone watch it?
- Are there professional players? How are they regarded?
- How did the sport originate?
- Consider: a recurring sports arc — the party joins a league and competes across multiple cities.
