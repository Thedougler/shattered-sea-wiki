# Stenmark
![[1778027722232~2 1.png]]
## Overview

Stenmark is the easternmost island in the [[content/shattered-sea/places/crown-islands/index|Crown Islands]] — the last Dravosi flag before the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]. The name is old: *sten-mark*, stone border. Whoever named it was not being poetic. From the eastern cliffs on a clear day, the water changes colour where the Maw begins — the blue-green of the Scatter going flat and dark, a shade that has no bottom in it. The threshold is visible from shore. People who live on Stenmark have looked at that line their entire lives.

The island is stone and cold water and wind from the east. The [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi Crown]] maintains a signal station here, the easternmost in the Crown chain, staffed by a small watch rotation that reports on Maw weather patterns and anything unusual coming out of the east. Beyond that, the Crown presence is thin. The island does not invite administration the way a trade port does. The people here are fishing people and watch people and the occasional salvager using Stenmark as a staging point for work on the [[Shelfworks|Shelfworks]]. They understand the eastern water in ways that the rest of the Crown Islands do not bother to.

## Geography

The island runs roughly north to south, long and narrow, with the western face gentler and the eastern face sheer. The western slopes hold the settlement, the harbour, and what farmland the soil supports — which is not much. The rock is close to the surface here. The eastern face is cliff from end to end, dropping straight into deep cold water, with no beach below and no approach from that side worth attempting under sail.

The Greyteeth reefs are at their worst in the approaches to Stenmark — the reef systems that were merely unreliable further west become genuinely dangerous here, and the Maw's deep current pushes cold water up along the eastern shelf in ways that change the behaviour of the surface swell without warning. Local pilots for the Stenmark approach charge more than local pilots anywhere else in the Crown Islands. The rates are not negotiable. ^[inferred]

The northern tip of the island is the closest point to [[High-Eyrie|the High Eyrie]] — the Sentinels' basalt sea stack sits to the northeast, and on clear mornings the Eyrie is visible from Stenmark's northern headland before the morning fog burns off. ^[inferred]

## The Signal Station

The Crown watch station sits on the eastern cliff edge, built low against the wind — stone walls, no windows facing the sea, a signal tower just tall enough to be seen from passing ships. The watch rotation is a three-month posting that most Crown sailors consider a punishment assignment and the long-service watch officers consider the only honest work in the fleet.

The station keeps a daily log of eastern conditions: Maw surface colouration, fog line position, compass variance readings, weather pattern anomalies, unusual sightings. These logs go to Harwick Admiralty on the monthly dispatch boat and are filed in the Maw observation archive alongside the Sentinels' records. Nobody cross-references them systematically. The people who have suggest that would be instructive. ^[inferred]

## The Settlement

The harbour town — also called Stenmark — is built on the western slope, facing away from the Maw and into the prevailing wind off the Scatter. Stone houses, low profiles, shutters that actually work. The population is small and has not grown in several generations, because the island does not offer the kind of opportunity that drives growth. What it offers is the eastern fishing grounds, which are cold and dangerous and unusually productive because the Maw upwelling pushes cold nutrient-rich water across the shelf year-round.

The fish out of Stenmark are different from fish caught further west. Deeper-water species, cold-water varieties, longline catches that come up from depths the western islands' fleets do not work. There is a modest trade in preserved Stenmark fish with a reputation for flavour that the island's residents have learned to leverage without explaining too carefully where the fish come from or what else shares the water with them. ^[inferred]

## Roads & Routes

Most travel uses the western harbour and slope settlement. The eastern cliff face has no useful landing, while northern approaches face Greyteeth reef trouble and Maw current effects.

## People & Power

The Crown maintains the signal station, but the island's daily authority rests with fishing families, watch officers, and pilots who understand the eastern water.

## Trade & Resources

Stenmark trades preserved cold-water fish, pilot knowledge, Maw observations, and occasional salvage support for the [[Shelfworks|Shelfworks]].

## Flora

The western slopes support rough pasture, heather, and gorse — shallow-soiled vegetation that does not need deep roots and does not mind salt wind. There are no significant forests. The cliffs carry stonecrop, sea campion, and thrift in the cliff-face crevices, the kind of plants that treat exposure as a normal condition. The harbour-side has a few wind-bent stands of coastal scrub and old hawthorn that the settlement uses for windbreaks. Kelp grows dense along the western shallows and below the cliff base on the eastern side wherever the current is not too strong. ^[inferred]

## Fauna

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/monstrosity/harpy|Harpies]] nest in the eastern cliffs — not in great numbers, but territorial and consistent. They have occupied the cliff faces since before the signal station was built, and the arrangement between the watch rotation and the nesting colonies is one of mutual avoidance maintained by long experience and a healthy respect for what a harpy can do to a person standing on an exposed cliff edge in wind. The Crown has attempted clearance operations twice. Both times the harpies returned within a season.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-vulture|Giant vultures]] ride the eastern thermals, particularly after storms, working the cliff updrafts for hours at a time. The watch log records them as routine. The offshore water is a different matter. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-shark|Giant sharks]] work the eastern shelf, drawn up by the cold upwelling and the deep-water species it lifts toward the surface. Longline crews know to pull gear before the light goes. The deeper water below the eastern cliff base — where the shelf drops quickly toward the Maw — is where the stories come from. Stenmark fishermen report occasional sightings of large wakes, surface disturbances, and shapes in the water that the light catches wrong. The Sentinels log the same things from the Eyrie and call them [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/dragons/young-sea-serpent|sea serpents]] without apparent concern. Stenmark residents use a different word and change the subject. ^[inferred]

## Hazards

Greyteeth reefs, Maw-adjacent current shifts, harpies, giant vultures, giant sharks, cliff exposure, and possible sea serpent sign make Stenmark unforgiving.
