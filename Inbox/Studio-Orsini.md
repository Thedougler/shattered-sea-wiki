# Studio Orsini

![[Marta-Orsini.webp|Marta Orsini, alchemist and proprietor of Studio Orsini]]

## Overview

Studio Orsini is an alchemist's workshop down a Le Paludi side canal that branches off the main channel and stops being on any canal map shortly after that. It is reachable by small flat-bottomed boat or by a narrow footpath that exists but has never been maintained. [[Calveno]] does not officially know Studio Orsini is there, which suits Marta Orsini well enough.

## Description

The smell reaches you before the door. It is not one smell but several, layered in a way that implies things have been processing here for a very long time. Inside: high shelves of sealed glass jars, a worktable with several ongoing processes in different states of completion, and a low lamp above each station that Orsini apparently never turns off. The walls are pale stone, the floor is flagged and slightly damp, and the ceiling drips once per minute in the far corner. A bucket is placed to catch it. It has been placed there for years.

## People & Operations

**Proprietor: [[Marta-Orsini|Marta Orsini]]** — Late forties, ash-pale skin with a faint violet cast and two short horns she keeps filed flat. Permanently damp in the way of someone who never leaves Le Paludi. Speaks with the precision of someone who knows exactly what she is making and exactly what it is likely to be used for. She does not ask. She specifies what her compounds will and will not do, gives instructions once, and expects them to be followed. Returns are not accepted.

## Access & Security

No sign. No canal marker. Orsini does not advertise and does not need to. The path and boat access are findable with local knowledge or a good description from someone who has been there. The studio door is locked when Orsini is not present; when she is present, it is open.

## Current Hooks

- Two of Orsini's last three orders for Bile of the Drowned came from the same address in the upper Mercatura. She has noted this and done nothing about it, which is her standard practice. The address belongs to a notary.
