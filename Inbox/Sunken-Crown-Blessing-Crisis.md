# Sunken Crown Blessing Crisis

## Situation

Umberlee's blessing has withdrawn from the Sunken Crown after the theft of the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls|Pearl of Souls]]. The Blue Hole's old protections, weather predictability, food security, shrine authority, and nesting-water stability are all degrading at once.

## Current State

Keth-Naar can still function, but its margin is narrowing. Hunters are debating whether to push beyond the Blue Hole into open water, watchers are reporting wrong patterns, and shrine petition no longer has the artifact that made terms with Umberlee legible. [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Stripes-Bitemore]] was sent to find why the sea turned, and his return can focus or fracture the elders' response.

## Key Facts

- The Pearl was stolen from Vel-Orn before the campaign began.
- The shrine still marks debts and remembers the drowned, but its deepest stabilizing function is failing.
- Keth-Naar's elders know more than they will give outsiders without proof of intent.
- Dath-Mar and Meth-Var are becoming more necessary and less reliable as the crisis deepens.

## DM Notes

### Sunken Crown / blessing_withdrawn

Umberlee's blessing has failed since the Pearl of Souls was stolen. Food security, weather protection, and the shrine's social authority over the five islands are all degrading.

### Sunken Crown / dragon_turtle_nesting

Dath-Mar and Meth-Var outer beaches are generational nesting sites — the elders' most protected knowledge. Nesting females are unsettled by the Blue Hole instability; the ancient dragon turtle near the Maw may be a returning female displaced by the disruption.

### Sunken Crown / pc_hook_stripes

[[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Stripes-Bitemore]] was sent from Keth-Naar to find why the sea turned. The region's survival depends on whether that question can be answered and acted on.

### Sunken Crown / shrine_consequence

Without the Pearl the shrine still marks debts and remembers the drowned, but its deepest function — stabilizing the Blue Hole and protecting nesting waters — is failing. See [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Pearl-of-Souls-Item]].

### Keth-Naar / blessing_crisis

Umberlee's blessing has withdrawn — catches unpredictable, weather volatile, unrecognized creatures appearing below the indigo line. Tribe can endure ~2 more seasons at reduced rations before being forced to hunt open waters or petition Umberlee directly (requires the Pearl or equivalent divine artifact).

### Keth-Naar / elder_anton

Grey muzzle, one clouded eye (old hunting wound). Sent [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Stripes-Bitemore]] to find answers. Managing the community without panic by keeping everyone working. Will share Archive Stone records (past times when the blessing wavered, what restored it) if the party brings evidence they're investigating the Pearl theft.

### Keth-Naar / stripes_return

The lookout will recognize Stripes immediately — his personal glyph is the most recent carving on the Central Stone. Return triggers elder assembly; outcome depends on what he brings.

### Keth-Naar / access

DC 16 Navigation to approach without guide (reef and strange currents). Arriving with Stripes or with evidence of Pearl theft investigation gets the party to Elder Anton. Outsiders are assessed, not refused.

### Keth-Naar / pressure

Hunters fracturing — some want to sail beyond the Blue Hole and hunt open waters; Stripes was the strongest voice for staying. Without him the debate is open. Belmore's absence is the visible proof the elders' judgment is being questioned.

### Vel-Orn / pearl_absence

The [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Pearl-of-Souls-Item|Pearl of Souls]] was stolen from this shrine. Its absence has made the surrounding waters feel exposed instead of forbidden — Vel-Orn is shifting from a living water-debt threshold to a place of failed account.

### Vel-Orn / keth_naar_impact

Without the Pearl, [[Keth-Naar]] loses the clearest place to petition Umberlee for terms. The community most directly affected; their guides already notice the change in the water's quality around the island.

### Umberlee Shrine / core_secret

The [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Pearl-of-Souls-Item|Pearl of Souls]] was held here in the deepest chamber over a tidal pool built to its exact requirements. [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]]'s crew stole it pre-campaign; the theft triggered the storm that sank his fleet. The shrine felt the severance instantly.

### Umberlee Shrine / rooms

Tide Gate marks all who enter — [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Stripes-Bitemore]] feels the crossing, Fisk's candle is guttered. Ledger Chamber holds Fisk's fleet scored as one breath. Pearl Chamber: absence registers as pressure and silence, detect magic finds nothing. War Room: maps, records of the theft, Fisk's name in the margin.

### Umberlee Shrine / senior_waveservant

Salt-grey skin, eyes that don't track normally, 30 years at this shrine. Knows Fisk stole the Pearl; does not know why. To Delmar: 'The Pearl knows your name. Umberlee is patient. You have a debt, and it is growing with every tide.' To Stripes: 'Your people sent you to set the seas right. This is where the anger began.'

### Umberlee Shrine / access

Not on colonial charts. Requires prior knowledge, DC 18 Navigation, or a guide. Surrounding waters patrolled by massive rays and unnamed deep things — present, not automatically hostile. Stripes gets a beacon sense near this shrine; Delmar gets dread instead.

### Ral-Arn / archive_stone

Holds records of prior seasons when Umberlee's blessing wavered — including what was required to restore it. Critical for Stripes to understand the full scope of his task.

### Ral-Arn / gm_npcs

Elder Anton knows the Pearl of Souls is the key to restoration but won't reveal this to outsiders without proof of intent.

### Ral-Arn / investigation_leads

If the party consults the Archive Stone, they can learn the historical pattern of blessing failures and what the prior restorations required.

### Ral-Arn / plot_relevance

The Pearl's theft triggered the current need to consult the Archive Stone; this island's records are the clearest inherited guidance for restoring the Blue Hole's protection.

### Dath-Mar / hidden_pressure

Pearl of Souls theft destabilized the currents that made this refuge predictable. Umberlee's failing protection makes island more necessary and less safe simultaneously — causes unknown to the Keth-Naar.

### Dath-Mar / dramatic_hook

Will the party use the refuge as shelter only, or take responsibility for a person or decision waiting there? If ignored, a stranded hunter reports neglect and Keth-Naar loses trust in outsiders.

### Dath-Mar / consequence

If Dath-Mar stops functioning as refuge, Keth-Naar loses its safest fallback during storms, hunting failures, or shrine trouble.

### Meth-Var / hidden_pressure

Since the Pearl's theft, watchers report shapes rising closer to the Blue Hole's edge and weather arriving without old warning patterns. Umberlee's withdrawn protection is letting deeper things approach — including things the Leviathan's passage disturbed in Maw-adjacent waters.

### Meth-Var / dramatic_hook

Will the party treat the watcher's warning as immediate evidence, or wait until the Blue Hole shows the threat itself? If ignored, the next warning arrives late.

### Meth-Var / consequence

If Meth-Var watch fails, Keth-Naar receives warning too late when Maw weather, predators, or worse cross into the Blue Hole.
