# The Sunken Crown

![[1778025771030~3.png|355x243]]
> *Five small islands around a blue wound in the sea.*

## Overview

The Sunken Crown is a small island region in the [[Outer-Reach|Outer Reach]], northeast of the Drowned Maw crossing. Five low islands ring a vast Blue Hole that occupies most of the region's usable geography. The islands are not a broad archipelago — they are the exposed rim of something drowned: black stone, salt grass, wind-bent scrub, and cliffs that show old waterlines like scars. The Blue Hole fills most of the view. Everything else clings to the edge.

Colonial charts thin out before these waters. Most pilots treat the region as a rumour or a navigation note to avoid. The people who live here have been here longer than any outside record acknowledges.

On land, the Sunken Crown is lean rather than lush. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-lizard|Giant lizards]] bask on the black stone, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-bat|giant bats]] crowd wind-cut caves, and scavenger birds gather wherever storm or tide leaves food behind. The Tabaxi of Keth-Naar know these threats by season and rock face. Outsiders mostly know them by surprise.

## Geography

The Blue Hole occupies the majority of the Sunken Crown. From any high point on the islands it reads as a near-perfect dark circle inside the reef rim. It is deep enough that soundings fail quickly, warm in places from lingering volcanic activity, and clear enough near the edges that large shadows can be seen moving far below. The boundary between the turquoise shallows and the indigo drop is clean and sudden.

The waters around the Blue Hole have been unsettled lately. Currents reversing without warning, fish avoiding channels they have always run, weather turning faster than the sky gives notice.

## Routes & Access

Getting here requires crossing east of the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]] into the [[Outer-Reach|Outer Reach]] — six hours over the Maw, then open water. The five islands are surrounded by reefs and channels that do not behave consistently. A ship without local knowledge risks reef damage or being drawn toward the Blue Hole's drop-off.

## People & Power

The Tabaxi of Keth-Naar organize the region through elders, watch rotations, seasonal knowledge, refuge practice, and shrine obligations. Without the Pearl of Souls, the shrine still marks debts and remembers the drowned, but its deepest function — stabilizing the Blue Hole and protecting nesting waters — is failing.

## Trade & Resources

The Sunken Crown is not a broad trade region. It survives through local hunting, tide-pool knowledge, watch discipline, shrine authority, emergency refuges, and careful management of dangerous shorelines. Food security and weather protection are degrading because Umberlee's blessing has failed since the Pearl of Souls was stolen.

## Flora

The land surface of the Sunken Crown is lean: salt grass, wind-bent scrub, and black stone that absorbs heat and releases it through the night.

## Fauna

The Crown's ordinary dangers gather around the edge of the drop: [[reef-shark|reef sharks]] in the shallows, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-octopus|giant octopuses]] in the cracks, and larger shadows below the indigo line. Outsiders use the word [[Sawek|Sawek]] too freely for anything that waits in a blue hole, but local watchers are more careful. Some shapes are predators. Some are old enough that naming them feels like an invitation. A [[dragon-turtle-wyrmling|dragon turtle wyrmling]] report is treated differently from the old island-sized stories: heat on the water, steam without weather, and a shell-ridge small enough to vanish between reef channels.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-lizard|Giant lizards]] occupy every basalt shelf with sun exposure, large enough that they drive the Tabaxi watch schedules around nesting territories rather than through them. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-bat|Giant bats]] colony in the wind-cut sea caves — the cave systems through the island foundations are extensive enough that the colonies are substantial, and the flight departures at dusk are audible from the water.

The Blue Hole's deeper shadows are harder to classify. Keth-Naar watchers describe heat events, shadow scale, and surface disturbances that most outside accounts attribute to a [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/dragons/dragon-turtle-wyrmling|dragon turtle wyrmling]] below the drop. The Tabaxi elders do not use that word for the current season's behaviour but have not offered a correction either.

## Hazards

The Sunken Crown's hazards are unstable reef channels, the Blue Hole drop-off, sudden current reversals, fast weather changes, giant lizards, giant bats, giant octopuses, possible Sawek, dragon turtle heat events, and the failure of the shrine's protection.
