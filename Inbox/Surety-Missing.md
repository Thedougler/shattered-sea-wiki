# Surety — Crown and Hollowell Search

## Situation

The *HCS Surety* is in party hands. Crown administration has flagged the cutter as missing, Rupert Knighton has started a private revenge search for his dead son, and Verity Hollowell is quietly tracking the same disappearance because Geoffrey Draves was on the crew manifest.

---

## Current State

The Crown clock started when the boarding ended. The party has roughly one month before Phase 2 watch notices harden around the *Surety*'s last logged description. Knighton's private search is faster and less procedural. Verity's search is quieter: she is using Hollowell secondary accounts and factor-network irregularity checks, not direct inquiries that would surface to her father.

## Narrative Devices

- **Loaded Gun:** Verity's bonded-cargo manifest gap can become leverage, danger, or a bridge to the party.
- **Foreshadowing:** Administrative language says "under investigation, no current port location confirmed" before anyone says stolen.
- **Clock:** Phase 2 notices in about two weeks; Phase 3 enforcement around one month; Knighton's people move independently.
- **Reversal Potential:** Verity may become an ally because her personal search intersects with Crown evidence before her father controls it.

## Key Facts

- Missing is not lost. The Crown treats the first absence as paperwork, not emergency.
- A repaint changes courtesy-stop recognition; it does not change cutter silhouette, wear pattern, or Crown interior fit-out.
- False registry plate and defensible papers are the key inspection fix.
- Knighton confirmed Cap'n Gorgeous' death and sent one Knight into the Central Strait corridor.
- Verity knows Geoffrey was aboard the *Surety* and that the ship went dark; Geoffrey does not know she knows.
- A Hollowell bonded-cargo line moved through Tidefall without a formal manifest entry six weeks ago.

## Consequences

- **Good disguise before Phase 2:** courtesy-stop officers look for the wrong ship.
- **No false plate:** officers assess by eye and interior fit-out if boarded.
- **Knighton gets a witness:** private pursuit accelerates regardless of Crown clock.
- **Verity engages first:** party may gain pier scheduling access, ledger leverage, or a volatile Geoffrey scene.
- **Ledger surfaces publicly:** Hollowell license, Cedric, and Verity all come under Crown pressure.

## DM Notes

### Crown Phases

Phase 1: paperwork, waypoint inquiries, no active hunt.

Phase 2: courtesy-stop notices describe the last logged *Surety*: naval grey, 80 ft patrol cutter, brass bow registry plate, Crown pennant, two cannon amidships, two swivel guns. Port Tidefall and Harwick receive automatically; Kalowe/Calveno only if remembered or escalated.

Phase 3: [[HCS-Warrant|HCS Warrant]] and Commander Gideon Ault are the most likely response. Authority includes detention, cargo seizure, crew arrest.

### What Repaint Buys

Repaint plus false plate gets an unrecognized vessel into the water before the right description circulates. It does not hide the cutter hull form, patrol maintenance wear, boarding locker, brig cage, chart table, or officer's aft cabin from a knowledgeable boarding officer.

### Verity's Position

Verity told Cedric about Geoffrey years ago and regrets the audience, not the disclosure. She has not accepted another arrangement despite Cedric's introductions. She found Geoffrey on the *Surety* manifest and has not told her father.

Her ledger problem: Hollowell paperwork moved bonded cargo through Tidefall with no formal manifest entry. The paperwork is real; the gap is not an error. She has been deciding what to do for six weeks.

Hooks:
- ledger leverage against Crown inspection or unlicensed cargo movement;
- Hollowell pier scheduling access;
- Geoffrey returning to Tidefall;
- one introduced suitor with Admiralty connections.
