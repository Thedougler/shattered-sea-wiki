# The Tail

![[1778025673931~2.png]]
![[The-Tail.webp|Storm-dark island chain with steep green ridges and white surf breaking through channels]]

## Overview

The Tail is the eastern end of the [[Verdant-Scatter|Verdant Scatter]], where both island arcs narrow and converge near the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]. The islands here grow fewer, taller, and more vertical than the rest of the Sea: high cliffs, wind exposure, and faces of stone dropping straight into cold deep water.

The communities here have lived beside the trench for generations. They know the shelf edge, the fog schedules, the reefs that changed last season, and where the Maw's discolouration begins on a clear day. Less settled than the [[Crown-Islands|Crown Islands]], not empty, and not forgiving.

The Tail is where piracy stops being a tavern story and starts affecting routing. Shelfworks crews, longliners, and Maw-crossing vessels all pass through tight weather windows, which gives a patient [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/humanoid/pirate-captain|pirate captain]] something to plan around.

## Geography

The Tail's waters are colder and darker than the rest of the Scatter. A deep current wells up along the Maw's western edge and pushes cold, nutrient-rich water across the shelf. Morning fog banks form overnight and burn off by midday. The Greyteeth reefs are at their worst in the eastern approaches.

Compass drift begins within five miles of the Maw's edge, so Maw-adjacent navigation runs by stars, local pilots, and judgement rather than instruments. From the eastern cliffs, the Maw's water discolouration is visible on clear days.

## Routes & Access

Salvage operations bound for the [[Shelfworks|Shelfworks]] stage from the closest inhabited Tail islands before heading out to the open-water dive site. The Tail is where crews take on pilots, supplies, rope, weights, replacement fittings, and the kind of advice nobody writes down because writing it down makes it look optional.

Weather, current, and depth change quickly here. A pleasant morning can become instrument work by noon. Maw-adjacent navigation depends on stars, local pilots, and judgement because compass drift begins near the edge.

## People & Power

The Tail is governed less by institutions than by practical expertise: pilot families, longline crews, salvage crews, Maw watchers, and people who know which reef changed last season. The region connects the [[Crown-Islands|Crown Islands]] and [[Midchain|Midchain]] while also staging the dangerous work that feeds the Shelfworks economy.

The [[The-Glass-Debt|Glass Debt]] is the name most associated with Tail pressure: too large for casual reef work, but perfect for forcing salvage escorts to choose between protecting divers and protecting the prize ship. The [[The-Velvet-Noose|Velvet Noose]] is rarer here, and much worse; sailors who believe the stories assume she carries a [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/humanoid/pirate-admiral|pirate admiral]], not merely a captain.

## Trade & Resources

The Tail has some of the best longline grounds in the Sea. Cold upwelling brings swordfish, albacore, and oilfish close to the surface at night. Serious longline crews work the shelf edge, where the water is rich and mistakes have less room to become survivable.

The Tail also supplies the Shelfworks: pilots, provisions, rope, weights, fittings, pump maintenance, hard-weather refuge, and salvage camp labour.

## Flora

The Tail's documented plant life is sparse in current canon. The region is defined more by high cliffs, cold water, fog, exposed saddles, and inhabited staging islands than by named flora.

## Fauna

The cliffs are land enough for things that like height and carrion. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-vulture|Giant vultures]] follow fishing waste and wreckage, [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-bat|giant bats]] crowd caves above the fog line, and [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-axe-beak|giant axe beaks]] sometimes run the high saddles where grass, fog, and cliff paths meet. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/monstrosity/harpy|Harpy]] stories cling to the same narrow places, where voices carry strangely in bad weather.

The cold upwelling that makes the Tail's longline grounds worth working pulls [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-shark|giant sharks]] up along the shelf edge. Tail crews distinguish them from ordinary hunter sharks by what happens to the gear — or doesn't come back with the gear. The attacks are less frequent than on the shallower grounds further west, but the animals are larger and the current makes unusual wakes harder to read before they commit.

[[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/monstrosity/harpy|Harpies]] occupy the same cliff faces as the vultures, in the narrow stretches where the rock splits into sea caves and exposure makes sound behave wrongly. Voices carry in bad weather along the Tail's eastern cliffs in ways that are not always wind, and sailors who have spent time here learn to treat unfamiliar sounds from above as a navigational input rather than curiosity. The harpy population is not large, but it is consistent.

## Hazards

The Tail's hazards are fog windows, compass drift, Maw discolouration, cold shelf-edge current, Greyteeth spillover, giant sharks, cliff scavengers, harpies, pirate pressure around salvage traffic, and the open question of ancient sea serpent attacks east of the Maw.
