# Taufa Fifita & Sons

![[Taufa-Fifita.webp|Taufa Fifita, Kalowe chandler and head of the Fifita trading family]]

## Overview

The Fifita family chandlery sits on the second island's main causeway, three buildings back from the water, identifiable by the coils of hempen rope hanging from the eaves and the smell of tar and linseed oil that the stone walls have absorbed over two generations. It is [[Kalowe]]'s primary supplier of nautical stores: rope, sailcloth, lamp oil, block and tackle, and a careful selection of navigation tools. The family also runs [[Fifitas-Slip|Fifita's Slip]] on the lagoon side of the same island, though the chandlery and the slip are kept strictly separate in conversation.

## Description

A double-fronted stone building, the lower floor used for trade and the upper floor for storage and family quarters. The front counter is wide, worn smooth, and always has a ledger open on it that Taufa Fifita is not actively consulting. The back storeroom smells of tar, hemp, and the slightly acrid note of oilskin canvas. Rope hangs from ceiling hooks in varying weights; canvas rolls lean against the far wall. The light is good near the counter, dim in back.

## Function

Chandlery: standard marine supplies, rigging materials, navigation tools, and a small selection of general-purpose dry goods. The Fifita family has enough Kalowe standing to run the chandlery openly and enough sense to keep the chandlery and the slip's books from touching each other.

## People & Operations

**[[Taufa-Fifita|Taufa Fifita]]** — proprietor, sixties, grey-green scaled, unhurried. He wears his authority lightly and his accounting precisely. Lizardfolk flat affect works in his favour: he asks no unnecessary questions and registers no personal reaction to the answers. He knows every ship that has bought rope from him in the last decade and what they used it for in general terms. He does not ask about specific terms. If a customer wants something he does not stock, he will tell them who might have it — once — without elaboration.

*Roleplay concept: family patriarch who survived in Kalowe by being useful and forgettable to people with power on both sides of the ledger.*

**Sample lines:**
- *"Hempen or tarred? How many fathoms?"*
- *"Compass is good quality. One in stock. Fifty crowns."*
- *"I don't keep a book on what people carry out. Just what I sell."*

Two adult sons work the back storeroom and handle deliveries to the dry dock. They are not introduced by name and do not volunteer them.

## Access & Security

Front entrance from the causeway. Side door to the storeroom for delivery and bulk pickup. The back of the storeroom connects to the rear of the building and a narrow alley; from that alley, the lagoon-side path runs to [[Fifitas-Slip|Fifita's Slip]], though this connection is not visible from inside the shop and is not discussed. Taufa locks the front shutter at sundown.
