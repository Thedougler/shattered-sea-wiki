# The *Bad Receipt*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A two-masted schooner cuts low through the channel under patched cream canvas and a merchant pennant that looks correct until it matters. Her hull is black-green, stained like old ink, with removable brass registry plates bolted over older scars. When the side screens drop, cannon mouths show in a neat row beneath cargo netting, and the figurehead resolves into a carved clerk holding a ship's hook behind his back.

---

## Overview

The *Bad Receipt* is a Tier 2 armed pirate schooner built for Midchain work: false papers, quick pursuit, selective violence, and retreat through channels where Crown captains start arguing with their own charts. She was refitted at [[Kalowe|Kalowe]] from a seized courier hull and now operates between the [[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/index|Midchain]] back passages and the southern edge of the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]].

At distance she reads as a legitimate but unlucky commercial vessel: patched sails, mismatched cargo nets, a tired merchant stern, and registry plates that make clerks feel useful. Up close, the lie becomes theatrical. Her deck is too clear, her gun crews move too fast, and every visible defect has been placed where an inspector expects to find one.

She is not a slaughter ship. Her captain wants cargo, letters of marque, ransomable officers, spell components, sealed dispatches, and anything whose owner cannot report the theft without explaining why it was aboard. The crew prefers a disabled rudder, a terrified deck, and a signed transfer under duress.

## Layout

### Weather Deck

The weather deck is staged as an argument with bureaucracy. Removable cargo screens hide gun tackle until the first volley. Brass registry plates can be swapped in under five minutes. The stern carries a tired merchant nameboard that hinges down over the real carving. The wheel, signal lantern hood, and speaking grates all sit aft, where the captain can command a boarding action without crossing the deck.

### Gun Deck

The gun deck holds five light cannons to a side, with two heavier chase guns mounted fore and aft for disabling rigging. The central lane is kept clear for powder, prisoners, and wounded crew. Aft, the false registry office doubles as the Ship's Logbook Room: seal wax, copied stamps, route permits, captured manifests, and a bolted desk with three drawers that all lock differently.

### Lower Hold

The lower hold is divided between useful lies and useful violence. The forward section carries the shrine basin, spare sail, rope, and boring cargo. Amidships are the crew berths and galley stores. Aft, behind a partition that looks like cheap repair work, the prize hold stores dispatch boxes, spell components, ransom trunks, and ledgers taken from people who should have known better.

## Crew

The ship can limp by with nine hands but works properly with twenty-six. Most crew are Midchain sailors, former courier hands, debtors, and a few Passage-adjacent runners who know how to avoid making their private work official.

## Operational Notes

**Preferred hunting ground:** Midchain approaches to the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]], especially eastbound traffic that has already paid Tidefall fees and is too tired to question a distressed courier.

**Opening move:** Raise false colours, ask for assistance, present a paperwork problem, then fire into rigging once the target slows.

**Escape plan:** Fog Cannon, Ghost Keel, and reef pilots. The ship is fast enough to choose many fights but not fast enough to outrun every patrol in open water.

**Legal posture:** Never admits to piracy. The Ship's Logbook Room maintains three competing paper identities: distressed courier, Chain Council survey vessel, and privateer under a commission whose issuing island currently denies knowing it.

**PC gravity:** This ship pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]] through privateer legitimacy and crew loyalty, [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Perrin-Black-Jaw]] through Passage-adjacent smuggling and ship-loss envy, and [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack]] if the prize hold ever carries captives from the [[Verdant-Teeth|Verdant Teeth]] trade.

## At The Table

- As an enemy, the *Bad Receipt* is a Tier 2 pirate escalation after the party has outgrown cutter-scale threats.
- As a prize, she is a complete mid-campaign ship bastion: useful, compromised, and immediately wanted by somebody.
- As a contact, she buys silence, sells papers, and knows which captains are carrying cargo they cannot report stolen.
