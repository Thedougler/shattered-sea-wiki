# The Flat Water

![[Reweti-Kahurangi.webp|Reweti Kahurangi, proprietor of The Flat Water]]

## Overview

The Flat Water is on the second island, set back from the main causeway behind a low salt-bleached wall. It is [[Kalowe]]'s primary eating house, drinking house, and lodging, named for the lagoon the building looks out over. Most crews working the Midchain pass through at least once. Most of the information worth having in Kalowe passes through it too, usually between the second and fourth drinks.

## Description

Low stone walls, a roof of salvaged timber and layered palm, and a wide terrace facing the lagoon. The interior is dim even at midday; the terrace is the room most people want. Hammock berths fill the upper floor — canvas slung from ceiling beams, close together, with a standing fan of woven palm that Reweti runs herself on hot nights using a rope pull from the bar. The smell is woodsmoke, rum, and the faintly sweet salt of the lagoon at low tide.

The bar is a single plank of dark timber, very old, set on two casks that have not moved in years. Reweti works behind it. She does not rush.

## Function

Food, drink, and a place to sleep. The Flat Water is not a social club and does not pretend otherwise. Reweti keeps the terrace quiet enough for business conversations and loud enough that no single conversation carries. This is a feature, not an accident.

## People & Operations

**[[Reweti-Kahurangi|Reweti Kahurangi]]** — proprietor, a tortle who works the bar herself from mid-morning until the last customer leaves. She moves with the unhurried deliberateness of someone who has been doing a job long enough to stop performing it — shell turned slightly to the room, arms free. She does not volunteer information. She does not pretend not to have heard things. If the party is straightforwardly polite and spends money without causing a scene, she will occasionally say something useful, once, without preamble, and then not say it again.

*Roleplay concept: ex-deckhand who retired into a room where she controls the exits.*

**Sample lines:**
- *"Two hammocks or the room?"*
- *"That ship's been in the primary berth eleven days. Arrived with twelve crew. I've counted nine on the causeways."* (volunteered once, not repeated)
- *"Food's what's on the board. If it's not on the board it's not tonight."*

One hired hand clears tables and refills the water cisterns. No name given; leaves before dark.

## Access & Security

Front entrance off the causeway alley. Terrace entrance from the lagoon-side path. Reweti closes the front shutter when she wants to stop new arrivals; the terrace stays open later. She keeps a belaying pin under the bar and has used it. The hired hand is gone by dark.
