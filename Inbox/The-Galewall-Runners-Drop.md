# The Galewall Runner's Drop

> *They sailed straight into the Galewall. Most people call that suicide. They called it a route.*

A legendary privateer treasure cache rumored to be hidden on one of the volcanic [[Ashwall-Islands]], deposited there during a desperate flight from the Dravosi Admiralty in the early colonial period.

---

## The Myth

In the early days of colonial occupation, an infamous privateer executed a flawless heist of a [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi]] payroll fleet. Hunted by the full Admiralty, the crew attempted the impossible: outrunning the navy by sailing straight into [[Galewall]].

---

## What Happened

The permanent storm belt shattered the ship's masts and pushed it back out, sinking and ruined. Before the ship slipped beneath the waves, the crew reportedly rowed the heavy lockboxes onto the black, sulfur-vented shores of one of the [[Ashwall-Islands]]. The privateer's name is lost to history — accounts differ — but the heist itself is corroborated by missing Dravosi payroll records from the colonial era.

The cache is supposedly marked by a massive carving of a rat extending its hand, cut into the volcanic rock near the drop site. The carving predates any known colonial settlement of the Ashwall chain.

---

## The Catch

The [[Ashwall-Islands]] are notoriously hostile, remote, and impossible to map with any reliability:

- Toxic sulfur vents make extended land exploration dangerous without alchemical protection
- Shifting volcanic rock causes landmarks to change season-to-season
- The island on which the cache sits is not identified in any surviving record
- The rat carving, if it survives, may have been buried, eroded, or submerged by volcanic activity

No successful recovery has been recorded. Several expeditions have returned from the Ashwall chain with sulfur burns and empty holds.
