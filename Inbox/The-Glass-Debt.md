# The *Glass Debt*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A three-masted frigate turns broadside with the patient confidence of a ship that does not need to hurry. Her hull is lacquered black over old navy-grey paint, and the gold scrollwork has been scratched through in places so the previous livery shows like a bruise. Forty gunports sit in two clean rows. At the prow, a glass-inlaid figurehead of a veiled woman holds a cracked balance scale in both hands. Every lantern aboard burns blue-white behind cut glass.

---

## Overview

The *Glass Debt* is a Tier 3 armed pirate frigate: too large for ordinary smuggling, too expensive for casual raiding, and too dangerous to dismiss as a local nuisance. She operates where the [[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/index|Midchain]] back routes open toward the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]], the Tail, and the western approaches to the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]. Smaller pirate ships take prizes. The *Glass Debt* changes how convoys behave.

Her hull began life as a Crown-built heavy escort, but every formal mark has been made ambiguous rather than erased. The grey still shows beneath black lacquer. Old registry scars remain under decorative brass. The ship's look is intentional: expensive, wounded, legal-looking at the edge of sight, and insulting up close.

She is a late-campaign pirate problem. A Tier 1 cutter can ambush. A Tier 2 schooner can make a career. The *Glass Debt* can blockade a harbour mouth for a night, break a convoy's formation, force a Dravosi captain to request support, or become the kind of prize that starts arguments between everyone with a claim.

## Layout

### Weather Deck

The weather deck is wide, clean, and arranged for command visibility. The helm sits aft under a black-lacquered quarterdeck canopy with cut-glass lanterns that can be shuttered by colour. Signal flags, false pennants, and captured naval codes are stored in two long lockers beneath the rail. Four swivel guns cover boarding lanes. The mainmast carries the cut-glass observatory: a small enclosed crown built around lenses, mirrors, and a speaking tube down to the chart room.

### Upper Gun Deck

The upper gun deck carries lighter long cannon and the crew's boarding stations. The Sparring Deck occupies a cleared amidships lane where marines drill when the guns are secured. The Officer's Mess sits aft under the quarterdeck, all polished dark wood, cut-glass cups, and chairs bolted to the floor. Nobody aboard calls it a gambling hall while money is on the table.

### Main Gun Deck

The main gun deck is the ship's real threat. Heavier cannon run nearly the full length of both sides, with the double broadside magazine protected behind reinforced doors and strict lantern rules. The Weapons Locker and Carpenter's Shop sit near the central hatch so shot, powder, boarding arms, and repair crews can move without crossing the whole deck. The crew leaves the central lane clear even when off watch.

### Orlop

The orlop is where the ship stops performing and starts surviving. The Glasshouse sickbay sits beneath a row of thick blue-white glass panes set into the deck above, giving the surgeon light without open flame. Crew berths and marine racks run forward. The Waveservant Shrine is below the foremast: a saltwater basin, storm tokens, broken compass needles, and a ledger of voyages paid before departure.

### Hold

The hold is divided into prize compartments, ballast, spare timber, water, food, and hidden paperwork. The Captain's Archive and Ship's Logbook Room occupy a protected stern section above the magazine, close enough to be guarded and far enough from the captain's cabin that one fire does not take every secret at once. The prize hold has separate locked partitions for ransom trunks, sealed cargo, and goods too cursed or politically useful to sell quickly.

## Crew

The *Glass Debt* can sail with twenty-four but only becomes itself at seventy-two. Her full complement includes sailors, gunners, marines, quartermasters, clerks, a surgeon, a dedicated prize master, and enough boarders to make surrender feel practical.

## Operational Notes

**Preferred hunting ground:** Convoy routes where the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]] begins to lose certainty: Tail approaches, Midchain exits, Maw-adjacent salvage lanes, and weather windows after a Galewall crossing.

**Opening move:** The *Glass Debt* does not pretend to be harmless. She pretends the target has already lost. She signals with captured codes, forces a convoy to hesitate, disables the most disciplined escort, then offers terms before the second broadside.

**Escape plan:** She does not outrun Tier 2 ships in open water. She survives by making pursuit expensive: fog, magical resistance, false signals, reef pilots, paid shrine terms, and enough guns that a pursuer has to explain the damage later.

**Reputation:** Wanted by the Dravosi Crown, feared by underinsured merchants, quietly useful to people who need a convoy humiliated, and unwelcome in any port whose harbourmaster cannot be bribed in private.

**PC gravity:** The ship pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]] through fleet command, lost-captain grief, and the question of what a privateer becomes when law stops pretending. It pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Perrin-Black-Jaw]] as a replacement dream too large to hide from his family obligations. It pressures [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss]] through crow's-nest command, dangerous navigation, and the temptation to fly above problems that still need solving below.

## At The Table

- As an enemy, the *Glass Debt* is not a random encounter. Use her when the party's ship decisions can affect regional power.
- As a prize, she is a complete late-campaign bastion with immediate political fallout.
- As a temporary ally, she solves naval problems by creating three new legal ones.
- As a rumour, she makes merchants hire escorts and makes escorts ask for money up front.
