# The Mud Pits

## Overview

The Mud Pits sit on Kalowe's third island, fifty feet back from the Grimaldi causeway in a natural limestone depression that catches lagoon silt when it rains and holds it until it is the right consistency. [[Kalowe]] maintains it as it maintains most things: because enough people want it to exist to keep it from not existing. On fight nights, the torchlight carries across the water and the crowd noise reaches [[The-Flat-Water|The Flat Water]] on the second island. You will hear it before you see it.

The fights are to unconsciousness. Not to surrender — surrender is not a category Bala Tane recognises. Weapons up to a dagger are technically permitted; no one uses them. This is not that kind of fight. Death magic is prohibited. Beyond that, the only rule is that when a fighter is down and stays down, the fight is over.

## Description

The pit is roughly oval, fifteen feet across, and the mud is thigh-deep at the lowest point in the centre. The limestone rim is wide enough to stand on and worn smooth where spectators lean. Torches on iron staves ring the outer edge. The smell is exactly what the smell is.

The crowd stands on the rim and on the sloped ground behind it — dockworkers, sailors, chandlery crews, people who came for business and stayed, people who came for this. Betting slips move through Bala's runners before the match starts and are settled in coin on the rim when it is over.

## Function

Competitive entertainment for Kalowe's working harbour population, with open gambling and the particular social function of being the one place in Kalowe where physical superiority is the only credential that matters.

The Pits also serve as an informal dispute resolution mechanism. Two crews with a problem can settle it here with Bala as witness, the outcome binding under harbour custom.

## People & Power

**[[Bala-Tane|Bala Tane]]** — An orc woman in her mid-forties who has been running the Pits for fifteen years. She announces a match like it is a royal proclamation, then goes completely still for the duration of the fight. Weight classes are her decision; she reads the crowd, reads the fighters, and makes the matchup she thinks the crowd wants to see rather than the one the fighters expect. She has never been wrong about what the crowd wants to see.

Her runners handle the betting slips and she handles everything else.

## Entering a Fight

Any fighter or group can enter by paying the entry fee and presenting themselves at the rim before Bala calls the match. She will look at you, look at the crowd, look at who else is available, and tell you who you are fighting and how many of them there will be. This is not negotiable, but it does take your size, your numbers, and your apparent confidence into account.

Groups may fight together. Bala has no opinion on the fairness of the matchup — she has an opinion on whether it will be interesting.

Prize money is drawn from the betting pool. A straight win against a standard matchup pays 2:1 on the entry fee as a base, before bettors. An upset against a heavier or more numerous opponent pays accordingly.

## Current Hooks

- Bala is currently looking for something to put against the reigning champion — a retired orc sailor named **Vort Kessen** who has not lost in two years and has started to visibly bore the crowd by winning too cleanly. She will pay the entry fee for any group she thinks might give him a real fight.
- The Five Blades have reportedly been banned from entering after a match three weeks ago that Bala has declined to describe in detail. They are watching from the rim most nights, which the crowd interprets as embarrassing for everyone.
