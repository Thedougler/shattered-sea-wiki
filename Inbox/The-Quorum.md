# The *Quorum*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A single-masted cutter sits low at the Kalowe dock, hull tarred black to the rail with no livery and no figurehead. The waterline is copper-sheathed, the rigging is tight and spare, and a plain reef-pilot's pennant hangs from the mast. She looks like a working boat that has been given very good rope.

---

## Overview

A single-masted cutter, 48 feet on the waterline, built at the Kalowe dry dock and kept by the [[Chain-Council|Chain Council]] as a dispatch vessel. No flag, no figurehead, no painted name — the *Quorum* is stenciled in small letters on the transom and nowhere else. The hull is tarred black with a copper-sheathed waterline, standard for reef navigation: practical, antifouling, and unremarkable in any Midchain harbour.

Built speed-first. She carries two swivel mounts on the rail for close defence and a single small stern cabin sealed with iron latches — the Council's field document room, barely large enough for three people and a locked chest. The rest of the deck is working space.

The name is procedural. The Chain Council only has authority when enough affected representatives are present. The boat named *Quorum* is the Council's reminder that legitimacy is not a fixed thing.

---

## Purpose

The *Quorum* moves what the Council cannot trust to ordinary couriers: sealed commission documents, vote records, draft proposals, representatives who need to arrive without announcing themselves. She also carries a Kalowe pilot on standing retainer — enough expertise to navigate the back channels and reef passages that the Dravosi patrol routes avoid.

She is not a warship and does not pretend to be one. The swivels are loaded but rarely uncovered. The pennant keeps most patrol vessels from boarding without cause.

## Layout

Single open deck with minimal below-deck space.

**Weather Deck** — Mast and boom forward, wheel aft. Two swivel mounts on the rail amidships, uncovered only when needed. Working space is the whole deck.

**Stern Cabin** — Low-headroom compartment aft, sealed with iron latches. Fitted with a locked chest bolted to the deck, a fold-down chart table, and two bench seats. Used for documents and small private deliberations. Not comfortable for long passages.

**Below Decks** — Shallow hold: water casks, lamp stores, sailcloth, rope. Crew hammocks slung forward. No galley — the *Quorum* does not make long passages by choice.
