# The *Tallow Bell*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A small grey sloop moves toward the harbour mouth with its sail reefed neat and low, the canvas stained the colour of old candle smoke. No bright paint marks the hull. A bronze handbell hangs beneath the short bowsprit, dark with salt, and the stern rail is crowded with sealed oilcloth bundles, rope coils, and covered basins that smell faintly of brine and tallow.

---

## Overview

The *Tallow Bell* is a Tier 1 Waveservant shrine sloop used to move clergy between working harbours. She carries priests, shrine attendants, tribute ledgers, candles, storm tokens, small sealed offerings, and ritual supplies. She does not carry comfort, prestige, or much cargo that anyone sensible would steal.

The ship is deliberately plain. Salt-grey hull, smoke-stained sail, no carved figurehead, no faction pennant beyond a [[Narrow]] grey streamer at the mast. The only ornament is the bronze bell under the bowsprit, rung once when departing a shrine harbour and once when entering another. Sailors know the sound. Most make room.

The *Tallow Bell* is neutral in the same way the [[Waveservants|Waveservants]] are neutral: not kind, not warm, not uninvolved. She has carried priests to merchant ports, pirate anchorages, Crown piers, fishing hamlets, and ships that needed a blessing performed in open water because the captain did not expect to survive the next shorefall.

## Purpose

The *Tallow Bell* exists to keep the shrine network moving. Harbour shrines are fixed; the sea is not. When a waterline shrine needs a replacement attendant, new candles, copied tribute schedules, a witness for a disputed payment, or a priest willing to board a ship nobody else wants to touch, a vessel like this makes the run.

She also moves records. The Waveservants remember unpaid debts with excellent accuracy. Some of that memory lives in shrine ledgers. Some travels in oilcloth chests aboard small grey ships that no one wants to explain robbing.

The *Tallow Bell* has no formal special facility slots. Her spaces are built around service, transport, and ritual work. If a party took her as a bastion, the portable shrine would be the obvious first formal facility to preserve.

## Layout

### Weather Deck

The weather deck is uncluttered except for work that must happen in public. The mast sits forward of center, the tiller aft, and two covered swivel mounts sit low on the rails. The bow carries the bronze bell. Near the mast is a covered travelling basin that can be lashed open for rites performed aboard. No one sits on it twice.

### Aft Shelter

A low aft shelter holds the locked offering chest, copied tribute ledgers, storm glass, candle bundles, and oilcloth packets transferred between shrines. The door has no decoration. The lock is better than the ship looks.

### Belowdecks

Belowdecks is close, dry, and smells of tallow, salt, old rope, and damp wool. Crew hammocks run forward. Clergy berths fold against the hull amidships. The repair locker and provisions crates sit aft beside water casks and spare basins packed in straw.

## Crew

The ship can move with three hands, but a normal run carries eight people: a captain-pilot, bosun, cook, carpenter, two sailors, and two Waveservant clergy or attendants.

| Role | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Captain-Pilot** | Usually a lay sailor in Waveservant employ; knows shrine harbours and when to say nothing. |
| **Navigator** | Often doubled by the captain on short runs; hired separately for Maw-adjacent routes. |
| **Bosun** | Keeps deck order and makes sure passengers understand they are not in command. |
| **Carpenter** | Handles constant small repairs; shrine ships do not arrive looking neglected. |
| **Cook** | Manages provisions and keeps clergy fed without asking what is in the sealed packets. |
| **Waveservant Attendants** | Maintain the travelling basin, ledgers, candles, and tribute transfers. |

## Operational Notes

**Route pattern:** Port to port, shrine to shrine, rarely more than a few days from land unless called to a ship in distress or a dangerous crossing.

**Neutral passage:** Most captains avoid interfering with Waveservant vessels. Not out of affection. Robbing a shrine sloop means explaining to every harbour why the sea's collectors should still treat you normally.

**Defence:** The swivel guns are for warning shots, deck control, and discouraging desperate theft. The *Tallow Bell* does not win fights. She survives by being more trouble to harm than she is worth.

**At sea rites:** If a captain pays for an open-water blessing, the ship comes alongside, the basin is uncovered, terms are spoken, coin or token is accepted, and the bell rings once. The Waveservants do not linger.

**PC gravity:** The ship pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]] through Umberlee's active claim and the cost of ignored tribute. It pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Perrin-Black-Jaw]] because hidden routes and hidden sailors still owe the sea. It gives any party ship a small, practical way to meet the Waveservants outside a formal harbour shrine.

## At The Table

- As a contact, the *Tallow Bell* can deliver a Waveservant priest without requiring the party to reach port.
- As an encounter, she is a neutral vessel everyone around the party treats carefully.
- As a prize, she is a bad idea: little money, many witnesses, and religious consequences.
- As a clue, her ledgers can point toward unpaid voyages, missing ships, or someone who paid tribute under a false name.
