# The Tithe of the Bitch Queen

> *Taking a single coin invites Umberlee's direct, personal spite. The sea follows you home.*

A legendary collection of divine treasure scattered across the seafloor of the [[Sunken-Crown]] region — the physical accumulation of beautiful things [[Umberlee-DM]] claimed from mortal ships over centuries.

---

## The Myth

Before the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]] was stolen by [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]]'s fleet, it was widely believed that [[Umberlee-DM]] maintained a physical collection of the most beautiful treasures her storms had claimed from mortal ships. Not a formal hoard — an unconscious curation. The finest things the sea had taken, kept.

---

## What Is Known

The Tithe is rumored to be scattered across the seafloor of the [[Sunken-Crown]] region, not consolidated in one location. It consists of unblemished jewels, pristine statues, and gold bars that never tarnish in salt water — items with apparent divine preservation that marks them as distinct from ordinary sunken cargo. The collection is supposedly accessible only through tidal caves that breach the surface during the lowest tides of the decade.

Sailors with religious knowledge note that Umberlee does not "collect" in any conscious theological sense. The Tithe exists because the sea takes beautiful things and preserves them. Whether the goddess intends this or simply does not notice is a matter of Waveservant debate.

---

## The Catch

Taking a single coin from the Tithe invites [[Umberlee-DM]]'s direct, personal spite. This is not standard maritime misfortune — it is localized and relentless:

- Sailors who possess a piece of the Tithe cannot sail without drawing unnatural, localized squalls that follow their ship
- The squalls persist until the sea physically reclaims its property — meaning the item must be returned to saltwater
- There is no theological workaround documented in Waveservant records
- The curse is on the object, not the bearer — transferring the item transfers the problem

The Tithe is entirely accessible, in principle. No one who has successfully taken from it has successfully kept it.

---

## Relationship to the [[Pearl-of-Souls|Pearl of Souls]]

The myth of the Tithe predates the theft of the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]]. The Pearl's theft has complicated the theological picture: the Tithe represents what [[Umberlee-DM]] passively accumulates, while the Pearl represents something actively stolen *from* her. Waveservants interviewed on the subject will not discuss whether the Pearl belongs to the Tithe in any formal sense.

---

## Relationships

- **[[Umberlee-DM]]** — the divine authority behind the Tithe; the squall-curse on taken items is hers
- **[[Sunken-Crown]]** — the region where the Tithe's seafloor cache is located; tidal caves breach the surface only at decade-low tides
- **[[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]]** — his theft of the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]] from an Umberlee shrine is the most famous recent instance of taking from Umberlee's collection
- **[[content/shattered-sea/situations/active/Pearl-of-Souls]]** — the stolen artifact; its theft is the event that contextualizes the Tithe myth in current maritime culture
