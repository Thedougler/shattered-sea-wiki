# The Uncharted Coast

A single sheet of heavy vellum, folded in quarters, with the smell of old ink and beeswax preservation coating. The cartography, when unfolded, is immediately recognisable as the work of a trained navigator.

---

## Properties

[RAW] Not magical. The chart depicts roughly 200 miles of coastline with professional-grade precision: depth soundings, current markers, anchorage notations in two or three languages, hazard markings consistent with real survey work. The coastline itself does not match any known stretch of the Shattered Sea, the Crown Islands, the Midchain, or the Central Strait. It does not match any published or official survey.

Morsani does not know who drew it or what coast it depicts. He is confident it is accurate.

## History

Acquired from a sailor who brought it in as part of a lot of navigational instruments. The sailor had bought the lot at estate sale and did not know what he had. Morsani knew immediately that it was worth more than the rest of the lot combined and paid accordingly.

*Note: Morsani has recently been quietly asking about this chart through intermediaries, describing it to others as a drawing of "a coast that should not be where it is." If he is asking others about it, he may be trying to find who it originally belonged to — or who is looking for it.*
