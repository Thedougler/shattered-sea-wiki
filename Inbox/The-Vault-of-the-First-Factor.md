# The Vault of the First Factor

> *The Tessarine don't use conventional traps. No one who has entered without credentials has returned.*

A legendary hidden depository constructed by the earliest [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] Envoy to the Shattered Sea, built to hold the physical collateral of the region's most desperate debtors.

---

## The Myth

The [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] never leaves its true wealth exposed in [[Calveno]]. The earliest Envoy to the Shattered Sea built a hidden depository on an uncharted, jungle-choked limestone spire somewhere in the [[Midchain]], designed to hold the physical collateral of captains, governors, and merchants who could not meet their notes.

---

## What Is Known

The vault is not a simple treasure hoard. It is a localized museum of institutional desperation — the accumulated collateral of every debtor who surrendered physical assets rather than face default. Known contents from oral tradition include:

- Masterwork weapons surrendered by captains who couldn't pay
- Magical family heirlooms
- Locked chests of trade-bars from governors and colonial administrators

The vault predates the modern Concordat structure. The "First Factor" refers to the founding Envoy role — an early Tessarine title for the regional representative. No current Tessarine official will confirm the vault's existence, which most observers interpret as confirmation.

---

## The Catch

The vault's defenses are entirely magical and entirely lethal to the uncredentialed:

- No conventional traps — pressure plates, locks, and mechanical triggers have never been reported
- Very advanced magical protections of an unspecified nature
- No one who has entered without proper Tessarine credentials has returned
- The Concordat has never acknowledged losses or described what happens to intruders

The vault's location on an uncharted limestone spire in the jungle interior of the Midchain means finding it is itself a significant navigation challenge. The spire is not on any public chart.

---

## Tessarine Context

The existence of the Vault represents a structural fact about how the [[Tessarine-Concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] operates: collateral is not symbolic. When a debtor surrenders physical assets, those assets go somewhere. The Vault of the First Factor is the most credible answer to where.

Current Concordat operations in [[Calveno]] are believed to maintain a separate, modern accounting system for live collateral. The Vault of the First Factor holds historical collateral from the early colonial period — assets from debtors long dead, whose estates no longer exist to reclaim them.
