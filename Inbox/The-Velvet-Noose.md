# The *Velvet Noose*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> A four-deck man-of-war fills the horizon before the sound reaches you. Her hull is black lacquer over dark red underpaint, banded in dull iron from waterline to gunwale. Three mastheads fly long velvet pennants the colour of dried wine. The gunports are closed, all ninety of them, and that is worse than if they were open. At the prow, a gilded figurehead of a laughing magistrate holds a silk rope in one hand and a broken crown seal in the other.

---

## Overview

The *Velvet Noose* is a Tier 4 pirate man-of-war: not a raider, not a privateer, not a ship that hides in a reef channel and hopes the patrol passes. She is a moving crisis. When she appears, captains calculate surrender terms, harbourmasters find reasons to delay paperwork, and the [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi Crown]] starts asking which squadron is close enough to matter.

Her hull is too large to be new-built in secret. Everyone with eyes knows she began life as somebody's naval project. Nobody agrees whose. The visible facts are harder to dispute: iron-banded black hull, red underpaint showing through old shot scars, reinforced stern galleries, and three full fighting decks designed for line battle. The pirate refit did not make her elegant. It made her humiliating. A warship meant to impose law now forces law to negotiate.

The *Velvet Noose* does not chase small prizes. She takes convoys, ransom officials, break blockades, shelter outlaw vessels, and carry enough cannon to make a fortified harbour choose between pride and architecture. Her best weapon is the first hour after she is sighted, when everyone with authority aboard the target starts arguing about who is allowed to surrender.

## Layout

### Weather Deck

The weather deck is ceremonial and practical in equal measure. The wheel and command rail sit under a stern canopy of black canvas and red tassels. Signal stations run along both rails, with false flags stored in lacquered lockers. The mainmast carries the high lens cage, a glass-and-brass observatory visible from half a mile away when the sun catches it. Boarding nets, swivel mounts, and marine posts sit where a naval officer would expect clear deck. The *Noose* wants boarders to discover the mistake after committing.

### Upper Gun Deck

The upper gun deck carries lighter cannon, officer berths, the Velvet gaming salon, and the false commission room. This is where surrendered captains are brought first: not below, not in chains, but to a polished table where their documents are corrected in front of them. The ship's style is violence in velvet gloves. Prisoners are offered wine before the ransom note is read aloud.

### Main Gun Deck

The main gun deck is the argument. Long cannon run almost the full length of the ship, with arcane artillery mounts placed near the central hatches where their crews can be protected. The marine drill deck occupies the clear space between gun teams when the guns are secured. The Armoured Magazine sits behind reinforced doors, with powder, shot, alchemical charges, and boarding arms catalogued by a quartermaster who trusts nobody.

### Lower Gun Deck

The lower gun deck sits close enough to the waterline that ports only open in good conditions. It carries the heaviest conventional guns, spare carriages, pump crews, and the hull repair foundry. This is where the ship shows its defended variant: iron bands, doubled interior bracing, patched shot scars left visible because hiding them would be vulgar.

### Orlop

The orlop holds the Glasshouse infirmary, Defender barracks, Sacred Hold, storm-glass navigation chapel, and the first secured cargo partitions. The Sacred Hold is not a warm chapel. It is a room of storm tokens, debt markers, saltwater bowls, and names of ships that paid too late. The Navigator's Sanctum sits just aft of it, because superstition and navigation overlap more often than officers admit.

### Hold

The hold is a political weapon. Three black cargo vaults divide prize cargo from ransom baggage, seized weapons, and evidence that keeps important people obedient. The Captain's Archive and Outlaw compact office sit above the aft vaults, guarded by marines and clerks who can make a stolen cargo look legally inconvenient in six different jurisdictions.

### Stern Gallery

The stern gallery houses the Silk-rope audience chamber and the Captain's Locker. This is where the flagship receives envoys, hostage negotiators, defecting officers, and captains who want protection badly enough to call it alliance. The room is beautiful in a way that makes people touch nothing.

## Crew

The *Velvet Noose* can be sailed by sixty but requires a full complement to command fear properly. At strength she carries sailors, gunners, marines, prize crews, clerks, doctors, quartermasters, signal officers, shipwrights, cooks, fences, and enough specialists that a captured vessel can be crewed and sent away within the hour.

## Operational Notes

**Preferred hunting ground:** Not reefs. Not alleys. The *Velvet Noose* works deep-water pressure points: Galewall recovery lanes, Crown convoy routes, Maw-adjacent salvage corridors, and harbours whose guns are pointed the wrong way.

**Opening move:** She sends terms before firing. The terms are insulting, precise, and legally literate. If refused, she disables the escort, captures the signal officer, and sends the same terms again with a shorter deadline.

**Escape plan:** She does not run fast. She runs early. Fog batteries, false fleet signals, Spirit Crew night watches, bribed pilots, and prearranged harbour debts give her options before a superior force arrives.

**Reputation:** Notorious. Some captains surrender because the stories say crews are treated better when nobody wastes powder. Some refuse because being known as the captain who yielded to the *Noose* can ruin a family name faster than dying.

**Strategic weakness:** She is too large to hide and too expensive to ignore. Every victory creates witnesses, claims, widows, admirals, and clerks. The ship survives by making each enemy ask whether someone else should pay the first cost.

**PC gravity:** The ship pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]] with the end-stage version of privateer ambition: a fleet, a flag, and no clean law left beneath it. It pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Perrin-Black-Jaw]] with the possibility of a floating power base too public for the Passage's normal methods. It pressures [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Stripes-Bitemore]] and [[Crissdalynn-Khinriss]] as a magical command platform whose systems reward expertise but corrupt every chain of authority around them.

## At The Table

- As an enemy, the *Velvet Noose* should change the map, not just start combat.
- As a prize, she is campaign-altering and immediately contested by every major naval, commercial, and religious power.
- As an ally, she solves impossible naval problems in ways that make the party complicit.
- As a rumour, she makes convoys delay, patrols consolidate, and smugglers charge more.
