## Overview

He is the largest man on the ship and also the most pleased to see you.

Thunk stands at the stern gun with the ease of someone who has been in this spot a long time and found it agreeable. He is broad across the shoulders in the way a smithy produces — not trained for it, just built up by years of work that left no other option. His hands are the tell: huge, knuckled, cross-hatched with scars running specific directions, the kind of damage that comes from knowing exactly where a hot surface is and touching it slightly wrong a predictable number of times. He keeps them clean. He cares about his tools.

When the party finds him, he is doing something to the cannon. Not anxiously — methodically. He looks up with an expression of complete, unironic warmth that assumes it's mutual, and says: *"Ah. You are the new crew, ja?"* He nods. *"Very good fight. I heard it from here. The explosions — very nice."*

He is complimenting them sincerely. He found the explosions impressive. He is not concerned that this is the primary thing he noticed.

## Before the Sea

He came from [[Port-Tidefall]] — eventually. Before that, somewhere else.

> [!DM] Not public knowledge — party has not asked
> Thunk holds a master's degree in chemistry from his homeland. His homeland has a recognizable academic tradition; it simply is not recognized in the Shattered Sea, and he has not volunteered the information because it has not come up.
>
> He talks the way he talks because everyone from home talks that way. It is not a register of intelligence. The party has clocked the accent and the size and the warmth and concluded something about him. They are wrong.
>
> He is not performing simplicity. He is not hiding depth. He is just himself, in a place where himself reads as a different kind of person than he is.

He worked the Crown Shipyards at Port Tidefall for eleven years — cannon casting first, then fitting, then repairs on pieces that had seen field use and come back bent in interesting ways. He was the one they called when a breach looked like a metallurgy problem rather than an operator problem. It usually wasn't, but he was always polite about it.

He produced work that held. Other people noticed. He received a commendation from the Crown Ordnance Office in his sixth year, which he kept in a tin.

The problem was not the work. The problem was that the shipyards were in Port Tidefall, which has a harbor, and the harbor has ships, and the ships have guns, and guns are where the interesting part of his work actually went. He watched them sail out. He would have liked to see what happened next.

*"The sea,"* he will explain to anyone who asks, *"she is very loud. I cannot sleep sometimes."*

This is literal. He found it pleasant. Eventually he decided that indicated something.

## The Surety

Getting a gunner posting on a Crown cutter required a year of campaigning — not political, just persistent. He showed up. He failed the written portion of the examination. He then performed the practical so correctly, so quickly, and with such apparent lack of effort that the examining officer passed him anyway and did not write down what the written score had been. The Ordnance Office took him on. It was cheaper than arguing about it.

He joined the [[HCS-Surety]] as rated Gunner under [[Barnaby-Rook|Barnaby Rook]]. He maintained both 6-pounders from that day. He knew their serial numbers. He had opinions about their individual quirks — the bow gun ran a fraction hot on the third consecutive charge; the stern gun had a touchhole that needed clearing more often than specification suggested. He wrote this down in a small notebook.

He was below with the rest of the crew during the boarding. He was not afraid. He was listening for which charges had gone off, counting the discharge intervals, and concluding that whoever was running guns on the attacking ship had either good training or good luck. He found this interesting. He has not yet learned it was nobody.

When Grigori gave the nod and the locked door opened, Thunk was last out. Not because he was afraid to go first. It simply did not occur to him to be in a hurry.

## His Gunnery

He counts as two crew when manning a ship weapon. This is not a figure of speech. He runs the loading cycle — charge, shot, ram, prime — at a pace that assumes a second loader who consistently isn't there. He compensates. He has always compensated.

He also has opinions about powder. He smells a charge and says it is wrong, or right, or almost. He is correct more often than anyone who can explain their reasoning. He will share his opinions if asked and will be unable to justify any of them beyond *"I don't know — she just smells like that."*

Then, in the same breath, without registering the shift, he will tell you that black powder is made from saltpeter, nitrogen, and carbon — dense carbon chains, specifically — that it is a flammable explosive and not shock-sensitive, which means friction and impact will not set it off; it requires fire, always fire. He offers this the way another man might mention the weather. He does not notice it lands differently than the rest of what he says.

> [!DM]
> When Thunk mans a cannon or ship weapon, he counts as two crew for the purpose of determining how many weapons can be manned simultaneously. A ship that normally needs four crew to work both 6-pounders needs only three with Thunk on a gun.
>
> He is proficient with smith's tools and can operate a **Carpenter's Shop** (Smithy facility) without additional staff if one is installed on the Surety. He will have views on how it should be organized. He will be correct about all of them.
>
> He hired on at Tidefall rate for a rated specialist. Whatever Rook was actually paying him, he has not done the arithmetic carefully — he assumes it was fair because the paperwork looked official.
>
> **The gimmick:** Thunk reads as a one-note joke — enormous, warm, slightly dim, very good at cannons. The party clocked this in Session 03. Then he casually explained black powder chemistry and noted that it is not shock-sensitive or friction-sensitive, only fire — delivered with the same flat affect as everything else. Drop this whenever it feels natural: a correct physics observation, an unexpected metallurgy note, a precise stoichiometry comment. He never signals that it's remarkable. The delivery does not change. The reveal, if the party ever gets there, is not that he's secretly smart — it's that he was always this, and they misread him from the start.

## Session History

### Session 03 — The Cannon Lesson

During the five-day crossing to Calveno, [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] sought Thunk out to learn the guns. Thunk's lesson was, by the DM's own assessment, "surprisingly thorough." He correctly broke down black powder composition — saltpeter, nitrogen, carbon chains — and explained the ignition principle: flammable, not shock-sensitive, always fire. Jean-Claude left the lesson gun-proficient.

Shore leave in Calveno: went to a Mercatura tavern with [[content/shattered-sea/characters/crew/Sem-Holst|Sem Holst]] and Jean-Claude, paid for the drinks, had a boys' night. The next morning he went to the Arsenal district alone. Stood at the quay gates long enough to identify a bad mounting bracket on a Crown ordnance barge — wrong casting angle on the pivot collar, will crack on the third or fourth sustained firing. Told a dock hand. The dock hand wrote nothing down. Thunk left. He is not certain anyone will fix it before it matters. He feels fine about this.
