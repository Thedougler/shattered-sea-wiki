# Truth Stone

## Description

A smooth grey stone, unremarkable to look at. It sits warm in the hand from being carried — that warmth is ordinary. What isn't ordinary is what happens when the holder knowingly states a falsehood.

The stone heats rapidly toward white-hot the moment a deliberate lie passes the holder's lips. The holder knows. Anyone watching knows.

## Mechanics

When a creature holding the Truth Stone makes a Deception check — whether the check succeeds or fails — the stone deals **1d6 fire damage** to the holder at the start of each of their turns for 1 minute, unless the stone is dropped or released. This effect **stacks**: each additional Deception check adds another 1d6 per turn to the ongoing damage, and each instance runs its own 1-minute duration.

A creature who does not know their statement is false feels nothing. The stone only reacts to deliberate deception.

Dropping the stone (no action required) ends all ongoing damage immediately.

## Notes

The Truth Stone does not compel honesty — it simply makes lying expensive. A creature willing to endure the damage can attempt deception regardless. Creatures with immunity to fire damage can lie freely while holding it. The DM may rule that extraordinary pain resistance or psychological detachment from consequence also mitigates the effect.

The stone does not distinguish between lies told aloud and elaborate performances — if the creature is consciously attempting to deceive, the stone responds.

## In the Campaign

[[Beaumont-Sel]] carries one as a matter of course. He has run the [[content/shattered-sea/places/midchain/index|Midchain]] for eleven years and has had occasion to use it. He produces it without ceremony and without apology — it is a tool, not a threat, and anyone who has nothing to hide will find it stays cool in their hand without effort.

The stone appeared in Session 02 when Beaumont used it to assess [[content/shattered-sea/characters/player/Jean-Claude-Tabarnack|Jean-Claude Tabarnack]] after the Saltwright boarding. See [[Scene-02b-Deck-Confrontation]].
