# Umberlee's Shrine — Sunken Crown

## Overview

Umberlee's shrine on [[Vel-Orn]] is cut into black stone in the [[Sunken-Crown|Sunken Crown]], where five small islands ring the Blue Hole and the water drops too cleanly from turquoise into dark indigo. Outsiders sometimes call Vel-Orn the shrine island. Locals use the older name, which carries the sense of water-debt.

This is not a bright harbour shrine collecting routine tribute from merchants. It is older, quieter, and pointed more toward the drowned than the living. The [[Waveservants|Waveservants]] here keep records of what the sea has taken and what the sea is owed in return.

## Description

The island has no friendly harbour. It rises sheer from pale reef, with no obvious landing until a boat is nearly on it. The shrine entrance is a mouth in the cliff face, worn smooth by salt spray and years of tide. Kelp wraps the pillars. Candles burn inside where wind should have killed them.

The shrine smells of brine, rendered tallow, wet stone, and something older underneath. Sound behaves strangely in the entrance. Footsteps soften. Water moves where no open wave reaches it.

## Function

The shrine marks debts, keeps drowned names, and preserves the older Umberlee worship of the Blue Hole. Inside, a ledger chamber holds names, tokens, coins, fish bones, carved shells, and bits of ship or sailor preserved as witness. Everything here has weight. Nothing feels decorative.

## Access & Approach

The shrine is not on colonial charts. It requires prior knowledge, a guide, or difficult navigation through dangerous surrounding waters. The approach to Vel-Orn is sheer, reef-bound, and unfriendly to casual landings.

## People & Power

The [[Waveservants|Waveservants]] control the shrine and its records. Their authority is older and quieter than harbour tribute, tied to debt, drowned names, and the Blue Hole rather than routine port religion.

## Notable Features

**Shrine Entrance** — A cliff-mouth worn smooth by salt spray and years of tide, with kelp-wrapped pillars and candles burning where wind should kill them.

**Ledger Chamber** — Holds names, tokens, coins, fish bones, carved shells, and preserved ship or sailor fragments as witness.

**Deepest Chamber** — DM-only: formerly held the [[content/shattered-sea/situations/reference/Pearl-of-Souls-Item|Pearl of Souls]] over a tidal pool built to its requirements.

## Flora

Kelp wraps the shrine pillars. Other flora is not yet documented.

## Fauna

The surrounding waters are patrolled by massive rays and unnamed deep things. They are present, not automatically hostile.

## Hazards

The shrine's hazards are hidden approach, reef-bound landing, sacred boundaries, water-debt, deep-water watchers, and the consequences of the missing Pearl of Souls.
