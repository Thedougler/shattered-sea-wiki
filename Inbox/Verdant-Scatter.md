# The Verdant Scatter

## Overview

The Verdant Scatter is the primary archipelago of the [[Shattered-Sea|Shattered Sea]], running east to west between the [[Galewall|Galewall]] and the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]. It is not one island chain so much as two arcs with a shipping road between them.

The northern arc is the [[Crown-Islands|Crown Islands]]: five larger, mountainous islands with deep harbours, forested highlands, fortifications, inspections, and the Dravosi Crown's strongest regional foothold.

The southern arc is the [[Midchain|Midchain]]: dozens of smaller islands packed close together, with free ports, reefs, local councils, pilot families, and routes that make more sense to people who learned them from grandparents than from charts.

Between them runs the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]], the main navigable corridor through the region. To the east, both arcs narrow and converge at [[Tail|the Tail]], where the water grows colder and the Maw starts shaping every decision.

## Geography

The Scatter runs east-west between the storm boundary of the [[Galewall|Galewall]] and the deep-water boundary of the [[Drowned-Maw|Drowned Maw]]. Its northern and southern arcs form a broad island-chain system divided by the [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]]. The arcs converge at [[Tail|the Tail]], where the water grows colder, reefs grow meaner, and Maw-adjacent navigation begins shaping every decision.

## Routes & Access

The [[Central-Strait|Central Strait]] is the obvious route through the Scatter, but not the only one. Crown inspections, Midchain back channels, reef changes, local pilots, storm windows, and pirate pressure all affect which route is actually usable. The eastern [[Tail|Tail]] narrows choices further as compass drift and Maw weather become relevant.

## People & Power

The northern arc carries the [[Dravosi-Crown|Dravosi Crown]]'s strongest regional authority. The southern arc belongs more to island councils, free ports, pilot families, market bosses, Grung trade boundaries, and local arrangements that do not fit comfortably into a colonial ledger.

## Trade & Resources

The Scatter moves inspected Crown cargo, free-port repair work, fish, shellfish, local market goods, Grung exports, salvage traffic, and contraband. Its economy depends on knowing when the official route is worth the price and when the local route is worth the risk.

## Flora

The Scatter is green, wet, and reef-bound: forested Crown highlands, Midchain rainforest, mangroves, wet margins, and island agriculture. Specific plants are documented on narrower region and island pages.

## Fauna

The Scatter's land threats change with the island underfoot. The Crown highlands have [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-boar|giant boars]] and sinkhole predators; [[Midchain|Midchain]] interiors are better known for [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-spider|giant spiders]], [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-venomous-snake|giant venomous snakes]], and wet-country ambush hunters. Islanders do not talk about these as wilderness trivia. They are part of route planning.

The Crown Island highlands carry [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-boar|giant boars]] in the older interior forest — a chronic hazard for logging crews and trail parties that have let their noise discipline slip. They are not the island chain's most dramatic threat, but they are consistent, and a sounder pushing through dense undergrowth does not give much warning.

The Midchain interiors are different country. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-spider|Giant spiders]] occupy the canopy and the older hollow-tree growth; routes through dense island jungle are assessed by pilots and guides partly by spider sign before anything else. [[content/shattered-sea/beastiary/beasts/giant-venomous-snake|Giant venomous snakes]] work the wet margins, river mouths, and flooded ground — slow until they are not, and common enough in certain island interiors that locals treat them as a route-planning variable rather than a wildlife oddity. Midchain islanders do not go into the deep interior at night without people who know the ground.

## Hazards

Its pirate threats scale the same way as its geography. The [[Knifes-Wake|Knife's Wake]] is the kind of cutter that makes a single bad channel choice expensive. The [[The-Bad-Receipt|Bad Receipt]] turns Midchain paperwork and repair harbours into a weapon. The [[The-Glass-Debt|Glass Debt]] is what happens when piracy grows large enough that convoys, patrols, and councils all have to react.

## Current Hooks

- The official Crown route and the local Midchain route both work, but they create different enemies and different costs.
- The eastern convergence at the [[Tail|Tail]] is where salvage staging, Maw crossings, longline work, and pirate pressure increasingly overlap.
