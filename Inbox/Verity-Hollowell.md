## Overview

Verity Hollowell is the older daughter of Cedric Hollowell, whose import agency operates out of offices near the [[Port-Tidefall|Tidefall]] inspection pier. She came into the ledger work sideways — the plan was never for her to run the accounts, but there was a series of events that revealed she caught errors faster than the men paid to catch them, and eventually her father stopped pretending the arrangement was temporary.

She met a sailor once on a shore leave dock, years ago. He was moving alone in the dark — something precise and physical and private, not the kind of dancing anyone puts in the public square. She watched for a while from the edge of the pier. Then she stepped in, because it seemed like a waste to keep watching something like that at a distance. They spent several hours like that — no names, no conversation that needed words, just movement and the sound of the water under the dock. Then a ship's bell called him back and he went.

She returned to that dock on subsequent evenings for a long time. He was not there.

Then her father went instead, one evening, and waited.

She does not speak about what was said, or what she said when she found out. She has continued her ledger work. She is careful now about what she brings home.

---

## Role

If the party arrives in Port Tidefall and Geoffrey's name surfaces, she already knows it is him. Her father's handling of the dock situation reached her through inference rather than direct report; the name she eventually connected to the face she remembered came through other channels. She does not volunteer this. She answers what is asked.

She is more useful than her father in most practical conversations — she has the same Tidefall access and considerably better judgment about when to use it. The party will figure this out approximately one exchange into talking to Cedric Hollowell.

---

## Appearance & Manner

She is twenty-four. She has dark hair kept pinned under a practical hat when she is working, and a particular stillness that people sometimes mistake for disinterest until they notice she has already catalogued everything in the room before the conversation started.

## Voice

Level. Complete sentences. Does not reach for warmth or frost — both cost something she is not spending yet on strangers. Answers the question asked, does not expand unless there is a reason.

> *"The secondary accounts are mine. The primary ones are my father's. If you need access to pier scheduling, that runs through my desk."*

> *"I'm aware of the Surety. Several factors have been watching that vessel's status. It has been missing from port logs for some time."*
