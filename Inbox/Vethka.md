# *Vethka*

> [!READ-ALOUD]
> Low on the water — lower than any cutter you have seen. The hull is carved from a single timber dark as heartwood, sealed with amber resin that catches the light like wet skin. Two outrigger floats ride the surface to port and starboard, lashed to the hull with braided vine cord that shows no sign of weakening. The bow is carved into a blunt open jaw, painted in purple and blue in alternating stripe-bands, and a line of small bones — finger-length, drilled and strung — hangs from the prow to the first outrigger crossbeam. There is no wheel, no cabin, no raised deck. The hull is open from stem to stern. Twelve Grung can crouch below the rail without showing a silhouette. You smell resin first. Underneath that, something sour and sharp that is not the sea.

---

## Overview

*Vethka* is the Grung term for this class of vessel — not a proper name but a caste-designation: roughly, the waterline that moves toward you. Every raiding proa built to the standard pattern is called *vethka* by those who crew them. The Colonial powers call them raiding canoes. Neither name is quite right.

The hull is single-piece: a trunk of Verdant Teeth ironpillar hardwood, adze-worked by orange-caste artisans over several weeks, sealed inside and out with tree resin boiled down from the Teeth's interior stands. The result is a hull that does not absorb water, does not swell in the rain, and sheds the spray of a following sea without effort. It is not soft wood and it does not give. When a *vethka* strikes a beach at speed, the beach loses.

The double outrigger arrangement — one float per side, lashed on crossed vine-wood spars — gives the proa stability through reef passages without sacrificing the flat profile that lets it move unseen at waterline. From more than half a mile away on a dark night, a *vethka* running low with no sail set reads as debris. This is by design.

## Caste and Crew

The *vethka* is a blue-and-purple operation. Blue-caste scouts navigate and pilot; they know the coastal approaches, the tide windows, the harbour markers, and the distance at which a lamp on shore stops seeing anything that isn't lit. Purple-caste warriors handle the assault and the restraint of captives taken in bondage raids. Green-caste do not crew raiding vessels. Red and gold do not get wet.

Orange-caste artisans build the *vethka* at interior yards on Veth and Karath, working under red-caste specification. The poison preparation benches — forward of amidships, sealed with a flat resin panel — are red-caste design: a [[Narrow]] work surface for coating weapons before the approach. Purple-caste warriors touch that surface. No other caste does.

## Construction

**Hull.** Single-trunk ironpillar hardwood. Orange-caste adze and chisel work, no saw. The interior is hollowed to a hull wall of roughly two inches — thick enough to resist reef strikes, thin enough to keep weight low. Sealed with boiled resin applied hot.

**Outriggers.** Two floats carved from lighter timber, lashed to crossbeam spars with braided vine cord renewed before each use. The lashing is blue-caste work; blue scouts are responsible for inspection and replacement. A loose outrigger lashing in a following sea is a kill on the crew.

**Sail.** A single mat-weave sail on a short mast stepped at the forward third of the hull. Woven from palm fronds by green-caste labour under orange-caste direction. The sail stows flat under the forward hull lip in seconds. The *vethka* can cover the approach to a target under paddle only, which it does whenever the target is close enough to hear canvas.

**Paddles.** Fourteen paddle stations, seven per side. All crew paddle when the sail is stowed. Grung Standing Leap means boarding from the hull rim is faster than any rope ladder — the crew exits over the rail the moment the bow hits beach.

**Bone cord.** The hanging bone strand at the prow is raid-specific: drilled finger-bones strung on vine cord, one bone added per successful operation. It is blue-caste log-keeping and purple-caste trophy at the same time.

---

## Poison Work

The preparation bench is a flat sealed surface forward of amidships, recessed below the rail. Before approach, purple-caste coat darts, spear tips, and climbing grips with prepared toxin drawn from sealed bone tubes carried aboard. The coating is done in silence, on blue-caste signal, once the target is confirmed and the approach angle is set.

The bench is never cleaned. Old resin from previous coatings builds up across the surface — archaeologists would find it extraordinary; anyone who touched it uninstructed would find it fatal within minutes.

## At Sea

The *vethka* does not fight other ships. It is not built to. A raiding proa caught in open water by anything with gun mounts runs — and it is usually faster than what is chasing it, at least in good wind through reef passages where a keel-heavy vessel cannot follow. Tier 2 and above ships cannot navigate the shallow back-channels the *vethka* uses by design; pursuing a *vethka* into Verdant Teeth reef country is a decision that ends careers.

If a *vethka* crew cannot run, they swim. Grung are amphibious. Abandoning the hull is not defeat; it is the last contingency built into the design. A *vethka* crew that goes over the side in familiar reef waters will reassemble on shore before an opposing crew can mount a pursuit. The hull is replaceable. The crew knowledge is not.
