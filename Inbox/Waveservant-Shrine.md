# Waveservant Shrine — Calveno

## Overview

The Waveservant Shrine is [[Umberlee|Umberlee]]'s waterfront shrine in [[Calveno]], set into a pale stone arch along the Mercatura canal front where boats pass close enough to read the posted tribute terms.

## Description

The shrine opens directly onto the canal — a low arch of pale Calveno stone, deep enough to block the worst of the wind off the water, shallow enough that anyone on a passing boat can see who is inside and what they are doing. The basin is fed from the channel directly; the Waveservants in Calveno stopped carrying seawater years ago because there was no point. A ring of tallow candles burns behind a glass screen. The posted terms face outward, toward the canal, not the door.

In a city that runs on witnessed transactions, the shrine fits without effort. Captains pay. Boats drift past. The record of who paid and who did not is maintained in the usual Calveno way: by everyone who happened to be looking.

## Function

The Waveservants operate on a tithe-and-communion model. Mariners pay before they sail or after they survive. Both are acceptable. Neither is optional. A junior attendant manages the candles and basin; a senior [[Waveservants|Waveservant]] manages everything else.

## People & Operations

A junior attendant manages the basin top-off and the candle screen. A senior [[Waveservants|Waveservant]] handles tribute collection, blessing, and the ledger. In Calveno the ledger is kept more formally than at most ports — witnessed commerce has a culture here, and the shrine absorbed it.

## Access & Security

Open to anyone approaching by canal or on foot along the Mercatura. The obligation to pay before sailing or after surviving is treated, in Calveno, the same way any commercial obligation is treated: publicly, efficiently, and with the understanding that defaulting on it is a kind of public announcement.

## Current Hooks

- A captain who skips tribute on departure and returns to Calveno has made a visible, remembered choice. The city will know before the Waveservants do.
