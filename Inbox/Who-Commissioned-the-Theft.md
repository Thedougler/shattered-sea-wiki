# Who Commissioned the Pearl Theft

## Situation

Four months before the campaign opens, a proposal reached the [[Chain-Council|Chain Council]] through [[Detto-Kalash|Detto Kalash]]. It warned that the [[Midchain]] was exposed to Umberlee's wrath and claimed the divine object at Vel-Orn could be moved before that wrath fell on the islands. The proposal offered a clean solution: hire [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk|Delmar Fisk]] and Fisk's Fleet to recover the relic, protect Midchain waters, and keep the Council's hands technically clean.

The Council believed it was buying protection. It was giving the Tessarine Concordat's hidden operative a local mandate, a privateer fleet, and plausible deniability.

The commission documents identify an unnamed third party as "the Principal" and use a Council representative as the visible authorizing hand. The Principal's real brief was not simply "steal the Pearl." It was "keep the Pearl away from [[Aldric-Drave|Aldric Drave]]." Tessarine intelligence believes Aldric could use the [[content/shattered-sea/items/Pearl-of-Souls|Pearl of Souls]] to push his Hierarch abilities beyond his own bloodline, turning a bloodline-bound survival engine into something that can draw on souls gathered outside the Dravosi genealogy. The Council never heard that part.

The result was the theft of the Pearl of Souls, Umberlee's retaliation against Fisk's Fleet, and the opening crisis now spreading through the Sunken Crown and Drowned Maw.

## Key Actors

- [[Chain-Council]] — authorized the commission unknowingly; believed they were protecting the Midchain; can be turned from employer into witness
- [[content/shattered-sea/private/players/Delmar-Fisk]] — executed the commission; believed he was hired by the Midchain's collective authority
- **The Principal** — unnamed in commission documents; now identified as [[Cosimo-Verantio|Cosimo Verantio]], a hidden Tessarine operative
- [[Detto-Kalash|Detto Kalash]] — carried the sealed proposal as an ordinary courier; his Tessarine correspondence route laundered the approach
- [[Rael-Corven|Rael Corven]] — flag captain whose documents aboard the Red Lady connect back to the commission
- [[Aldric-Drave|Aldric Drave]] — Dravosi founder-lich; the Principal's true target; Tessarine intelligence wanted to intercept the Pearl before he could reach it
- [[Tessarine-Concordat]] — institution whose methods and correspondence routes made the approach possible

## Who Knew What

| Actor | What They Believed | What Was True |
|---|---|---|
| Chain Council representatives | The Pearl could protect the Midchain if removed from Vel-Orn. | Removing it destabilized the shrine and helped open the fissure crisis. |
| Council signatory | The Principal was a backer with information and money. | The Principal was using Council authority as a mask to keep the Pearl from Aldric Drave. |
| Detto Kalash | He was carrying a sealed proposal as a courier courtesy. | His ordinary Tessarine correspondence route laundered the approach. |
| Delmar Fisk | He was hired by the Midchain's collective authority. | He was hired through the Council by someone exploiting it. |
| Rael Corven | He was monitoring a salvage and intelligence problem. | His documents connect back to the same operation. |

## Evidence the Party Can Find

- The Red Lady Intelligence Room documents (at the Shelfworks wreck site)
- A sealed letter aboard the Red Lady naming "the Principal"
- Evidence that Tessarine correspondence routes carried the proposal (traceable through Detto Kalash's route)
- Proof that the Pearl's removal endangered the Sunken Crown rather than protected the Midchain
- Chain Council session records in Kalowe, if the party gains access

---

## What the Council Can Do With This

Once the party brings evidence, the Council becomes a political vehicle rather than a dormant employer. It can:

- Vote to fund a search or rescue operation
- Issue a Midchain warning
- Revoke a commission
- Protect witnesses
- Authorize harbour denial against a named vessel
- Quietly ask the [[Passage|Passage]] to move documents no official courier should touch

The Council will not act cleanly. It will argue, assign blame, count costs, and protect itself first. The useful question becomes who wants the truth public, who wants the Principal named, and who wants the Midchain to survive the scandal without becoming easier for the Crown or Concordat to occupy.

## What Advances It

- The party reaching the Shelfworks and accessing the Red Lady Intelligence Room
- Any party member tracing the commission back through Detto Kalash's courier route
- Evidence that the Pearl's removal caused the fissure crisis reaching a Council representative
- The Tessarine Concordat attempting to suppress or purchase the evidence before it surfaces

---

## What Blocks It

- The Red Lady wreck is in the boundary zone — reaching it requires solving the Maw approach problem
- Tessarine operatives will move to contain the evidence once they learn it surfaced
- The Council's first instinct is denial and blame-assignment, not action
- The party may not know the commission and the Pearl crisis are the same thread

If the evidence surfaces but is not acted on: frightened representatives deny collective responsibility, smaller islands blame Kalowe, and Tessarine factors offer private legal protection to anyone willing to describe the commission as a rogue Council session.
