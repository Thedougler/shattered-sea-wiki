## Overview

Calm, slight, and currently in the medic's cabinet doing inventory. She finds this calming. Before any of this started, she reorganized the cabinet after a loose shelf tried to make itself her problem, then returned to dinner without mentioning it.

She discusses triage and recovery with clinical warmth — as if everyone in the room shares her comfort with medical detail. They do not, but she has never quite registered this.

She kept the Murrat casualties alive. She is precise about the limits of her own work: *"They pulled through because of him. I'll say that plainly."* She means [[wiki/situations/islands/shepherd-grigori|Shepherd Grigori]]. She does not know what that credit means, and she is not wrong to give it.

## Aboard Ship

She will announce, without particular inflection, that healer's kit supply is adequate for two more field engagements and that the surgical instruments are well-maintained except for one probe that needs replacing. She is not asking to stay. She is not asking to leave. She is performing the inventory because someone has to and she is the most qualified.

## Recruitment

She wants that fact acknowledged — that she is the most qualified, that this is a real post, that the Surgeon's Berth is a real facility and not a cabinet someone stocked once. Formally name the role — **Surgeon**, ship's medical officer, something that constitutes an actual position — and she stays.

If the party mentions they intend to keep the Surgeon's Berth staffed and stocked, she looks at the cabinet the way Holst looked at the hull: assessing what it could be.

> [!dm]
> She is the only trained surgeon in this crew. Without her, Beaumont's comment that "the cabinet is stocked but Alys has been handling things" becomes "nobody is handling things." She also fills the Surgeon's Berth bastion facility requirement the moment she's formally hired. The recruitment bar is low: just call it a real job.
