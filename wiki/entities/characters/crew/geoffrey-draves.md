## Overview

He doesn't look like a turncoat.

Geoffrey Draves stands easy on deck, loose-limbed, not quite at rest, fidgeting with a length of rope that probably doesn't need doing. He's young, lean for a sailor, and his expression is the carefully neutral kind that takes practice to maintain.

During the boarding of the [[saltwright|Saltwright]], when the fight turned and the men around him went down faster than he could make sense of it, he dropped his sword.

[[barnaby-rook|Barnaby Rook]]'s voice cut across the deck: *"I'll shoot you myself for that, turncoat."*

[[delmar-fisk|Delmar Fisk]] looked at him. "Would you rather fight for men who'll threaten to shoot you, or men who'll protect you?"

*"I'll do whatever keeps me alive today."* Delmar handed him his sword back. He was the party's first crew member — before they had a ship to put him on.

## Before the Sea

Geoffrey didn't choose the water. His father chose it for him. He was good at it, in the resigned way capable people are good at things they never wanted.

His father's connections produced a berth on the [[hcs-surety|HCS Surety]] — not an officer's post, a carpenter's posting, plus marine duties on boarding operations for a supplement. Seven and a half gold a week, which sounded like progress. He has not yet done the full arithmetic. A rated marine earns fourteen gold weekly, plus the carpenter rate on top. [[barnaby-rook|Barnaby Rook]] knew. Crew costs are operational line items. The arrangement was not an accident.

At night, below deck, in the gaps between watches, he danced. Contemporary movement, nimble and precise, the kind of dancing that has no place on a naval vessel. He was certain no one had ever seen him. On shore leave, someone did.

A young woman — [[verity-hollowell|Verity Hollowell]] — caught him mid-movement and didn't laugh. They spent that night dancing under open sky. They came close to a first kiss. Then the ship's bell called him back.

He returned to that same spot every time his ship made port. For years, she wasn't there. Then one evening, her father, [[cedric-hollowell|Cedric Hollowell]], was. The terms were simple: Geoffrey was not a man of means. Until he could provide for a wife and family for the rest of their days, he was not to see her again.

Geoffrey agreed. He didn't see another choice.

## The Vow

That conversation changed something in him. He stopped drifting and started calculating. The plan hardened into a single shape: earn enough as a sailor, become a professional dancer in the Grand Opera Halls, marry the woman he'd spent years trying to find again. The end would justify any means. He was prepared to sail with vagabonds if that was what it took.

It's been just shy of a year since he made that vow.

## His Carpentry

He can read a hull the way a good medic reads a patient: which seams need attention now, which can wait a week, which look fine but won't hold through the crossing being planned. If the party acquires a ship, Geoffrey can run actual maintenance — not patch-and-pray, but a real assessment of what the vessel needs and in what order, mostly done himself given materials and time.

With the party, his terms are carpenter only, six gold a week, no boarding duties.

## Session History

**Session 01:** Defected mid-boarding when [[delmar-fisk|Delmar]] offered protection. Party's first crew member.

**Session 03 (transit):** Delmar training him — being called "Mr. James" throughout. Delmar told him they are not going to a second location alone (context: Umberlee's servant situation). Ashore in Calveno on shore leave.

**Shore Leave — Calveno:** First evening ashore he found a quiet stretch of Le Paludi canal away from the noise and danced alone for two hours. Nobody watched. He was back aboard before anyone noticed he'd been gone.
