## Overview

Fifteen, maybe. Was behind the water barrels the whole fight and has now emerged with the energy of someone who has been waiting to be useful. They had a sailing manual open and were reading it while standing up.

They know every sailing manual by heart. They have sailed on almost none of them. This gap between theory and experience is the thing they are most actively trying to close, and any ship going somewhere new is a better classroom than Port Tidefall.

[[wiki/situations/islands/shepherd-grigori|Shepherd Grigori]] referred to them obliquely as *"our guest below is useful in a galley"* — which is true. They learned from watching, which is how they learn everything.

## Aboard Ship

They are the easiest crew member to direct and the most likely to ask follow-up questions. They manage provisions, keep the galley running on voyages over three days, and do whatever else needs doing with genuine enthusiasm. They will check the manual for any situation they haven't encountered yet. If the situation isn't in the manual, they find this a point in its favor.

## Recruitment

They haven't decided where they're going and they want, more than anything, to be on a ship that goes somewhere interesting. Name any destination that isn't immediately familiar and they check the manual. If it's not in the manual, they look thoughtful in a way that means yes.

There is no bar to clear. They are already staying if anyone asks.

> [!dm]
> Noor is the most low-maintenance recruit in this crew. The only scenario where they leave is if the party explicitly names a destination so boring that even Noor's enthusiasm can't find a foothold — somewhere like "back to Port Tidefall, permanently." Any forward motion keeps them. They are not complicated. They are fifteen and they want to go somewhere.
