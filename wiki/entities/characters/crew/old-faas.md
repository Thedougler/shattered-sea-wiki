## Overview

Old Faas is a rigger of uncertain age who has outlasted several captains, a number of ships, and at least two situations that should have killed him outright. He answers questions with short, accurate sentences. He does not raise his voice. Very little surprises him, and the things that do surprise him do not seem to bother him either.

He was the one who told the party about Murrat, flatly, between courses: *"He took us to Murrat. Wanted a live one. Went ashore with six crew. Four came back needing the surgeon."* He reported it the way you report a weather event you survived.

He returned from shore leave in [[calveno|Calveno]] with two peg legs.

## The Head

When Faas was seven years old, he fell from a hay loft and landed on his head on the stone floor of a barn. He was unconscious for three days. When he woke up, something was gone.

He does not experience fear. Not suppressed fear, not managed fear — simply absent, the way a man born without a sense of smell does not smell things. He will tell you this if you ask. He does not think it is interesting.

> [!dm]
> This is not a death wish. He is not trying to die; he simply cannot feel the thing that would stop him from doing dangerous things. No anxiety about heights, no dread when outnumbered, no instinct to flinch. He is not brave. Bravery requires something to overcome. He just goes ahead and does it.
>
> He has never mentioned this to a captain. It has never come up.

## The Legs

He returned from shore leave in [[calveno|Calveno]] with two well-fitted peg legs — good timber, brass ferrules, proper socket work. He is slightly shorter than he was. Slightly less stable standing still on a flat surface. He is faster on the rigging.

When asked about the legs, he has given three accounts:

**Account One:** *"The bull. Part of the festival. They were running it through the Mercatura and it got loose near the Bridge. The right leg. I stopped it. Eventually."*

**Account Two:** *"The scaffolding on the south side of the Bridge. The view from the top was better for watching the Beffa in Le Paludi. I could see it was not stable before I climbed. The left leg. I was correct about the view."*

**Account Three:** *"Both legs, actually. The canal under the Mercatura dock. I misjudged the depth. Current took me under the moorings and pinned me between the hull and the stone. Both legs. That was Tuesday."*

He has offered all three accounts to different people with identical conviction.

> [!dm]
> The party will never learn which story is real, if any. If directly confronted with all three stories at once, he will listen carefully, nod, and say: *"Ja. It was a busy week."*
>
> The peg legs do not slow him on the rigging. Do not explain it.

## Aboard Ship

He manages rigging, sails, and on-deck crew. He is the kind of bosun who doesn't raise his voice because the crew learned early that quiet from Faas means worse things than loud. He knows every line on this ship, what it should feel like under tension, and how long it has left.

He will go aloft in any weather. He has no strong opinion about staying or leaving. He is a sailor. There is a ship. That is sufficient.

## Session History

**Session 03 (transit):** Showed [[perrin-black-jaw|Perrin]] the ropes during the five-day crossing to Calveno. Told stories about running cargo with [[barnaby-rook|Rook]] in his old days — an asset, given Rook's history of unusual cargo and contacts.

**Session 03 (dinner):** Told the party about the Murrat expedition flatly, between courses. Six crew went ashore with Rook to capture a live [[moucheron|Moucheron]]. Four came back needing the surgeon. Faas was not among those four. He offered this without prompting and did not elaborate.

**Shore Leave — Calveno:** Returned to the ship with two peg legs. Gave three contradictory accounts of how he lost them over the course of one week. Went back up the rigging approximately forty minutes after returning aboard.
