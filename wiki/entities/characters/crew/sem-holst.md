## Overview

Lean, weathered, sawdust still in his hair. He takes in the party the way he'd take in a cracked frame: assessing damage, not people. He fixes things without being asked and has opinions about how they should have been built in the first place.

He is already cataloguing damage [[geoffrey-draves|Geoffrey]] didn't mention. He will tell you exactly what's wrong with this hull and exactly how long it will take to fix, and he will not soften either number.

## Aboard Ship

He works alongside [[geoffrey-draves|Geoffrey]] and does not resent the reporting line. He is more specialized — hull structure and planking — and he knows it. If the ship takes significant hull damage and both are working, repairs go faster and come out better.

He does not make small talk. He makes assessments. If you ask him how the ship is, he will tell you, in structural terms, with time estimates.

## Recruitment

He wants to know if the party is serious about maintaining the vessel — not what it can do, but the ship itself. If a PC demonstrates they know the difference between a caulked seam and a packed one, or asks a question about the hull that isn't about speed or cargo, he reconsiders.

*"Not many privateers bother learning that."* He says it the way you admit something surprised you. That's the moment. He's in.

> [!dm]
> Any PC with proficiency in carpenter's tools, navigator's tools, or a relevant background (sailor, marine) can make this approach credibly. Asking a specific question about the Surety's current hull condition — and listening to the answer — clears the bar. He doesn't need expertise. He needs evidence of interest.

## Session History

**Session 03 (transit):** Worked hull repairs alongside [[jean-claude-tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] during the five-day crossing to Calveno. JC cast Mending; Sem quietly fixed what JC missed without commenting on it.

**Shore Leave — Calveno:** First morning ashore he was at the dry dock where the *Uncertainty* is being repainted. Found a problem in the forward keel joint — a fatigued timber hiding under fresh caulk, the kind the repaint covered without addressing. Fixable before departure, but needs hardwood stock and at least a day of dry dock access. He documented it in his own notes. Nobody asked him to. He has not mentioned it to the party yet and will not until someone asks him about the ship.
