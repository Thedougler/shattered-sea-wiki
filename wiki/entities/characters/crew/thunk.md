## Overview

He is the largest man on the ship and also the most pleased to see you.

Thunk stands at the stern gun with the ease of someone who has been in this spot a long time and found it agreeable. His hands are the tell: huge, knuckled, cross-hatched with scars from knowing exactly where a hot surface is and touching it slightly wrong a predictable number of times. He keeps them clean. He cares about his tools.

When the party finds him: *"Ah. You are the new crew, ja?"* He nods. *"Very good fight. I heard it from here. The explosions — very nice."*

He is complimenting them sincerely. He found the explosions impressive. He is not concerned that this is the primary thing he noticed.

## Before the Sea

> [!dm]
> Thunk holds a master's degree in chemistry from his homeland. His homeland has a recognizable academic tradition; it is simply not recognized in the Shattered Sea, and he has not volunteered the information because it has not come up.
>
> He talks the way he talks because everyone from home talks that way. It is not a register of intelligence. The party has clocked the accent and the size and the warmth and concluded something about him. They are wrong.

He worked the Crown Shipyards at Port Tidefall for eleven years — cannon casting first, then fitting, then repairs on pieces that had come back bent in interesting ways. He was the one they called when a breach looked like a metallurgy problem rather than an operator problem. He received a commendation from the Crown Ordnance Office in his sixth year, which he kept in a tin.

*"The sea,"* he will explain to anyone who asks, *"she is very loud. I cannot sleep sometimes."* This is literal. He found it pleasant. Eventually he decided that indicated something.

## His Gunnery

He counts as two crew when manning a ship weapon. He runs the loading cycle at a pace that assumes a second loader who consistently isn't there. He compensates. He has always compensated.

He also has opinions about powder. He smells a charge and says it is wrong, or right, or almost. He is correct more often than anyone who can explain their reasoning. He will share his opinions if asked and will be unable to justify any of them beyond *"I don't know — she just smells like that."*

Then, in the same breath, without registering the shift, he will tell you that black powder is made from saltpeter, nitrogen, and carbon — dense carbon chains — that it is a flammable explosive and not shock-sensitive, meaning friction and impact will not set it off; it requires fire, always fire. He offers this the way another man might mention the weather.

> [!dm]
> When Thunk mans a cannon or ship weapon, he counts as two crew for the purpose of determining how many weapons can be manned simultaneously.
>
> He is proficient with smith's tools and can operate a Smithy facility without additional staff. He will have views on how it should be organized. He will be correct about all of them.
>
> **The gimmick:** Thunk reads as a one-note joke — enormous, warm, slightly dim, very good at cannons. Drop the depth whenever it feels natural: a correct physics observation, an unexpected metallurgy note, a precise stoichiometry comment. He never signals that it's remarkable. The reveal, if the party ever gets there, is not that he's secretly smart — it's that he was always this, and they misread him from the start.

## Session History

**Session 03 (transit):** Taught [[jean-claude-tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] the guns. The lesson was thorough — correct breakdown of black powder composition, ignition principle, non-shock-sensitive mechanics. Jean-Claude left gun-proficient.

**Shore Leave — Calveno:** Went to a Mercatura tavern with [[sem-holst|Sem Holst]] and Jean-Claude, paid for the drinks. The next morning went to the Arsenal district alone. Stood at the quay gates long enough to identify a bad mounting bracket on a Crown ordnance barge — wrong casting angle on the pivot collar, will crack on the third or fourth sustained firing. Told a dock hand. The dock hand wrote nothing down. Thunk left. He is not certain anyone will fix it before it matters. He feels fine about this.
