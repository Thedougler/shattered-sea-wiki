## Overview

Aldous Draves is a Crown-licensed import factor operating out of [[port-tidefall|Port Tidefall]] — which means he has spent thirty years reading shipping manifests, filing inspection appeals, attending maritime court proceedings, and learning the names of every clerk worth knowing. He is not a naval man. He is a man who has spent his life adjacent to the naval world, which is a different thing, and which he has never fully admitted is not the same thing.

He arranged his son's berth on the [[hcs-surety|HCS Surety]] through patient cultivation of the right connections. A patrol cutter posting is not a commission. It was what those connections could produce, and Aldous presented it to [[geoffrey-draves|Geoffrey]] as the opening move in a plan rather than the ceiling of his reach.

His son has been somewhere on the eastern patrol routes and has not written in some time. He has filed formal inquiries with the Harbourmaster's Office. He is waiting.

## Appearance & Manner

When you meet him, the first thing you notice is the clothes. The cut is correct; the insignia accurate to Crown administrative rank; the whole presentation reads as respectable Dravosi gentleman from a distance. Up close, the cut is a decade out of date.

He is formal the way people are formal who were taught that informality is how family names become forgettable. He uses titles correctly and consistently. He does not smile easily, and when he does, it looks like a decision he made after weighing the alternatives.

## Wants

To close the gap. The Draves family has Crown access and the right connections, but the line between *access* and *inside* has never been crossed. His son becoming a man whose name reaches the ears of important Crown officers without being attached to a problem: that would be something. He does not say this plainly.

## Voice

Formal, economical, precise. Refers to Geoffrey as "Geoffrey" in every context — no warmth or distance attached to it, just designation.

> *"Geoffrey is a capable young man. Whether capable becomes something more is a question of whether he applies it in the right direction."*
>
> *"The berth on the Surety was not a small thing to arrange. The Crown does not distribute patrol postings casually. I want to be clear about that."*

## The Wage Situation

He has been a licensed factor for thirty years. Crown privateer marine ratings and their baseline wage schedule are standard knowledge in the Port Tidefall commercial tier. He knows that a rated marine earns considerably more than a carpenter-plus-supplement for the same boarding work. Whether he explained the difference to [[geoffrey-draves|Geoffrey]] is the question to hold. If pressed directly, he will cite the contract language. The contract is accurate. He knows this. He is counting on it.

## The Knighton Question

If Geoffrey's activities reach [[rupert-knighton|Knighton's]] desk — a defecting Crown sailor working for foreign privateers — whether Aldous distances himself or frames the whole sequence as bold initiative will depend entirely on how things land. He is not a disloyal man. He is a careful one.

## If Geoffrey Returns

**Successful, with money and connections:** He arranged the berth. He always believed the sea was the right path.

**Disgraced, wanted by the Crown:** He has no record of the arrangement. The connections he used were informal. Informal connections leave no paper trail.

**Dead:** He will have the Harbourmaster's inquiry letter framed and kept. He has already drafted it in his head.

## Hooks

- Geoffrey returning with money, a warrant, or proof of desertion forces Aldous to choose which version of the story he will claim.
- A formal inquiry into the [[hcs-surety|HCS Surety]] gives him a reason to enter play before Geoffrey is ready.
