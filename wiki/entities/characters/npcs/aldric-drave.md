# Aldric Drave

> [!read-aloud]
> The man standing at the far end of the room wears a Dravosi dress uniform that is not quite right — the cut is correct, the insignia accurate, but the standard it matches has not been in use for two hundred years. He is tall, gaunt, and utterly still. When he turns to look at you, it is with the unhurried attention of someone who has been waiting in this room for a very long time and considers your arrival a minor event in a long sequence of minor events.

## Overview

Aldric Drave is the hidden founder of the [[dravosi-crown|Dravosi Crown]] and the undead architect behind its bloodline bureaucracy. He is a late-game pressure point, not a casual contact: his existence explains why lineage, archives, and Crown administration have become instruments of power.

Use him when the party starts pulling on deep Crown records, the [[pearl-of-souls|Pearl of Souls]], or the question of why Dravosi genealogy has been maintained with religious precision.

## Appearance & Manner

Tall and gaunt, with the bearing of a man who has never once doubted that the room belongs to him. His dress uniform is Dravosi colonial — maintained to a standard retired two centuries ago. The fabric is immaculate. His skin is the colour of old vellum. His eyes are pale grey and do not catch light the way living eyes do.

He looks like a portrait of a man rather than the man himself. The resemblance to certain faces in the oldest Dravosi genealogical records, if the party thinks to look, is exact.

**Roleplay Concept:** A dead king who hasn't told anyone yet.

## Voice & Delivery

Formal, clipped Dravosi register — old-fashioned phrasing that sounds like documents rather than conversation. Refers to current Crown officials by childhood nicknames or family diminutives they have never heard anyone use.

**Floor:** Entirely at ease. Patient in the way of something that measures time in decades.

**Crack:** When his Heir candidacy is threatened, the patience evaporates and something much older and colder surfaces. He has not needed to act directly in a long time. He remembers how.

> *"The Governor is a capable administrator. His grandfather was better. His great-grandmother was exceptional. The blood thins, as it always does. That is why I am here."*
>
> *"I have read every genealogical record the Crown has produced in this sea. I compiled the first ones myself. You will understand why I find your situation interesting."*

## Deep Records

Aldric Drave was the founder of the colonial project that became the Dravosi Crown — a sorcerer who achieved Hierarch lichdom before the first Dravosi ship entered the Scatter, then seeded his bloodline deliberately into every noble family the Crown would later produce. He designed the administrative apparatus — the ledgers, the genealogical records, the documentation theology — because he needed a system that would track every living phylactery across three centuries of colonial expansion.

It worked. For two hundred years the Dravosi Crown was, without knowing it, a self-maintaining map of his bloodline.

The problem is time. Three centuries of dilution have weakened the blood. His phylactery network is fraying at the edges. He is beginning the process of selecting an Heir — the most powerful sorcerous descendant currently living — to perform the Renewal ritual and begin the cycle again.

The [[tessarine-concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] believes Aldric has identified the [[pearl-of-souls|Pearl of Souls]] as a way out of that bloodline bottleneck — external anchors, non-descendant phylacteries, or a reservoir of souls that lets him stabilize without waiting for a suitable Heir.

He lives beneath [[port-tidefall|Port Tidefall]], in the original [[drave-vaults|Drave family vaults]] sealed into the cliff face below Fort Crestwall. The current Dravosi administration does not know the vaults exist.

> [!SECRET]
> **Hierarch mechanic:** Every living descendant carrying Dravosi noble blood is a phylactery. The Crown's genealogical archives are an inadvertent map of his phylactery network.
>
> **The encounter problem:** Destroying Aldric permanently requires eliminating every living blood-descendant — which includes the Governor, senior naval officers, and dozens of Dravosi noble families who have no idea what they are. He will reform within weeks depending on how many descendants remain.
>
> **The Heir:** The strongest sorcerous blood currently living in the Dravosi lineage is someone the party will already know by the time this reveal lands. Identity is a campaign decision — but whoever it is, Aldric has been watching them.
>
> **Late-game reveal.** He surfaces when the party pulls on Dravosi genealogical threads, accesses deep records at Port Tidefall, or when the Heir's sorcerous power attracts his attention.

## Hooks

- Access to [[port-tidefall|Port Tidefall's]] deep genealogical records can expose the shape of Aldric's phylactery network.
- The [[pearl-of-souls|Pearl of Souls]] may let him escape the limits of bloodline anchoring.
- The Heir reveal should land only after the party knows enough Dravosi descendants for the choice to hurt.
