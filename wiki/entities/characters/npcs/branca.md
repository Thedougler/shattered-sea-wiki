# Branca

> [!read-aloud]
> A woman in her mid-forties, salt-white hair pulled back hard, blue-grey Waveservant robes still crisp from the sea air. She has a slight limp — the kind that has been ignored so long it's become posture. She produces a small leather booklet from her sleeve, consults it without urgency, and looks up at Fisk with the specific warmth of someone who has delivered very bad news many times and decided the kindest thing you can do is make it easy. "You're a difficult man to find," she says, pleasantly. "We have an appointment."

## Overview

Branca is the senior Waveservant assigned to [[delmar-fisk|Delmar Fisk]]. She can appear at the shrine in [[calveno|Calveno]], [[port-tidefall|Port Tidefall]], or [[kalowe|Kalowe]] — use whichever the party reaches first. Her role, manner, and function are identical regardless of port. She is [[umberlee|Umberlee]]'s soft instrument: the appointment, the booklet, the warm delivery that comes before harder ones.

## Role

Use Branca when Umberlee's claim on Fisk needs to arrive in public without starting as open violence. She makes divine coercion feel like scheduling.

## Appearance & Manner

Mid-forties. Salt-white hair pulled back tight, sea-grey robes pressed and spotless. She carries a small leather tide-table booklet and consults it before she speaks — not for show, she actually checks it. She looks at Fisk the way a very good executive assistant looks at someone who missed a meeting: not annoyed, already rescheduling.

**Voice & Delivery:**

- Upbeat, clipped, slightly too much eye contact. The energy of someone who color-codes their calendar and means it.
- Opens with "So!" Closes with "Does that work for you?" Everything in between is scheduling language applied to theological horror.
- Consults the tide-table booklet mid-sentence. References it as though it resolves all objections.
- **Floor:** bubbly, efficient, unfailingly helpful — she genuinely wants this to go smoothly. **Crack:** when someone asks what happens if Fisk doesn't show, the brightness doesn't dim — she just tilts her head: *"Then it escalates to the next stakeholder. I'd really rather we didn't get there."*

*"So! I've gone ahead and blocked the third tide for your shrine communion — does that work, or should I find something that fits your schedule a little better?"*
*"The goddess had my neck for — gosh, forty seconds? Maybe less. I was back before the candles even dripped. Honestly it was such a clarifying experience, I recommend it."*
*"I just want to make sure we're all aligned before this moves up the chain."*

## Wants

Branca wants Fisk to attend the goddess's appointment cleanly, with as little escalation as possible. She believes making the process pleasant is a kindness.

## Background

Senior Waveservant assigned to Fisk at Port Tidefall. Umberlee has used her as a vessel, snapped her neck in front of witnesses, and resurrected her in the same minute. She described the experience afterward to her junior attendants as an extraordinary privilege. She was not performing. The goddess's capacity for casual cruelty toward her own faithful is not a flaw in the theology — it is the theology, and Branca has built her entire self around it.

## Hooks

- Branca can turn up with a polite appointment before Umberlee uses a harsher messenger.
- Her resurrection story shows the party what Umberlee's devotion costs even loyal servants.
