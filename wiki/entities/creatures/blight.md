# Blight

![[Blight.webp|The Blight grown from the Death Bloom at the heart of Aruhe]]

> Full stat block in the Pointy Hat source. The Blight's stat block is tiered — it degrades in stages as the Death Bloom phylactery is damaged.

## What a Blight Is

A Blight begins as an Archdruid who performed a ritual to fuse their soul and body to a piece of land they were trying to protect — killing themselves in the process. The result is a druid lich whose phylactery is not an object or a living being but **a place**: the Death Bloom, a location at the heart of the protected land.

Unlike other liches, a Blight cannot be destroyed by finding and smashing a phylactery object. The Death Bloom is a location — a dungeon to be cleared, not an item to be stolen.

## Combat Notes

- **Stage 1 (Death Bloom intact):** Full power — high CR, full action economy.
- **Stage 2 (Death Bloom damaged):** Degraded — reduced HP, weakened abilities.
- **Stage 3 (Death Bloom nearly destroyed):** Desperate — erratic, may use environmental hazards.
- **Death Bloom destroyed:** Blight cannot reform; final death.

## In The Shattered Sea

**Aruhe** hosts an active Blight. The abandoned island interior is its dungeon. The Death Bloom is the encounter at the island's interior center. The acid drainage dissolving the old agricultural terraces is the Blight's byproduct working on the soil.

The [[grung-clans|Grung Clans]] patrol the surrounding reefs but refuse to land. Their name for Aruhe translates roughly as "the island that is already eaten." They know what is there.

The Blight is an **optional encounter**. It enters play if the party shelters at Aruhe, follows a drifting wreck there, needs to avoid patrols, or investigates a chart note. It is not connected to the main campaign threads.
