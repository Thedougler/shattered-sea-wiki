## Lore

[[kalowe|Kalowe]]'s fishermen call it the Welak. Colonial sailors call it the whip shark, after the tail.

The whip shark is a shark by any ordinary reckoning: massive, cartilaginous, built for open ocean. The lower lobe of its tail extends 10–20 feet beyond the upper and terminates in a hooked sickle-like barb, dense and sharp enough to punch through ship timber. Older whip sharks have been recorded up to 55 feet long from nose to tail end, with a 35-foot body and 20-foot tail.

At the [[shelfworks|Shelfworks]], they patrol the Abyssal Bore perimeter (40–200 ft). They are drawn upward from depth by the rhythmic vibrating hum of surface-supplied air pumps — the same equipment that keeps a diver alive at depth is an acoustic dinner bell for what lives below.

## Session 03 — Encounter

A specimen over sixty feet long attacked the ship at anchor in a storm west of Calveno. Three hull impacts, two inches of bilge water. It took concentrated fire across multiple rounds before [[crissdalynn-khinriss|Crissdalynn]] grappled it out of the water entirely. It died when [[jean-claude-tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] put an arrow through sixty feet of it from posterior to anterior.

Harvested: barb spike (4 feet, serrated bone/cartilage), teeth, scales (used to patch hull), three fertilized eggs. The crew noted this specimen was far west of its known range — whip sharks are not supposed to leave the [[the-drowned-maw|Drowned Maw]].

[[perrin-black-jaw|Perrin Black-Jaw]] received a vision the same night, polymorphed into a whip shark hunting a whale from the deep. The word *"Grow"* was communicated from below the worked stone floor of the abyss.
