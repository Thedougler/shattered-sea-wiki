# Dravosi Crown

Imperial Crown authority. First colonial presence in the Shattered Sea established in 1195 DR: claiming the [[crown-islands|Crown Islands]] and placing inspection forces at the western entrance to the [[central-strait|Central Strait]], forming [[tidefall|Port Tidefall]].

Naval presence is enforced through patrol cutters and privateers. Rook operated under their flag. Their boarding crew carried Grung-made toxin sourced from [[simone-tabarnack|Simone Tabarnack]] — the supply arrangement is unknown to the party.

## Public Face

The Crown presents itself as lawful order at the edge of an unmapped sea: fortress ports, naval inspections, rating stamps, safe-passage papers, reliable charts, piracy suppression, and ship standards.

The price is submission to the document. A vessel is safe because its papers say so. A captain is honest because the manifest agrees with the rating stamp.

[[tyr|Tyr]] is the state faith of the Dravosi military and administrative apparatus. Commission papers, courts martial, and high-value contracts carry Tyr's seal because the Crown wants law to feel older than the people enforcing it.

## Legal Tools

- **Rating inspection:** Vessel, crew, cargo, and destination checked against Crown registry standards.
- **Impound:** Ships trapped in fee disputes, seizure yards, and temporary holds.
- **Privateer commission:** Legal cover for approved private captains to inspect non-Crown vessels in contested waters.
- **Species relocation bounty:** Rattkin may be listed for live-capture relocation in Crown territories.
- **Admiralty pass:** Named document opening forts, restricted piers, military archives, and sealed roads.

## Known Capabilities
- Patrol cutters (HCS-class) with cannon and armed crew.
- Contracted Grung toxin canisters.
- Privateer captains with authority to board and seize vessels.

## Texture

Dravosi authority sounds like boots on plank, paper slapped flat, a brass stamp striking wet ink, the morning gun, and the silence after an officer asks for papers. The system is oppressive because it is large enough to absorb individual decency.
