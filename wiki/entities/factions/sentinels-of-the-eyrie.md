# Sentinels Of The Eyrie

Kensei monastic tradition established at [[high-eyrie|High Eyrie]] in 895 DR to counter the [[waveservants|Waveservants]] of [[umberlee|Umberlee]].

They began their current unbroken observation record of [[the-drowned-maw|the Drowned Maw]] in 1295 DR. The ledger series still runs in 1495 DR.

## Doctrine

Objective Observation: record what you see, exactly as you see it, without conclusion, interpretation, or editorializing.

The order fights, rescues, defends its own, and helps those in genuine need. What it avoids is being used as scouts, witnesses, analysts, or instruments by outside powers.

Principles:

- Record without conclusion.
- Presence changes the record.
- Perception is discipline.
- Long memory beats certainty.

## The Long Record

The Sentinels record weather, water, drift, wrecks, storms, compass failures, ship movements, light anomalies, surface stillness, survivor sightings, and unexplained events. Entries are standardized by date, time, position, weather, water condition, observed event, duration, and circuit observer.

Researchers are sometimes granted access to public records at [[high-eyrie|High Eyrie]]. Few have the patience or context to use what they read.

## Pilgrimage and Needle

The pilgrimage is the final stage of training. Each student receives a task from their master and leaves the Eyrie until it is complete. Tasks include Maw perimeter circuits, named anomaly documentation, and mapping Maw-adjacent reef systems from observation rather than rumor.

At the close of formal training, a master presents the student with a tattoo needle. The resulting mark is an Eldritch Claw Tattoo; the order calls its active ability the Long Reach.

> [!dm]
> Ask a Sentinel what was recorded, not what it means. Refusal to interpret is doctrine, not evasion.
