# Waveservants

Practical clergy of [[umberlee|Umberlee]]. They collect tribute, witness maritime obligations, operate harbor shrines, and refuse almost no one because tribute from an enemy spends as cleanly as tribute from a friend.

Public sailor-facing lore lives in [[umberlee-and-waveservants|Umberlee and the Waveservants]].

## Shrine Practice

- Salt-bleached grey robes, damp hems, tallow and brine smell.
- Basins of seawater near harbor entries.
- Offerings in coin, rope, fish bones, brass buttons, compass needles, carved tokens, or private promises.
- Blessings acknowledge that proper respect has been shown. They are not safety guarantees.
- They collect from merchants, pirates, naval vessels, smugglers, pilgrims, and private travelers on equal terms.

The [[sentinels-of-the-eyrie|Sentinels of the Eyrie]] established their monastic tradition to counter them.

## Other Powers

- The [[dravosi-crown|Dravosi Crown]] governs inspection piers, but Crown sailors still pay shrine tribute.
- The [[tessarine-concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] treats tribute as a standard operational cost.
- The [[sentinels-of-the-eyrie|Sentinels of the Eyrie]] and Waveservants both operate with restraint around [[the-drowned-maw|the Drowned Maw]], independently and for separate reasons.
- [[the-passage|Passage]] routes hidden sailors through hidden ways, but those sailors still sail on water.
