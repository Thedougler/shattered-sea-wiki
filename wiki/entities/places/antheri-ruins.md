# Antheri Ruins

> *They built something. No one knows what it did.*

## Overview

The Antheri Ruins occupy the western wall of the [[drowned-maw|Drowned Maw]], beginning at the surface shelf around sixty feet down and continuing into depths no current diver has returned from with anything useful to say. The stone is a pale composite, unfouled and uncorroded, that no living materials expert has identified. Biological growth does not adhere to it. A piece brought to the surface is as clean as the day it was placed.

The architecture is disorienting at the accessible tier and outright wrong deeper in. There are no stairs. No flat floors designed as floors. No provision whatsoever for beings that needed to breathe. Massive vertical shafts drop into the dark. Spherical rooms connect by sweeping archways. Whatever the Antheri were, they moved in three dimensions, in water, without equipment.

## The Shelfworks Tier (0–60 ft)

The accessible tier runs from the surface to roughly sixty feet. Above that line, ordinary dive operations with air pumps and rope discipline can work the ruins.

**The civic halls** are the largest spaces — atrium volumes with channel-grooves in the floor in branching patterns. Whatever the channels carried, it no longer flows. Seagrass has found the grooves.

**The workshop tier** sits one level below, accessible through mid-height passages requiring a diver to orient horizontally. Inside: fixed equipment stations with tool-holder geometry five-fingered but narrower than a human handspan. Most mechanisms no longer operate. Some still do, in ways salvagers have learned not to trigger by accident.

**The residential sections** are cut directly into the trench wall: identical alcoves, dimensioned for something roughly human in size but narrower. Empty — not ransacked, but left deliberately.

**Dark alloy** appears everywhere functional: cold to the touch even at forty feet, never patinas or stains. [[catarina-davirelli|Catarina DaVirelli]] was the first to successfully adapt a recovered mechanism into active use — that demonstration is what brought everyone here.

**Wall reliefs** run throughout — geometric, not pictographic. A triangle set inside a circle recurs near sealed rooms. Salvagers' practical translation: rooms with significant concentrations of the triangle-in-circle symbol are not worth the time to open.

## The Abyssal Bore (60–200 ft)

Past the thermocline, sunlight fails and the Maw's compass drift begins. Heavily armoured crustaceans occupy deeper passages. Blind bioluminescent eels nest in ancient pipework. [[whip-shark|Whip sharks]] patrol the deeper perimeter, drawn upward by the rhythmic vibration of surface air pumps — audible to them from well above sixty feet.

What is changing is the depth profile of the danger. Crews that worked the eighty-foot tier last season without incident are not taking return contracts this season. Severed air lines, unexplained cold bands in the upper dive water, and light changing colour at depth without a cloud crossing overhead.

## The Abyssal Threshold (200+ ft)

No one who has reached the lower depths has returned with a coherent account. What comes back: air hoses cut clean, diving suits crushed from outside, manifest entries that read *recovered equipment only* with a blank where the crew count should be. Three expeditions have attempted organised descents below two hundred feet. The results are in the salvage records filed at Tidefall, under incident reports, and they do not read like accidents.

## People & Power

The [[tessarine-concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] controls the intelligence war — the most accurate early maps have disappeared into debt-settlement arrangements. The [[dravosi-crown|Dravosi Crown]] attempts to assert naval authority over a frontier that refuses to be cleanly taxed. Independent crews work the gaps.
