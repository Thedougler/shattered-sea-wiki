# Aldenmere

## Overview

Aldenmere is the fourth island of the [[crown-islands|Crown Islands]], one step east of [[harwick|Harwick]] and one step west of [[stenmark|Stenmark]]. There is no fortress here — no admiralty office, no naval yard, no inspection pier. The [[dravosi-crown|Dravosi Crown]] holds the island on paper and sends a revenue officer twice a year with a ledger and expectations about the fishing tithe. The island pays and the officer leaves.

The name comes from the mere — a broad, still tidal lake in the island's interior, connected to the sea by a narrow inlet channel. The farms run down to its eastern shore. The fishing fleets work both the mere and the offshore grounds. The town grew where the ground was solid enough for stone, and has not grown much since.

## Geography

The island is lower than [[harwick|Harwick]] and greener than [[calven|Calven]] — rolling agricultural land, hedgerow-bordered fields, and a central depression that floods with the tides and never fully drains. That depression is the mere: perhaps a mile across at its widest, shallower in summer and deeper in spring, ringed by reed beds and low pasture and old alder stands.

The coast is mostly gentle: short cliffs on the northern face, shelving beaches and tidal flat on the south. The harbour sits on the southwestern side, sheltered by a natural headland. The approaches are straightforward enough that a visiting captain does not strictly need a local pilot, though local pilots will tell you otherwise.

## The Mere

The inland lake is the island's defining feature and its oldest source of protein — freshwater eels, crayfish, and reed-filtered fish, harvested on a seasonal rotation established well before the Crown arrived. The inlet channel is navigable by flat-bottomed boats at high tide, giving the mere a working function as sheltered anchorage for small craft in bad weather.

The deeper sections stay murky year-round. Locals know which channels are safe and which are not, and they do not always explain the difference to visitors.

## The Town

The main settlement runs along the eastern shore of the mere and back up the slope toward the harbour road. Stone houses, low and practical. A market square that doubles as a fish-drying ground in season. A harbourmaster's office that is genuinely just an office — no rating station, no Crown registry desk.

Ships stop here for water, provisions, and occasionally shelter, pay a modest berthing fee, and move on.

## People & Power

The island pays its tithe and is otherwise governed by local habit, fishing families, and practical harbour officials. Crown administration appears as a revenue officer twice a year.

## Trade & Resources

Aldenmere sends fish, preserved catch, eels, crayfish, grain, root vegetables, orchard fruit, reeds, and provisions toward [[harwick|Harwick]] and [[tidefall|Tidefall]].

## Fauna & Hazards

The mere holds giant frogs in the reed beds — a seasonal nuisance for nearby livestock, a hazard for anyone wading at dusk. Giant toads occupy the deeper waterline. The serious concern is a giant crocodile population in the inland inlet channel: the island keeps a culling record, but the population is old and the mere is large and the record is not always current. Locals do not swim in the mere. They do not explain this to visitors in advance.

Offshore, the southern approaches carry hunter sharks. Giant crabs occupy the northern cliff rock pools. Giant owls nest in the oldest alder stands and hunt the farmland at night — considered good luck by the island's farmers.

## Current Hooks

- Visitors are not always told which mere channels are unsafe.
- The fishing tithe can become a pressure point when the Crown wants more than the island usually pays.
