# Cape Solitude

## Overview

Cape Solitude is the northernmost point of [[calders-tooth|Calder's Tooth]], where the island runs out of island and faces open ocean directly. Heavy surf, constant wind off the [[galewall|Galewall]], and no shelter. Local fisherfolk won't anchor within two miles of the cape. They don't explain why if you ask them directly, but the short version is the Dead Light.

## Description

Cape Solitude faces open ocean with no shelter: hard surf, constant Galewall wind, boulder fields at the cape base, updrafts along the cliff, and a ruined pre-colonial tower standing on the point.

## Access & Approach

The approach is exposed to open ocean and heavy surf. Local fisherfolk avoid anchoring within two miles. The cape base is a boulder field with aggressive giant crabs, and the clifftop wind makes voices carry in ways locals do not trust.

## People & Power

The [[dravosi-crown|Dravosi Crown]] attempted a lighthouse project here and abandoned it after three successive work crews disappeared without signs of struggle. No bodies, no tools left behind, nothing disturbed. The project was quietly struck from the ledgers.

## Notable Features

**The Dead Light** — A ruined stone tower on the point, pre-colonial construction older than the Crown's claim to the island.

**Abandoned Lighthouse Project** — Three Crown work crews vanished here without signs of struggle.

## Fauna

The surf at the cape base runs hard against a boulder field, which suits giant crabs — the colony here is larger than the ones on Calder's Tooth's eastern mudflats, and more aggressive. Giant vultures use the cape's updrafts daily, circling the point in numbers that suggest they expect something to die here regularly.

The locals' reluctance to anchor near the cape predates the lighthouse project. The fisherfolk say voices carry from the clifftop on windy nights — not wind noise, but voices with phrasing in them. Harpies nest in cliff-face crevices across the northern headland. Whether the three lighthouse crews followed a sound toward the cliff edge is a question nobody has gone back to confirm.

## Hazards

Open-ocean exposure, heavy surf, giant crabs, harpy cliff voices, giant vultures, and the unresolved disappearance of three lighthouse crews.

## Current Hooks

- The tower still stands. Nothing has moved into it that anyone has reported, and no one has gone back to look carefully.
- Whether the vanished lighthouse crews followed a sound toward the cliff edge remains unconfirmed.
