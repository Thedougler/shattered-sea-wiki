# Uncertainty — DM Guide

Companion to [[hcs-surety-owners-manual|the Owner's Manual]]. The Manual tells players what to roll; this tells you what happens when they do.

The ship is now sailing as [[uncertainty|the Uncertainty]] — private registry, Crown markings removed, La Vasca refit complete. The Crown recognition clock is active. See below.

---

## Navigation Failures

Players roll **Intelligence (Navigator's Tools)** against a DC you set. On a failure, delay the voyage by **1d4 days** and introduce a complication scaled to the waters:

| Waters | Complication Ideas |
|---|---|
| Charted, calm (DC 10) | Minor course deviation. Bad weather front adds a day. Missed a supply stop. |
| Unfamiliar, reef approaches (DC 14) | Grounded on a sandbar (Carpenter's DC 14 to free, half-day lost). Hostile vessel spotted. A port the party intended to bypass gets threaded too close — awkward questions. |
| Maw approaches, active storm (DC 18) | Separated from landmarks in fog for 1d4 days. Emergency hull damage (**2d10**). A crew member overboard (DC 13 Athletics or lost). The compass starts behaving strangely even after the Maw. |

Don't use the same complication twice in a row. Failures in charted waters should feel inconvenient; failures near the Maw should feel dangerous.

**Crown charts and advantage.** The *Uncertainty*'s chart set covers the Central Strait and Crown Islands — Admiralty markings scraped, Calveno notation overlaid. The coverage is real and grants advantage in those waters. Midchain charts are contradictory; grant no advantage and feel free to introduce complications even on a success if the charts are the source of the route.

**Shallow draft.** The helmsman has advantage on Dexterity checks to navigate reef channels and shallow coastal passages. This is a real operational edge — Crown patrol cutters drawing 8 ft cannot follow into 4 ft of water.

## Chase Complications

Chases use range bands (Boarding / Cannon / Distant). Two rounds at Distant = quarry escapes. Call one complication per chase, at a dramatically appropriate moment.

| Complication Type | Check Called | Failure Result |
|---|---|---|
| Fog bank | **DC 14 Perception** (Navigator or lookout) | Both ships move to Distant regardless of speed this round; quarry may escape |
| Reef channel | **DC 14 Intelligence (Navigator's Tools)** | Pursuing ship's speed drops one band for **1d4** rounds |
| Wind shift | **DC 12 Dexterity (Bosun)** | Pursuing ship loses one band of movement this round |
| Shallow draft warning | **DC 14 Intelligence (Navigator's Tools)** | Larger vessel runs aground; pursuit ends or continues by jolly boat |

**Ram in a chase.** If the party is pursuing and reaches Boarding range, the helmsman can use the Ram action instead of standard maneuvering. Fleeing vessels at Boarding range take 2d10 (party takes half); DC 13 Strength save or pushed 15 ft and next turn's movement halved. Dramatically effective against slower targets; risky against vessels of equal speed.

## Crew Casualties

When Hull Integrity crosses these thresholds during combat, roll for crew losses. Each threshold fires once per engagement.

| Hull Integrity Remaining | Roll |
|---|---|
| First time below 97 HP | 1 NPC crew member incapacitated |
| First time below 65 HP | **1d4** NPC crew incapacitated |
| First time below 33 HP | **2d4** NPC crew incapacitated or killed |

*Thresholds scaled to 130 HP.*

**PCs are never on the casualty table.** They take damage from specific attacks only.

**Announcing casualties.** Name the ones who go down. If a named NPC crew member is involved, pull from the muster. If a specific hireling fills a role, a casualty can take that role offline mid-fight.

**Minimum complement impact.** If casualties drop the vessel below 4 crew, all ship checks go to disadvantage and speed drops 20%. If a specific role goes unfilled mid-combat (Gunner down), the no-Gunner rule applies immediately.

## Bastion — Facility Events

When a facility is assigned **Maintain**, roll a Facility Event.

| Roll (d6) | Shipboard Event |
|---|---|
| 1 | **Hostile contact.** A vessel has been tracking the *Uncertainty*. Run as an encounter or surface at a dramatically appropriate moment. |
| 2 | **Beneficial contact.** A passing ship offers trade, intelligence, or an unexpected opportunity. |
| 3 | **Facility damage.** A storm or rough seas damage one facility. Repair requires a Carpenter's check and one day. |
| 4 | **Windfall.** Favourable current — voyage shortened by **1d4** days. |
| 5 | **Hireling issue.** A crew member has a matter requiring officer attention — dispute, health issue, demand for back pay, a rumour they want to act on. |
| 6 | **Clear sailing.** No event this week. |

## Bastion — Defence of the Vessel

When the Officer Complement is absent and a hostile action event occurs, resolve it with dice.

Roll **6d6**. Each result of **1** kills one Defender. If all Defenders are lost, one facility (your choice — pick the most narratively interesting) goes dormant for the following week.

| Vessel Condition | Defence Modifier |
|---|---|
| Officer Complement aboard | Don't roll — play it out |
| Vessel in port, complement absent | Standard 2024 bastion defence |
| Underway, full experienced crew | +3 to each die (results of 1–3 don't kill) |
| Underway, skeleton crew | No modifier |
| Underway, below minimum complement | −1 to each die (results of 1–2 kill) |

The Weapons Locker upgrades Defender dice from **d6 to d8** while stocked.

**Keeping Defenders stocked.** A single Recruit order fills up to 12 Defenders. Don't let the party forget this — it's cheap insurance.

## Crown Registration — Timeline (NOW ACTIVE)

The *Uncertainty* was registered as HCS Surety, active Crown patrol vessel out of [[port-tidefall|Port Tidefall]], under Lieutenant B. Rook. The ship has been missing since Session 01. The refit at [[la-vasca|La Vasca]] completed approximately when Session 03 ended. **The clock is running.**

- **Weeks 1–2:** No action. Patrol cutters sometimes run long without check-in. *(Party is likely in this window.)*
- **Week 3:** Harbourmaster's Register flags the vessel as overdue. Query to nearest Crown waypoint.
- **Week 4–5:** Listed missing. Description circulated to Crown ports in the Eastern Crown Islands.
- **Week 6+:** If spotted operating under Crown colours — boarding and inspection authorised. If operating under different colours or no colours and identified by hull or crew — wanted vessel at Crown ports.

**The Calveno refit changes the clock:** the identity erasure (paint, name, registry plate, flags) handles port authority at a glance. It does not handle a Dravosi naval officer who has served on this class of vessel. A cutter's profile is distinctive.

**What slows the clock:**
- Operating far from Crown waters (Midchain, Outer Reach)
- No Crown colours flying (done — nothing flies)
- Avoiding Crown ports and inspection vessels

**What accelerates it:**
- Operating in the Central Strait or Crown Islands
- Delmar Fisk walking openly in Crown ports (he is already a known name; see [[surety-missing|Crown Search]])
- The vessel being spotted by anyone who served on the Eastern Islands patrol circuit

See [[kalowe-takowan|Ship Disguise — Kalowe]] for further identity options. See [[surety-missing|Surety — Crown Search]] for the active search situation.

## Running Notes

**When to abstract vs. play out.** Routine passages don't need navigation rolls. Call for one when the waters are actually dangerous or unfamiliar, when failure would be interesting, or when the party has made a choice that deserves a consequence.

**Ship combat pacing.** Ship combat works best when boarding is the goal. The *Uncertainty* at Tier 1 cannot absorb extended broadside exchange with anything larger than herself. Position enemies accordingly. If the party is taking a prize: chain shot to slow, grapeshot to suppress the deck, board. The ram opens an alternative — use it for dramatic moments, not every engagement.

**The ram telegraphs intent.** Anyone watching the approach sees the prow and knows what is coming. Use this — let NPCs react before contact. Some will scatter; some will brace; some will target the helmsman.

**Crew personalities.** Give at least two crew members names and a single trait. When casualties happen, name the ones who go down. [[sem-holst|Sem Holst]], [[old-faas|Old Faas]], and [[thunk|Mr. Thunk]] are already named — use them. Geoffrey Draves is an ex-Crown carpenter who chose the party; that choice can surface in interesting moments.

**Upkeep as pressure.** ~32 gp/week is a soft pressure tool. Miss a week — fine. Miss three — someone isn't getting paid and the party hears about it. The Provisions Store Harvest order can reduce this pressure if managed.

**The missing Cook and Surgeon.** The Surgeon's Berth is installed and stocked but locked behind a hireling. Alys Kuiper not returning from shore leave is a clean departure — no drama, no loose thread unless you build one. The Cook gap means no long-rest HP recovery bonus at sea until someone fills the role. 4 gp/week is the cheapest operational gap on the ship.
