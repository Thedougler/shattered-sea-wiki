**STANDING ORDERS AND OPERATIONAL REGULATIONS**
**Private Vessel *UNCERTAINTY***
*(Formerly HCS Surety, Registry No. CS-1147 — Crown markings removed)*

*Issued upon transfer of command. To be retained aboard at all times.*

**Commanding Officer:** [[delmar-fisk|Delmar Fisk]], Captain

---

## Article I — Vessel Particulars

The *[[uncertainty|Uncertainty]]* is a Tier 1 patrol cutter, refitted for private independent operation. Her two cannon are sufficient to discourage uncooperative traffic and adequate for boarding-range combat. She is fast, draws little water, and carries a ram bow fitted at [[la-vasca|La Vasca]]. She may be taken through reef passages and canal approaches that would strand anything larger — including the Crown vessels she was built alongside.

### Vessel Statistics

| Particulars | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Classification** | Patrol cutter, Tier 1 (private) |
| **Hull Integrity** | 130 points |
| **Hull AC** | 11 (13 against head-on strikes) |
| **Rate of Passage — favourable wind** | 70 miles/day |
| **Rate of Passage — poor wind** | 30 miles/day |
| **Rate of Passage — calm, oars deployed** | 15 miles/day |
| **Minimum Draft** | 4 ft (standard cutter: ~8 ft) |
| **Crew Complement — minimum / full** | 4 / 10 |
| **Cargo Capacity** | 15 tons |
| **Ordnance** | 2 × 6-lb cannon (port and starboard); 2 × swivel gun (bow, stern); Ram |
| **Weekly Upkeep** | ~32 gp |
| **Condition** | Pristine |

**On Hull Integrity and Armour Class.** Hull Integrity functions as hit points. When it reaches zero, the vessel is sinking. Hull AC represents the difficulty of landing a meaningful strike against a ship-scale target. The forward hull has AC 13 against head-on strikes only — the reinforced bow from the ram fitting.

**On Repairs.** At-sea repair requires a skilled Carpenter aboard. During a short rest, the Carpenter makes an **Intelligence (Carpenter's Tools) DC 15** check; on a success, the vessel recovers **2d8 + proficiency bonus** Hull Integrity. Mending Resin (2 doses in the carpenter's locker) can repair **2d8 Hull HP over 8 hours** without a roll; this does not repair structural damage. In port, repairs run 1 gp per Hull Point at 25 Hull Points per workday.

## Article II — Special Modifications

### Ram Bow

The forward hull has been rebuilt with doubled planking, iron-pin cross-bracing, and an iron-capped ram plate.

**Ram Attack.** Helmsman's action. The ship must move at least 20 feet in a straight line before the strike. Both ships take **2d10 bludgeoning damage**; the *Uncertainty* takes half (rounded down) due to the reinforced bow. A target of Tier 1 or smaller must succeed on a **DC 13 Strength save** or be pushed 15 feet and have its next turn's movement halved.

The ram is not subtle. Anyone watching the approach sees the intent before contact.

### Shallow Draft

The keel depth has been reduced by 18 inches.

**Shallow Draft.** The *Uncertainty* can operate in water as shallow as **4 feet** (standard patrol cutter minimum: ~8 ft). This opens river mouths, tidal flats, lagoon approaches, and Calveno's inner canal network. The helmsman has **advantage** on Dexterity checks to navigate reef channels and shallow coastal passages. **Trade-off:** Speed in poor wind is 30 miles/day instead of 35.

## Article III — Deck Arrangement

**Weather Deck.** Open upper deck. Bow swivel gun with 360° arc. Two 6-lb cannon amidships, one per side. Ram plate at the waterline forward. Quarterdeck is raised two feet at the stern; attacks from it against deck-level targets have advantage, and ascending costs an additional five feet of movement. Rigging may be climbed with a **DC 10 Athletics** check as an action. The mainmast boom may be swung as an improvised weapon (**DC 12 Strength** check): **DC 13 Dexterity** save or 1d8 bludgeoning and pushed ten feet.

**Gun Deck.** Interior living space, ~6'4" overhead. Crew quarters forward, galley hatch, Weapons Locker amidships, chart table, Surgeon's Berth, crew mess aft. The Weapons Locker may be accessed as a bonus action. Signal lantern partner unit stowed near the chart table.

**Hold.** Galley (cast-iron stove) directly below crew mess, connected by iron ladder. Provisions Store occupying the former brig space. Powder magazine aft.

Full deck-by-deck dimensions and tactical notes: [[hcs-surety-layout|Deck Layouts]].

## Article IV — Ship's Company

Each role below must be filled for the vessel to operate at standard. A role may be filled by a crew member or a hired professional. An unfilled role cannot perform its function or does so at disadvantage.

**On minimum complement.** The *Uncertainty* requires a minimum of four souls to operate. Below that number, all ship-related checks are made with disadvantage and rate of passage is reduced by twenty percent.

### Roles and Rates of Pay

| Role | Function | Key Ability | Rate | Actions While in Role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Captain** | Strategic command — where to sail, what to target, when to fight or run. Final authority in action. | Charisma | — | **Action:** *Rallying Order* — one creature within 60 ft gains advantage on their next check or attack before the start of your next turn. |
| **Quartermaster** | Crew welfare, discipline, stores management, internal order. Senior to the Bosun. | Charisma | 10 gp/wk | **Action:** *Crew's Voice* — one ally rerolls any failed check, attack, or save. **Reaction:** *Fair Witness* — crew member within 60 ft rolls death save or Frightened save with advantage. |
| **Navigator** | Course-plotting, reef avoidance, Maw crossing | Intelligence (Navigator's Tools) | 10 gp/wk | **Action:** Navigation check (see Article V). **Reaction:** *Hazard Warning* — declare before a navigation check; vessel rolls with advantage. |
| **Bosun** | Rigging, sails, deck crew management | Str or Dex | 8 gp/wk | **Action:** *Trim Sails* — DC 10 Dex; success grants helmsman one extra band this turn. **Reaction:** *Brace!* — one creature aboard halves damage from a ram or collision. |
| **Gunner** | Ordnance direction and maintenance | Dexterity | 8 gp/wk | **Action:** Salvo, Aimed Shot, or Reload (see Article VII). |
| **Carpenter** | Hull repair, damage control | Intelligence (Carpenter's Tools) | 6 gp/wk | **Action:** Emergency Repair (Article I). **Bonus Action:** *Plug the Breach* — DC 10 Int (Carpenter's Tools); success stops fire spread for one round. |
| **Cook** | Provisions management and crew sustenance | Wisdom | 4 gp/wk | No combat action. Each crew member recovers an additional **1d6 HP** on a long rest. |
| **Surgeon** | Treatment of wounds and casualties | Wisdom (Medicine) | 8 gp/wk | **Action:** Stabilize one incapacitated creature (DC 10 Wis/Medicine) or restore **1d6 + 4 HP** (expends one Healer's Kit use). **Bonus Action:** *Field Triage* — DC 10 Wis (Medicine); success gives one creature advantage on their next death save. |
| **Marine** | Boarding actions, armed watch, vessel security | Str or Dex | 14 gp/wk | **Action:** Attack, Grapple, or Shove. **Bonus Action:** *Hold the Rail* — one boarding enemy makes DC 13 Athletics or is shoved back. |
| **Ordinary Sailor** | All other duties | — | 2 gp/wk | **Action:** Help — grant one creature advantage on their next check or attack this round. |

**Required always:** Captain, Bosun.
**Required for open-water passages:** Navigator.
**Required when ordnance is employed:** Gunner.
**Required for at-sea repair:** Carpenter.
**Required on voyages exceeding three days:** Cook.
**Required to operate the Surgeon's Berth:** Surgeon.
**Required for boarding operations:** Marine.

**Current gaps:** Navigator (partially covered by Crissdalynn on known routes), Cook, Surgeon. [[oleandro-fuschi|Oleandro]] at Ponte Bassa can direct you to available crew in Calveno.

## Article VI — Passage and Navigation

| Conditions | DC |
|---|---|
| Familiar or charted waters, clear weather | 10 |
| Unfamiliar waters, reef approaches, poor visibility | 14 |
| Maw approaches, active storm, uncharted shallows | 18 |

On a success, the passage proceeds without incident. On a failure, the voyage is delayed and complications arise. The *Uncertainty*'s navigational chart set covers the Crown Islands and Central Strait — coverage is good. Within five miles of the [[the-drowned-maw|Drowned Maw]], standard compasses fail; celestial navigation remains unaffected.

**Shallow draft advantage.** The helmsman has advantage on Dexterity checks to navigate reef channels and shallow coastal passages.

**On long rests at sea.** Standard rules apply. If the Cook role is filled, each character recovers an additional **1d6 HP**. If a storm strikes during the rest period, each character makes a **DC 12 Constitution** save; failure means the rest confers no benefit.

## Article VII — Action Against Hostile Vessels

Ships act in initiative order as creatures do.

### Range Bands

| Band | Description | What's Possible |
|---|---|---|
| **Boarding** | Ships alongside, locked, or grappled | Crew can board. Cannon hit automatically — no attack roll. Swivels effective. |
| **Cannon** | Standard engagement distance | Normal cannon fire. Swivels cannot reach. |
| **Distant** | Extreme range | Cannon fire at disadvantage. Swivels cannot reach. |

The helmsman uses their **Action** to move the vessel one band. Dash moves an additional band. When both vessels have been Distant for two consecutive rounds, the quarry escapes.

### Ramming

Helmsman's action at Boarding range (see Article II — Ram Bow for full mechanics). Both vessels halt upon impact; the ramming vessel may not use the Helm action until the start of its next turn.

### Ordnance Statistics

| Ordnance | Damage | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 6-lb cannon | d8 hull damage | Effective at Cannon and Boarding range |
| Swivel gun | d6 crew damage | Boarding range only; cannot damage hull |

Attack roll: **Dexterity + proficiency** vs. target Hull AC. Hit: roll damage. Miss: half damage. Critical hit: maximum damage.

### Gunner's Actions

**Salvo.** All guns bearing on the target fire simultaneously. One attack roll vs. Hull AC. Hit: full damage from all guns on that side. Miss: half damage. All fired guns must reload before the next Salvo.

**Aimed Shot.** One gun at −2 to the attack roll. On a hit, the targeted component is disabled rather than dealing standard hull damage. That gun must reload before firing again.

**Reload — All Guns.** All guns reload in parallel. No attack is made this round.

### Shot Types

| Shot Type | Effect — Hit | Effect — Miss | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Round Shot** | Full hull damage | Half hull damage | Standard; always available |
| **Chain Shot** | Speed drops one band. Three reductions = dead in the water. | Half reduction | Use to slow a fleeing vessel or set up boarding |
| **Grapeshot** | All exposed deck crew: DC 14 Dex or incapacitated; PCs take 2d6 (save halves) | PCs save with advantage | Boarding range only; cannot damage hull |

Any PC aware of incoming grapeshot may drop below deck as a Reaction before it lands.

## Article VIII — Ship's Facilities and Weekly Management

Each week, the Officer Complement conducts a **Facility Review**. All special facilities are assigned an order.

| Facility | Status | Function |
|---|---|---|
| **Weapons Locker** | Commissioned | Upgrades defender dice from d6 to d8 while stocked. Trade order: buy or sell arms at volume. |
| **Surgeon's Berth** | Commissioned (needs Surgeon hireling) | Once per weekly review: stabilize all incapacitated crew; remove one disease or poison over eight hours; or cast *Lesser Restoration* without expending a spell slot. |
| **Provisions Store** | Commissioned | Harvest order: produces rations, Healer's Kits, Healing Potions, or antitoxin each week. |
| **Chart Archive** | — | *Upgrade option.* Replaces Provisions Store if installed. Research order: yields three pieces of intelligence. |

### Weekly Facility Orders

| Order | Effect |
|---|---|
| **Craft** | Produce items or magical items permitted by the facility. |
| **Empower** | Trigger the facility's active operational effect. |
| **Harvest** | Collect facility outputs — potions, rations, poisons, materials. |
| **Maintain** | No directed activity. A facility event may occur. |
| **Recruit** | Engage hirelings, Defenders, or specialist personnel. |
| **Research** | Gather intelligence, lore, or item identification. |
| **Trade** | Generate income via commerce. |

Any facility that goes three consecutive weeks without orders goes dormant — hirelings have found other employment. Reactivating a dormant facility costs 100 gp and one week in port.

### On Defenders

Defenders are the vessel's hired security — enlisted via a Recruit order to the Crew Berths. They absorb hostile action events when the Officer Complement is absent. A single Recruit order enlists up to twelve. Keep them recruited.

## Article IX — Vessel Standing and Port Records

The *Uncertainty* carries no flag and no identifiable marking. She reads as a private working coastal vessel at a glance. She does not read that way to a Dravosi naval officer who has served on this class of vessel.

| Standing | Practical Effect |
|---|---|
| **Respected** | Reduced berthing fees; willing hirelings; faction contacts accessible. |
| **Known** | Treated according to actual conduct. Neutral default. |
| **Wanted** | Port access restricted or denied; bounties registered. |
| **Notorious** | Capable crew seek the vessel out; some quarries surrender rather than engage. |

The *Uncertainty*'s prior Crown registration is an active liability. See [[hcs-surety-dm-guide|DM Guide]] for the Crown recognition timeline.
