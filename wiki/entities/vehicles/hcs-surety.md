# HCS Surety

> [!read-aloud]
> A small Crown patrol cutter rides low in the water, painted naval grey with a white stripe at the waterline and a brass registry plate fixed to the bow. Two cannon sit amidships, with swivel guns fore and aft, and the Crown pennant flies from the masthead. The ship shows patrol wear: salt-caked seams, lifting copper at the bow, and the tired look of a vessel overdue for proper dock work.

*Registry No. CS-1147. Now sailing as [[uncertainty|the Uncertainty]] under private colours.*

---

## Overview

A Tier 1 Dravosi patrol cutter built for inspection, pursuit, and boarding rather than sustained ship-to-ship combat. Lighter-armed than HCS Relentless but dangerous at close range. Assigned to the eastern Crown Island waypoints under [[barnaby-rook|Barnaby Rook]] on Crown commission.

The hull was worn but sound at time of capture. Caulking overdue on two strakes, copper sheathing lifting at the bow. Interior reflected Crown patrol service: officer cabin aft, crew hammocks forward, boarding locker amidships, chart table forward, two-prisoner cage bolted below.

## Crown Recognition Clock

The *Surety* was registered out of [[port-tidefall|Port Tidefall]] under Lieutenant B. Rook.

- **Weeks 1–2:** No action. Patrol cutters sometimes run long without check-in.
- **Week 3:** Harbourmaster's Register flags as overdue. Query to nearest Crown waypoint.
- **Week 4–5:** Listed missing. Description circulated to Eastern Crown Islands ports.
- **Week 6+:** If spotted under Crown colours — boarding authorised. If under different colours and identified by hull or crew — wanted vessel at Crown ports.

See [[surety-missing|Surety — Crown Search]] for the active situation. See [[hcs-surety-dm-guide|DM Guide]] for full chase and recognition tooling.
