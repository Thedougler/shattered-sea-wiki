# The Antheri

Ancient aquatic humanoid civilization. At its height around -2000 DR, the Antheri built into the western wall of [[the-drowned-maw|the Drowned Maw]], reaching toward the planar boundary with the [[elemental-plane-of-water|Elemental Plane of Water]].

The common salvage assumption is wrong. The Antheri did not begin on the safe upper shelf and build down. Their oldest work is at the bottom, anchored near the fissure. The visible [[shelfworks|Shelfworks]] are newer upper structures and exhaust systems for the deep engine below.

## Origin

The Antheri began as surface nomads in the sunlit sea. A localized current collapse drove them downward toward hydrothermal vents and the remaining food chain. Survival forced a chemosynthetic turn: vent bacteria, tube worms, magically sustained kelp gardens, and body adaptations for pressure, darkness, and deep water.

They discovered the Maw by following those vents deeper. The fissure at the trench floor bled elemental power. Early attempts to harness it killed entire settlements. Their society reorganized around the people who could keep the city alive: architects, biomancers, and planar engineers.

## Physical Evidence

No preserved record confirms exact appearance. The ruins imply:

- Humanoid bodies about 5-6 feet tall.
- Narrower shoulders than a standard human.
- Five-fingered hands.
- No stairs, walkways, air pockets, or pressure bulkheads.
- Architecture designed for open-water movement in three dimensions.

> [!dm]
> The exact physical form is intentionally unresolved. Do not specify it until a stronger source establishes it.

## The Vertical City

| Tier | Depth | What It Means |
|---|---:|---|
| Shelfworks | 60-200 ft. | Salvageable upper structures. Recent damage is mostly salvage and entity incursion, not original collapse. |
| Mid-Works | 200-500 ft. | Residential and research zone. Better preserved. Soul-pledge symbolic language appears integrated into structure, not decoration. |
| Deep Works | 500+ ft. | Boundary-zone conditions dominate. Pristine, singular-purpose structures. Soul-pledge chamber and fissure-oriented terminus are here. |

> [!mechanic]
> Reaching the Deep Works requires more than water breathing. Elemental diffusion causes ongoing Constitution saves, exhaustion risk, spell failure chances, and instrument unreliability.

## Technology

- **Corrosion-proof composite stone:** does not foul or carry biological growth.
- **Bio-metallurgic alloy:** current metallurgy cannot reproduce it.
- **Bioluminescent data channels:** algae seeded into capillary-like alloy geometry pulsed light through the city.
- **Accelerated aquaculture:** deep kelp systems regrew harvestable mass within days.
- **Living infrastructure:** pressure regulators grown from sponge-like tissue; transit currents driven by stimulated cilia networks.
- **Soul-binding:** formal ritual architecture and repeated symbolic vocabulary. [[auralis|Auralis]] is one known product, not the whole tradition.

## The Soul-Pledge

The soul-pledge chamber predates Auralis and appears built for repeated use. The Antheri combined biomancy, soul-binding, and planar mechanics to extract a consciousness from a body and bind it into an artificial immortal framework.

[[auralis|Auralis]] is not a natural deity. He is Antheri technology pushed to its endpoint: a manufactured guardian capable of surviving at the bottom of the trench and holding a specific purpose.

> [!dm]
> If other soul-pledges remain active, they may still be protecting things the party has not reached. Do not decide that until a source or table event demands it.

## The Fall

The Antheri vanished in -495 DR. The ruin record supports rapid failure, not slow decline:

- No evidence of external attack on the shelf structures.
- No mass casualty evidence in the residential tier.
- Workspaces abandoned mid-process.
- Deep activity was ongoing at the time of collapse.
- The fissure widened after the city was built around it.

The strongest DM-facing implication from the source: the upper city may be sealed exhaust infrastructure for a planar engine that is still running. Salvagers at the Shelfworks may be dismantling safety systems without understanding them.

## What Current Civilizations Know

- **Sailors and civilians:** old underwater ruins; valuable material; dangerous diving.
- **[[tessarine-concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] salvage interests:** know the Antheri name and basic shelf layout, not the depth or function of the full city.
- **Scholars:** no working public translation of Antheri symbolic language.
- **[[catarina-davirelli|Catarina DaVirelli]]:** best living non-Antheri expert on salvageable Antheri material culture.
- **Halythion Remembrancers:** may know the outside sequence of the Antheri fall, if the party reaches them and earns access.
