# Peoples of the Shattered Sea

The Shattered Sea sits at the edge of the colonial world. West of the Galewall is the known: kingdoms, records, recognized law. East of it is the Scatter, and the Scatter does not ask where you came from.

The further east you travel, the less the colonial map means. The [[crown-islands|Crown Islands]] look like the western homelands transplanted to warmer water. [[kalowe|Kalowe]] and the [[midchain|Midchain]] look like what happens when those homelands stop being able to enforce anything. The Tail and the Outer Reach look like what was here before the homelands arrived.

This is not a census. The sea moves people too quickly for counts to hold.

## Presence Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Dominant | The majority of any given town or crew. |
| Common | Reliably present. You will see them every day. No one comments on it. |
| Present | Visible in any large port. You might go a week without one in a small settlement. |
| Rare | Occasional. Noticed. Not remarkable enough to cause trouble. |
| Isolated | A specific community exists. Everywhere else: almost never. |

## The Crown Islands

*Dravosi colonial core. The western homelands with a tropical accent.*

The Crown Islands are the oldest colonial foothold in the Scatter. The majority population is human and came with the Dravosi flag. [[rattkin|Rattkin]] are present in every Crown port even though the Dravosi Crown's relationship with [[the-passage|The Passage]] is adversarial. The Warren prefers it that way.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Human | Dominant | Dravosi colonial stock; also Tessarine merchants and independent families. |
| Halfling | Common | Chandlers, bookkeepers, small merchants. |
| Dwarf | Common | Naval engineering, fortification work, armaments manufacture. |
| [[rattkin|Rattkin]] | Common | Port infrastructure, message networks, The Passage. Officially discouraged; practically irreplaceable. |
| Gnome | Present | Instrument makers, cartographers, Tessarine ledger work. |
| Tiefling | Present | Mixed colonial heritage. More visible in Calveno than in Tidefall. |
| Orc | Present | Dock crews, ship labour, garrison soldiers. Often underranked relative to competence. |
| Dragonborn | Present | Scattered across military and merchant roles. |
| Aasimar | Rare | Occasional pilgrims and Tyr-aligned advocates. |
| [[aarakocra|Aarakocra]] | Rare | Mostly [[sentinels-of-the-eyrie|Sentinels]]. High Eyrie maintains a small permanent population near the eastern Crown approaches. |
| Other | Rare | The Galewall crossing drops all kinds onto Crown docks. Most do not stay long. |

## The Central Strait

*The main highway. The most transient, and therefore the most everything.*

The Strait is not a place people live — it is a place people pass through. Port Tidefall processes hundreds of ships a year. Calveno turns over its dockside population on the tide. [[calveno|Calveno]] specifically skews more diverse than the rest of the Crown Islands — the [[tessarine-concordat|Tessarine Concordat]] does not care about your heritage. It cares about your credit.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Human | Dominant | Still the majority, but less so than the interior Crown Islands. |
| Halfling | Common | Merchants. Calveno's counting houses run heavy halfling. |
| Dwarf | Common | Port engineering and armaments. |
| [[rattkin|Rattkin]] | Common | Higher density than the Crown interior. Every major port in the Strait has a Run. |
| Orc | Common | Crew and dock work. A mixed crew on the Strait is more common than a human-only one. |
| Tortle | Common | Island-adapted, long-lived, calm under pressure. Excellent captains and pilots. [[beaumont-sel|Beaumont Sel]] is the most visible example. |
| Tiefling | Present | Tessarine merchant families in particular. |
| Gnome | Present | Navigation instruments, trade tools. |
| Dragonborn | Present | Military and merchant both. |
| Water Genasi | Present | Natural affinity for this work. Unremarkable on a working ship. |
| [[aarakocra|Aarakocra]] | Present | Sentinels and a small number of independent messengers and scouts. |
| Kenku | Present | Port scavengers, couriers, and fast verbal relay. |
| Aasimar | Rare | |
| Air Genasi | Rare | Valued as weather-sense sailors. |
| [[tabaxi|Tabaxi]] | Rare | Far from home. Usually working. |
| [[grung|Grung]] | Rare | Blue-caste traders and occasional scouts. |

## The Midchain

*Free ports and local councils. The colonial map stops meaning much here.*

The Midchain has been occupied by too many different peoples for too long for any one of them to claim it. The free ports and reef-knowledge economy select for people who can work outside recognized law. Humans remain common but are not dominant.

## The Verdant Teeth

*Grung territory. The beach is as far as outsiders go.*

The Verdant Teeth interior belongs to the [[grung-clans|Grung Clans]]. The coastline operates a strict beach-trade system: outsiders come to the waterline, goods change hands, outsiders leave. The clans do not negotiate on this.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[grung|Grung]] | Dominant | Interior and island communities. All castes present. |
| Visiting traders | Present | At designated beaches only. No overnight presence. |
| [[tabaxi|Tabaxi]] | Rare | Southeastern approaches. The Grung and the Tabaxi have a history neither translates clearly for outsiders. |

## The Tail

*Fishing communities and salvage crews at the edge of the charts.*

The Tail does not have colonial administration. It has pilot families. What this produces is a working meritocracy defined by capability rather than heritage. The Maw's proximity draws the [[sentinels-of-the-eyrie|Sentinels of the Eyrie]] and anyone with elemental affinity who has felt the Maw's pull and cannot stay away.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Human | Common | Longline crews, salvage camp labour, staging island residents. |
| Orc | Common | Physical work in hard conditions. |
| Tortle | Common | Long-lived, cold-resistant, comfortable at depth. Ideal salvage crew. |
| Goliath | Common | Cliff-work, heavy salvage, cold-water diving. Tail communities have had Goliath pilot families for generations. |
| Sea Elf | Present | Halythion contact. Surface occasionally near the Shelfworks approaches. |
| [[aarakocra|Aarakocra]] | Present | Sentinels of the Eyrie maintain watch presence here. |
| Water Genasi | Present | Drawn to the Maw-adjacent water. |
| Air Genasi | Present | Navigation in fog and bad weather. |
| Fire Genasi | Rare | Occasionally drawn here by the Maw's elemental bleed. |
| Halfling | Rare | Provisioning. The staging islands need someone to run the stores. |
| [[rattkin|Rattkin]] | Rare | No major Run. Individual operators working salvage information routes. |
| Triton | Rare | Deep-water presence near the Shelfworks. Encounter rather than community. |

## The Sunken Crown and Outer Reach

*Past the Maw. The oldest maps and the newest ignorance.*

The Sunken Crown is [[tabaxi|Tabaxi]] territory. The Keth-Naar community has been here longer than any colonial record acknowledges. They do not seek outside contact; they manage it when it arrives. Beyond the Blue Hole, the Outer Reach belongs to whatever was here before anyone started naming things.

| Species | Presence | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| [[tabaxi|Tabaxi]] | Dominant | Keth-Naar and its island network. Isolated from other Tabaxi populations for generations. |
| Sea Elf | Rare | Halythion trade contact. Respectful, careful, usually carrying specific purpose. |
| Triton | Rare | Deep-water beyond the Blue Hole. |
| Water Genasi | Rare | Drawn to the Blue Hole's elemental behaviour. |
| Other outsiders | Rare | Maw-crossers, pilgrims, salvagers, the lost. Keth-Naar has protocols for all of them. |

## On the Water

Ships are their own demographic. A working crew in the Scatter does not look like any port's population — it looks like whoever the captain could hire and keep.

A Midchain free-trade crew might be half orc and tortle with a rattkin pilot and a grung surgeon who does not discuss what caste they left. A Crown naval vessel runs formally human with dwarf engineers and a rattkin aboard in an unofficial capacity everyone pretends not to notice. A [[the-passage|Passage]] runner has no species requirement and one rule.

The ship is the smallest unit of real integration in the Scatter. It works because it has to.
