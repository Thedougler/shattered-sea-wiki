# Peoples of the Shattered Sea

The Shattered Sea sits at the edge of the colonial world. West of the Galewall is recognized law and recorded ownership. East of it is the Scatter, and the Scatter cares more about who can sail, repair, bargain, dive, and survive.

This is not a census. The sea moves people too quickly for numbers to hold.

## Presence Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Dominant | The majority in a town, crew, or region. |
| Common | Reliably present; no one comments on it. |
| Present | Visible in major ports; easy to miss in smaller settlements. |
| Rare | Occasional and noticed, but not automatically disruptive. |
| Isolated | A specific community exists; elsewhere, almost never. |

## Regional Pattern

| Region | Population Shape |
|---|---|
| [[crown-islands|Crown Islands]] | Dravosi colonial core. Humans are dominant; halflings, dwarves, rattkin, gnomes, tieflings, orcs, and dragonborn are normal port presences. Aarakocra are rare, mostly tied to [[high-eyrie|High Eyrie]] and the [[sentinels-of-the-eyrie|Sentinels]]. |
| [[central-strait|Central Strait]] | The main highway. Calveno and Tidefall are more diverse than Crown interiors; tortles, orcs, rattkin, dwarves, halflings, gnomes, dragonborn, water genasi, aarakocra, kenku, tabaxi, and grung all appear in working ports. |
| [[midchain|Midchain]] | Free ports and local councils. Humans are common but not default; tortles, orcs, halflings, rattkin, lizardfolk, water genasi, grung, tabaxi, and sea elves all have visible footholds. |
| [[verdant-teeth|Verdant Teeth]] | Grung territory. The interior belongs to the [[grung-clans|Grung Clans]]. Outsiders trade at designated beaches and leave. |
| The Tail | Fishing communities and salvage crews near the edge of charts. Humans, orcs, tortles, goliaths, sea elves, aarakocra, genasi, and independent rattkin operators appear around Maw-adjacent work. |
| [[sunken-crown|Sunken Crown]] and Outer Reach | Tabaxi territory around [[keth-naar|Keth Naar]]. Outsider presence is an event, not a pattern. |

## On the Water

Ships are their own demographic. A working crew looks like whoever the captain could hire and keep.

A Midchain free-trade crew might be half orc and tortle with a rattkin pilot and a grung surgeon who does not discuss what caste they left. A Crown naval vessel runs formally human with dwarf engineers and a rattkin aboard in an unofficial capacity everyone pretends not to notice. A [[the-passage|Passage]] runner has no species requirement and one rule.

The ship is the smallest unit of real integration in the Scatter. It works because it has to.
