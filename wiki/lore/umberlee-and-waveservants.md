# Umberlee and the Waveservants

Umberlee is the sea as sailors fear it: black water under moonless cloud, a mast cracking in a squall, a reef where the chart promised depth, the wave that takes one person and leaves the rest alive to remember it. Her common title is the Bitch Queen. In the Shattered Sea, the name is spoken with caution more often than blasphemy.

No one has to love Umberlee to pay her. That is the point.

## What Sailors Know

Umberlee is worshiped because the sea kills, not because she is kind. Captains give tribute before risky crossings. Deckhands mutter her name when the wind turns wrong. Survivors pay after landfall because gratitude and fear look much the same at a shrine basin.

Her worship is ordinary port infrastructure. A harbor may change flags, governors, tariffs, languages, and laws; the Umberlee shrine usually remains where sailors can find it.

## The Waveservants

The [[waveservants|Waveservants]] are Umberlee's clergy in the Shattered Sea. They are collectors, witnesses, and ritual functionaries for a goddess who does not ask twice.

They wear salt-bleached grey robes, keep their hems damp, and smell of tallow and brine even in open air. They refuse almost no one because tribute from an enemy spends as cleanly as tribute from a friend.

Their neutrality is practical. A shrine that takes sides stops collecting from half the harbor.

## Shrines and Tribute

Most ports of any size keep an Umberlee shrine close to the waterline or harbor entry.

- Basin of seawater.
- Low candles burning in tallow.
- Posted fees, spoken terms, or a clerk who knows both.
- Offerings from crews that survived what they had no right to survive.
- A priest who treats the exchange as business already settled.

Payment is not always coin. Rope, fish bones, brass buttons, broken compass needles, carved tokens, or a captain's private promise may appear on an offering shelf. Coin is simpler. Coin is preferred.

## Player Character Hooks

- A sailor who pays because ritual is cheaper than regret.
- A former Waveservant attendant who knows what crews leave behind.
- A storm survivor who believes Umberlee spared them for a reason.
- A skeptic who still drops coin in the basin when no one is watching.
- A divine caster whose relationship with the sea is transactional, resentful, reverent, or all three.
