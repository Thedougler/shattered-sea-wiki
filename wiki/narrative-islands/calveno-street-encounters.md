# Calveno — Street Encounters

Drop these when the scene needs texture, the party is restless, or you want to press a specific PC. None are mandatory. All can be played social-first and allowed to escalate, or bypassed entirely with no consequence.

Festival context: these are set during or just after [[il-gioco-delle-beffe|Il Gioco delle Beffe]], which means crowds are large, watch attention is diffuse, and unusual behaviour reads as prank or performance until it clearly isn't.

## 1. The Wrong Mark

**Setup:** A Beffa team — three young Calveno dockworkers with a bucket of fish oil, a rope, and a signed permission slip from someone they have clearly coerced — has targeted one of the party as their mark. They have been following for two blocks. They have the wrong person, and they are about to commit.

> [!read-aloud]
> The oil lands before the sound reaches you — a wide cold splash from above, and a cheer from the balcony. Three young Calvenans lean over the railing, rope still swinging. The crowd turns. Someone laughs. The smell arrives a moment later: fish, and not the fresh kind.

**What happens:** DC 12 Dexterity save or the PC is drenched in fish oil and loses the contents of one jacket pocket to the splash.

**If pursued:** Three Commoners and one Spy (the team captain). Catch check: DC 13 Athletics or Acrobatics through festival crowd. If cornered: they fold immediately. They have a written Beffa permit, a team name (*La Trota Gloriosa*), and a completely wrong name for their intended target.

> [!dm]
> Pull this when the Beffa needs to feel real and ambient rather than plot-adjacent. The permit is real. The name on it belongs to a Tessarine factor who has been running a Beffa team for six years and apparently has enemies — a loose thread, not a prepared character.

## 2. Ponte Muscle

**Setup:** A broad man in a dockworker's coat is blocking a canal walkway on one of the Bridge district footbridges, collecting a "festival toll" from anyone trying to cross. Two friends wait on the far side. The man they are currently collecting from is clearly not going to pay.

> [!read-aloud]
> A broad man in a dockworker's coat stands in the middle of the walkway. Not moving through it — standing in it. His two friends wait on the far side of the bridge. There is a man between him and you who is nearly out of options.

**What the party can do:**
- Pay (2 cp each — this is about the delay, not the money)
- Push past without paying: DC 10 Persuasion or Intimidation; failure brings the two friends off the far side
- Intervene for the man being held: the man being held turns out to be a Passage runner for a Warren contact, and [[nona-black-jaw|Nona]] hears about it within a day

> [!mechanic]
> If combat: 1 Thug (leader, AC 11, HP 32, club +4, 2d4+2) and 2 Bandits (AC 12, HP 11, scimitar +3, 1d6+1). They do not fight to the death — at half HP the leader calls it off.

> [!dm]
> The Passage runner is named Orso. He is not a significant character. If the party helps him, Nona knows before they reach the Warren. She does not mention it first; she waits to see if they do.

## 3. The Chase

**Setup:** Someone has just lifted a purse — either from a party member or from a well-dressed merchant who immediately starts screaming. The thief is already moving, weaving into the Beffa crowd.

> [!read-aloud]
> A hand lifts clean from a coat pocket and disappears into the crowd. The merchant reaches for his purse and finds air. He spins in a full circle. Ten feet ahead of you, a small shape is already three bodies deep and not slowing down.

**The thief:** Fen, a teenage halfling, fast and small, working the festival crowds for three days. Not violent. Not part of any operation. Use Spy stats, +5 Stealth, +5 Acrobatics.

> [!mechanic]
> **Chase structure:** Three checks, each DC 12. Acrobatics for cutting through the crowd, Athletics for straightaways, Perception to keep eyes on. Two successes: cornered in a dead-end calle off Le Paludi. One success: Fen makes it to a canal and goes in the water (swim check, then either resurfaces in the Rialto or ducks into a maintenance hatch). Zero successes: gone.

**If cornered:** Fen has the purse, a folding knife she will not use, and a sob story that is about 40% true. DC 14 Insight to pick the true parts. The true part: she is paying off a debt to a Bridge district broker who found her working his territory.

> [!dm]
> Fen carries a maintenance hatch key she will not explain. It fits the sewer network below the Bridge connector. She doesn't know this — she stole it thinking it was worth something. It is worth something. Leave it in her possession unless the party is specifically looking for it. The key is a loaded gun for the [[warren-grung-sewers|Warren — Grung in the Sewers]] investigation.

## 4. A Man in the Canal

**Setup:** Late evening, narrow calle beside a Le Paludi canal. A man goes in — tripped by festival crowd surge, pushed by persons unknown, or stepped off a gangplank he misjudged. He is not swimming. The canal current runs under a low stone arch twelve feet away; past the arch it is dark and fast.

> [!read-aloud]
> The sound is wrong — not a splash, a drop. A man surfaces in the canal beside the walkway, already turned the wrong direction. He is not swimming. Twelve feet ahead, the canal narrows under a stone arch into the dark. The current is pulling.

> [!mechanic]
> Two rounds before he goes under the arch. DC 10 Athletics to swim out in round one; DC 12 in round two (current pulling). A rope thrown from the bank: DC 10 Dexterity to land it on him, DC 12 Strength to haul against the current. A boat pole, a Beffa banner pole, an awning rope — the canal side has options if someone looks (DC 8 Perception).

**Who he is (roll or choose):**

| d4 | Identity | Consequence |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | A Tessarine factor's ledger clerk, drunk on festival wine | His employer gives the party a minor factor discount as thanks — 10% off one Calveno merchant purchase |
| 2 | A Dravosi junior watch officer, off duty and embarrassed | He owes the party a favour he cannot officially acknowledge; the party has his name |
| 3 | A Warren Kin runner, Passage courier | Nona hears. The party's Tangle credit goes up without them knowing why |
| 4 | The thief from encounter 3, if she ran into the canal during the chase | She still has the purse |

> [!dm]
> Use the watch-and-do-nothing outcome only if it creates interesting character pressure, not as punishment.

## 5. Papers at the Gate

**Setup:** A Dravosi canal-gate checkpoint at the entry to the Arsenal district, staffed by one watch sergeant (Veteran) and four guards.

> [!read-aloud]
> The canal gate is a stone arch with a portcullis drawn halfway. A sergeant in Dravosi grey leans against the frame with a ledger. He looks at the party the way men who have been standing in one place for six hours look at things that require effort.

**He wants:** Ship registry or travelling papers. For the *Uncertainty*: the new Calveno private-vessel papers (if Perrin arranged them through the Warren) or an explanation. For PCs on foot: guild affiliation, residential address, or a credible reason to be at the Arsenal end during a festival.

**Delmar:** Any Dravosi officer who has served on this class of cutter will clock the *Uncertainty*'s silhouette. The sergeant has not — he is Canal Commission, not Navy. But DC 14 Charisma (Deception) to maintain the Fisk persona under direct questioning; the sergeant asks the same question twice, slightly differently.

**Jean-Claude:** DC 12 Deception with working papers. DC 15 without. If the sergeant has received a flag notice for a deserter matching his description: DC 17, disadvantage.

> [!mechanic]
> **If it goes wrong:** The sergeant calls for two more guards (now 6 total) and asks the party to step to the side. Window for talking out of this closes after three rounds; after that he sends for a lieutenant. Sergeant: Veteran stats (AC 17, HP 58, multiattack, +5 to hit). Guards: AC 16, HP 11, spear +3. The sergeant will not draw first; he will call for backup.

> [!dm]
> This works best as a papers check the party can pass with preparation, not a fight they stumble into. If Perrin handled the Warren ship papers, this is where that investment pays off cleanly.

## 6. The Festival Drunk

**Setup:** The Mercatura, mid-afternoon, festival crowd. A very large half-orc in a sellsword's rig is at the centre of a growing circle of space. He has knocked over a wine stall and is currently singing something in what might be Orcish.

> [!read-aloud]
> A ring of empty space has opened in the Mercatura crowd. At the centre of it, a half-orc in a sellsword's rig holds a wine bottle in one hand and his sword — still sheathed — in the other. A wooden stall is on its side behind him. The stall owner is looking at you.

> [!mechanic]
> **Gost:** Berserker stats, CR 2, AC 13, HP 67, greataxe +5, 2d12+3. Not violent yet. Watch is four minutes away.

**What the party can do:**
- Talk him down: DC 13 Charisma (Persuasion) or DC 10 Intimidation. Jean-Claude telling him the watch is coming and this is going to be a bad four minutes: automatic success.
- Get him moving: DC 10 if someone offers to buy him another drink somewhere quieter. No fight, no watch.
- Wait for the watch: Gost ends up in a holding cell.
- Let it escalate: he throws the bottle; now there are two problems.

**If he fights:** Reckless Attack, no Danger Sense (too drunk). First two rounds he is at disadvantage on saves; after that the adrenaline is burning the alcohol.

> [!dm]
> Gost is looking for work. If the party handled this without bloodshed and without the watch, he finds them the next morning and offers his sword at going rate (4–6 gp/week). If they never follow up, he disappears into the festival. He is a loose hiring hook, not a required NPC.

## Combat Escalations — Jean-Claude Arc

These three encounters connect to the handler cell plot. Unlike the general encounters above, they require setup: Jean-Claude active in the city, or the party already moving on the [[warren-marrows-crate|Marrow's Crate]] / [[warren-ferrin-locke|Ferrin Locke]] investigation. Do not drop them cold.

---

### Handler Cell

**Setup:** Jean-Claude spots what looks like handler reconnaissance — a pair of Grung in nondescript trade clothes asking systematic questions about boat shifts, bridge crush timing, or which yard workers sleep aboard. The questions are wrong for tourists. They are exactly right for a blue-caste handler mapping a captive extraction route.

He is the only one in the party who recognises it.

> [!read-aloud]
> Two figures in trade clothes at the same Bridge pillar Jean-Claude clocked earlier — different position now, same posture. They are not watching the performer. They are watching the crowd move around the performer. The hour is wrong for the spot.

**Trigger:** Jean-Claude's player acts on the suspicion (confirms with Insight DC 13, or just acts on it). If the party engages, the handlers try to withdraw and break contact. If cornered, they fight.

**Location:** Le Paludi — narrow canal, 5 ft alleys, two boats tied at the nearest mooring. Poor light. Festival crowd two streets over.

> [!mechanic]
> **Blue-Caste Handler × 2.** HP 16, AC 13. Scout (MM p.349). Shortbow +4, 1d8+3 piercing + Grung Poison (DC 11 Con or Poisoned 1 min). Standing Leap 20 ft as part of movement. Breaks and runs at half HP.

> [!dm]
> One handler carries binding rope and a sealed wax message cylinder — blank on inspection, but the wax is pressed with a mark Jean-Claude recognises from Botukuri shipping labels. Stakes: capture alive = handler knows arrival timing and the extraction canal route, telling the Warren closes one extraction lane. Kill both = Simone does not learn the cell was made, one lane goes dark without explanation. Let them go = Simone learns the party is in Calveno; Jean-Claude's face is now a report.

---

### Serin Haut at Bay

**Setup:** The party has moved on the [[warren-marrows-crate|Marrow's Crate]] investigation and [[serin-haut|Haut]] has been made. She does not run to the Warren (she doesn't know where it is). She has a backup — a local hired thug watching her door — and she will fight to protect her room and her codebook.

> [!read-aloud]
> The corridor outside her room is three feet wide. Haut is at the far end of it, facing you. The door behind her is closed. A broad man with a club stands between you and her. There is no comfortable angle in this hallway.

**Trigger:** Party approaches Haut's lodgings without neutralising her first, or she has identified them and moved to intercept.

**Location:** The narrow corridor outside her rented room above a canal-facing notary's office.

> [!mechanic]
> **Serin Haut:** HP 27, AC 12. Spy (MM p.349). Concealed shortsword. 3 doses basic poison (ingestion DC 11, 2d6 poison on fail). +1 Deception. Uses Cunning Action to disengage and break line of sight. Below 10 HP she offers the codebook and cooperation in exchange for her life — genuine; she is a professional, not a martyr.
> **Hired backup:** HP 32, AC 11. Thug (MM p.350). Club +4, 2d4+2. Stays between Haut and the exit.

### Le Paludi Hired Muscle

**Setup:** The party has been asking loud questions, making deals that offended someone, or become conspicuous enough that someone in Calveno wants them to stop. Four thugs are waiting on the mooring where the party tied their shore boat.

> [!read-aloud]
> Four people on the mooring where you tied your boat. They are already facing your direction. Your shore boat is behind them. The one in front has not uncrossed his arms. The other three have.

**Trigger:** DM discretion. Use when the party has been visibly active in one district for too long, or when they've crossed a named Calveno figure.

**Location:** Canal-side mooring, night. Water on one side, wall on the other, a 10 ft walkway.

> [!mechanic]
> **Thug × 4.** HP 32, AC 11. Pack Tactics (advantage if ally adjacent to target). Heavy crossbow on one.
> **Bandit Captain (optional).** HP 65, AC 15. Present if the sender wanted this done right.

> [!dm]
> Who sent them: leave unresolved. One of: [[iacopo-fieschi|Iacopo Fieschi]] (embarrassed by something the party made public), a broker whose client is the Red Lady salvage commission, or a Dravosi commercial factor who noticed them at the wrong time. Stakes: take a prisoner = learn who's watching; win without one = someone spent money on this; flee = the boat is still there, with the muscle standing on it.
