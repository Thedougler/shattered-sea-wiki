# Il Gioco delle Beffe — DM Notes

*Public lore: [[il-gioco-delle-beffe|Il Gioco delle Beffe]]*
*Current status: Registration opens tomorrow morning at the Mercatura bridge.*

---

## Situation

The Beffa is a five-day festival structured as a multi-phase team skill challenge. Every party member contributes through their own best skills. The money is real. The social capital is better. The fun comes from asking each player what *their character* does — then assigning skills to match.

The multi-day execution window (Days 2–4) is the core design element. Marks are nervous for three full days. Teams can strike early, wait, or false-alarm their mark first. That uncertainty is the game.

---

## The Team Symbol

On Day One, the party registers their symbol with the committee. Let the players design it — a sigil, a stamp, a wax seal shape, a color band, anything. It belongs to them.

Mechanically, the symbol is proof of authorship. The team must leave it at the scene for the prank to count. How they leave it is their problem and part of the fun. It should be the last thing the mark finds — not a tip-off, not a clue, but a signature delivered after the fall.

**Beffa doppia (optional):** a rival team can attempt to steal the party's registered symbol and place it on their own prank. Requires knowing the symbol (possible if they observed registration) and a contested Stealth check against the party's Perception during Phase 2. If it works, the committee adjudicates. The rules on this are historically contested and the committee enjoys the chaos.

## Phase Structure

### Phase 1 — La Scelta: Design (Day 1)

*No checks. Pure roleplay.*

The party picks their mark, designs their concept, and registers their symbol. The key question is not "what is the prank" but **"what does each character do?"**

| What they say | Likely check |
|---|---|
| "I scout the mark's daily routine" | Investigation or Perception |
| "I create a distraction in the market" | Performance or Deception |
| "I acquire a prop without being noticed" | Sleight of Hand or Stealth |
| "I plant false information in advance" | Deception or Persuasion |
| "I build a physical element" | Tool check or Athletics |
| "I recruit a civilian accomplice" | Persuasion |
| "I research the mark's weaknesses" | History or Insight |
| "I use magic for preparation" | Arcana or direct spell |

**Declare scale:** Minor (DC 12), Notable (DC 14), Legendary (DC 17, 50 gp stake). The party decides.

---

### Phase 2 — La Preparazione: Setup (Days 2–4, any day chosen)

*Each party member makes one check based on Phase 1 commitments.*

The party decides when during the window to strike. Earlier = less risk of sabotage; later = more time to prep and a more relaxed mark.

**Complications on failure:**
- A prop is wrong → disadvantage on one Phase 3 check
- The mark spotted something → raise Phase 3 DC by 2
- A rival team noticed → they attempt a counter move
- A committee adjudicator observed more than intended → pointed question on judgment day

**Rival interference:** one rival team may attempt to sabotage preparation. Contested Stealth vs. Perception. If the rival succeeds, assign a complication. If the party succeeds, they identify the rival — and earn a free sabotage action against them in Phase 3.

**False-alarm gambit:** the party may deliberately tip off their mark on an early day to lower their guard before the real strike. Deception check (DC = mark's Wisdom + 2). Success: mark's Phase 3 save at disadvantage. Failure: mark is now actively suspicious — DC raised by 3.

---

### Phase 3 — L'Esecuzione: The Strike (chosen day)

*Each party member makes one live-pressure check. The mark makes a Wisdom save. The symbol must be placed.*

**The mark's save:** DC = 8 + number of party successes in Phase 2.
- Fails: completely fooled. Scores 4–5.
- Succeeds by 1–4: partially fooled, something felt off. Scores 2–3.
- Succeeds by 5+: spotted the trick. Scores 0–1. The prank still proceeds — craft and recovery can still win it.

**Party checks:** each player makes the execution version of their Phase 2 role. Failure = something goes visibly wrong — crowd notices, judges note it.

**Placing the symbol:** one party member must place the team symbol during or at the end of the prank, without tipping off the mark prematurely. Sleight of Hand or Deception, DC 12. Failure: symbol found too early, reducing Surprise by 1.

**Recovery check:** after the prank, one party member walks it back. Performance or Persuasion (Deception or Medicine as alternates). Good recovery is worth up to 2 extra craft points from the committee.

## Rival Teams

Present their results at sundown on Day Five. You do not need to play them out — just describe what happened.

**The Candle Brothers** — two humans, twelve consecutive entries, four second-place finishes. Technically brilliant, emotionally inert. They will score 13–15 regardless of their prank.

**Serena Voss** — a halfling factor who entered on a dare, going for the Tessarine factor as her mark. No team. No experience. One very good idea. Roll her score secretly: 2d6+4. If she rolls high, the party has real competition — and she wants to buy everyone drinks regardless of result.

**Party Unknown** — three people in matching grey coats, registered under a name nobody recognizes, declared "Compound / Legendary," and refused to state their mark. Roll their score at Day Five: 1d20. On 1–8: disqualified (the crowd loves it). On 9–14: score 11. On 15–19: score 16. On 20: score 19 — the party needs to beat or tie this to win.

## Running Notes

**Ask what each character does, not what the plan is.** The party's plan is their business. Your job is to assign a skill to whatever they commit to in Phase 1 and hold them to it in Phase 2.

**Play the mark.** When the prank lands, embody whoever they picked. Be genuinely fooled by good ideas. The mark's reaction is the scene.

**The window creates tension for free.** On Days 2 and 3, describe marks being jumpy, rivals seen in unexpected places, committee adjudicators moving through the district. The uncertainty is doing the work.

**The medallion matters more than the money.** In Calveno, the Beffa Medallion is social proof that you are clever enough to deceive people in public and charming enough to get away with it. The Council cannot buy it. [[iacopo-fieschi|Iacopo Fieschi]] will mention it to [[cosimo-verantio|Cosimo]]. [[prospero-morsani|Morsani]] will nod at the party across the Mercatura, and the nod will mean something.
