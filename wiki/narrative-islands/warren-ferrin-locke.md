# Warren — Ferrin Locke

## Island Premise

Three Passage surface operations have been intercepted in the last three months. A pattern exists. The Tangle identified a canal broker named Faro as a common thread; Faro leads to Ferrin Locke, who has been feeding technically accurate but slightly delayed route timing to a Dravosi intelligence handler under Tessarine cover. Ferrin is not a traitor — he is a frightened man with an old smuggling conviction being squeezed by someone who knows exactly how to hold it over him. He wants to stop. He does not know how to stop safely.

---

## Strong Start

Deliver through a Warren contact or Nona directly: *"We have three intercepts in three months. They share a canal broker named Faro. Faro's clients include a man named Ferrin Locke — chandlery factor in Le Paludi, mid-tier, clean reputation except for a conviction from twelve years ago. Find out if Locke is the leak and what it costs to close it."*

The Tangle does not specify how. They want it stopped and they want the handler identified.

## The Investigation

**Step one: broker Faro.** Canal district inquiry on the three intercept routes (DC 12, Tangle provides the route details). Succeeds: Faro is a known mid-level broker who handled logistics for all three operations. He works from a canal-side office in Le Paludi.

**Step two: Faro to Locke.** Confronting or surveilling Faro (DC 10 — he is not a trained intelligence asset and Insight DC 10 reads his discomfort immediately). He will give up Locke under mild pressure. He does not know Locke is a source — he only knows Locke asked for route details on behalf of a client.

**Step three: Ferrin Locke.** Ferrin is a broad-shouldered human, early forties, runs a minor chandlery factor from a waterfront office in Le Paludi. He has a wife and a daughter. He has the look of someone who has not slept well in three months.

> [!read-aloud]
> A waterfront office in Le Paludi, facing the canal. No nameplate on the door. It opens before the third knock — Ferrin Locke, broad-shouldered, early forties, already stepping back to let you in. He has the look of a man who expected someone to come eventually and is not relieved that they did.

He will not volunteer anything. He is aware he is being watched and that watching him is how someone finds Venn.

- **If offered a way out:** he talks freely. DC 10 Insight to confirm he is telling the truth.
- **If pressured:** DC 15 Persuasion or Intimidation. He talks, but he is shaking, and everything he says is accurate.
- **If treated with basic human decency:** DC 13 Insight to read how frightened he is. After that, he talks without needing a roll.

**What he knows:**
- Handler's name: Petra Venn, posing as a Tessarine trade arbitration consultant. Meets him every nine days at a coffeehouse in Le Paludi.
- What he provides: route timing for Passage surface operations, gathered passively through Faro and two other brokers who do not know they are sources.
- The conviction: smuggling, twelve years ago, petty and resolved. Venn has documentation. He does not believe the Crown will be lenient if she surfaces it.
- The thing he did not report to Venn: approximately four months ago, Venn gave him a standing query — flag any Grung passenger travelling with false documentation through Calveno's broker network. He noticed. He said nothing to her. He says it now.

## Jean-Claude Hook

Venn's Grung passenger query is months old and unfulfilled. If Jean-Claude is involved in this scene and Ferrin treats him as trustworthy, Ferrin mentions it unprompted. A Dravosi intelligence handler has been looking for a Grung traveller with false papers. She did not say why. She asked four months ago and has not asked again, which Ferrin finds more unnerving, not less.

## Petra Venn

Dravosi intelligence attaché. Tessarine trade arbitration consultant cover. Operating in Calveno four months. Professional, patient, not violent by preference.

**Venn's next check-in:** 9 days from when the party begins asking questions. If the party moves slowly, she arrives at the coffeehouse and reads that something has changed. If the party is fast, they can intercept her on her terms or on theirs.

> [!read-aloud]
> A canal-facing coffeehouse in Le Paludi. Corner table, window seat. The woman in trade arbitration clothes has a document open in front of her that she has not turned in twenty minutes. When the door opens, her eyes move to it before anything else.

**Venn's lodgings** (locatable via Ferrin's description, DC 12 Le Paludi inquiry): a rented room above a canal-facing notary's office. Strongbox contents: codebook (500 gp value to Tangle or rivals), 420 gp operational coin, list of three non-Passage informants, and Ferrin Locke's signed cooperation agreement.

> [!dm]
> If the party already found Haut's room via [[warren-marrows-crate|Warren — Marrow's Crate]], Ferrin's cooperation agreement is already in hand — confirming his account and giving the party leverage without needing to locate Venn separately.

## Outcome Options

| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| Expose Locke to the Tangle | Leak stops; Ferrin disappears non-lethally (Passage handles it); 300 gp and trust |
| Help Locke escape Calveno | Protected contact outside the network; Passage owes a debt for the loose end they didn't have to clean up |
| Neutralize Venn before the check-in | Leak stops at source; codebook and operational coin available; Ferrin is free |
| Turn Venn over to the Tangle | As above; Passage handles the rest; they will not say what happens to her |
| Sell codebook to a third party | 500 gp; Venn notices within a week; Passage eventually asks where it came from |
| Nothing — let the 9-day clock expire | Venn concludes the network is compromised, closes Ferrin, and goes quiet; the Tangle loses the thread and operations exposure continues through a new asset |

## Loaded Guns

- Ferrin's wife and daughter are not leverage unless someone makes them leverage. They are in Le Paludi. This is enough context.
- The Grung passenger query is months old and unfulfilled. Venn has moved on to other priorities. But the query existed and Dravosi records are not deleted — it is still somewhere.
- If Venn and [[serin-haut|Serin Haut]] are both active in Calveno, they are aware of each other but not running coordinated operations. The party bumping into both may cause one or both to accelerate their timelines.

---

## Clocks

> [!mechanic]
> **Venn check-in:** 9 days from inquiry start. Tick at day 3, day 6, day 9. On day 9 she arrives at the coffeehouse.
> **Ferrin's nerve:** each day the party delays after meeting him, he considers going directly to Venn. He won't — he's too frightened — but his fear is visible if they check in on him.

## Player Choices

- Extract Ferrin first, then find Venn (safe approach; Venn may go quiet before they reach her)
- Find Venn first, then deal with Ferrin (faster; riskier; Venn may have already made Ferrin)
- Use Ferrin as bait for Venn's next check-in (elegant; Ferrin needs to agree; his wife is in Le Paludi)
- Give the Tangle the name and step back (clean; the Tangle handles it; the party gets credit without knowing what happened)

---

## Exit States

- **Resolved:** leak closed, Venn neutralised or turned over, Ferrin's situation settled. Tangle registers the close.
- **Complicated:** Ferrin protected but Venn escapes; or Venn neutralised but Ferrin exposed; one loose end remains.
- **Ignored:** Venn's check-in arrives, she reads the changed situation, closes the operation, and starts over with a new asset. The intercepts stop for a few weeks, then resume.
