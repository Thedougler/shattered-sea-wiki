# Warren — Marrow's Crate

## Island Premise

Dren, a Dry runner who handled cargo for the Warren's broker network, delivered a crate to Marrow's three weeks ago and never returned. His body was found near the Harbourmaster's Office ten days ago, ruled accidental drowning. The crate is still on Marrow's shelf, its consignment window expiring. The crate contains a letter that implicates House Vretek and Commendant Alessi Brol in feeding Passage cargo timing to Tessarine merchants. The person who killed Dren is still watching the canal approaches.

---

## Strong Start

> [!read-aloud]
> Marrow is already watching when you step down into the alcove. He weighs something on his brass scale, sets it aside, and folds his hands. "Three weeks on the shelf," he says. "Policy is three weeks. I'll pay fifty gold for the intended recipient or for the person who can tell me what's in it." He does not say what happens at the end of the week.

Ask the party what they do first. Marrow will answer direct questions with direct answers — he does not know what is in the crate, he does not know who sent it or who it was for, and he did not ask. He knows Dren, he knows Dren is dead, and he knows the crate has to move.

## The Investigation

The party has three starting threads. They do not need to use all three.

**Canal inquiry.** Dren was a known runner in Le Paludi. DC 12 — dock workers, ferryman, or tavern regulars can place Dren on the canal that night. On a success: a canal worker saw Dren arguing with a woman in a green coat near the Harbourmaster's Office, thirty minutes before the drowning was estimated.

**Watch records.** DC 15 — official records from the district watch office confirm drowning verdict and list one witness: a canal hand who "heard a splash." The witness cannot be located. On a success or a 5 gp bribe (reduces DC to 12): the watch log notes a "foreign-trade enquiry woman" who identified the body and then left without signing the record book.

**Employers.** DC 13 — tracking Dren's other regular clients in the canal district turns up two legitimate deliveries that week and one unusual job: a pickup from a boat tied at the outer basin, unusual time, unusual route. The employer for that pickup is not a name anyone knows.

**The woman in the green coat.** Once the party has this detail from any source, a second inquiry in Le Paludi (DC 12) identifies her as a regular at a specific canal-side coffeehouse — a Tessarine trade arbitration consultant who has been in Calveno four months.

## Serin Haut

Haut is a Dravosi intelligence attaché operating under Tessarine trade-assessor cover. She has been watching the canal approaches for ten days, monitoring for anyone who shows interest in the crate or Dren's death. She is patient and experienced. She is not watching from one fixed position.

> [!mechanic]
> **Serin Haut:** Spy (MM p.349), +1 Deception, concealed shortsword, 3 doses basic poison (ingestion, DC 11 Con save, 2d6 poison damage on fail).

> [!dm]
> Haut will not engage in the Warren — she does not know where it is. She engages in Le Paludi if she believes the party has the crate or can identify her. If the party makes two or more canal inquiries in the same day, she begins following the last person to ask questions. Passive Perception DC 14 to notice the tail. If noticed, she makes no hostile move — she stops following and changes her route. She reports the development and waits.

**Her room:** if the party locates her lodgings (Persuasion DC 13 with a Le Paludi contact, or Dren's employer thread leads there): a locked strongbox holds a codebook worth 500 gp to the Tangle or to Tessarine rivals, 420 gp operational coin, a list of three non-Passage informants in Calveno's canal district, and Ferrin Locke's signed cooperation agreement (see [[warren-ferrin-locke|Warren — Ferrin Locke]]).

## The Crate

Sealed with Dren's wax and courier notation. Inside:
- A sealed letter implicating House Vretek and Commendant Alessi Brol in selling Passage cargo timing windows to Tessarine factor [[iacopo-fieschi|Iacopo Fieschi]]'s advance agents.
- 280 gp in Tessarine silver.
- A secondary House Vretek signet ring worth 120 gp as jewelry or considerably more as evidence.

The letter is addressed to no one. It was meant to be collected by a Tangle intermediary who has since gone quiet.

## Loaded Guns

- Ferrin Locke's cooperation agreement is in Haut's strongbox. The party may find it before they find Ferrin, which makes [[warren-ferrin-locke|Warren — Ferrin Locke]] faster to start.
- The Vretek signet ring is portable and recognizable. Anyone who handles it in the wrong company will be asked where it came from.
- Iacopo Fieschi — Tessarine envoy in Calveno — is connected to this operation. The letter names his advance agents. He is also a Beffa mark and a sitting Council factor. These facts are separate. They can be connected.

## Player Choices

- Claim the crate and deal with Haut (direct; the exposure risk is immediate)
- Identify Haut before approaching the crate (safer; requires the investigation path)
- Let the crate expire and tail whoever Marrow hands it to (possible; Marrow will not confirm or deny)
- Open the letter and use the contents directly (political; multiple outcome branches)

---

## Exit States

- **Resolved:** crate handled, Haut neutralised or driven off, letter delivered or burned. The Tangle knows the outcome.
- **Complicated:** letter surfaces but Haut escapes; or letter is held and not used; Vretek enmity or Brol paranoia activates later.
- **Ignored:** Marrow moves the crate himself at week's end. The Tangle loses the letter and does not know who has it. Haut concludes no one is coming and departs Calveno.
