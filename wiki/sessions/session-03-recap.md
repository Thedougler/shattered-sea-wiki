# 03 — Getting in on the Joke

---

[[perrin-black-jaw|Perrin]] was on watch. The storm had thinned to hard sideways rain and the water below the rail gave nothing back. For one breath the black surface held still. His knees buckled. He went somewhere else.

The pressure arrived first — fathoms of it, cold and absolute. Beneath his feet: worked stone, worn smooth, channels cut in long deliberate lines toward the dark. Around him: pale bioluminescent things drifting in loose constellations, completely unbothered. He breathed. His chest didn't move. Something in the space turned its attention toward him. Not hunting. Curious.

Then his body wasn't his. Sixty feet of muscle. A barbed tail, ancient. His skin read the water in frequencies he couldn't name. Above, through dark fathoms: a hull, and the smell of blood. Below: something longer than two ships end to end, moving in an unhurried arc. His instincts were sharpened over generations to hunt exactly this. He dove. He drove the barb in and pulled. The whale sounded into the dark and took him with it.

He was back at the bottom. Back to his own size. He watched from below as the shape rose — fast, the lower lobe streaming — and the hull above him exploded inward. Then, at enormous cost, one word burned into his mind:

*"Grow."*

His knees finished buckling. One second had passed.

---

[[perrin-black-jaw|Perrin]] was thirty feet up the rigging. He rolled a one.

[[jean-claude-tabarnack|Jean-Claude]] was within ten feet. He lashed out with his tongue, braced himself, and arrested approximately none of it. He came down with Perrin, both tangled together in a fall they hadn't finished taking.

[[crissdalynn-khinriss|Crissdalynn]] came off the crow's nest and caught both of them before they hit the deck.

**Crissdalynn:** *(setting Perrin down carefully)* It's okay. I only eat rats.

The first impact struck the ship from below.

The shark was over sixty feet long. Longer than the ship itself. Perrin climbed three rungs of rigging and raised his voice above the rain.

**Perrin:** Listen to me, everybody. This here's a whip shark. Big long fucker. I know everything about it. Get back.

He gave bardic inspiration to all three of them and started to drum.

[[delmar-fisk|Delmar]] put all the guns on it simultaneously — two swivels and the broadside in one coordinated shot. Red mist burst from the water. The shark dove and came up angrier. *"You're not even food,"* Delmar told it, leveling the blunderbuss. *"You're the food that my food eats."*

Jean-Claude put an arrow in it from the rigging. The shark studied him from the waterline, then swept its tail across the deck and opened his chest. He held on by one roll. Perrin's song in the rigging reminded him what happened if he didn't, and he held on.

Round three. Crissdalynn flew out over the water, seized sixty feet of shark, and lifted it clean out of the sea. Contested strength. The shark rolled a natural one.

She held it there, the creature thrashing, its entire underside exposed.

Jean-Claude watched from the deck. Bowstring drawn.

*"She's holding it by the tail. The anus is basically a bullseye. If I fire directly into it, the arrow could conceivably come out its mouth."*

He released. It went in the back door and came out the other end.

The thrashing stopped. The fight was over.

*"Turns out,"* Jean-Claude said, *"the only way to kill a shark is to shoot it in the butt. That's all it takes."*

Delmar's voice carried up from the gun deck:

*"We have learned much today."*

[[wiki/situations/islands/shepherd-grigori|Shepherd Grigori]] came up from below carrying tins of pale healing liquid for the crew. He found Jean-Claude and extended his hand. A red, viscous light flowed from his wrist and settled into the wound. Flesh knit together, perfectly, silently. No components. No words. No incantation. He said nothing else and went back below.

Perrin rolled an 18 on arcana and still couldn't place it.

Delmar dove in manta ray form and retrieved the shark's barb. A four-foot spike of bone and cartilage, serrated on every edge but the base. The party also cut loose scales for hull patching, pocketed the teeth, and someone took the meat. Jean-Claude recovered three fertilized eggs from the body.

**Jean-Claude:** We're going to a port. We have fertilized whip shark eggs. Do you know what that means?

---

Four days. Long gray swells. [[old-faas|Old Faas]] showed Perrin the ropes and told stories about the old days running cargo with [[barnaby-rook|Rook]]. It turned out the old man was built like a brick shithouse, faster up the rigging than any rat. Jean-Claude and [[sem-holst|Sem Holst]] worked the hull in companionable silence: Jean-Claude casting Mending, Sem quietly fixing what he missed without mentioning it. [[crissdalynn-khinriss|Crissdalynn]] dug through Rook's chart archive and found, among other things, that he had broken Imperial rules about the [[the-drowned-maw|Drowned Maw]] twice and kept no record of what he found there.

Delmar ran ship's administration in his Admiral Fisk form. Long enough that his hair sat wrong and his cadence shifted and he called Crissdalynn *Crystalline.* She handed him her flask. He drank. The brown faded back toward red.

**Delmar:** *Thank you, Miss Khinriss. You're right. I was acting a little strange, wasn't I?*

He went back to training [[geoffrey-draves|Geoffrey]], calling him Mr. James.

Day two, Crissdalynn spotted it first from the crow's nest: a glowing outline in the water, moving slowly on a parallel heading. Perrin climbed to the rigging. He recognized it. He watched it for a long time.

---

*"Land ho."*

[[calveno|Calveno]] was a very, very busy port. Flying Crown colours up the main approach in a stolen government ship would draw eyes. Perrin knew the other way in.

The name question came up on the approach.

**Crissdalynn:** I vote *Uncertainty.*

**Jean-Claude:** Drop the prefix. Just *Uncertainty*, private vessel. HCS would be a target.

**Crissdalynn:** I like the idea it comes out naturally — they ask the name, someone says we're uncertain, and it just gets written down.

They agreed. The [[hcs-surety|HCS Surety]] became the *Uncertainty* somewhere in the approach channel.

[[la-vasca|La Vasca]] — a covered dry dock, Black-Jaw colors — had a ship's cradle already waiting. [[cobb|Cobb]], a stocky brown rat Perrin had known for years, was at the water's edge. Perrin shook his hand, said it was all sorted, and bolted for the door without finishing the sentence. One look back at the group:

*"You guys fucking handle the ship. I'm going."*

As Delmar stepped from the gangplank onto shore, the water reached up toward him for a moment, and he thought he heard his name on the wind. Then he was on land and it was gone.

[[wiki/situations/islands/shepherd-grigori|Shepherd Grigori]] came down after. He shook Delmar's hand. The cold hit first. Deep cold, nothing to do with the dock or the season. He thanked the crew and said his business in the city would take a week or two. Perhaps their paths would cross again. He left.

---

Jean-Claude changed one thing before heading into the city: he was green instead of blue. Festival preparations were already underway; people were half in costume. He drew far less attention than usual. He found his way to [[casa-lupo|Casa Lupo]] and inquired about a shortbow. The shopkeeper pointed him toward [[studio-orsini|Studio Orsini]] for the egg.

**Jean-Claude:** *(producing the egg)* I fertilized it myself. The infant will have multiple extra muscles.

Delmar and Crissdalynn were stopped in the [[le-paludi|Mercatura district]] by an older woman in blue-gray robes: white hair, slight limp, a small leather book. She had found Delmar specifically. Umberlee's Bitch Queen had a message, and she would be at the [[waveservant-shrine|Waveservant Shrine]] all day, and she was not sleeping until he came. Delmar rolled a 19 on deception and told her that Umberlee herself had required this errand first. She believed him and left. Once she was gone, he told [[geoffrey-draves|Geoffrey]] they were not going to a second location alone.

Crissdalynn had attempted to scout the city from above, found more people than she had ever seen in one place, and given up. She found Delmar instead.

---

[[le-paludi|Le Paludi]]. [[nona-black-jaw|Nona]]'s kitchen was warm. She was at the stove on her stool, back to the door, holding an enormous spatula. [[enzo|Enzo]] leaned against the far wall; [[ruk|Ruk]] stood near the door. A rattkin mother was just finishing at the table. She was crying. Asking Nona what to do about a son who hadn't come home. *Ruk: "Don't worry. Nona will take care of it."* Nona: *"Yes. Yes."*

She looked up. She saw Perrin.

She waited until the other family had gone.

**Nona:** *Peron. What does it mean when a rat king doesn't meet their check-in?*

Perrin told her about the Vestra. Nona's hand tightened on the wooden spoon until it cracked. She discarded the piece and kept stirring.

**Nona:** *I don't care about the Vestra, Peron. I do not care about Vincenzo's boat. I miss him dearly, but we lost so much money on that stupid talking boat. I am glad it is at the bottom of the ocean. Now you, though — how many men must we kill to put its head on my wall?*

He tried to explain the Leviathan to her. The water boiling, the lightning, and then nothing. He did not say plainly that there were no other survivors. She understood it anyway. She told [[enzo|Enzo]] to make sure the Vestra's crew had consolation.

Then she understood what she'd assumed when he stopped checking in. What she'd already set in motion.

**Nona:** *Enzo — before you go. Call off the attacks. I thought you were captured.*

Delmar had locked his Dravosi red coat in the captain's chest before coming ashore.

Nona had a favor to ask. She hadn't said what it was.

**Perrin:** For you, Nona? Absolutely.

She gave him a sending stone.

---

Later that afternoon, Crissdalynn and Delmar and Jean-Claude were back on the streets of Le Paludi. The wind went quiet. The afternoon sun was high and behind them.

Something had the sun behind it. It dove in silence. Crissdalynn was knocked back twenty feet, two arms around her from the front, the wind out of her chest.

She recognized the smell before she could see.

**Kyzil:** *Oh my God, Priscilla, you're alive. Thank God.*

He straightened himself. Looked at the others.

**Kyzil:** *Hi, I'm — I'm [[master-kyzil|Master Kyzil]]. Nice to meet you. What happened?*

Crissdalynn broke down.

**Kyzil:** *I'm so glad you survived. I heard the ship was down. A foul wind came from the south. I looked through [[kalowe|Kalowe]] for you. I looked in [[tidefall|Tidefall]]. I can't believe you ended up here.*

Perrin had arrived at some point and seen a large owl-like figure dive-bomb Crissdalynn. He was currently in a barrel. He emerged very slowly.

**Perrin:** Perrin's the name. That's all you need to know. Nice to meet ya.

Kyzil slowly knelt and extended one finger.

**Kyzil:** *It is a pleasure to make your acquaintance, Master Perrin.*

Perrin touched the finger.

**Perrin:** Yeah.
