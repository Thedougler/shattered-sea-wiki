# Governor's Seat — Voss and the Sealed Orders

## Situation

Governor Aldric Voss, 7 years in post at [[port-tidefall|Port Tidefall]], is boxed out of his own fort. Six weeks ago two extra companies arrived under sealed Admiralty orders he was not given. He sent three requests for clarification to the Admiralty — none answered. He is willing to trade access and information for Admiralty-level leverage: specifically, someone who can explain what is being staged from his port.

---

## Current State

The sealed orders remain in [[fort-crestwall|Fort Crestwall]] under the Commandant's lock. The supply manifest includes reef-diving equipment and underwater charges — destination: [[the-drowned-maw|Drowned Maw]]. The orders are routed through the Maw Surveillance Directorate, a body with no official charter Voss can find.

**Delmar Fisk's Admiral name is still in the guard rotation** — gate officer Crowe never updated it. The door opens on his name alone.

---

## What Voss Can Provide

In exchange for Admiralty-level leverage or intelligence:
- Name of the officer who delivered the sealed orders
- Supply manifest addendum (reef-diving equipment, underwater charges — destination: Drowned Maw)
- A letter of introduction that functions as a [[fort-crestwall|Fort Crestwall]] pass

---

## DC Gates

- DC 13 Persuasion: get the manifest addendum without full quid pro quo
- DC 16 Deception: bluff past guards without using Fisk's name

---

## DM Notes

Voss is not hostile — he is frustrated and outmaneuvered. He represents the institutional Crown that bureaucracy has left behind. A party that treats him as a trade partner rather than an obstacle will find him cooperative.

The Maw Surveillance Directorate is the lead worth pulling on. It has no official charter Voss can find — which means it is either very new, very classified, or operating under a different authority structure than the Admiralty he knows.
