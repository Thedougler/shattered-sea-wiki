# Maw Pearl Crisis

## Situation

The [[pearl-of-souls|Pearl of Souls]] is intact inside the *[[red-lady|Red Lady]]* wreck at the Shelfworks eastern edge, below [[umberlee|Umberlee]]'s domain boundary. It broadcasts a soul-collection signal across the planar fissure, drawing entities from the Elemental Plane of Water into the mortal sea. Removing it does not close the fissure, but it removes the attractor.

---

## Current State

The fissure is geological and widening slowly. Under normal conditions it would leak elemental water and occasional passive drift. The Pearl changed the behaviour: the [[leviathan|Leviathan]] followed the signal, a Ridgeback followed the signal, and the next incursion will do the same unless the Pearl is moved or silenced.

[[auralis|Auralis]] has shifted from passive watch to active containment. His old wards are failing under the Pearl's beacon and the elemental surge. Umberlee cannot retrieve the Pearl because the wreck sits below her jurisdiction, so she is keeping [[delmar-fisk|Delmar Fisk]] alive and pressured as her proxy.

---

## Delmar's Secret

[[delmar-fisk|Delmar Fisk]] stole the Pearl from [[umberlee|Umberlee]]'s Blue Shrine at [[vel-orn|Vel Orn]] before the campaign began. When the fleet sank, Umberlee pressed five captains' souls into him — the five who stayed loyal. The five he could have saved.

**The secret Delmar carries:** The crime that triggered the sinking was his. He made the call. He didn't intend the outcome, but five people paid with their souls and he knows it.

**What Delmar wants:** Resurrect the five captains. If resurrection is impossible, release them so they can find their afterlives. Full resurrection is where his guardrails come off.

**If the party learns the secret:** The question is not whether they forgive him — the question is whether the five captains do.

> [!dm]
> Players do not know the full shape of this. Delmar knows he carries voices. The party does not know about the five captains or what Delmar did.

- **Auralis line fails:** upper tiers flood, the gold rush collapses, and the Antheri machine is tested openly.

## DM Notes

### Auralis and the Antheri Machine

Auralis is a manufactured demigod — the last Antheri consciousness fused into an immortal framework engineered to survive the trench bottom. The visible Shelfworks are the newest layer of a bottom-up machine: vents, pressure bleeds, and safety architecture. Salvagers are pulling valves off a pressurized planar system.

Checks at the Shelfworks:
- Perception DC 12: temperature drops and light changes colour below 80 ft.
- Arcana/History DC 14: lower channels are too regular for habitation and too large for plumbing.
- Investigation DC 15: salvage damage concentrates on flow/pressure-regulation structures.
- Nature DC 13 + Sentinel records: identify safer approach windows to the *Red Lady*.

Party level 4–5 should work around or time the danger, not fight the Leviathan.

### Umberlee Pressure

[[branca|Branca]] stays polite until Fisk stalls too long. The Dead Lady exists to remind him what is at stake, not to kill him. Umberlee needs Fisk functional — killing him risks losing the five captains and the Pearl permanently.

Waveservants will not discuss the theological problem: Umberlee does not own the deepest water in the known world.

### The Five Captains

Maren, Rael Corven, and three others are pressed into Delmar. Each has a thread that can surface via the mortis mechanic:
- [[maren|Maren]] — knows Halythion exists; personal lead from a sea elf friend.
- [[rael-corven|Rael Corven]] — his Cloak of Useful Things went down with the Red Lady; potentially recoverable.
