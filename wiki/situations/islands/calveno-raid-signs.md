# Calveno — Raid Prep Signs

The handlers are already in Calveno. These details are available whenever the party is in the right place — use one or two per session day, not all at once. None announce themselves as clues. They read as festival texture until they don't.

**Use these as ambient colour first.** Only give them weight if players reach for them. If no one notices, note it and move on.

---

## Location-Keyed Observations

**Ponte Bassa / Le Paludi canals**

- A pair of small figures in festival half-masks bought one length of rope from a canal supply stall and spent ten minutes watching the boat traffic before leaving without asking for change. The stall keeper mentions it if anyone buys rope — they were very specific about knowing which boatmen sleep aboard overnight.
- A Le Paludi local is grumbling that someone was measuring one of the storm-drain overflow hatches this morning with a knotted cord, called herself a Canal Commission inspector, and had no lantern. The hatch is in a section of the district that has no active maintenance flagged.
- A ferryman points out an access grate in the Le Paludi wall, now set slightly off its seating. He noticed it yesterday. It has not been reported because no one is sure whose job that is during festival week.

**The Bridge / Mercatura**

- A Beffa team registered two days ago under a name no one recognises. Their declared category is "Crowd / Notable." When the registration clerk asked their concept, one of them said: *"We want to know how many people you can move through the pinch point in one minute before the ones at the back lose sight of the ones at the front."* The clerk wrote it down verbatim — in two years of registration he had never received a crowd-compression timing query as a prank concept.
- A small figure in a short cloak has been standing at the same Bridge pillar on two separate mornings, at different hours. Not watching performers. Watching the crowd compress and expand at the narrowest point.

**Ponte Bassa / Moretti & Sons**

- Giacomo Moretti mentions, while filling an order, that a buyer this morning wanted to know which of his regular dock customers take their lunch break aboard their vessel and which come ashore. *"Bought nothing. Asked three questions about my customers and left."* He found it rude. He does not connect it to anything.

**Harbour approach / La Vasca**

- One regular Crown-adjacent commercial observer — a factor's representative who checks the south anchorage every morning and has done so for two years — did not appear on two consecutive days this week. Cobb, if asked, can name which two days. A harbour regular: *"He's never missed a morning run. Someone said his firm called him in. But his firm is closed for the festival."*
- Three small, low-profile fishing hulls arrived at anchor in the outer Calven Roads two nights ago. They are flying no pennant. They have not approached the harbour. They are sitting very still for fishing vessels.

**Velo (festival entertainment district)**

- A blue-caste Grung is watching a crowd performance from the edge of a Velo square. He is not enjoying it. He is watching how the crowd moves when a performer creates a scene — where people face, where the gaps open, how long it takes for a knot of onlookers to dissolve. Jean-Claude can read this immediately *(see Jean-Claude — Handler Recognition below)*.

## Jean-Claude — Handler Recognition

Jean-Claude was blue-caste. He ran capture operations. He knows the difference between a Grung enjoying a city and a Grung working one.

If Jean-Claude is in any public space, offer him one of these — once per day, quiet and unprompted. Do not offer more than one per session:

- **Crowd positioning.** A Grung at the Bridge or Velo is standing in the one position that gives them sightlines to three exits without appearing to watch any of them. Jean-Claude has stood in that position. It is not instinctive unless someone taught you to stand there.
- **Target assessment.** A small figure in festival colours near the harbour briefly looks at a working dockhand — takes in his build, his tools, whether he is wearing a guild mark — and looks away. It is not a glance. It is an appraisal. Jean-Claude has made that look hundreds of times.
- **Exit logic.** One of the trade-cover Grung in the city walks a specific Le Paludi route twice. Not shopping. Not visiting anyone. Walking the route at pace and marking the time. Jean-Claude recognises it: someone ran that route and handed the time to the handlers.

None of these are proof. All of them are dread.

> [!dm]
> Offer these as private sensory notes to Jean-Claude's player: *"That Grung's been standing in the exit-watch position for ten minutes."* Let the player decide what Jean-Claude does with it. Do not announce to the table.

## If Players Pull On Any Of This

Give the next layer, not the answer.

| They investigate... | What they find |
|---|---|
| The Canal Commission inspector with no lantern | No inspector matching the description is on duty this week. The hatch she was measuring connects to the main collector under the Mercatura. |
| The Beffa registration entry | The registered team name is a string of words in a Verdant Teeth dialect. Roughly: "the patience of still water." |
| The three anchored hulls | They are low-profile, sealed with dark resin, double-outrigged, and too clean for fishing. Anyone with Grung or Midchain maritime knowledge identifies them as *vethka*. They are sitting on the seaward side of the patrol minimum. |
| The missing Crown observer | His firm confirms they did not call him in. He has not reported to work. He is not at his rooms. His landlady says he left with a small bag three nights ago and seemed calm. |
| The Velo Grung watching the crowd | If approached, he is polite and says he is here for the festival. His Midchain accent is from Karath, not Veth. He has no trade goods, no festival registration, and no declared purpose for being this far west. |
