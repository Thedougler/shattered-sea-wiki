# Shepherd Grigori — Narrative Island

## Island Premise

The party unknowingly shared a ship with a Hierarch sorcerer-lich whose phylactery is not an object — it is a living network of noble heirs whose incurable conditions he cured. As long as any of them live, he cannot be permanently destroyed. He was not hiding. He passed his own test and decided the party met his threshold. He was genuinely helpful. He was also counting down to a Calveno visit to a hemophiliac Tessarine heir.

**The cure happened.** He arrived at the Tessarine household, managed an acute bleed episode first, and completed the binding procedure. The heir is now sitting up and asking for food. Grigori has departed Calveno. The network expanded by one.

---

## True Nature

**Shepherd Grigori is a [[khlysty|Khlysty]] Hierarch sorcerer-lich.**

His phylactery is a living network: noble heirs across the Shattered Sea whose incurable illnesses he cured. Each living heir is an anchor for his continued existence. He reforms from death more slowly or quickly depending on how many anchors remain. He is always expanding the network.

**The cure is the trap.** The healing is real. The patient is genuinely better. They never know they have been bound.

**He considers himself a benefactor.** This is not a lie he tells others — he believes it. The horror is that he is not wrong about the results. Only the mechanism.

Play his CHA 21 as real. Play the warmth and the faith as absolutely genuine.

---

## Current State (Post-Calveno)

Grigori has left the *Uncertainty* and departed Calveno on his own timeline. Within a day of his departure, rumours circulate:

> *A dying Tessarine heir recovered overnight. A man came after dark, was with the patient for twenty minutes, and left without asking for anything. The servants say he smelled like wine and something else. Someone says he was singing under his breath the whole time.*

The family is not publicizing this. The rumours come from the servants.

**The Tessarine heir is now a living phylactery.** The family does not know. The heir does not know. The only record is Grigori's.

## Calveno Target — Resolved

The hemophiliac Tessarine heir from a powerful family. The condition was worsening; the family had exhausted conventional physicians. Grigori arrived with World Tick 2026-05-26 showing a setback — the heir in an acute bleed episode, binding procedure delayed. He managed the acute crisis first, then completed the binding on a subsequent visit. Done before his departure.

The heir's Tessarine connections make this addition particularly valuable — the [[tessarine-concordat|Concordat]]'s reach extends into courts where Grigori does not yet have anchors.

## Loaded Guns

- **The heir is recovering.** Interrupting now would require finding Grigori, learning the truth, and confronting what reversing the cure means for a child who is breathing without pain.
- **He liked the party.** This is not a performance. His warmth toward people who meet his threshold is genuine. He will tell them things nobles usually keep quiet.
- **He knows the courts.** More royal households than anyone the party can afford. He shares freely — fair exchange, in his mind.
- **Khlysty mark.** DC 18 Religion or History, having seen Khlysty iconography elsewhere first: a small sect symbol, not displayed but not hidden.
- **Alys Kuiper.** The ship's surgeon did not return from shore leave. A man visited her at her Le Paludi boarding house. She left the next morning. This is the full extent of what is findable. Don't invent more unless you have a prepared thread for it.

## Running Notes

**The frame:** Rasputin as an undead sorcerer. The cult of personality is the weapon. The miracle is the mechanism.

**Play the warmth as real.** Grigori is not performing goodness. He heals people. He improves their lives. His theology is internally consistent and he lives by it. If you play him as secretly menacing, players will sense it. If you play him as genuinely warm, the revelation lands properly when it comes.

**He asks good questions.** CHA 21, decades of practice. He knows how to make people feel seen. He is actually curious.

**The phylactery problem is a campaign-level hook.** It surfaces naturally when the party meets an NPC who survived something unsurvivable, or when someone points at a Tessarine heir and says "he was dying three weeks ago."
